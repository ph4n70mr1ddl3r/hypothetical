## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the formal organizational structure of BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. ("BRHI" or "the Company"). It defines reporting relationships, functional responsibilities, delegation of authority, and governance mechanisms across all levels of the enterprise. Its objectives are to:

- Provide a single authoritative reference for the Company's organizational hierarchy and functional boundaries.
- Clarify direct reporting lines, dotted-line (advisory) relationships, and cross-functional coordination points.
- Establish consistent spans of control and management-layer standards across stores, distribution centers, and corporate functions.
- Support workforce planning, talent development, and succession management by documenting every major role and its scope.
- Enable new leadership and external partners to rapidly understand how the Company is structured and how decisions flow.
- Align the organizational structure with the Company's strategic priorities: customer obsession, operational excellence, associate development, and digital transformation.

This document is owned by the Chief Human Resources Officer and reviewed annually by the Executive Leadership Team. Any structural changes—new departments, reorganizations, or title standardization—must be submitted through the Organizational Change Request (OCR) process administered by HR Business Partners and approved at the appropriate executive level.

## 2. Executive Leadership Team (C-Suite)

The Executive Leadership Team (ELT) is the Company's highest management body, responsible for setting enterprise strategy, allocating capital, and ensuring operational performance across all business units and support functions. All ELT members report directly to the Chief Executive Officer unless otherwise noted.

### 2.1 Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

- **Incumbent:** (Position held)
- **Reports to:** Board of Directors
- **Direct Reports:** COO, CFO, CMO, CHRO, CIO, CMktO, CSCO, General Counsel/CLO, SVP Store Operations, SVP Pro Business, SVP E-Commerce
- **Staff Functions Reporting to CEO:** VP Ethics & Compliance, VP Corporate Communications, Executive Assistant to the CEO, Chief of Staff
- **Primary Responsibilities:**
  - Sets overall strategic direction and long-term vision for the Company in partnership with the Board of Directors.
  - Chairs the Executive Leadership Team and Management Operating Committee.
  - Serves as the primary spokesperson to investors, media, and external stakeholders on matters of corporate strategy and performance.
  - Approves all capital expenditures above $25 million and all mergers, acquisitions, or divestitures.
  - Ensures the Company's culture, values, and ethical standards are embedded across all operations.
  - Oversees enterprise risk management and provides quarterly risk briefings to the Board.
  - Drives executive talent reviews and succession planning in coordination with the CHRO.
  - Represents the Company in industry associations, government forums, and community leadership initiatives.

### 2.2 Chief Operating Officer (COO)

- **Reports to:** CEO
- **Direct Reports:** SVP Store Operations (dotted line to CEO), VP Loss Prevention / Asset Protection, VP Real Estate & Store Development, VP Installation Services, VP Customer Care / Contact Center
- **Primary Responsibilities:**
  - Oversees day-to-day operational execution across all stores, distribution centers, and customer-facing service operations.
  - Responsible for store productivity, labor efficiency, in-stock position, planogram compliance, and customer satisfaction scores (NPS).
  - Drives continuous improvement initiatives (lean, Six Sigma, process re-engineering) across operations.
  - Partners with the CSCO to ensure supply chain performance meets store replenishment targets.
  - Owns the Company's operational budget and manages P&L accountability for store operations and distribution.
  - Leads crisis response and business continuity planning for operational disruptions.
  - Chairs the Operations Council, which includes regional VPs, district managers, and functional heads.

### 2.3 Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

- **Reports to:** CEO
- **Direct Reports:** SVP Finance & Accounting, VP Treasury, VP Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A), VP Investor Relations, VP Corporate Strategy & Business Development, VP Tax
- **Primary Responsibilities:**
  - Manages the financial integrity of the Company, including financial reporting, internal controls, and regulatory compliance (SEC, SOX).
  - Leads the annual operating plan (AOP), long-range financial planning, and quarterly reforecasting processes.
  - Oversees capital structure, liquidity management, debt covenants, and share repurchase programs.
  - Presents quarterly financial results to the Board of Directors and the Investment Committee.
  - Manages relationships with external auditors, credit rating agencies, and banking partners.
  - Evaluates and recommends capital allocation strategies, including dividend policy and M&A financial modeling.
  - Ensures enterprise risk management frameworks are integrated into financial planning and reporting.
  - Chairs the Finance Committee and co-chairs the Investment Committee with the CEO.

### 2.4 Chief Merchandising Officer (CMO)

- **Reports to:** CEO
- **Direct Reports:** VP Hardlines Merchandising, VP Building Materials Merchandising, VP Décor & Seasonal Merchandising, VP Private Brands & Exclusive Lines, VP Merchandise Planning & Allocation, VP Vendor Management
- **Primary Responsibilities:**
  - Owns the Company's merchandise strategy, including assortment planning, pricing architecture, vendor negotiations, and promotional calendar.
  - Manages a product portfolio spanning approximately 500,000 SKUs across 40+ merchandise departments.
  - Drives gross margin performance through vendor negotiations, private-brand development, and markdown optimization.
  - Leads seasonal planning and product launches in partnership with Marketing and Supply Chain.
  - Oversees vendor onboarding, performance scorecards, and compliance with Company sourcing standards.
  - Chairs the Merchandising Committee, which reviews assortment changes, vendor agreements, and category strategies quarterly.

### 2.5 Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO)

- **Reports to:** CEO
- **Direct Reports:** VP Talent Acquisition, VP Total Rewards & Benefits, VP Learning & Development, VP HR Business Partners, VP Associate Relations & Labor Relations, VP Diversity, Equity & Inclusion
- **Primary Responsibilities:**
  - Develops and executes the Company's human capital strategy, aligning talent management with business objectives.
  - Oversees recruitment, onboarding, performance management, succession planning, and leadership development programs.
  - Manages total compensation programs, including base pay structures, incentive plans, equity programs, and health & welfare benefits.

  - Administers the Company's associate engagement survey and develops action plans based on results.

  - Chairs the People Committee and reports diversity and talent metrics to the Board annually.

  - Manages the Company's cybersecurity program, including threat detection, incident response, and compliance with PCI-DSS, CCPA, and other data privacy regulations.
  - Drives technology innovation, including IoT (smart store), AI/ML demand forecasting, and autonomous supply chain capabilities.
  - Manages the IT capital and operating budget (approximately $500M annually) and ensures project delivery on time and within budget.
  - Partners with the SVP E-Commerce to deliver seamless omnichannel technology experiences.
  - Chairs the Technology Governance Committee and presents the annual technology roadmap to the Board.

## 4. Corporate Departments

### 4.1 Store Operations

**Mission:** Deliver an exceptional, consistent customer experience in every store while achieving sales and profitability targets through effective execution, associate engagement, and operational discipline.

**Key Functions:**
- Store performance management: daily, weekly, and monthly sales tracking with variance analysis at the store, district, and regional levels.
- Labor planning and scheduling: forecasting customer traffic patterns and optimizing associate schedules to balance service levels with payroll targets.
- Store standards execution: ensuring planogram compliance, signage accuracy, endcap and promotional display setup, and overall store condition.
- Customer complaint escalation and resolution at the store, district, and regional levels.
- New store opening playbooks, including hiring plans, inventory receipt scheduling, and grand opening event coordination.
- Store remodel and refresh program execution in partnership with Real Estate & Store Development.

**Sub-Units:**
- Regional Operations (6 regions, each led by a Regional Vice President)
- District Operations (approximately 90 districts, each led by a District Manager overseeing 20–30 stores)
- Store Standards & Execution Team
- Store Labor Planning & Analytics Team
- Customer Experience Measurement Team (NPS, mystery shopping, customer satisfaction surveys)

---

### 4.2 Merchandising

**Mission:** Curate a compelling, differentiated product assortment that meets the needs of DIY customers, professional contractors, and commercial clients while maximizing gross margin through strategic vendor partnerships and pricing optimization.

**Key Functions:**
- Assortment planning: determining the optimal SKU count and product mix for each category based on customer demand, market trends, and competitive analysis.
- Vendor negotiations: managing relationships with 10,000+ vendors to secure favorable cost, terms, co-op advertising, and margin improvement.
- Pricing strategy: establishing retail pricing architectures, competitive price monitoring, and markdown optimization.
- Promotional planning: developing the annual promotional calendar, including seasonal events (Spring Black Friday, Holiday Tool Event), circulars, and digital promotions.
- Private brand management: developing and growing the Company's private label and exclusive brands, targeting 20% of total sales.
- Merchandise planning & allocation: distributing inventory across the store fleet based on store size, demographics, and historical performance.

**Sub-Units:**
- Hardlines Merchandising (tools, hardware, electrical, plumbing, paint, lawn & garden)
- Building Materials Merchandising (lumber, building materials, millwork, roofing, siding)
- Décor & Seasonal Merchandising (flooring, kitchen & bath, lighting, appliances, holiday/seasonal)
- Private Brands & Exclusive Lines (in-house brand development, licensing, quality assurance)
- Merchandise Planning & Allocation (SKU-level planning, size/color optimization, new store sets)
- Vendor Management (vendor onboarding, scorecards, terms negotiation, diversity sourcing)

---

### 4.3 Supply Chain & Distribution

**Mission:** Operate a world-class, resilient supply chain that delivers the right product to the right location at the right time while minimizing total delivered cost and maximizing speed to shelf.

**Key Functions:**
- Distribution center operations: managing 85+ distribution centers (DCs) and 15 import distribution centers (IDCs) that process inbound freight, manage inventory, and ship to stores and customers.
- Transportation management: operating a dedicated fleet of 3,000+ trucks and managing third-party carrier relationships for inbound and outbound freight.
- Demand forecasting & supply planning: using AI/ML models to forecast demand at the SKU-store level and generate replenishment orders.
- Global sourcing: managing supplier relationships across 50+ countries, ensuring ethical sourcing, quality standards, and trade compliance.
- Inventory management: maintaining optimal inventory levels across the network, minimizing out-of-stocks and excess inventory.
- Reverse logistics: processing returns, damages, and recalls across the supply chain.

**Sub-Units:**
- Distribution Center Operations (DC General Managers, Operations Managers, Shift Supervisors)
- Transportation & Fleet (dedicated fleet management, carrier procurement, freight audit)
- Supply Chain Planning (demand planning, supply planning, network optimization)
- Global Sourcing (international buying offices in Shenzhen, Bangalore, and Monterrey)
- Inventory Management (stock ledger, shrink control, ABC analysis, safety stock policy)
- Reverse Logistics & Liquidation (returns processing, outlet channels, recycling programs)

---

### 4.4 Finance & Accounting

**Mission:** Safeguard the Company's financial assets, provide accurate and timely financial information, and support strategic decision-making through rigorous analysis and planning.

**Key Functions:**
- Financial reporting: preparing SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, proxy statement), ensuring GAAP compliance, and managing external audit.
- Corporate accounting: general ledger, accounts payable, accounts receivable, fixed assets, and intercompany transactions.
- Financial planning & analysis: annual operating plan, monthly forecasting, long-range planning, and ad hoc financial analysis.
- Treasury management: cash management, debt administration, share repurchase execution, and banking relationships.
- Tax: federal, state, and international tax compliance, tax planning, and transfer pricing.
- Internal audit: independent assurance and advisory services covering financial controls, operational processes, and compliance.

**Sub-Units:**
- Corporate Accounting (general ledger, close process, accounts payable, receivable)
- Financial Reporting (SEC reporting, technical accounting, SOX compliance)
- Financial Planning & Analysis (budgeting, forecasting, store-level P&L analysis)
- Treasury (cash management, debt management, liquidity planning)
- Tax (compliance, planning, audits, transfer pricing)
- Internal Audit (financial audit, operational audit, IT audit, fraud investigations)
- Investor Relations (analyst coverage, earnings calls, shareholder engagement)

### 4.8 Legal & Compliance

**Mission:** Protect the Company from legal risk, ensure regulatory compliance across all jurisdictions, and support ethical business practices.

**Key Functions:**
- Litigation & risk management: managing all litigation, pre-litigation disputes, and insurance programs, including general liability, product liability, D&O, and workers' compensation.
- Corporate & securities law: advising on corporate governance, SEC compliance, M&A transactions, and Board matters.

### 5.2 Role Descriptions and Responsibilities

**Store Manager (SM)**
- Reports to: District Manager
- Span of control: 5–7 Assistant Store Managers plus HR Manager
- Primary responsibilities: Full P&L accountability for the store; sets sales targets and drives revenue; ensures customer satisfaction scores meet or exceed targets; manages store budget including labor, supplies, and shrink; leads store team meetings and huddles; conducts performance reviews for ASMs; ensures compliance with all Company policies and regulatory requirements; serves as the store's community ambassador. Annual revenue responsibility: $40M–$100M+ depending on store format.

**Assistant Store Manager — Operations (ASM-OPS)**
- Reports to: Store Manager
- Span of control: 2–4 supervisors, 15–40 associates
- Primary responsibilities: Oversees all operational functions including freight processing, receiving, inventory management, lot operations, and store cleanliness. Ensures overnight/early morning freight flow meets productivity targets (cases per hour). Manages the receiving dock and vendor check-in procedures. Coordinates seasonal inventory resets and remerchandising projects. Ensures forklift safety compliance and OSHA standards. Manages store equipment and supply procurement.

**Assistant Store Manager — Hardlines (ASM-HL)**
- Reports to: Store Manager
- Span of control: 4–5 Department Supervisors, 20–35 associates
- Primary responsibilities: Drives sales performance in the hardlines departments (tools, hardware, electrical, plumbing, paint, lawn & garden). Ensures department merchandising standards are maintained. Coaches associates on product knowledge and selling skills. Manages department inventory levels and works with Merchandising on assortment issues. Coordinates with the CMO's team on promotional displays and endcaps. Drives attachment selling (e.g., drill bits with drills, wire with electrical boxes).

**Assistant Store Manager — Building Materials (ASM-BM)**
- Reports to: Store Manager
- Span of control: 2–3 Department Supervisors, 10–25 associates
- Primary responsibilities: Manages the building materials departments, which drive significant volume but require specialized equipment (forklifts, lumber carts). Ensures safe operation of material handling equipment. Coordinates with the Pro Desk on large commercial orders. Manages the lumber yard and outside yard areas. Works with the Supply Chain team on special-order building materials. Drives sales of big-ticket items (windows, doors, roofing packages).

**Assistant Store Manager — Decor (ASM-DEC)**
- Reports to: Store Manager
- Span of control: 3–4 Department Supervisors, 15–25 associates
- Primary responsibilities: Manages the décor departments (flooring, kitchen & bath, appliances, lighting). These departments have higher average transaction values and longer sales cycles. Ensures design consultants are available during peak hours. Coordinates with Installation Services for project sales. Manages appliance delivery scheduling. Drives special-order sales for flooring and kitchen cabinets.

**Assistant Store Manager — Front End (ASM-FE)**
- Reports to: Store Manager
- Span of control: Head Cashier, Customer Service Desk Supervisor, LP Specialist; 20–50 associates total
- Primary responsibilities: Manages all front-of-store operations including cashier scheduling, queue management, and customer service desk operations. Ensures checkout speed and accuracy standards are met. Manages returns processing and exchange procedures. Oversees loss prevention at the front end, including receipt verification and high-value merchandise controls. Coordinates with LP Specialist on shrink reduction initiatives. Manages cashier training and certification.

**Assistant Store Manager — Special Services (ASM-SS)**
- Reports to: Store Manager
- Span of control: 3 supervisors/coordinators, 8–15 associates
- Primary responsibilities: Manages the Pro Desk, Installation Services coordination, and Design Center. Serves as the primary point of contact for Pro customers in the store. Coordinates with the SVP Pro Business team on Pro programs and promotions. Manages installation project sales and scheduling. Ensures design consultants meet sales targets for kitchen/bath and closet projects. Drives the store's interconnected retail experience (BOPIS, ship-from-store).

**Human Resources Manager (HRM)**
- Reports to: Store Manager (solid line); District HR Manager (dotted line)
- Span of control: HR Coordinator, Training Coordinator
- Primary responsibilities: Manages all HR functions for the store, including hiring, onboarding, performance management, associate relations, and benefits administration. Conducts new hire orientation. Manages associate engagement survey action planning. Handles initial workplace investigations and escalates to District HR as needed. Ensures compliance with employment laws and Company policies. Coordinates training schedules and tracks completion of required training modules. Manages associate attendance and scheduling compliance.

**Department Supervisor**
- Reports to: Assigned Assistant Store Manager
- Span of control: 3–12 associates depending on department
- Primary responsibilities: First-level management in the store. Leads daily department huddles, assigns tasks, and monitors associate productivity. Ensures merchandising standards (facing, labeling, stocking) are maintained. Provides on-the-spot coaching and product knowledge training. Manages department inventory, including out-of-stock reporting and overstock management. Opens/closes the department area, including register tills. Serves as the subject matter expert for their department's products.

**Sales Associate**
- Reports to: Department Supervisor
- Primary responsibilities: Provides knowledgeable, friendly customer service. Assists customers in finding products, provides project advice, and suggests complementary items. Maintains department merchandising standards (stocking, facing, labeling, endcap maintenance). Processes customer transactions at department registers. Participates in store events, promotions, and product knowledge training. Monitors and reports inventory issues (out-of-stocks, damages, mis-ticketed items). Maintains a clean and safe shopping environment.

**Lot Associate**
- Reports to: Freight Team Supervisor
- Primary responsibilities: Manages the parking lot and exterior areas. Retrieves shopping carts from the parking lot and cart corrals. Assists customers with loading merchandise into vehicles. Maintains cleanliness of the parking lot, entryways, and cart areas. Monitors exterior areas for safety hazards and reports maintenance issues. Supports garden center operations during peak season.

**Cashier**
- Reports to: Head Cashier
- Primary responsibilities: Processes customer transactions accurately and efficiently at checkout. Scans merchandise, processes payments (cash, credit, debit, gift cards), and applies discounts and promotions. Promotes the Company's credit card, protection plans, and Pro Xtra loyalty program. Maintains a clean and organized checkout area. Adheres to age-restricted product verification procedures. Provides friendly, professional customer interaction.

**Freight Team Associate**
- Reports to: Freight Team Supervisor
- Primary responsibilities: Processes inbound freight during overnight or early morning shifts. Unloads delivery trucks, sorts merchandise by department, and stocks shelves per planogram. Works at a target rate of 40–60 cases per hour. Builds displays and sets endcaps per promotional instructions. Operates pallet jacks and reach trucks (with certification). Maintains the backroom organization and ensures accurate slotting of overstock.

**Special Services Associate (Pro Desk / Installation / Design)**
- Reports to: Pro Desk Supervisor, Installation Services Coordinator, or Design Center Coordinator
- Primary responsibilities: Provides specialized service to Pro customers and project customers. At the Pro Desk: processes Pro orders, manages will-call orders, and coordinates delivery scheduling. For Installation Services: schedules in-home consultations, manages project files, and coordinates between customers and installers. In the Design Center: creates kitchen/bath designs using design software, presents options to customers, and generates quotes. Requires specialized product knowledge and customer relationship skills.

| **DC Team Lead** | 8–15 associates | 6–18 | Per function |
| **Corporate Manager** | 6–10 individual contributors | 4–12 | Varies by function |
| **Corporate Team Lead** | 4–8 individual contributors | 3–10 | Project or functional teams |

**Guidelines for Span Adjustments:**
- Spans below the minimum range should be justified by unusual complexity, strategic importance, or regulatory requirements.
- Spans above the maximum range require additional support structures (assistant managers, coordinators, or team leads) to be added within 90 days.
- All span-of-control metrics are reviewed quarterly as part of the Organizational Health Dashboard maintained by HR.

## 10. Governance Committees

### 10.1 Board of Directors Committees

#### Audit Committee
- **Charter:** Oversees the integrity of the Company's financial statements, the independent auditor's qualifications and independence, the performance of the internal audit function, and compliance with legal and regulatory requirements. Reviews and approves the annual audit plan. Oversees the Company's enterprise risk management framework as it relates to financial risks.
- **Membership:** Minimum 3 independent directors; at least one member must be a financial expert as defined by SEC regulations. Chair designated annually by the Board.
- **Meeting Cadence:** Quarterly, with additional sessions as needed for special matters.
- **Key Responsibilities:**
  - Appoint, compensate, and oversee the independent external auditor.
  - Review and pre-approve all audit and permissible non-audit services.
  - Review quarterly and annual financial statements before filing.
  - Oversee internal audit function and review audit findings.
  - Monitor compliance with Code of Ethics and receive reports from VP Ethics & Compliance.
  - Oversee cybersecurity risk management program and receive annual briefing from CISO.
  - Review whistleblower reports and investigation summaries.

#### Compensation & Talent Committee
- **Charter:** Discharges the Board's responsibilities relating to compensation of the Company's executive officers and directors, oversees the Company's talent management and succession planning programs, and administers the Company's equity-based compensation plans.
- **Membership:** Minimum 3 independent directors. Chair designated annually by the Board.
- **Meeting Cadence:** Quarterly, with additional sessions during annual compensation cycle.
- **Key Responsibilities:**
  - Set compensation for the CEO and approve compensation for all other NEOs.
  - Oversee the annual incentive plan design and target-setting process.
  - Administer equity compensation plans and approve all equity grants.
  - Review and approve the Company's Compensation Discussion & Analysis (CD&A) for the proxy statement.
  - Oversee CEO succession planning and review succession plans for all ELT members.
  - Monitor diversity and inclusion progress at the executive level.
  - Review and approve severance and change-in-control arrangements.

#### Nominating & Governance Committee
- **Charter:** Identifies and recommends qualified candidates for election to the Board, leads the Board self-evaluation process, develops and recommends corporate governance guidelines, and oversees Board composition, independence, and effectiveness.
- **Membership:** Minimum 3 independent directors. Chair designated annually by the Board.
- **Meeting Cadence:** Quarterly, with additional sessions during director recruitment.
- **Key Responsibilities:**
  - Identify, evaluate, and recommend director candidates to the Board.
  - Assess Board composition, skills matrix, and independence on an annual basis.
  - Lead the annual Board and committee self-evaluation process.
  - Recommend committee assignments and committee chairs.
  - Review and recommend amendments to the Corporate Governance Guidelines, Code of Business Conduct & Ethics, and Board committee charters.
  - Oversee director onboarding and continuing education programs.
  - Review related-person transactions and recommend action to the Board.

#### Finance & Investment Committee
- **Charter:** Oversees the Company's financial strategy, capital structure, dividend policy, share repurchase programs, and major investment decisions, including mergers, acquisitions, and divestitures.
- **Membership:** Minimum 3 directors, majority independent. Chair designated annually by the Board.
- **Meeting Cadence:** Quarterly, with additional sessions for major transactions.
- **Key Responsibilities:**
  - Review and recommend the annual capital plan to the Board.
  - Oversee dividend policy and recommend dividend changes to the Board.
  - Review and approve share repurchase programs and execution.
  - Evaluate and recommend M&A transactions exceeding $100M to the Board.
  - Review the Company's capital structure, debt levels, and credit rating.
  - Oversee pension and retirement plan investment strategy.
  - Review significant capital allocation decisions.

#### Safety, Environment & Social Responsibility Committee
- **Charter:** Oversees the Company's environmental, social, and governance (ESG) performance, including workplace safety, environmental sustainability, community engagement, and social responsibility programs.
- **Membership:** Minimum 3 directors. Chair designated annually by the Board.
- **Meeting Cadence:** Quarterly, with annual ESG report review session.
- **Key Responsibilities:**
  - Monitor workplace safety performance and review serious incident reports.
  - Oversee the Company's environmental sustainability goals and progress.
  - Review the Company's ESG disclosures and sustainability report.
  - Monitor community engagement and philanthropic impact.
  - Review human rights policies and supply chain labor practices.
  - Oversee the Company's disaster response and community resilience programs.

### 10.2 Management Committees

#### Executive Leadership Team (ELT)
- **Membership:** CEO (chair), COO, CFO, CMO, CHRO, CIO, CMktO, CSCO, CLO, SVP Store Operations, SVP Pro Business, SVP E-Commerce
- **Meeting Cadence:** Weekly (Monday mornings, 2 hours)
- **Purpose:** The Company's principal management decision-making body. Reviews business performance, addresses cross-functional issues, makes resource allocation decisions, and ensures alignment on strategic priorities.
- **Standing Agenda:**
  1. Safety moment and associate update (5 min)
  2. Sales and traffic flash — prior week performance (15 min)
  3. Key initiative status updates (30 min, rotating topics)
  4. Cross-functional issue resolution (30 min)
  5. Strategic discussion or external speaker (30 min)
  6. Closing comments and action items (10 min)

#### Management Operating Committee (MOC)
- **Membership:** COO (chair), CFO, CMO, CSCO, SVP Store Operations, VP Loss Prevention, VP Real Estate, VP Installation Services, VP Customer Care, RVPs (rotating)
- **Meeting Cadence:** Bi-weekly (Wednesday afternoons, 90 minutes)
- **Purpose:** Drives operational execution across the enterprise. Reviews operational KPIs, addresses supply chain and store execution issues, and coordinates operational initiatives.
- **Standing Agenda:**
  1. Operations scorecard review — safety, service, in-stock, shrink (20 min)
  2. Supply chain performance update (15 min)
  3. Store execution spotlight — 2 districts per session (30 min)
  4. Initiative execution review (20 min)
  5. Open forum and action items (5 min)

#### Merchandising Committee
- **Membership:** CMO (chair), CEO, CFO, COO, CMktO, SVP Stores, SVP Pro, SVP E-Commerce, VP Merchandise Planning
- **Meeting Cadence:** Monthly (first Thursday, 3 hours)
- **Purpose:** Reviews and approves merchandise strategy, major assortment changes, vendor agreements exceeding $5M, promotional calendar, and private brand initiatives.
- **Key Decisions Requiring Committee Approval:**
  - New category introductions or category eliminations affecting >100 SKUs
  - Vendor agreements exceeding $5M annualized
  - Private brand launches or brand relaunches
  - Major promotional events (Spring Black Friday, Holiday events)
  - Pricing architecture changes affecting >500 SKUs

#### Technology Governance Committee
- **Membership:** CIO (chair), CEO, CFO, COO, SVP E-Commerce, VP Information Security
- **Meeting Cadence:** Monthly (second Wednesday, 90 minutes)
- **Purpose:** Reviews and prioritizes the Company's technology investment portfolio, oversees major technology projects, and manages technology risk.
- **Key Decisions Requiring Committee Approval:**
  - Technology projects with budgets exceeding $1M
  - Technology architecture decisions affecting multiple systems
  - Cybersecurity policy changes and major security investments
  - Vendor selection for technology platforms exceeding $500K annually
  - Technology roadmap changes affecting committed timelines

#### People Committee
- **Membership:** CHRO (chair), CEO, CFO, CLO, SVP Stores
- **Meeting Cadence:** Monthly (third Tuesday, 90 minutes)
- **Purpose:** Oversees talent management, organizational health, and people strategy across the enterprise.
- **Key Decisions Requiring Committee Approval:**
  - Annual compensation structure changes
  - Organizational restructurings affecting >50 positions
  - Leadership development program designs
  - Benefits plan changes
  - DEI strategy and annual goals
  - Executive succession plans (shared with Compensation & Talent Committee of the Board)

#### Supply Chain Council
- **Membership:** CSCO (chair), COO, CMO, CFO, CIO, SVP Stores
- **Meeting Cadence:** Bi-monthly (first Monday of alternating months, 2 hours)
- **Purpose:** Aligns supply chain strategy with business requirements, reviews network performance, and approves supply chain investments.
- **Key Decisions Requiring Committee Approval:**
  - New distribution center location or closure
  - Transportation strategy changes (carrier contracts, fleet expansion)
  - Major supply chain technology investments (WMS, TMS, planning systems)
  - Inventory policy changes affecting safety stock targets
  - Global sourcing strategy changes

#### Digital Steering Committee
- **Membership:** SVP E-Commerce (chair), CEO, CIO, CMktO, CMO, CFO, SVP Pro
- **Meeting Cadence:** Bi-weekly (Fridays, 60 minutes)
- **Purpose:** Guides the Company's digital transformation strategy, reviews digital performance metrics, and prioritizes digital initiatives.
- **Key Decisions Requiring Committee Approval:**
  - Digital product roadmap changes
  - E-commerce platform architecture decisions
  - Omnichannel fulfillment strategy changes
  - Digital vendor and partner selections
  - Mobile app major release decisions

#### Disclosure Committee
- **Membership:** CLO (chair), CFO, VP Corporate Communications, VP Investor Relations, VP Financial Reporting, VP Ethics & Compliance
- **Meeting Cadence:** Quarterly (before each earnings release) and as needed for material events
- **Purpose:** Ensures the Company's public disclosures are accurate, complete, timely, and comply with SEC regulations and Company disclosure policy. Reviews all material public statements, earnings releases, and SEC filings before publication.

#### Risk Management Committee
- **Membership:** CLO (chair), CFO, COO, CIO, CISO, VP Insurance & Risk Management, VP Loss Prevention
- **Meeting Cadence:** Quarterly (aligned with Audit Committee calendar)
- **Purpose:** Oversees the enterprise risk management (ERM) program, identifies emerging risks, and ensures mitigation plans are in place. Reports risk assessment results and mitigation status to the Audit Committee of the Board.
