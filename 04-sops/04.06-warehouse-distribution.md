## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes uniform policies, standards, and step-by-step procedures for all warehouse and distribution center operations at BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. (BRHI). It is designed to:

- Ensure consistent, safe, and efficient operations across all 90+ BRHI distribution centers, cross-dock facilities, and fulfillment centers.
- Protect company assets through rigorous inventory control, quality assurance, and safety practices.
- Provide associates and managers with clear, actionable instructions for inbound, storage, outbound, transportation, and reverse logistics operations.
- Support compliance with OSHA regulations (29 CFR 1910), DOT regulations (49 CFR), EPA hazmat requirements, and all applicable federal, state, and local regulations.
- Serve as the primary training reference for new warehouse associates and the authoritative guide for resolving operational questions.

Every distribution center associate is expected to read, understand, and follow this SOP. Failure to comply may result in corrective action up to and including termination.

## 5. Inbound Operations

### 5.1 Carrier Appointment Scheduling

All inbound carriers must schedule delivery appointments through the BRHI Transportation Management System (TMS). No unscheduled deliveries will be accepted unless authorized by the Operations Manager — Inbound.

**Appointment Parameters:**

| Parameter | Standard |
|---|---|
| Appointment window | 2 hours (e.g., 8:00 AM – 10:00 AM) |
| Scheduling lead time | Minimum 48 hours in advance |
| Maximum appointments per dock door per shift | 3 |
| Grace period (late arrival) | 30 minutes; after 30 minutes, appointment is forfeited |
| Rescheduling | Must be completed 24 hours prior to appointment |
| EDI/ASN requirement | ASN must be received in WMS at least 24 hours before arrival |

**Appointment Process:**

1. Carrier or vendor accesses the BRHI Carrier Portal (or sends EDI 204 Motor Carrier Load Tender).
2. TMS assigns available dock doors and time windows based on commodity type, volume, and labor availability.
3. Confirmation is sent via EDI 990 (Application Advice) or email with appointment number.
4. Carrier arrives at guard shack and presents appointment number, BOL, and carrier identification.
5. Guard verifies appointment and issues a Dock Door Assignment Slip.
6. Carrier proceeds to assigned dock door; check-in time is recorded in TMS.

### 5.2 Receiving Process

#### 5.2.1 Pre-Receiving Preparation

Before a trailer arrives at the dock:

1. Operations Manager — Inbound reviews the daily appointment schedule by 4:00 PM the prior day.
2. Shift Supervisor confirms staffing levels and assigns Team Leads to dock doors.
3. Team Lead verifies dock door area is clear, clean, and free of obstructions.
4. Staging area is confirmed ready with adequate empty pallets, shrink wrap, and labeling supplies.
5. WMS receiving queue is reviewed for expected POs and ASNs.

#### 5.2.2 Receiving Steps

**Step 1: Trailer Spot and Seal Verification**

- Yard driver spots trailer at assigned dock door.
- Receiving associate verifies trailer seal number matches BOL seal number.
- If seal is broken or numbers do not match, immediately notify Shift Supervisor. Do not begin unloading. Photograph seal condition. Supervisor contacts Security and initiates the Seal Discrepancy Report (Form BRHI-DC-012).
- Document seal number, trailer number, and dock door on the Receiving Log.

**Step 2: Trailer Inspection**

- Before entering, verify trailer is chocked and dock plate/leveler is secured.
- Inspect trailer interior for signs of pest infestation, water damage, unusual odors, or structural damage.
- If any deficiency is found, photograph and document on the Trailer Condition Report (Form BRHI-DC-013). Unloading may proceed only with Shift Supervisor approval after assessment.

**Step 3: Unload and Stage**

- Unload freight using appropriate equipment (pallet jack, forklift, conveyor) based on commodity type.
- Stage product on the receiving dock in staging lanes by PO number.
- Product must not be left on the dock floor for more than 2 hours during standard operations or 1 hour during perishable/temperature-sensitive receiving.
- Mixed PO pallets are separated during unloading.

**Step 4: Quantity Verification**

- Associate counts all cartons, cases, or units against the ASN and BOL.
- Counting method: RF gun scan (preferred) or manual count with dual verification for variances >5 units.
- **Tolerance: ±2% carton count.** If the variance exceeds 2% of the total shipment:
  - Stop receiving for that PO.
  - Notify Shift Supervisor immediately.
  - Complete a Quantity Variance Report (Form BRHI-DC-014).
  - Supervisor contacts Vendor Compliance within 4 hours.

**Step 5: Quality Inspection**

- Visual inspection of all palletized freight for visible damage (crushed cartons, water stains, broken shrink wrap, tilted pallets).
- Sampling per AQL standards (see Section 11).
- Open-case inspection for fragile items (tile, glass, light fixtures): minimum 10% of cartons on the first pallet, then 5% of subsequent pallets.
- Photograph all damage with DC receiving station tablet; attach photos to the receiving record in WMS.

**Step 6: System Receipt**

- Scan each pallet/case license plate barcode into the WMS.
- WMS validates against PO: SKU, quantity, lot/serial number (if applicable).
- System generates a pallet license plate (LP) and assigns a putaway location.
- Print and apply LP label to each pallet.
- System receipt is confirmed; PO status changes from "Open" to "Received" in the ERP.

**Step 7: Documentation and Closeout**

- Receiving associate signs the carrier's BOL, noting any exceptions (overages, shortages, damage).
- Carrier retains original BOL; DC retains copy (filed in dock door binder for 90 days, then archived).
- Receiving record is finalized in WMS within 4 hours of completing the unload.
- Shift Supervisor reviews daily receiving report for open POs not received by appointment date.

### 5.3 Over, Short, and Damage (OS&D) Reporting

All OS&D incidents must be documented and reported within **24 hours** of receipt.

| OS&D Type | Action | Form | Notification |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Overage** | Segregate and stage in OS&D area. Do not putaway. | BRHI-DC-015 (OS&D Report) | Vendor Compliance, Inventory Control Lead |
| **Shortage** | Complete count verification with witness. Update WMS with actual received quantity. | BRHI-DC-015 | Vendor Compliance, Buyer |
| **Concealed Damage** | Discovered after carrier departure. Photograph damage. Retain original packaging. | BRHI-DC-015 | Carrier Claims (within 5 business days of delivery), Vendor Compliance |
| **Visible Damage** | Noted on BOL at time of delivery. Refuse damaged portion or accept with notation. | BRHI-DC-015 + BOL notation | Carrier Claims (immediate), Vendor Compliance |
| **Wrong Item** | Segregate. Do not putaway. Verify against PO line items. | BRHI-DC-015 | Buyer, Vendor Compliance |

**OS&D Workflow:**

1. Associate identifies discrepancy during Step 4 or Step 5 of receiving.
2. Associate completes Form BRHI-DC-015 with PO number, SKU, expected vs. actual quantities, and damage description.
3. Associate photographs discrepancy (minimum 3 photos: overview, close-up, label/lot number).
4. Shift Supervisor reviews and signs the form within 2 hours.
5. QA Lead enters the OS&D into the Claims Management System (CMS) within 24 hours.
6. CMS auto-generates notifications to Vendor Compliance, Buyer, and (if carrier-related) Transportation.
7. Resolution target: 15 business days for carrier claims, 30 business days for vendor claims.

### 5.4 Vendor Compliance and Chargebacks

BRHI maintains a Vendor Compliance Program to ensure suppliers adhere to shipping, labeling, and packaging standards.

**Chargeback Schedule:**

| Violation | Chargeback Amount | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Late shipment (past PO ship date) | 2% of PO value per week late | >3 business days |
| Missing or non-compliant ASN | $250 per shipment | Any occurrence |
| Non-compliant pallet configuration | $150 per pallet | Any occurrence |
| Missing SSCC/GS1-128 labels | $100 per carton | >5% of shipment |
| Mixed SKU pallets (single-SKU PO) | $75 per pallet | Any occurrence |
| Unauthorized substitution | 15% of substituted item value | Any occurrence |
| Advance shipment notice inaccuracy (>5% variance) | $500 per occurrence | Any occurrence |

The Vendor Compliance team issues chargebacks monthly. Vendors may dispute chargebacks within 30 days of notification through the BRHI Vendor Portal.

## 6. Storage and Warehousing

### 6.1 Storage Types

| Storage Type | Description | Locations |
|---|---|---|
| **Selective Pallet Rack** | Standard single-deep pallet storage; 4–5 levels | Bulk zones A, B, C |
| **Double-Deep Rack** | Two pallets deep; used for high-quantity SKUs | Reserve zones D, E |
| **Push-Back Rack** | 2–5 pallets deep, LIFO flow; high-density | Seasonal overflow zone F |
| **Drive-In/Drive-Through Rack** | 6–8 pallets deep; bulk commodities (lumber, drywall) | Zone G |
| **Carton Flow Rack** | Gravity roller; split-case / each-pick SKUs | Active pick zones H, J |
| **Pallet Flow Rack (FIFO)** | Gravity flow; date-sensitive or FIFO products | Zone K |
| **Mezzanine / Shelving** | Small parts, hardware, fasteners | Zone L (mezzanine level) |
| **Floor Stack** | Block stacking for palletized goods; max 3 high | Zones M, N |
| **Temperature-Controlled** | 35°F – 45°F (refrigerated); ambient maintained per product spec | Zone T |
| **Hazmat Storage** | Segregated, ventilated, sprinkler-equipped; per SDS requirements | Zone HAZ |
| **Outdoor Yard** | Lumber, building materials, pipe; weather-resistant items | Yard areas Y1–Y5 |
| **Cage / High-Value** | Secured wire cage; power tools, high-value items | Zone HV (adjacent to office) |

### 6.2 Location Naming Convention

All storage locations follow the format: **[Facility Code]-[Zone]-[Aisle]-[Bay]-[Level]-[Position]**

| Component | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Facility Code | 3-letter code | RDC (Regional DC), MDC (Market DC), EFC (E-Commerce FC) |
| Zone | 1–2 letters | A, B, HV, HAZ |
| Aisle | 3 digits, zero-padded | 001, 012, 099 |
| Bay | 2 digits, zero-padded | 01, 15 |
| Level | 1 digit (1 = floor, 2+ = upper levels) | 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 |
| Position | 1 digit (left to right within bay) | 1, 2 |

**Example:** `RDC-A-012-05-3-2` = Regional DC, Zone A, Aisle 12, Bay 05, Level 3, Position 2.

Locations are barcoded with weather-resistant labels (minimum 2.5" × 1.0") affixed to the rack upright or shelf edge at each pick face.

### 6.3 Putaway Strategy

#### 6.3.1 Zone-Based Assignment

Product is assigned to storage zones based on commodity group:

| Commodity Group | Primary Zone | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lumber & Building Materials | Y1–Y3, G | Floor stack or drive-in; weight limits per rack spec |
| Plumbing | A, B | Pallet rack |
| Electrical | H, L | Carton flow or mezzanine shelving |
| Paint & Sundries | T (if temp-controlled), A | FIFO flow rack for paint |
| Tools & Hardware | HV, L | High-value cage and mezzanine |
| Appliances | C, D | Selective or double-deep; weight-rated racks |
| Seasonal / Outdoor | F | Push-back rack for seasonal overflow |
| Hazmat | HAZ | Segregated per SDS and fire code |
| E-Commerce each-pick | H, J | Carton flow rack; dedicated EFC zones |

#### 6.3.2 ABC Velocity Classification

SKUs are classified by velocity using trailing 13-week demand data, updated weekly by the Inventory Control Lead:

| Class | % of SKUs | % of Throughput | Storage Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** (High velocity) | 15% | 65% | Golden zone (waist-to-shoulder level, near dock), carton flow |
| **B** (Medium velocity) | 25% | 20% | Mid-level rack, standard aisle positions |
| **C** (Slow velocity) | 60% | 15% | Upper/lower rack positions, overflow zones |

- Velocity assignments are reviewed quarterly by the Inventory Control Lead.
- A-class SKUs within 50 feet of the primary pick path; C-class SKUs in perimeter or upper-level locations.

#### 6.3.3 FIFO Compliance

- All date-sensitive products (paint, adhesives, chemicals, seasonal) follow **First-In, First-Out (FIFO)** discipline.
- At putaway, the associate scans the lot date or manufacture date. WMS enforces FIFO at the pick face.
- For non-date-sensitive product, **FEFO (First-Expired, First-Out)** is used if expiration dates are present; otherwise, standard FIFO applies.
- FIFO violations discovered during cycle counts or audits are documented and result in a corrective action review with the responsible associate and Team Lead.

#### 6.3.4 Putaway Process

1. WMS generates a putaway task linked to the received pallet LP.
2. Associate retrieves pallet from receiving staging area using appropriate PIT.
3. Associate scans the pallet LP barcode; WMS directs to the assigned location.
4. Associate travels to location, scans location barcode to confirm.
5. Associate places pallet/product in location (ensuring proper orientation and weight distribution).
6. Associate confirms putaway in WMS; system updates on-hand inventory and location occupancy.
7. If location is full or occupied, associate reports discrepancy via RF exception menu; WMS assigns alternate location.
8. **Putaway target:** All received product must be putaway within **4 hours** of receipt confirmation during standard operations, **2 hours** during peak.

### 6.4 Temperature-Controlled Storage

- Refrigerated zones are maintained at 35°F – 45°F (+/- 2°F tolerance).
- Temperature is monitored by a centralized building management system (BMS) with sensors in each zone.
- If temperature exceeds tolerance for more than 30 minutes, BMS sends an automated alert to the Maintenance Supervisor and Shift Supervisor.
- Product in a temperature excursion event is quarantined until QA Lead clears it.
- Temperature logs are retained for 3 years per regulatory requirements.

### 6.5 Hazmat Storage and Handling

- All hazmat materials are stored in Zone HAZ, which is a dedicated, ventilated, sprinkler-equipped area with secondary containment.
- Storage segregation per SDS and DOT compatibility chart (49 CFR 177.848).
- Maximum container quantities per control area comply with IFC Chapter 50 and local fire codes.
- SDS sheets for all hazmat products are maintained in the Hazmat Reference Binder at the zone entrance and in the BRHI SDS digital system.
- Only associates who have completed Hazmat Awareness Training (BRHI-TRN-015) may handle hazmat product.
- Spill kits are positioned at the entrance to Zone HAZ and at each dock door. Spill response procedure is posted at each spill kit station.

### 6.6 Yard Management

Outdoor yard areas (Y1–Y5) are used for weather-resistant building materials (lumber, drywall, concrete, pipe, roofing).

- All product in the yard must be on pallets or dunnage; no ground contact.
- Product is covered with weather-resistant tarps or stored under canopy where available.
- Yard locations follow the same naming convention; locations are marked with painted lines and location signs on posts.
- Yard associate performs a daily visual inspection of tarps, dunnage condition, and product integrity.
- Yard inventory is cycle counted weekly (every Monday) by the Inventory Control team.

## 7. Outbound Operations

### 7.1 Order Release and Wave Planning

#### 7.1.1 Wave Planning Schedule

| Wave | Release Time | Pick Start | Pick Complete | Ship Time | Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wave 1 | 6:30 AM | 7:00 AM | 11:00 AM | 1:00 PM | Store replenishment orders |
| Wave 2 | 10:00 AM | 10:30 AM | 3:00 PM | 5:00 PM | Store replenishment + DTC |
| Wave 3 | 2:00 PM | 2:30 PM | 7:00 PM | 9:00 PM | E-commerce, priority orders |
| Wave 4 (Peak only) | 6:30 PM | 7:00 PM | 11:00 PM | 1:00 AM | Overflow, next-day orders |

- The Wave Planner (Shift Supervisor or designated Team Lead) reviews the order pool in WMS at least 30 minutes before wave release.
- Orders are prioritized by: (1) delivery appointment time, (2) customer priority tier, (3) carrier cutoff, (4) order age.
- Wave release is confirmed in WMS; pick tasks are generated and assigned to zones.

#### 7.1.2 Wave Composition

- Minimum wave size: 500 lines or 20 store orders, whichever is met first.
- Maximum wave size: 3,000 lines or 60 store orders, unless approved by Operations Manager.
- E-commerce waves may be smaller (minimum 100 lines) to maintain same-day shipping SLAs.
- Cross-dock transfers are released as a separate wave category with expedited processing.

### 7.2 Pick Process

#### 7.2.1 Pick Methods

| Method | Description | Used For |
|---|---|---|
| **RF-Directed Picking** | Associate follows WMS-directed path via RF gun; scans location and item to confirm | All standard picking; primary method |
| **Batch Picking** | Multiple orders picked simultaneously into separate totes on a pick cart | Small-item, multi-order waves; e-commerce |
| **Zone Picking** | Associates pick only within assigned zones; orders move zone to zone via conveyor or cart | Large DCs with conveyor systems |
| **Cluster Picking** | Associate picks for multiple orders using multi-tote cart with put-to-light | High-velocity each-pick SKUs |
| **Voice Picking** | Voice-directed pick instructions via headset (hands-free) | Cold chain, freezer, hazmat zones |

#### 7.2.2 Pick Rate Standards

| Pick Type | Rate Standard | Measurement Period |
|---|---|---|
| Full-case pick (pallet) | 120 cases/hour | Rolling 2-hour average |
| Full-case pick (conveyor) | 150 cases/hour | Rolling 2-hour average |
| Each-pick (unit) | 80 units/hour | Rolling 2-hour average |
| Each-pick (e-commerce) | 100 units/hour | Rolling 2-hour average |
| Batch pick (multi-order) | 90 units/hour per order | Rolling 2-hour average |

- Associates who fall below 85% of standard for 3 consecutive days receive coaching from Team Lead.
- Associates exceeding 110% of standard for 5 consecutive days are recognized in the weekly performance review.
- New hires (first 30 days) are held to 75% of standard during training ramp-up.

#### 7.2.3 Pick Process Steps

1. WMS assigns pick task to associate's RF device (or voice terminal).
2. Associate travels to the directed location using the optimized pick path.
3. Associate scans the location barcode to confirm correct location.
4. System displays item description, quantity, and target tote/pallet.
5. Associate picks the required quantity and scans the item barcode (or confirms via voice).
6. WMS validates the pick (correct SKU, correct quantity). If mismatch, associate is prompted to rescan or report exception.
7. Associate places item(s) in the designated tote, carton, or pallet per the pick label.
8. Once all tasks for the order/batch are complete, associate delivers picked product to the pack station or staging lane.
9. WMS updates order status to "Picked" and triggers the pack task.

### 7.3 Pack and Ship

#### 7.3.1 Packing Standards

| Order Type | Packaging Standard | Label Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Store replenishment (full-case) | Original case; no repack required | Pallet license plate, store destination label |
| Store replenishment (split-case) | Ship in corrugated shipper; void fill required | GS1-128 shipping label, store destination |
| E-Commerce (standard) | Single-wall corrugated box; kraft paper or air pillow void fill | Carrier shipping label, packing slip |
| E-Commerce (fragile) | Double-wall corrugated; foam or bubble wrap protection | Carrier shipping label, "FRAGILE" sticker, packing slip |
| Lumber / Building materials | Band to pallet (min 2 bands); corner protectors required | Pallet license plate, BOL attached |

#### 7.3.2 Pallet Build Standards

- Pallet height: Maximum 72 inches (including pallet).
- Pallet weight: Maximum 2,500 lbs per pallet (unless floor-loaded).
- Stack pattern: Column stack for uniform cases; interlock for mixed sizes.
- Shrink wrap: Minimum 3 revolutions of 80-gauge stretch film; top sheet required for water-sensitive product.
- Label placement: Pallet license plate label on two adjacent sides, at eye level.
- Store-specific pallets are staged in the shipping lane corresponding to the delivery route.

#### 7.3.3 Shipping Documentation

| Document | Purpose | Retention |
|---|---|---|
| **Bill of Lading (BOL)** | Carrier contract; itemizes shipment | 3 years |
| **Packing List** | Detail of contents per order | 3 years |
| **Manifest** | Lists all shipments on a trailer | 3 years |
| **HAZMAT Shipping Papers** | Required for regulated materials (49 CFR 172) | 3 years |
| **Certificate of Destruction** | For damaged/disposed product | 7 years |
| **Customs Documentation** | For import/export shipments | 7 years |

- BOL is generated by WMS and printed at the shipping desk.
- Shipper associate verifies trailer number, seal number, and BOL accuracy before signing.
- Carrier driver signs BOL; copies are distributed (original to carrier, copy to DC file, electronic copy to TMS).

#### 7.3.4 Loading Process

1. Shipping associate retrieves the load plan from WMS.
2. Associate verifies trailer is clean, dry, and free of damage or odors.
3. Associate inspects trailer floor for protruding nails or damage; documents any issues.
4. Load product per the load plan: heavy items on bottom, store stop sequence in reverse order (last stop loaded first).
5. Use load bars, straps, or air bags to prevent shifting during transit.
6. Final pallet count is verified against the manifest.
7. Associate records seal number on BOL and applies seal to trailer.
8. Trailer is released from dock and staged in the yard for carrier pickup or dispatched to company driver.

### 8.3 Route Optimization

- Routes are planned using the BRHI TMS route optimization module, which considers delivery windows, vehicle capacity, distance, traffic patterns, and fuel costs.
- Routes are generated daily by the Transportation Supervisor and released to drivers by 4:00 PM the day prior.
- Drivers receive route details via the BRHI Driver Mobile App (including turn-by-turn navigation, delivery sequence, and proof-of-delivery capture).
- Unplanned route deviations must be reported to the Transportation Supervisor in real time via the mobile app.

### 8.4 DOT Compliance

## 9. Inventory Control

### 9.1 Cycle Counting Program

#### 9.1.1 Count Frequency

| Category | Frequency | Minimum Annual Counts per Location |
|---|---|---|
| A-class SKUs (high velocity) | Daily | 52 (weekly per location) |
| B-class SKUs (medium velocity) | Weekly | 12 (monthly per location) |
| C-class SKUs (slow velocity) | Monthly | 4 (quarterly per location) |
| High-value items (>$100/unit) | Daily | 52 (weekly per location) |
| Hazmat inventory | Weekly | 12 (monthly per location) |
| All locations (minimum) | Quarterly | 1 (every location counted at least once per quarter) |

- Daily count tasks are generated by WMS and assigned to Inventory Control associates at the start of each shift.
- The Inventory Control Lead reviews daily count completion by end of shift; uncompleted counts are escalated to the Shift Supervisor.

#### 9.1.2 Cycle Count Process

1. WMS generates count tasks based on the frequency schedule and locations due for count.
2. Associate travels to the assigned location with an RF gun.
3. Associate scans the location barcode; WMS displays the expected SKU and quantity.
4. Associate physically counts all units in the location.
5. Associate enters the actual count into the RF gun.
6. If the count matches: WMS confirms the location as accurate; no further action required.
7. If the count does not match: WMS flags the discrepancy and generates a recount task.
8. A second associate (different from the first counter) performs the recount.
9. If the recount confirms the discrepancy, the Inventory Control Lead reviews and determines the appropriate adjustment (see Section 9.2).
10. All counts and adjustments are logged in the WMS cycle count history with associate ID, timestamp, and reason code.

### 9.2 Adjustment Authority Levels

**Reason Codes:**

|---|---|

| RC-03 | Pick error (wrong item or quantity picked) |

### 9.4 Dead Stock Review and Disposition

#### 9.4.1 Dead Stock Definition

Dead stock is inventory with **zero movement (no sales, no transfers, no picks) for 180+ days**.

#### 9.4.2 Review Process

2. Report is distributed to: DC General Manager, Merchandising, and the Replenishment team.
3. Merchandising has **15 business days** to provide disposition instructions for each SKU.
4. **Disposition options:**

| Disposition | Action | Timeline |

6. Dead stock that is not dispositioned within 60 days is escalated to the VP, Supply Chain Operations.

#### 10.1.2 Daily Pre-Shift Inspection

Every PIT operator must complete a pre-shift inspection before operating any powered industrial truck. The inspection uses the BRHI PIT Daily Inspection Checklist (Form BRHI-DC-030).

**Inspection Items:**

1. **Visual Inspection:** Fork condition, mast chains, tires (no chunks, proper inflation), overhead guard integrity, data plate legible.
2. **Fluid Levels:** Engine oil, hydraulic oil, coolant (IC trucks); battery water level and charge (electric trucks).
3. **Operational Checks:** Horn, lights (headlights, strobe, tail lights), backup alarm, seat belt, steering, brakes (service and parking), tilt function, lift function, side-shift.
4. **Safety Devices:** Fire extinguisher (charged and current inspection tag), seat belt functional.

If any item fails inspection:

- The operator immediately removes the PIT from service by attaching a "DO NOT OPERATE" tag to the steering wheel.
- The operator notifies the Shift Supervisor and Maintenance Supervisor.
- The PIT is not returned to service until repaired and released by the Maintenance team.
- Operators who operate a PIT without completing the pre-shift inspection are subject to corrective action up to and including termination.

#### 10.1.3 Preventive Maintenance Schedule

| PM Level | Interval | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| PM-1 (Minor) | Every 250 operating hours | Oil change, filter replacement, fluid top-off, belt inspection, lubrication |
| PM-2 (Standard) | Every 500 operating hours | PM-1 + brake inspection, tire condition assessment, hydraulic hose inspection, mast chain adjustment |
| PM-3 (Major) | Every 1,000 operating hours | PM-2 + complete fluid replacement, electrical system check, structural inspection, load test |
| Annual Comprehensive | Every 12 months | Full tear-down inspection per manufacturer specifications; structural weld inspection |

- Hour-meter readings are recorded by the Maintenance team weekly (every Monday).
- The Maintenance Supervisor maintains a PM schedule board (physical and digital) visible to all shifts.
- PM completion rate target: 100%. Any missed PM must be completed within 48 hours.

#### 10.1.4 Operator Certification

- All PIT operators must be certified per OSHA standard 29 CFR 1910.178.
- **Initial Certification:** Complete BRHI PIT Training Program (BRHI-TRN-010), which includes:
  - 4 hours classroom instruction (OSHA regulations, BRHI policies, load capacity, stability triangle, refueling/recharging, pedestrian safety).
  - 4 hours hands-on practical training on the specific truck type(s) the operator will use.
  - Written exam (minimum passing score: 80%) and practical evaluation (pass/fail).
- **Refresher Training:** Every 3 years, or immediately following:
  - An accident or near-miss involving the operator.
  - An observation of unsafe operation by a supervisor.
  - Assignment to a different truck type.
  - A change in workplace conditions that affects safe operation.
- Certifications are tracked in the BRHI Learning Management System (LMS). The Safety Manager generates an expiration report monthly.

### 12.1 Safety Organization

### 12.2 Daily Safety Walkthrough

The Shift Supervisor (or Safety Manager on day shift) conducts a formal safety walkthrough at the beginning of each shift.

**Checklist (Form BRHI-DC-050):**

1. Aisles clear and free of obstructions (no product, pallets, or debris in travel lanes).
2. Emergency exits unobstructed; exit signs illuminated.
3. Fire extinguishers accessible and current inspection tags (monthly visual, annual professional).
4. Spill kits stocked and accessible at each designated location.
5. PPE compliance observed (hard hats, safety vests, steel-toe boots, safety glasses as required).
6. PIT operations: speed compliance, horn usage at intersections, seat belts worn.
7. Dock doors: wheel chocks in place for all loaded trailers; dock plates secured.
8. Conveyor guards in place; emergency stop cords accessible.
9. Hazmat area: signage posted, segregation maintained, spill kit accessible.
10. Housekeeping: floors clean, no tripping hazards, proper waste disposal.

- Findings are documented and assigned a corrective action with a responsible party and due date.
- Critical safety findings (imminent danger) are corrected immediately; operations in the affected area are suspended until resolved.

### 12.3 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

### 12.4 Emergency Procedures

#### 12.4.1 Fire

1. Any associate who discovers a fire activates the nearest fire alarm pull station.
2. Call 911 and notify the Shift Supervisor immediately.
3. Only attempt to extinguish small, contained fires using available fire extinguishers if trained and safe to do so (PASS method: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep).
4. Evacuate the building via the nearest marked exit; proceed to the designated assembly point (parking lot flagpole area).
5. Shift Supervisors conduct a head count using the shift roster; report missing persons to the Incident Commander (Safety Manager or highest-ranking manager on site).
6. Do not re-enter the building until cleared by the fire department and the Incident Commander.

#### 12.4.2 Severe Weather (Tornado / High Wind)

1. Notification is received via the NOAA Weather Radio and BRHI mass notification system.
2. All PIT operations cease immediately; associates move to designated interior shelter areas (marked "Tornado Shelter").
3. Away from exterior walls, windows, overhead doors, and rack structures.
4. Shift Supervisors conduct head count at the shelter area.
5. Resume operations only after the all-clear is issued by the Safety Manager.

#### 12.4.3 Chemical Spill

1. If safe, contain the spill using the nearest spill kit. Do not attempt to clean unknown substances.
2. Evacuate the immediate area (minimum 25-foot radius for unknown substances).
3. Notify the Shift Supervisor and Safety Manager immediately.
4. Safety Manager assesses the material using the SDS and determines if the spill is within BRHI response capability or if an outside Hazmat team is required.
5. spills exceeding reportable quantities (per 40 CFR 302) are reported to the National Response Center (1-800-424-8802) and local emergency services.

#### 12.4.4 Medical Emergency

1. Call 911 immediately for life-threatening emergencies.
2. Trained associates administer first aid / CPR / AED as appropriate.
3. AED locations: main office, each dock office, break room, and near Zone HAZ entrance.
4. Shift Supervisor meets emergency responders at the main entrance and guides them to the scene.
5. Incident is documented on the Incident Report Form (BRHI-DC-051) within 4 hours.

#### 12.4.5 Active Threat

1. **Run:** If there is a safe path, evacuate the building immediately. Leave belongings behind. Do not use elevators.
2. **Hide:** If evacuation is not possible, find a secure room, lock and barricade the door, silence phones, and remain quiet.
3. **Fight:** As a last resort only. Attempt to incapacitate the threat with any available objects.
4. Call 911 when safe to do so.
5. Follow law enforcement instructions. Hands visible, empty, and raised.
6. Do not re-enter until law enforcement issues an all-clear.

### 12.5 Incident Reporting

- All workplace injuries, illnesses, near-misses, and property damage incidents must be reported.
- The associate involved (or a witness) reports the incident to the Shift Supervisor immediately.
- Shift Supervisor completes the Incident Report Form (BRHI-DC-051) within 4 hours.
- The Safety Manager conducts an investigation within 24 hours using the BRHI Root Cause Analysis (RCA) methodology (5-Why analysis).
- OSHA-recordable incidents are logged on the OSHA 300 Log within 7 calendar days of notification.
- The OSHA 300A Summary is posted in the DC office from February 1 through April 30 each year.
- Serious injuries (hospitalization, amputation, loss of an eye) are reported to OSHA within 8 hours (fatality) or 24 hours (hospitalization, amputation, loss of an eye) per 29 CFR 1904.39.

| Days Away, Restricted, or Transferred (DART) Rate | < 2.0 | Annual |

## 13. Reverse Logistics

### 13.1 Store Returns Processing

#### 13.1.1 Return Categories

| Category | Condition | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Resalable** | Unopened, original packaging, no damage | Return to active inventory (putaway) |
| **B — Open Box / Like New** | Opened, complete, functional, minor cosmetic wear | Refurbish and repackage; sell as "Open Box" via designated channel |
| **C — Damaged / Defective** | Damaged packaging, functional product | Assess for markdown, RTV, or disposition |
| **D — Non-Functional / Scrap** | Broken, missing parts, non-functional | RTV, dispose, or recycle |

#### 13.1.2 Returns Receiving Process

1. Store returns are received at designated dock doors (separate from regular inbound when possible).
2. Returns are scanned against the store's Return Authorization (RA) in WMS.
3. Each item is inspected and graded (A, B, C, or D) by the Returns Processing Team.
4. Graded items are dispositioned per the table above.
5. Category A items are putaway to active inventory within 24 hours.
6. Category B items are sent to the Refurbish Station for repackaging and relabeling within 48 hours.
7. Category C and D items are staged in the Returns Holding area for weekly disposition review.
8. All returns processing must be completed within **5 business days** of receipt at the DC.

### 13.2 Vendor Returns (RTV)

1. The Merchandising team or Inventory Control Lead identifies product eligible for return to vendor (overstock, defective, recall).
2. A Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) is requested from the vendor via the BRHI Vendor Portal.
3. Vendor issues RMA number with return shipping instructions and deadline.
4. Returns Processing Team picks, packs, and stages RTV product at the designated dock door.
5. RTV shipment is scheduled through TMS; BOL references the RMA number.
6. RTV credit is tracked in the ERP by Accounts Payable; uncredited RMAs older than 30 days are escalated to Vendor Compliance.

### 13.3 Product Recalls

1. BRHI Product Safety team issues a Recall Notification to all affected DCs and stores via email, the BRHI Alert System, and WMS hold.
2. Upon receipt of notification, the DC Safety Manager and Inventory Control Lead immediately place all affected product on hold in WMS (status: "Recall Hold").
3. Affected product in the DC is physically segregated and labeled with a "RECALL — DO NOT SHIP" tag.
4. The Shift Supervisor confirms recall hold compliance within **4 hours** of notification.
5. The Inventory Control Lead generates a report of all affected product quantities (on-hand and in-transit) within **24 hours**.
6. Disposition instructions are provided by the Product Safety team. Common dispositions:
   - Return to vendor (with RMA).
   - Destroy on-site under supervision (with Certificate of Destruction).
   - Quarantine pending regulatory investigation.
7. **Target: All recalled product is identified, segregated, and dispositioned within 48 hours of notification.**

### 13.4 Hazmat Returns and Disposal

- Hazmat returns are processed only by associates with current Hazmat Awareness Training (BRHI-TRN-015).
- Hazmat waste is stored in Zone HAZ in EPA-compliant containers, labeled per 40 CFR 262.
- A licensed hazmat waste hauler picks up accumulated waste per the BRHI Hazardous Waste Management Contract (monthly pickup schedule minimum).
- Manifests are retained for 3 years per EPA requirements.
- The Safety Manager maintains the hazmat waste storage area inspection log (weekly inspections).

### 14.2 Same-Day Shipping

- **Cutoff time: 2:00 PM local time** for same-day carrier pickup.
- Orders received before 2:00 PM are released to the pick queue within 15 minutes.
- Pick, pack, and ship must be completed by 5:00 PM for carrier cutoff.
- **Target: 98% of same-day eligible orders shipped same day.**

#### Same-Day Processing Workflow:

1. Order is received from the OMS (Order Management System) and routed to the nearest EFC or ship-from-store location.
2. WMS generates a pick task in the next available wave (waves run every 30 minutes for e-commerce).
3. Associate picks the order using RF-directed or voice-directed picking.
4. Order moves to the pack station. Packer verifies SKU and quantity, selects correct box size, adds void fill and packing slip, and seals.
5. Packer scans the order barcode; WMS generates the carrier shipping label and any required customs documentation.
6. Label is applied; order is placed on the outbound conveyor to the carrier sort lane.
7. Carrier scans and accepts the shipment; tracking number is transmitted to the OMS and customer notification is triggered.

### 14.3 Ship-from-Store (SFS)

Select BRHI retail locations operate as ship-from-store nodes to expand fulfillment capacity and reduce delivery times.

- **SLA: 4 hours from order receipt to carrier handoff.**
- SFS-eligible SKUs are flagged in WMS with an "SFS" attribute.
- Store associates receive pick tasks via the BRHI Associate Mobile App.
- Packing stations are located in the store stockroom with label printers and shipping supplies.
- Daily carrier pickup: 4:00 PM (cutoff for same-day ship) and 6:00 PM (second pickup during peak).
- SFS order accuracy target: 99.5%.
- SFS orders are audited at the same 2% rate as DC outbound orders (see Section 11.2).

### 14.4 E-Commerce Returns

1. Customer initiates return via BRHI.com or the Mobile App; system generates a Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) and prepaid return label.
2. Returned item is received at the designated EFC returns dock or at a participating retail store.
3. Returns associate scans the RMA barcode; WMS displays the expected item(s).
4. Associate inspects the returned item and assigns a grade (A, B, C, or D per Section 13.1.1).
5. **Refund processing:** Grade A and B items: refund authorized within 24 hours of receipt. Grade C and D items: refund authorized after inspection (up to 5 business days).
6. Grade A items are returned to active inventory; Grade B items are refurbished; Grade C/D items are dispositioned per reverse logistics policy.
7. **Return processing SLA:** All e-commerce returns must be inspected and dispositioned within 2 business days of receipt.
