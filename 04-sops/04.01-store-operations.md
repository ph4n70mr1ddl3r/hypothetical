## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes uniform policies and step-by-step procedures for all store-level operations at BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. (BRHI). It is designed to:

- Ensure a consistent customer experience across all 2,300+ BRHI locations.
- Protect company assets through rigorous cash handling, loss prevention, and safety practices.
- Provide associates and managers with clear, actionable instructions for daily opening, closing, and ongoing operations.
- Support compliance with federal, state, and local regulations.
- Serve as the primary training reference for new associates and the authoritative guide for resolving operational questions.

Every associate is expected to read, understand, and follow this SOP. Failure to comply may result in corrective action up to and including termination.

---

## 2. Scope

This document applies to:

- **All store associates** (full-time, part-time, seasonal, and temporary).
- **Store management** (Department Supervisors, Assistant Store Managers, Store Managers).
- **District and regional support functions** when performing duties inside a store location.
- **Third-party vendors and service providers** when on BRHI premises (see Section 10 for vendor check-in).

Exclusions: Distribution center operations, corporate office procedures, and e-commerce fulfillment center operations are covered under separate SOPs.

### 3.2 Oracle Self-Service Procurement for Store Supplies

Store associates use Oracle Self-Service Procurement for ordering non-merchandise items:

**Process Overview:**
1. Associate identifies need for store supplies or equipment
2. Associate logs into Oracle Self-Service Procurement via store tablet or back-office PC
3. Associate searches catalog or creates requisition
4. Requisition routes to Store Manager for approval
5. Approved requisition converts to purchase order
6. Goods received via Oracle receiving module
7. Invoice matched and paid via Oracle Accounts Payable

**Approval Thresholds:**
- Up to $500: Store Manager approval
- $501 - $2,500: District Manager approval
- Over $2,500: Regional VP approval

## 4. Store Opening Procedures

### 4.1 Overview

The store opening process begins **90 minutes before the published opening time** and follows a strict chronological sequence. The Manager on Duty (MOD) is responsible for ensuring every step is completed on time. The MOD initials each item on the opening checklist (see Section 4.3).

### 4.2 Detailed Timeline

#### T-90 Minutes — Management Arrival and Facility Security

**Responsible:** Manager on Duty (MOD)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | MOD arrives at the store. Use personal access card at the designated employee entrance. |
| 2 | Disarm the security alarm system using your unique code. The code must not be shared or written down. |
| 3 | Conduct a full facility walkthrough. Inspect the following in order: (a) Exterior perimeter — check doors, windows, loading dock, and dumpster area for signs of forced entry or vandalism. (b) Sales floor — check for water leaks, ceiling damage, fallen merchandise, or any safety hazard. (c) Restrooms — verify fixtures are intact, no flooding. (d) Break room and offices — general security check. (e) Backroom and receiving — confirm overhead doors are closed and locked, no unauthorized persons present. |
| 4 | If any security breach or hazard is found: do not enter further. Retreat to a safe location, call 911 if appropriate, then contact the District Manager and the BRHI Security Operations Center at 1-800-555-0199. |
| 5 | Turn on sales floor lighting (main overhead lights, aisle lighting, and department accent lighting). |
| 6 | Verify that the HVAC system is operating. The store temperature target is 68°F–72°F (20°C–22°C) year-round. Adjust thermostat if outside this range. |
| 7 | Unlock the cash office. Power on the cash office workstation and log into the BRHI Point-of-Sale (POS) management console. |
| 8 | Confirm the overnight environmental monitoring report (temperature, humidity, security sensors) shows no alerts. If alerts exist, document them in the Store Communication Log and escalate per the alert protocol. |

**Time Target:** All T-90 steps completed by T-75.

---

#### T-60 Minutes — Cash Office and System Startup

**Responsible:** MOD + Cash Office Associate (if scheduled)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Open the safe using dual-custody procedure (MOD + Cash Office Associate). Both individuals must be present. |
| 2 | Count the starting safe balance. Compare against the previous night's closing safe count recorded on Form BRHI-CASH-02. Any discrepancy must be reported immediately to the Store Manager and documented on Form BRHI-CASH-03 (Cash Variance Report). |
| 3 | Prepare register tills. Each register receives a standard starting till of **$200.00** with the following denomination breakdown: |
| | — $1 bills: 40 ($40.00) |
| | — $5 bills: 20 ($100.00) |
| | — $10 bills: 4 ($40.00) |
| | — $20 bills: 1 ($20.00) |
| | — Coin rolls: 2 × quarters ($20.00), 2 × dimes ($10.00), 1 × nickels ($10.00), 1 × pennies ($10.00) — totaling $50.00 in coin. |
| | — **Total: $200.00** — NOTE: adjusted breakdown sums to $200 due to rounding; individual denominations may vary by ±$5 provided total is $200. |
| 4 | Seal each till in a tamper-evident cash bag labeled with the register number, date, and preparer initials. |
| 5 | Boot all POS registers. Allow each to complete its startup sequence and confirm the network connection indicator shows green. |
| 6 | Power on self-checkout terminals. Run the diagnostic routine; verify that the bill acceptor, coin dispenser, barcode scanner, and receipt printer are functional. |
| 7 | Boot the Pro Desk POS workstation, garden center register (if seasonal), and any auxiliary registers. |
| 8 | Print the Daily Sales Forecast report from the POS management console. Note any planned promotions, price changes, or system messages. |
| 9 | Verify credit/debit card processing connectivity by running a $0.01 authorization test on one register, then voiding the transaction. |

**Time Target:** All T-60 steps completed by T-45.

---

#### T-45 Minutes — Department Readiness

**Responsible:** Department Supervisors + Floor Associates

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | All scheduled associates clock in no later than T-45. Late arrivals must be documented by the MOD. |
| 2 | Department Supervisors perform an aisle-by-aisle walkthrough of their assigned departments. Check for: (a) Downstocked shelves — top-stock pulled forward, no empty facings on primary displays. (b) Blocked aisles — remove any pallets, ladders, or equipment from customer areas. (c) Safety hazards — wet spots, broken glass, protruding nails or screws, loose wires, tripping hazards. Correct immediately or barricade and report. (d) Product placement — verify key promotional items are in the correct display locations per the current weekly ad plan. |
| 3 | Verify endcap displays match the current promotional planogram. Confirm signage is present, correct, and undamaged. Take a photo of each endcap with the BRHI Store Ops mobile app for compliance documentation. |
| 4 | Spot-check pricing accuracy on a minimum of 5 items per department. Compare shelf label price to the POS price (use a handheld scanner). Any discrepancy must be corrected before the store opens. |
| 5 | Confirm all power equipment (forklifts, reach trucks, order pickers) that will be used during business hours has a completed pre-trip inspection form (Form BRHI-EQ-01) signed by the certified operator. Equipment without a valid pre-trip form must not be operated. |
| 6 | Verify that all sample displays (e.g., appliance vignettes, tool demo stations) are powered on, clean, and presentable. |
| 7 | Check department-specific items: (a) Lumber — verify stack alignment, no leaning bundles. (b) Garden Center — turn on misters/irrigation if applicable, check plant health. (c) Paint — confirm mixer is operational, sample chips stocked. (d) Electrical/Plumbing — confirm commodity bins are full. |

**Time Target:** All T-45 steps completed by T-30.

---

#### T-30 Minutes — Lot Preparation

**Responsible:** Lot Associate(s) + MOD oversight

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Retrieve all shopping carts from the parking lot corrals and perimeter. Use the cart retrieval vehicle if available. |
| 2 | Stage a minimum of **40 standard carts** and **10 flatbed carts** at the front entrance vestibule. Adjust quantity based on the day of week and anticipated volume (weekends: 60 standard, 15 flatbed). |
| 3 | Inspect each cart for damage (wobbly wheels, bent frames, child seat belt integrity). Remove damaged carts from service and tag them for repair. |
| 4 | Place seasonal or promotional sidewalk signage in the designated positions in front of the store entrance. Signage must be weighted or secured to prevent wind displacement. |
| 5 | Verify that all exterior lighting (parking lot lights, entrance canopy, building signage) is functioning. If any are out, submit a maintenance request (Priority: Urgent) via the Facilities Management System. |
| 6 | Check the cart corrals for debris, trash, or hazards. Clean as needed. |
| 7 | If applicable, set up any outdoor promotional displays (e.g., seasonal plant tables, propane cage verification). |

**Time Target:** All T-30 steps completed by T-15.

---

#### T-15 Minutes — Final Readiness and Associate Deployment

**Responsible:** MOD + All Associates

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Conduct the **Daily Huddle** (see Section 14.1 for full agenda). Brief duration: 10 minutes maximum. Cover: daily sales goal, current promotions, safety reminder, schedule notes, and any customer service focus. |
| 2 | Distribute register tills to assigned cashiers. Each cashier counts their till in the presence of the Cash Office Associate, signs the Till Verification Log (Form BRHI-CASH-01), and proceeds to their register. |
| 3 | Confirm all cashiers are at their registers and have logged in. Self-checkout attendant should be stationed at the SCO bank. |
| 4 | Deploy floor associates to their assigned departments. Each department must have at least one associate present before the store opens. |
| 5 | Pro Desk associate(s) must be at the Pro Desk and logged in by T-5. |
| 6 | Verify background music is playing at an appropriate volume. |
| 7 | Confirm the greeter or designated front-end associate is positioned at the entrance. |
| 8 | MOD completes the **Opening Readiness Checklist** (Form BRHI-OPS-01) by initialing each section. If any item is incomplete, the MOD must note the exception and the expected resolution time. |

**Time Target:** All T-15 steps completed by T-0.

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | At the exact published opening time, unlock the front entrance doors (both sets, if double-vestibule). |
| 2 | The greeter or designated associate delivers the standard greeting to every customer entering: **"Welcome to BuildRight, how can I help you build it today?"** |
| 3 | MOD monitors the entrance for the first 15 minutes to ensure smooth flow and address any issues. |
| 4 | All associates on the sales floor immediately engage the 10-Foot Rule (see Section 8.1). |

## 5. Store Closing Procedures

### 5.1 Overview

The store closing process begins **30 minutes before the published closing time**. The MOD is responsible for ensuring every step is completed and all associates depart safely.

### 5.2 Detailed Timeline

#### T-30 Minutes — Last Call and Department Recovery Begin

**Responsible:** MOD + All Floor Associates

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Make the **last call announcement** over the PA system: *"Attention BuildRight customers, our store will be closing in 30 minutes. Please bring your final selections to the checkout area. Thank you for shopping at BuildRight."* |
| 2 | Floor associates in each department begin preliminary recovery: straighten merchandise on shelves, return go-backs to their home locations, and clear aisles of any obstacles. |
| 3 | The garden center associate secures the garden center gate or entrance if the garden center has a separate closing time (typically closes 30 minutes before the main store). |
| 4 | Department Supervisors begin verifying that all ladders, rolling equipment, and power tools are returned to their storage locations and are not left in customer aisles. |
| 5 | Lot associate begins final cart retrieval round. |

---

#### T-15 Minutes — Front Entrance Secured

**Responsible:** MOD + Front-End Supervisor

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Make the **15-minute warning announcement**: *"Attention BuildRight customers, our store will be closing in 15 minutes. Please proceed to the checkout area with your purchases. Thank you for shopping at BuildRight."* |
| 2 | Secure one set of the front entrance double doors (lock the outer vestibule doors). Leave the inner doors open so exiting customers can leave freely. Place a sign on the exterior: *"We are closing in 15 minutes. Entrance through this door is closed."* |
| 3 | The MOD or designated associate performs a **customer count**. Walk the entire sales floor and count every remaining customer. Radio the count to the front-end supervisor. |
| 4 | Assign one associate per major department zone to politely approach remaining customers and offer assistance in completing their shopping. Use the script: *"Good evening, we'll be closing shortly. Can I help you find anything else tonight?"* |
| 5 | Close the Pro Desk for the evening. The Pro Desk associate assists with remaining front-end tasks. |

---

#### T-0 — Doors Closed

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Make the **closing announcement**: *"BuildRight is now closed. Please bring all purchases to the checkout. Thank you for shopping with us, and have a great evening."* |
| 2 | Lock the remaining front entrance doors. |
| 3 | Station an associate at the exit to politely redirect any customers attempting to enter. |
| 4 | Allow all remaining customers already inside the store to complete their transactions. **Never rush a customer.** If a customer is still shopping, an associate should accompany them and help them finish efficiently. |
| 5 | Once the last customer has exited, the MOD confirms a **zero customer count** by radio from all department zones. |

---

#### T+15 Minutes — Register Reconciliation and Cash Pickup

**Responsible:** Cash Office Associate + MOD

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Each cashier closes their register by running the end-of-day (EOD) close procedure on the POS terminal. The register will print a reconciliation tape. |
| 2 | Cashier counts the physical cash in the drawer and records the amount on the Register Close Form (Form BRHI-CASH-04). |
| 3 | Compare the physical count to the POS system-reported total. Calculate the variance. |
| 4 | **Variance thresholds:** |
| | — Variance of **$0.00 to $5.00**: acceptable. Record on the form, no further action required. |
| | — Variance of **$5.01 to $20.00**: cashier must write an explanation on the form. MOD reviews and initials. |
| | — Variance of **more than $20.00**: MOD must conduct an investigation, review transaction journal, and complete Form BRHI-CASH-03 (Cash Variance Report). Notify the Store Manager. |
| 5 | Cash Office Associate performs the **cash pickup** from each register using dual-custody procedure (Cash Office Associate + one witness, who may be the MOD or a supervisor). Both individuals must observe the count. |
| 6 | All cash, checks, and credit card batch summaries are transported to the cash office in a sealed tamper-evident bag. |
| 7 | Self-checkout attendant closes all SCO terminals, removes cash cassettes, and brings them to the cash office under dual custody. |
| 8 | In the cash office, the Cash Office Associate counts all funds, reconciles against system totals, and prepares the bank deposit. The bank deposit must be balanced and sealed by T+30. |

---

#### T+30 Minutes — Department Recovery

**Responsible:** All Associates (assigned departments)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Each associate returns to their assigned department and performs full recovery: |
| | — Face all shelves (pull products to the front of the shelf, align labels facing outward). |
| | — Return all go-backs (items left in wrong locations) to their correct home positions. |
| | — Remove all trash and debris from shelves and the floor. |
| | — Wipe down any visibly soiled surfaces. |
| | — Straighten all promotional and seasonal displays. |
| 2 | Sweep or spot-mop any spills or debris on the floor. If the floor requires more extensive cleaning, submit a request for the overnight cleaning crew via the Facilities Management System. |
| 3 | Remove all safety hazards: fallen merchandise, loose banding straps, spilled product (e.g., broken bags of concrete mix, spilled nails). |
| 4 | Return all power equipment (forklifts, reach trucks, order pickers, pallet jacks) to the equipment charging area. Plug in charging cables. |
| 5 | Department Supervisors verify their departments are recovery-complete and report to the MOD via radio. |
| 6 | Stock any critical out-of-stock items from the backroom that are expected to sell first thing the next morning. |

**Responsible:** MOD

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | MOD conducts a **final security walkthrough** of the entire store: |
| | — Verify all customers and non-essential personnel have exited the building. |
| | — Check all restrooms, fitting rooms, and hiding places (under displays, in appliance vignettes). |
| | — Confirm all fire exits are unobstructed and properly closed. |
| | — Verify all backroom doors, receiving dock doors, and emergency exits are closed and locked. |

| 2 | Verify all registers and POS terminals are logged out and powered down. |
| 3 | Turn off non-essential lighting (department accent lights, demo stations, display case lights). Leave emergency lighting and overnight security lighting on. |

**Time Target:** All closing steps completed within 45 minutes of store close. No associate should remain in the building more than 60 minutes after close without Store Manager authorization.

## 6. Cash Handling Procedures

### 6.1 Register Opening — Standard Till

- **Standard starting till amount:** $200.00 per register.
- Tills are prepared by the Cash Office Associate during the opening process (see Section 4.2, T-60).
- Each cashier must count their assigned till in the presence of the Cash Office Associate and sign the Till Verification Log (Form BRHI-CASH-01) before placing the till in the register.
- If a till count does not match $200.00, the discrepancy must be documented and resolved before the till is placed in service. The Cash Office Associate will adjust the till to $200.00 and note the adjustment on the form.

### 6.2 Transaction Processing

| Task | Procedure |
|---|---|
| **Scanning** | Scan every item using the barcode scanner. Verify the item description and price on the screen before completing the transaction. If a barcode is damaged, enter the UPC manually. If the UPC is unknown, contact the Department Supervisor for a SKU lookup. |
| **Manual Price Entry** | Only authorized associates (Supervisor level and above) may enter a manual price. The manual price must be justified (e.g., price override from a valid competitor ad, system error). The associate must enter their employee ID and a reason code. |
| **Price Overrides** | Price overrides exceeding 10% of the retail price require Supervisor approval. Overrides exceeding 25% require Assistant Store Manager approval. All overrides are logged in the POS audit trail. |
| **Coupons** | Accept manufacturer coupons per the coupon policy (one coupon per item, must scan, not expired). Accept BRHI-issued coupons per the terms printed on the coupon. Internet coupons accepted up to $10.00 face value. |
| **Tax Exemption** | Verify the customer's tax-exempt certificate is current (check expiration date). Scan the certificate barcode or enter the tax-exempt ID. File a copy of the certificate with the daily paperwork. |
| **Split Tender** | Customers may split payment across multiple tender types. Process each tender type in the order the customer requests. |
| **Layaway** | BRHI does not currently offer layaway. Direct customers to the BRHI Consumer Credit Card or project financing options. |

### 6.3 Cash Drops

- **Threshold:** When the cash in a register drawer exceeds **$500.00**, a cash drop must be performed.
- **Procedure:**
  1. The cashier signals the front-end supervisor for a cash drop.
  2. The cashier counts the amount to be dropped in view of the supervisor.
  3. Both the cashier and supervisor sign the Cash Drop Slip (Form BRHI-CASH-05), noting the register number, amount, date, and time.
  4. The cash is placed in a tamper-evident deposit bag, sealed in front of both parties.
  5. The supervisor transports the deposit bag directly to the cash office safe. The cashier never leaves the register unattended.
  6. The Cash Office Associate logs the drop in the Cash Drop Log and stores the bag in the safe.
- **Frequency:** Drops should be performed proactively; do not wait for the drawer to become unmanageably full. During high-volume periods (weekends, promotional events), the MOD should schedule drops every 2 hours.

### 6.4 Safe Procedures

- **Dual Custody:** The safe must always be accessed by two authorized individuals. Both must be present when the safe is opened. The only exception is during the alarm-disarm process at store opening, when the MOD may access the safe alone if no Cash Office Associate is yet scheduled (this exception must be logged on Form BRHI-CASH-02).
- **Daily Count:** The safe must be counted at minimum twice per day — once during the opening procedure and once during the closing procedure. Counts must be reconciled to the expected balance.
- **Discrepancy Handling:**
  - Discrepancy of $0.01–$5.00: Document on the safe count form. Investigate and resolve within 24 hours.
  - Discrepancy of $5.01–$50.00: Document, notify the Store Manager immediately, and notify the District Manager within 4 hours.
  - Discrepancy exceeding $50.00: Document, notify the Store Manager and District Manager immediately. The Loss Prevention department must be notified within 1 hour. Do not discuss the discrepancy with any associate not directly involved.
- **Bank Deposits:** The daily bank deposit is prepared during the closing procedure. The deposit must be balanced, sealed in a tamper-evident bag, and placed in the safe for armored car pickup. The armored car pickup is scheduled by the District Office. If the armored car does not arrive, the MOD must contact the carrier and document the delay.

### 6.5 Register Closing — Reconciliation

- Each cashier is responsible for balancing their own register at the end of their shift or at store closing, whichever comes first.
- The cashier runs the EOD or shift-close report, counts the physical cash, and records the amount on Form BRHI-CASH-04.
- **Variance Thresholds:**

  | Variance Range | Action |
  |---|---|
  | $0.00 – $5.00 (over or under) | Record on form. No further action. |
  | $5.01 – $20.00 | Cashier writes explanation. MOD reviews and initials. Pattern of repeated variances (3 or more in a 30-day period) triggers counseling by the Store Manager. |
  | $20.01 – $50.00 | MOD investigates transaction journal. Cash Variance Report (Form BRHI-CASH-03) completed. Store Manager notified. |
  | Over $50.00 | Store Manager and Loss Prevention notified immediately. Cash Variance Report completed. Cashier may be suspended pending investigation per HR Policy BRHI-HR-12. |

- All variance reports are retained in the store's cash handling file for 12 months and are subject to audit by the Internal Audit team.

### 6.6 Refund Processing

| Refund Amount | Authorization Level | Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| $0.00 – $50.00 | Any trained cashier | Original receipt or order lookup; valid photo ID for cash refunds; refund to original tender type |
| $50.01 – $200.00 | Supervisor (Department Supervisor, Front-End Supervisor, or above) | All of the above plus Supervisor override code; item(s) must be physically inspected for condition |
| $200.01 and above | Manager (Assistant Store Manager, Store Manager, or MOD) | All of the above plus Manager override code; customer signature on the Refund Authorization Form (BRHI-REF-01); item(s) routed to the Returns Processing area for disposition |

- **Refund without receipt:** One-time exception allowed per customer per 12-month period, up to $25.00 store credit only. Requires MOD approval and valid photo ID, which is recorded in the system.
- **Refund time limit:** Standard returns accepted within 90 days of purchase with receipt. Major appliances and special-order items: 30 days. Plants and live goods: 1 year guarantee (replace or store credit).
- **Fraud indicators:** Associates must report any of the following to Loss Prevention: frequent returns without receipts, returns of clearly used items presented as new, returns from multiple stores on the same day, returns where the customer becomes aggressive when asked for ID.

### 6.7 Petty Cash

- **Amount:** Each store is allocated a **$100.00 petty cash fund**.
- **Authorized Uses:** Small office supplies, emergency cleaning supplies, postage, small vendor tips (e.g., delivery drivers), and other minor expenses approved by the Store Manager.
- **Prohibited Uses:** Personal loans to associates, cashing personal checks, purchasing inventory for resale, any expense exceeding $25.00 per single transaction.
- **Documentation:** Every disbursement must be recorded on the Petty Cash Log (Form BRHI-CASH-06) with the date, amount, payee, purpose, and the associate's initials. A receipt must be attached for every transaction.
- **Replenishment:** The petty cash fund is replenished weekly by the Cash Office Associate from the store's operating cash. The replenishment must be approved by the Store Manager.
- **Audit:** The petty cash fund must be counted and reconciled monthly by the Assistant Store Manager (not the Cash Office Associate). Results are documented on Form BRHI-CASH-07 and filed.

## 7. Customer Service Standards

### 7.1 The 10-Foot Rule

Every associate, regardless of role or department, must acknowledge any customer who comes within **10 feet** (approximately 3 meters). Acknowledgment may be:

- A verbal greeting ("Good morning!" or "How are you doing today?").
- A smile and a nod (if the associate is actively helping another customer).
- Brief eye contact followed by a verbal offer to help as soon as the current task is complete.

The 10-Foot Rule applies at all times the associate is on the sales floor, including during recovery, stocking, and other task-based activities.

### 7.2 Greeting Protocol

- **Standard greeting at store entrance:** *"Welcome to BuildRight, how can I help you build it today?"*
- **Department greeting:** *"Hi there! Are you finding everything okay in [department name]?"*
- **When approaching a customer who appears to need help:** *"What project are you working on? I'd love to help you get the right materials."*
- **When a customer declines help:** *"No problem! My name is [Name] and I'll be right over here if you need anything."*

### 7.3 Product Location — Walk, Don't Point

When a customer asks where an item is located, the associate must **physically walk the customer to the product's location**. This applies without exception.

- If the associate does not know the location, they must use a handheld device to look up the SKU and aisle location, then walk the customer there.
- If the associate's department is not staffed and they must walk away, they must radio for coverage or ask a neighboring department associate to watch their area.
- **Never say "It's in Aisle 12" and point.** Always escort the customer.

### 7.4 Pro Desk Service Standards

The Pro Desk is BRHI's dedicated service point for professional contractors, remodelers, and trade customers enrolled in the BRHI Pro program.

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Dedicated Pro Associates** | Minimum 1 Pro Desk associate scheduled at all times during Pro Desk hours. During peak hours (Mon–Fri 6:00–10:00 AM and 3:00–6:00 PM, Sat 7:00–11:00 AM), minimum 2 associates scheduled. |
| **Priority Checkout** | Pro customers receive checkout at the Pro Desk. Pro Desk associates must not leave the desk unattended to handle non-Pro tasks during peak hours. |
| **Job-Site Delivery** | Pro customers may schedule job-site delivery for purchases over $250. Deliveries are scheduled in 2-hour windows. The Pro Desk associate must confirm delivery address, contact phone, and any site-access instructions (gate codes, Hazmat restrictions) at the time of order. |
| **Pro Account Management** | Pro customers with commercial credit accounts may charge purchases to their account. The Pro Desk associate must verify the account is in good standing (no past-due balance exceeding 30 days) before processing a charge. |
| **Product Sourcing** | If the store does not stock an item a Pro customer needs, the Pro Desk associate must offer to: (1) check nearby BRHI stores for availability, (2) order the item for delivery to the store or job site, or (3) provide the estimated lead time for special order. |
| **Relationship Building** | Pro Desk associates should know their top 20 Pro customers by name. The Pro Desk should maintain a log of frequent special orders and proactively notify Pro customers when relevant items go on promotion. |

### 7.5 Phone Answering

- All incoming calls must be answered within **3 rings**.
- **Standard greeting:** *"Thank you for calling BuildRight [City/Store Name], this is [Name]. How can I help you today?"*
- If the call needs to be transferred: *"Let me connect you with someone in [Department]. Please hold for just a moment."* Wait for the receiving associate to pick up before releasing the call. Do not cold-transfer — provide the caller's name and question to the receiving associate.
- If no one answers the transfer after 5 rings, return to the caller: *"I'm sorry, my colleague is with another customer right now. May I take a message and have them call you back, or can I help you find an answer?"*
- If the caller needs to hold: *"May I place you on a brief hold?"* Wait for confirmation. Check back with the caller every **60 seconds**. If the hold will exceed 3 minutes, offer a callback instead.

### 7.6 Customer Complaints — The LAST Method

All associates are trained to handle customer complaints using the **LAST** method:

| Step | Action | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **L — Listen** | Let the customer fully explain their issue without interrupting. Maintain eye contact and open body language. Take notes if the issue is complex. | *"I understand. Please tell me more about what happened."* |
| **A — Apologize** | Sincerely apologize for the customer's experience, regardless of fault. Do not blame other associates, vendors, or the customer. | *"I'm really sorry you had that experience. That's not what we want for you."* |
| **S — Solve** | Take ownership and offer a solution. If the solution is within your authority, resolve it immediately. If it requires a supervisor or manager, explain: *"I'm going to get my manager involved right now so we can get this resolved for you."* Stay with the customer until the solution is in motion. | *"Here's what I can do: I'll [specific solution]. Does that work for you?"* |
| **T — Thank** | Thank the customer for bringing the issue to your attention. Confirm that their satisfaction is a priority. | *"Thank you for letting us know. We appreciate your business and I want to make sure you leave happy."* |

- **Escalation:** If a customer is not satisfied with the solution offered by an associate, the associate must escalate to a Supervisor. If the Supervisor cannot resolve it, escalate to the MOD. If the MOD cannot resolve it, the Store Manager must be contacted. Under no circumstances should a customer be told "there's nothing we can do."
- **Documentation:** All complaints that reach the Supervisor level or above must be logged in the Customer Feedback System (CFS) within 24 hours, including the resolution and any follow-up actions.

### 7.7 Mystery Shopper Program

- **Frequency:** Each store is shopped **once per month** by an independent mystery shopping firm.
- **Scope:** Mystery shoppers evaluate the following dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | Scoring Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| Greeting and Acknowledgment | 20% | Was the shopper acknowledged within 30 seconds of entering a department? Was the 10-Foot Rule observed? |
| Product Knowledge | 20% | Was the associate able to answer questions about the product? Did they offer alternatives or complementary items? |
| Walk to Product | 15% | Did the associate physically walk the shopper to the product location (no pointing)? |
| Suggestive Selling | 10% | Did the associate suggest related items, accessories, or services (e.g., installation, delivery, tool rental)? |
| Checkout Experience | 15% | Was the checkout efficient? Was the cashier friendly? Were all items scanned correctly? Was the shopper thanked? |
| Store Condition | 10% | Was the store clean, well-lit, and organized? Were restrooms clean and stocked? |
| Overall Satisfaction | 10% | The mystery shopper's overall rating of their experience on a 1–10 scale. |

- **Scoring:** Each dimension is scored 0–100. The weighted total produces a composite score of 0–100.
- **Target:** All stores must achieve a minimum composite score of **80**. Stores scoring below 70 for two consecutive months must submit a corrective action plan to the District Manager within 7 days.
- **Results:** Mystery shop results are shared with the Store Manager and District Manager within 10 business days. Individual associate scores are not shared with the associate's name — results are aggregated by department to protect anonymity of the shopper process.

## 8. Department Management

### 8.1 Planogram Compliance

- **Weekly Walk:** Every Department Supervisor must perform a planogram compliance walk once per week (recommended: Tuesday or Wednesday, after the weekend recovery and promotional changeover).
- **What to Check:** Compare the current shelf layout to the corporate planogram for each aisle section. Verify: (a) Products are in the correct shelf position. (b) Facings are set to the correct count. (c) Shelf labels match the product and price. (d) No unauthorized products are placed in planogrammed locations.

- **Non-Compliance:** If a section does not comply, the Department Supervisor must correct it within 48 hours. If the non-compliance is due to out-of-stocks (the correct product is unavailable), the supervisor must note this in the app and request replenishment from the distribution center.
- **Corporate Visits:** The Planogram Compliance team at the Home Office reviews a random sample of 10% of stores per month. Stores found to have less than 85% planogram compliance receive a written action plan requirement from the Regional Vice President.

### 8.2 Endcap and Promotional Display Execution

All signage, product, and pricing must be in place by the store opening on the first day of the promotional period. |
| **Verification** | Sunday by 12:00 PM | MOD or Assistant Store Manager verifies all endcaps are set correctly and photographs them for the compliance dashboard. |

- **Seasonal Displays:** Large seasonal setups (Spring Garden, Holiday Décor, Back-to-School, etc.) follow a separate timeline distributed by the Merchandising team 6 weeks in advance. These may require overnight or early-morning setup teams.

### 8.3 Price Accuracy

- **Weekly Audit:** Each department must conduct a price accuracy audit of a minimum of **100 SKUs per week**. SKUs should be selected randomly (use the POS price audit function to generate a random sample).
- **Method:** Scan the shelf label barcode and the product barcode. Compare both to the POS system price. Record any discrepancies.
- **Target:** **98% price accuracy** (no more than 2 errors per 100 items scanned).
- **Correction:** Any price discrepancy must be corrected immediately. If the shelf label is wrong, print a new label. If the POS system is wrong, contact the Pricing Help Desk at ext. 7700 or pricing.help@brhi.com.
- **Reporting:** The Department Supervisor records the weekly audit results in the Price Accuracy Log (Form BRHI-PRICE-01). The Store Manager reviews all logs weekly and signs off. Stores below 98% for two consecutive weeks must submit a corrective action plan to the District Manager.

### 8.4 Downstocking and Recovery

- **Continuous Downstocking:** Associates are expected to pull product from the top stock (overstock shelves above the main shelf level) to fill empty facings throughout the day. This is a continuous task, not a one-time event.
- **End-of-Day Recovery:** At the end of each business day, every department must perform a full recovery (see Section 5.2, T+30). Shelves must be faced, go-backs returned, and the department left in "grand opening" condition.
- **Priority Areas:** The front entrance, main aisles, checkout area, and Pro Desk area receive the highest recovery priority and should be continuously maintained throughout the day.

### 8.5 Markdown Procedures

## 9. Receiving and Backroom

### 9.1 Delivery Scheduling

- All inbound shipments from the BRHI Distribution Centers (DCs) are scheduled by the Transportation team.
- **Standard appointment windows:** Deliveries arrive during one of the following windows:
  - Morning: 5:00 AM – 9:00 AM
  - Midday: 11:00 AM – 2:00 PM
  - Afternoon: 3:00 PM – 6:00 PM
- Stores receive a **Delivery Notification** via the Receiving Module in the POS system 24 hours before the scheduled delivery. This notification includes: PO number(s), expected carton count, total weight, and any special handling requirements (hazmat, oversized, etc.).
- **Direct Store Deliveries (DSD)** from local vendors must be scheduled by the vendor through the BRHI Vendor Portal. Vendors must confirm their appointment at least 48 hours in advance. Walk-up deliveries (no appointment) are accepted only with MOD approval and must be processed within 2 hours.

### 9.2 Receiving Process

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | The Receiving Associate verifies the delivery truck at the dock. Confirm the carrier name, trailer number, and BOL (Bill of Lading) number against the scheduled appointment in the Receiving Module. |
| 2 | Before unloading, inspect the truck: check for signs of water intrusion, pest infestation, unusual odors, or temperature concerns (for temperature-sensitive products). If any issues are found, photograph the condition, document on the BOL, and contact the MOD before unloading. |
| 3 | Unload the truck using appropriate equipment (pallet jack, forklift). Verify each pallet/carton against the BOL. **Count every carton.** Do not accept the driver's count — perform an independent verification. |
| 4 | Inspect cartons for visible damage (crushed, torn, water-stained, open). If damage is found: photograph it, note the specific carton and damage description on the BOL, and set the damaged carton aside for a damage claim. |
| 5 | Scan each carton's barcode label against the Purchase Order (PO) in the Receiving Module. The system will confirm or flag discrepancies. Resolve any discrepancies (over-shipments, short-shipments, wrong items) before signing the BOL. |
| 6 | Sign the BOL only after all cartons are counted and scanned. Note any exceptions (shortages, overages, damages) on the BOL before signing. The driver and the Receiving Associate both sign. Retain a copy; the driver retains a copy. |
| 7 | For DSD deliveries, the vendor must provide an invoice. The Receiving Associate verifies the invoice against the PO (quantities, prices, items). Any pricing discrepancy must be resolved with the vendor before the invoice is approved for payment. |
| 8 | Move received product to the staging area in the backroom. Label all pallets/cartons with the receiving date, PO number, and department using the BRHI staging labels. |

### 9.3 Vendor Check-In Procedures

- All vendors (DSD, service providers, demo representatives) must check in at the receiving desk upon arrival.
- The Receiving Associate verifies the vendor's appointment in the system and checks the vendor's government-issued photo ID.
- The vendor is issued a **Vendor Badge** (bright orange lanyard) that must be worn visibly at all times while on BRHI premises.
- Vendors are not permitted on the sales floor unescorted unless they have a current BRHI Vendor Access Agreement on file. All other vendors must be escorted by a BRHI associate.
- When the vendor's work is complete, they must check out at the receiving desk, return the Vendor Badge, and retrieve their ID.
- The Receiving Associate logs the vendor's check-in and check-out times in the Vendor Log.

### 9.4 Putaway Standards

- All received product must be moved from the receiving staging area to its designated backroom storage location or directly to the sales floor within **24 hours of receipt**.
- Priority putaway (within 4 hours): Critical out-of-stocks identified by the Department Supervisor, promotional product for a current or imminent sale event, and perishable items (live plants, seasonal items with limited shelf life).
- Backroom locations must be labeled with a bin location code (format: `Aisle-Rack-Shelf`, e.g., `BR-A3-S2`). The Receiving Module assigns a putaway location for each SKU. Associates must place product in the assigned location and confirm the putaway in the system by scanning the location barcode and the product barcode.
- Product must not be left on the receiving dock or in the staging area beyond the 24-hour window. The MOD must review the receiving dock at the end of each day and escalate any overdue putaways.

### 9.5 Backroom Organization

- **Labeled Locations:** Every storage location in the backroom must be labeled with a unique bin location code. No product may be stored in an unlabeled location.
- **FIFO Rotation (First In, First Out):** All products with expiration dates (e.g., adhesives, caulks, fertilizers, seeds, food items) must be rotated using FIFO. New product is placed behind existing product. The Receiving Associate must check expiration dates during putaway and rotate as needed.
- **Clean and Clear Aisles:** Backroom aisles must be kept clear of obstructions at all times. No product may be stored in aisles, in front of fire exits, or blocking emergency equipment (fire extinguishers, electrical panels, eyewash stations).
- **Overstock Management:** The Department Supervisor reviews backroom overstock weekly. Product that has been in the backroom for more than 60 days without a sale must be flagged for markdown or RTV consideration.
- **Security:** High-value merchandise (power tools, compressors, generators) must be stored in the secure cage in the backroom. Access to the secure cage is restricted to Department Supervisors and above.

### 9.6 Hazardous Material Receiving

- **Definition:** Hazardous materials include, but are not limited to: paints, solvents, adhesives, pesticides, herbicides, propane, concrete mix, insulation, and cleaning chemicals.
- **Receiving Procedures:**
  1. Verify the SDS (Safety Data Sheet) for the product is on file and accessible in the store's SDS binder (located at the receiving desk and at the customer service desk).
  2. Inspect containers for leaks, damage, or compromised seals. Do not accept leaking containers. If a leak is discovered during unloading, contain the spill immediately using the spill kit located at the receiving dock, notify the MOD, and follow the SDS instructions for cleanup.
  3. Transport hazardous materials to their designated storage locations using the prescribed handling equipment (no open flames, proper ventilation, no stacking beyond recommended limits).
  4. Store flammable materials in the flammable storage cabinet. Store corrosive materials on corrosion-resistant shelving. Store all hazardous materials below eye level.
  5. Propane tanks must be stored in the exterior propane cage. Never store propane tanks indoors.
- **Spill Response:** In the event of a hazardous material spill, refer to the SDS for the specific product. Small spills (less than 1 gallon of liquid or 1 pound of solid) may be cleaned by trained associates using the spill kit. Large spills require evacuation of the immediate area, notification of the MOD, and contact with the BRHI Environmental Health & Safety (EHS) hotline at 1-800-555-0177.

## 10. Pricing and Signage

### 10.1 Price Change Execution

- **Weekly Batch:** The corporate Pricing team generates a weekly batch of price changes every Thursday. The batch is processed in the POS system **Saturday overnight** (typically between 11:00 PM Saturday and 4:00 AM Sunday).
- **Sunday Morning Verification:** Before store opening on Sunday, the Department Supervisors must verify that all new prices are reflected on the shelf labels and in the POS system. The POS system will flag any price changes that require manual label printing.
- **Label Printing:** New shelf labels are printed from the POS system's Price Change Report. Labels must be printed and applied by **Sunday 6:00 AM**. Old labels must be removed — do not layer new labels over old ones.
- **Mid-Week Changes:** Emergency or competitive price changes may be issued mid-week by the Pricing team. These are communicated via the POS system and email to the Store Manager. Mid-week changes must be executed within **4 hours** of notification during business hours.

### 10.2 Promotional Signage

- **Setup:** All promotional signage for the new promotional week must be in place by **Sunday 6:00 AM**. Signage kits are shipped to the store the preceding Wednesday and include: endcap signs, aisle violators, hanging banners, register signs, and window clings.
- **Placement:** Follow the promotional signage planogram included in the signage kit. Each sign is numbered and corresponds to a specific location in the store. Do not improvise sign placement.
- **Condition:** All signage must be clean, undamaged, and current. Replace any torn, faded, or handwritten signs immediately.
- **Teardown:** Remove promotional signage on the last day of the promotional period, after store close. Do not leave expired promotional signage on the sales floor. expired signs create customer confusion and price disputes.

### 10.3 Clearance Management

- **Percentage-Off Clearance:** Used for broad clearance events (e.g., "All Summer Patio — 50% Off"). Signage must state the percentage off and the original price range. The POS system applies the discount at checkout — associates do not need to re-price individual items.
- **Specific Price Clearance:** Used when individual items are marked to a specific price. A clearance sticker must be applied to each item with the new price clearly visible. The clearance sticker barcode must be scanned at checkout (it overrides the original price in the POS system).
- **Clearance Rack/Table:** Each department should maintain a designated clearance area. Clearance items must not be intermingled with full-price merchandise.
- **Final Clearance:** Items that have been on clearance for more than 60 days and are still unsold must be reviewed for further markdown (to a maximum of 75% off), RTV, or disposal.

### 10.4 Price Match Policy

- **Policy:** BRHI will match the advertised price of any identical in-stock item from a local competitor, plus an additional **10% of the difference**.
  - Example: BRHI sells a drill for $129. Competitor advertises it for $109. Difference = $20. BRHI matches $109 and takes an additional 10% of $20 = $2.00 off. Customer pays **$107.00**.
- **Requirements:** The customer must present the competitor's current printed advertisement or show the competitor's website price on their mobile device at the time of purchase. The item must be identical (same brand, model number, and condition).
- **Exclusions:**
  - Online-only retailers without a local brick-and-mortar presence (e.g., Amazon third-party sellers).
  - Marketplace or auction prices.
  - Clearance, closeout, liquidation, or going-out-of-business sales.
  - Membership- or club-based pricing (e.g., Costco member-only prices).
  - Installation, labor, or service charges.
  - Typographical errors in competitor advertising.
  - Competitors located more than 50 miles from the BRHI store.
- **Processing:** Price matches are processed at the register by a Supervisor or above. The Supervisor enters the price match in the POS system, selecting the "Price Match" reason code, and records the competitor name and the advertised price. The customer's copy of the ad or a screenshot is retained and filed with the daily register paperwork.

### 11.4 Shopping Cart Management

- **Collection Schedule:** Cart retrieval runs are conducted at the following times:

  - Every 30 minutes during peak periods (weekends, promotional events)
  - Final retrieval at T-30 during closing

## 12. Loss Prevention Integration

### 12.1 High-Theft Merchandise Procedures

- **High-Theft Categories:** The following product categories are designated as high-theft and require enhanced security measures:
  - Power tools (cordless drills, circular saws, impact drivers)
  - Hand tools (premium brands — DeWalt, Milwaukee, Makita)
  - Copper wire and copper pipe
  - Lithium-ion batteries and battery packs
  - Premium adhesives and sealants
  - Small electronics (security cameras, smart home devices)
- **Security Measures:**
  - High-theft items must be merchandised in the secure display case or fitted with electronic article surveillance (EAS) tags.
  - The secure display case is locked. Customers must request assistance to view or purchase items from the case. The associate unlocks the case, retrieves the item, and walks it to the register for the customer (the customer does not carry the item through the store).
  - EAS-tagged items that trigger the entrance/exit tower alarm must be handled per the EAS Response Procedure: the associate at the exit politely asks the customer to step back inside, verifies the receipt against the merchandise, and removes or deactivates the tag. If the customer does not have a receipt, the MOD is called immediately.
  - Copper wire and pipe must be stored in the secure cage in the backroom. A small display sample may be on the sales floor with a "See Associate for Purchase" sign.

### 12.2 Fitting Room Procedures

- Applicable departments: Workwear and apparel (where offered in BRHI locations with a workwear department).
- **Procedure:**
  1. An associate must count the items the customer is bringing into the fitting room. Record the count on the fitting room count board.
  2. The customer may take a maximum of 6 items into the fitting room at one time.
  3. When the customer exits, the associate counts the items again and verifies the count matches.
  4. Any clothing left behind is inspected for damage, theft tags, or sensor removal. Re-hang or re-fold and return to the sales floor.
  5. Fitting rooms are checked and cleared every 30 minutes during peak periods and hourly otherwise.

### 12.3 Receipt Checking at Exits

- **Approach:** BRHI practices a customer-friendly receipt verification process. Receipt checking is conducted at designated high-volume periods (weekends, promotional events) and at the MOD's discretion.
- **Procedure:**
  1. The exit associate stands near (not blocking) the exit.
  2. The associate makes eye contact, smiles, and says: *"Hi there! Do you have your receipt handy? I just need a quick glance."*
  3. The associate visually verifies that the number of large or unbagged items (e.g., lumber, appliances, large boxes) matches the receipt.
  4. This should take no more than 10–15 seconds per customer.
  5. If a discrepancy is suspected, the associate does not accuse the customer. Instead: *"I just want to make sure you weren't overcharged — let me have a cashier double-check this item for you."* The MOD is discreetly notified.
  6. **Never** physically restrain or block a customer. Safety first.

### 12.4 Internal Theft Indicators and Reporting

- **Indicators:** Associates should be aware of the following potential indicators of internal theft:
  - Cash register variances that exceed thresholds repeatedly.
  - Excessive voids, no-sales, or refund transactions by a single associate.
  - Associate observed placing merchandise in personal vehicles or bags without scanning.
  - Associate observed scanning items at incorrect (lower) prices for friends or family.
  - Associate who insists on working alone or in isolated areas.
  - Inventory shrinkage significantly higher in one department compared to similar departments.
  - Lifestyle inconsistent with compensation level (not conclusive, but may warrant attention).
- **Reporting:** Any associate who observes or suspects internal theft must report it to the **Loss Prevention Hotline** at 1-800-555-0199 or via the anonymous online reporting form at brhi.com/ethics. Reports may be made anonymously. **No associate will face retaliation for making a good-faith report.**
- **Investigation:** All reports are investigated by the Loss Prevention department. Associates under investigation may be reassigned or suspended pending the outcome, per HR Policy BRHI-HR-12. Loss Prevention will coordinate with the Store Manager and HR as appropriate.

## 13. Store Meetings and Communication

### 13.1 Daily Huddle

- **Frequency:** Every day, conducted T-15 (15 minutes before store opening).
- **Duration:** 10 minutes maximum.
- **Led by:** Manager on Duty (MOD).
- **Location:** Front of the store, near the entrance vestibule or in the break room.
- **Agenda:**
  1. Safety moment (1 minute): A brief reminder or tip related to store safety (e.g., proper lifting technique, forklift awareness, seasonal hazard).
  2. Daily sales goal (1 minute): Yesterday's results vs. plan, today's sales target.
  3. Promotions and priorities (3 minutes): Key promotional items, new ads, any items requiring special attention.
  4. Schedule notes (2 minutes): Call-outs, schedule changes, coverage gaps, and how they are addressed.
  5. Customer service focus (2 minutes): A specific customer service standard to emphasize today (e.g., walk to product, suggestive selling, LAST method).
  6. Open floor (1 minute): Quick questions or announcements from associates.
- **Attendance:** All associates scheduled to work the opening shift must attend. Associates arriving after the huddle must be briefed by their Department Supervisor before beginning work.

### 13.2 Weekly Department Meeting

- **Frequency:** Once per week (recommended: Tuesday or Wednesday, before store opening or during a scheduled overlap period).
- **Duration:** 30 minutes.
- **Led by:** Department Supervisor.
- **Attendees:** All associates assigned to the department.
- **Agenda:**
  1. Department sales performance review — compare to plan and to last year.
  2. Planogram compliance status — any outstanding issues.
  3. Price accuracy audit results — review errors and corrective actions.
  4. Inventory management — overstock, out-of-stocks, markdowns needed.
  5. Customer feedback — review any complaints, compliments, or mystery shop results for the department.
  6. Training topic — rotate through product knowledge, safety, and customer service topics.
  7. Associate input — listen to associate suggestions, concerns, and observations.
- **Documentation:** The Department Supervisor records meeting notes on Form BRHI-MTG-02 and posts them in the department's communication binder.

### 13.3 Monthly All-Store Meeting

- **Frequency:** Once per month, typically the first Monday or Tuesday of the month.
- **Duration:** 60 minutes.
- **Led by:** Store Manager.
- **Attendees:** All store associates. Multiple sessions may be held to accommodate all shifts. Attendance is mandatory; associates must attend one of the scheduled sessions.
- **Agenda:**
  1. Store performance review — sales, customer satisfaction, shrinkage, safety metrics (15 minutes).
  2. Company updates — new initiatives, policy changes, corporate communications (10 minutes).
  3. Recognition — associate of the month, safety awards, tenure milestones, customer shout-outs (10 minutes).
  4. Training — monthly focus topic (safety training, product knowledge, customer service skill-building) (15 minutes).
  5. Open forum — associate questions and feedback (10 minutes).
- **Compensation:** Associates attending outside their scheduled shift are compensated for 1 hour at their regular rate.

### 13.4 Communication Log — Shift-to-Shift Handoff

- **Purpose:** Ensure continuity of operations between shifts. Every shift change requires a handoff entry in the **Store Communication Log** (a physical binder at the service desk and a digital version in the BRHI Store Ops app).
- **Required Entries:**
  - Date and time of handoff.
  - Outgoing MOD name and incoming MOD name.
  - Customer issues in progress (complaints, special orders pending, deliveries scheduled).
  - Operational issues (equipment out of service, maintenance requests pending, out-of-stocks on key items).
  - Personnel notes (call-outs, late arrivals, schedule changes).
  - Safety concerns or incidents.
  - Any directive from the District Manager or above.
- **Process:** The outgoing MOD completes the entry and reviews it verbally with the incoming MOD. Both MODs initial the log. The incoming MOD is responsible for acting on all open items.

## 14. Emergency Procedures

### 14.1 Medical Emergency Response

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Call 911** immediately. Provide the store address, the nature of the emergency, and the location within the store (e.g., "BuildRight, 1234 Main Street, customer collapsed in Aisle 7 — electrical department"). |
| 2 | **Assign one associate** to meet the ambulance at the front entrance and guide paramedics to the patient's location. |
| 3 | **Clear the area** around the patient. Move bystanders back at least 10 feet. Do not move the patient unless they are in immediate danger (e.g., in the path of a forklift). |
| 4 | **Render first aid** if trained and if it is safe to do so. Use the nearest first aid kit (locations: receiving dock, break room, customer service desk, and at each end of the main aisle). AED devices are located at the customer service desk and at the Pro Desk. Follow AED voice prompts. |
| 5 | **Do not administer medication** to the patient. Do not give the patient food or water. |
| 6 | **MOD notifies the Store Manager and the District Manager** as soon as the situation is stabilized. |
| 7 | After the patient is transported, **complete an Incident Report** (Form BRHI-SAF-01) within 2 hours. Include: date, time, location, description of the incident, actions taken, names of associates who responded, and names of witnesses. |
| 8 | **Clean and restore the area** as needed. If blood or bodily fluids are present, use the biohazard cleanup kit (located at the receiving dock) and follow the BRHI Bloodborne Pathogen Exposure Control Plan. |

### 14.2 Fire Evacuation

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Upon discovering a fire or hearing the fire alarm: **do not attempt to fight the fire** unless it is small (wastebasket size) and you have a clear exit behind you. Use the nearest fire extinguisher (PASS method: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep). |
| 2 | The MOD or designated associate announces over the PA: *"Attention all BuildRight associates and customers. This is an emergency. Please evacuate the building immediately through the nearest exit. Do not use the elevators. Leave all merchandise and proceed to the designated assembly area in the parking lot. Do not re-enter the building until instructed."* |
| 3 | **Call 911** immediately. |
| 4 | All associates assist customers in evacuating. Direct customers to the nearest exit. Check restrooms and fitting rooms for anyone who may not have heard the alarm. |
| 5 | Department Supervisors are responsible for conducting a **head count** of their associates at the assembly area (designated location: the far end of the parking lot, away from the building — look for the orange "Assembly Area" flag). |
| 6 | The MOD verifies that all associates and any known customers have evacuated. If anyone is unaccounted for, report to the fire department incident commander upon their arrival. **Do not re-enter the building.** |
| 7 | The MOD or Store Manager meets the fire department at the front entrance and provides: building layout, location of hazardous materials, location of any known persons still inside, and status of utilities. |
| 8 | **Do not re-enter the building** until the fire department gives the all-clear. The MOD will communicate the all-clear to all associates. |
| 9 | After the all-clear, the MOD contacts the District Manager, the Facilities Hotline, and the BRHI Risk Management team. Complete an Incident Report (Form BRHI-SAF-01). |

### 14.3 Severe Weather Shelter

| Weather Event | Action |
|---|---|
| **Tornado Warning** (tornado has been spotted or indicated by radar for the store's area) | MOD announces: *"Attention all BuildRight associates and customers. A tornado warning has been issued for our area. Please move immediately to the designated severe weather shelter area. Do not leave the building."* Direct all customers and associates to the designated shelter area (typically the backroom interior corridor away from exterior walls, windows, and overhead doors). Department Supervisors conduct a head count. Remain in the shelter area until the MOD announces the all-clear or 30 minutes after the last thunder is heard. |
| **Severe Thunderstorm Warning** with hail or damaging winds | MOD announces the warning. Associates move customers away from the garden center and exterior areas. Monitor the weather via the NOAA weather radio (located at the customer service desk). If conditions worsen to a tornado warning, follow tornado procedures. |
| **Flooding** | If rising water threatens the building, the MOD evaluates whether to evacuate. Follow the evacuation procedure in Section 14.2, directing to high ground in the parking lot. Move merchandise off the lowest shelves in flood-prone areas if time safely permits. |
| **Extreme Heat** (excessive heat warning) | Ensure all associates take hydration breaks every 30 minutes when working in non-air-conditioned areas (garden center, lumber yard, receiving dock). Watch for signs of heat-related illness in associates and customers. |

### 14.4 Power Outage Procedures

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Emergency lighting will activate automatically. The MOD assesses the situation — is the outage limited to the store or is it area-wide? |
| 2 | Contact the utility company to report the outage and get an estimated restoration time. |
| 3 | If the outage is expected to last **less than 30 minutes:** keep the store open. Use flashlights (located at each register and at the customer service desk) to assist customers. Pause all forklift and reach truck operations until power is restored. |
| 4 | If the outage is expected to last **more than 30 minutes:** the MOD announces: *"Attention BuildRight customers, we are experiencing a power outage. For your safety, we ask that you bring your current selections to the checkout area. We will process your purchases using manual backup procedures."* Begin an orderly wind-down of store operations. Follow the closing procedure (Section 5) but do not set the alarm until power is restored. |
| 5 | **POS Backup:** Each register has a battery backup that lasts approximately 15 minutes. The Cash Office Associate should instruct cashiers to complete the current transaction and then log out. For continued sales during a short outage, accept cash only and use the manual sales pad (located at each register) to record transactions. Enter these transactions into the POS system when power is restored. |
| 6 | **Refrigerated and frozen items:** Monitor temperature-sensitive products. If power is out for more than 2 hours, check product temperatures. Dispose of any items that have exceeded safe temperature thresholds per the Food Safety guidelines. |
| 7 | **When power is restored:** Verify all systems are operational (POS, security, HVAC, lighting). Resume normal operations. If the store was closed, follow the opening procedure to reopen. |

### 14.5 Active Threat Response (Run-Hide-Fight)

BRHI follows the nationally recognized **Run-Hide-Fight** protocol for active threat situations.

| Response | When | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **RUN** (preferred) | If you can safely evacuate the building | Evacuate immediately. Leave belongings behind. Help others escape if possible, but do not let others slow your escape. Do not use the main entrance if the threat is near it — use any available exit, including fire exits and loading dock doors. Once outside, do not return. Call 911 when safe. |
| **HIDE** | If you cannot safely evacuate | Find a room that can be locked. Lock the door and barricade it with heavy objects. Turn off lights. Silence cell phones (vibrate and ringers off). Hide behind large objects, away from doors and windows. Remain quiet. Do not open the door for anyone until law enforcement identifies themselves. |
| **FIGHT** (last resort) | Only if your life is in imminent danger and you cannot run or hide | Act with physical aggression. Improvise weapons (fire extinguisher, chair, tool). Commit to your actions. Work as a team with others if possible. |

- **When law enforcement arrives:** Officers will not know who the threat is. Follow all commands. Keep hands visible and empty. Do not reach for anything. Do not point or shout. Provide information only when asked.
- **After the incident:** The Store Manager (or highest-ranking surviving manager) contacts the District Manager, BRHI Corporate Security (1-800-555-0199), and BRHI Crisis Response Team (1-800-555-0200). BRHI will deploy its Employee Assistance Program (EAP) resources to support affected associates.

### 14.6 Bomb Threat Response

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | If a bomb threat is received by phone, the call-taker must remain calm and keep the caller on the line as long as possible. Use the **Bomb Threat Checklist** (posted at every phone and in the Store Emergency Binder) to record: exact words of the threat, caller's voice characteristics (male/female, accent, age, calm/agitated), background noises (traffic, music, machinery), and any other details. |
| 2 | After the call ends, **do not hang up** the phone (to preserve the call trace). Use a different phone to call 911 and the MOD. |
| 3 | The MOD contacts the District Manager and BRHI Corporate Security. |
| 4 | **Evacuate the building** using the fire evacuation procedure (Section 14.2). Do not use the PA system (some bombs may be triggered by radio frequencies). Send runners to each department with the evacuation order. |
| 5 | **Do not** use cell phones or radios within 300 feet of the building. |
| 6 | Assemble at the designated assembly area. Do not re-enter the building until law enforcement gives the all-clear. |
| 7 | If a suspicious package is found, **do not touch it, move it, or approach it.** Evacuate the immediate area (minimum 300 feet in all directions) and call 911. |

### 14.7 Robbery Response

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Cooperate fully.** Do not resist, argue, or make sudden movements. The safety of associates and customers is the absolute priority. |
| 2 | **Follow the robber's instructions.** Open the register, give cash, and comply with demands. Do not try to be a hero. |
| 3 | **Observe and remember** as many details as possible without staring: approximate height and weight, gender, race, age, clothing, distinctive features (tattoos, scars, facial hair), type of weapon, direction of escape, and vehicle description (make, model, color, license plate if visible). |
| 4 | **Do not activate the panic alarm** during the robbery. Activate it **after** the robber has left the building and the immediate area is safe. |
| 5 | After the robber has left: activate the panic alarm, call 911, and lock the front doors. Do not allow anyone to enter or leave until law enforcement arrives (except for medical emergencies). |
| 6 | **Preserve the scene.** Do not touch anything the robber may have touched (counter, door handle, register). Do not clean up. Law enforcement will process the scene for evidence. |
| 7 | The MOD gathers all associates and customers who witnessed the robbery in a comfortable area (break room). Ask them to remain until law enforcement interviews them. Offer water and a quiet space. |
| 8 | The MOD contacts the Store Manager, District Manager, and BRHI Corporate Security. |
| 9 | **Complete an Incident Report** (Form BRHI-SAF-01) within 2 hours. |
| 10 | BRHI will deploy EAP counselors to the store within 24 hours for any associate who requests support. Associates involved in a robbery are offered the remainder of their shift off with pay, no questions asked. |
