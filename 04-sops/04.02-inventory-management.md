## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes comprehensive policies, procedures, and controls for inventory management across all BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. (BRHI) locations. It is designed to:

- Maintain inventory accuracy at or above 97% at SKU level and 99% at dollar level.
- Minimize shrink to less than 1.5% of net sales through disciplined receiving, counting, and disposition practices.
- Ensure optimal stock levels that support a 97% in-store in-stock rate while maintaining inventory turns of 7.0x annually.
- Provide clear, step-by-step procedures for receiving, putaway, cycle counting, replenishment, and disposition of all inventory.
- Integrate store-level inventory operations with BRHI's enterprise ERP, warehouse management system (WMS), and transportation management system (TMS).
- Support compliance with federal, state, and local regulations regarding hazardous materials, product disposal, and consumer protection.

Every associate who handles inventory — from receiving to the sales floor — is expected to read, understand, and follow this SOP. Failure to comply may result in corrective action up to and including termination.

## 2. Scope

This document applies to:

- **All store associates** (full-time, part-time, seasonal, and temporary) who receive, move, count, or sell inventory.
- **Store management** (Department Supervisors, Assistant Store Managers, Store Managers) responsible for inventory accuracy within their departments and the store as a whole.
- **Distribution center personnel** performing store-directed replenishment, cross-docking, and inter-store transfer shipping.
- **Merchandising and supply chain teams** at the corporate level responsible for demand planning, ABC classification, vendor-managed inventory programs, and replenishment parameter maintenance.
- **Third-party vendors and service providers** operating under VMI agreements, direct store delivery (DSD) arrangements, or performing inventory audits.

Exclusions: E-commerce fulfillment center inventory operations are covered under BRHI-SOP-009. Vendor procurement and purchase order creation are covered under BRHI-SOP-003 (Procurement).

## 3. Inventory Management Framework

### 3.1 Perpetual Inventory System

BRHI operates a perpetual inventory system integrated across all retail locations, distribution centers, and the corporate merchandising platform. Every inventory transaction — sale, receipt, transfer, adjustment, return, and write-off — updates the on-hand quantity in real time.

**Core Principles:**

- Every unit is tracked at the individual SKU level by location (store + bin/shelf/overhead/backroom).
- On-hand quantities are updated immediately upon scan at the point of sale (POS), receiving dock, or during inventory adjustments.
- The system of record is the BRHI Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) platform (Oracle Fusion Cloud ERP). All subsystems feed into and reconcile against the ERP.
- No inventory movement occurs without a corresponding system transaction. Physical movement without system recording is a policy violation.

**System Architecture:**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   BRHI ERP (Oracle Fusion Cloud ERP)            │
│   Master Data · Financials · Procurement · Sales    │
└──────────┬──────────────────────────┬───────────────┘
           │                          │
    ┌──────┴───────┐          ┌───────┴───────┐
    │     WMS      │          │      TMS      │
    │ (Manhattan   │          │  (Blue Yonder)│
    │  Active)     │          │               │
    └──────┬───────┘          └───────┬───────┘
           │                          │
    ┌──────┴──────────────────────────┴───────┐
    │           Store-Level Systems            │
    │  POS (NCR) · RFID Readers · Handheld     │
    │  Scanners (Zebra TC73) · Price Labels    │
    └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Data Flow:**

| Transaction Type | Origin System | Update Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| POS sale / return | NCR POS → ERP | Real-time (< 3 seconds) |
| Receiving receipt | Zebra handheld → WMS → ERP | Real-time |
| Cycle count adjustment | Zebra handheld → ERP | Real-time (after approval) |
| Transfer shipment | WMS/TMS → ERP | Upon scan at origin |
| Transfer receipt | Zebra handheld → ERP | Upon scan at destination |
| Vendor DSD receipt | Zebra handheld → ERP | Real-time |
| Write-off / damage | Zebra handheld → ERP | Upon approval |
| VMI sync | Vendor portal → ERP | Hourly batch |

### 3.2 RFID and Barcode Scanning

BRHI employs a dual-technology approach for inventory identification:

**Barcode (Primary — All SKUs):**

- Every SKU carries a GS1-128 barcode on the unit label and a GS1 DataMatrix on the case/pallet label.
- All handheld scanners (Zebra TC73) decode 1D, 2D, and DataMatrix symbologies.
- Associates must scan — never key-enter — barcodes for all inventory transactions. Manual entry requires supervisor override and is logged as an exception.

**RFID (High-Value and High-Shrink Categories):**

- RFID tags (UHF EPC Gen2, Avery Dennison AD-229r6) are applied at the source or at the DC for the following categories:
  - Power tools (SKUs > $50 retail)
  - Electrical wire and cable (by spool)
  - Copper pipe and fittings
  - Premium plumbing fixtures (SKUs > $75 retail)
  - Fastener master cartons (high-count items)
- RFID overhead readers (TyngsLabs ECHO) are installed at receiving dock doors and store exits for automated inbound verification and shrink detection.
- Handheld RFID wands (Zebra MC3390xR) are used for rapid cycle counts in RFID-enabled departments. A single wand sweep can count an entire bay in under 60 seconds.

**Tagging Standards:**

| Category | Tag Type | Placement | Encoding |
|---|---|---|---|
| Power tools | Rugged RFID tag | Inside carrying case, under handle | EPC: GTIN + Serial |
| Wire/cable spools | RFID hang tag | Through arbor hole | EPC: GTIN + Lot |
| Copper pipe | RFID label | Wrapped on bundle band | EPC: GTIN + Length |
| Premium fixtures | RFID label | Inside product packaging | EPC: GTIN + Serial |
| Fastener cartons | RFID label | Top-right corner of carton | EPC: GTIN + Count |

### 3.3 Integration with ERP / WMS / TMS

**ERP (Oracle Fusion Cloud ERP):**

- Holds the master data for all SKUs, including descriptions, dimensions, weight, cost, retail price, vendor, classification, and replenishment parameters.
- Maintains the financial inventory ledger. Every on-hand change creates a corresponding financial journal entry (debit/credit to inventory and COGS accounts).
- Generates automated alerts for exception conditions: negative on-hand, receiving discrepancies, abnormal shrink patterns, and reorder point breaches.

**WMS (Manhattan Active):**

- Manages DC-level inventory, pick/pack/ship operations, and store-directed replenishment wave planning.
- Sends Advance Shipping Notices (ASNs) to stores prior to each delivery.
- Tracks putaway location assignments within the store (bin, shelf, overhead, backroom) and provides directed putaway instructions.

**TMS (Blue Yonder):**

- Optimizes delivery routes and schedules for DC-to-store and inter-store transfers.
- Provides real-time delivery ETAs pushed to the store receiving team via the BRHI Store Companion app.
- Manages carrier selection, freight cost allocation, and delivery proof-of-delivery (POD) capture.

## 4. Inventory Classification

### 4.1 ABC Classification

All SKUs are classified using ABC analysis based on trailing 12-month revenue contribution. Classification is recalculated monthly by the Merchandise Planning team and updated in the ERP.

| Class | Revenue Contribution | SKU Percentage | Management Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Top 80% of revenue | ~20% of SKUs | Tight inventory control; monthly cycle counts; daily in-stock monitoring; priority replenishment; safety stock maintained at 2 weeks |
| **B** | Next 15% of revenue | ~30% of SKUs | Standard control; quarterly cycle counts; weekly in-stock monitoring; standard replenishment; safety stock maintained at 1 week |
| **C** | Bottom 5% of revenue | ~50% of SKUs | Minimal control; semi-annual cycle counts; bi-weekly in-stock monitoring; lean replenishment; minimal safety stock |

**ABC Calculation Method:**

1. Extract trailing 12-month unit sales × average selling price for every active SKU.
2. Rank all SKUs in descending order by annualized revenue.
3. Calculate cumulative revenue percentage.
4. Assign classifications:
   - **A** = all SKUs comprising the first 80% of cumulative revenue.
   - **B** = all SKUs comprising the next 15% (80%–95%) of cumulative revenue.
   - **C** = all remaining SKUs (95%–100%).

**Classification Review Triggers:**

- Scheduled monthly recalculation (first business day of each month).
- Immediate review when an SKU's revenue ranking changes by more than two deciles month-over-month.
- New item classification assigned at planogram set based on comparable SKU performance; reclassified at 13 weeks of selling history.

### 4.2 Seasonal vs. Core Assortment

| Assortment Type | Definition | Replenishment | Markdown Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Core** | Items sold year-round with stable demand (e.g., 2×4 lumber, drywall screws, PVC pipe) | Perpetual auto-replenishment via min/max | Aging review at 180 days |
| **Seasonal — Spring/Summer** | Items with peak demand March–August (e.g., lawn mowers, potting soil, patio furniture) | Pre-season forward buy; taper replenishment by July 15 | Markdown begins August 15; clearance by September 30 |
| **Seasonal — Fall/Winter** | Items with peak demand September–February (e.g., snow blowers, ice melt, holiday lighting) | Pre-season forward buy; taper replenishment by December 15 | Markdown begins January 2; clearance by February 28 |
| **Event-Driven** | Items tied to specific promotional events (e.g., Black Friday doorbusters, Spring Black Friday) | One-time buy; no replenishment | Markdown begins event + 7 days; clearance by event + 21 days |

**Seasonal Transition Calendar:**

### 4.4 Direct-Ship Items

Certain SKUs bypass the DC entirely and ship directly from the vendor to the customer (drop-ship) or to the store for customer pickup. These items are tracked in the ERP as non-stocked SKUs with a special inventory indicator.

**Direct-Ship Categories:**

| Category | Example Items | Fulfillment |
|---|---|---|
| Custom cabinetry | KraftMaid, Merillat | Vendor ships to store for customer pickup |
| Custom countertops | Granite, quartz slabs | Vendor ships and installs directly at customer home |
| Special-order appliances | High-end ranges, wine coolers | Vendor ships to customer home |
| Bulk landscape materials | Mulch (10+ cubic yards), soil | Vendor delivers directly to jobsite |
| Large sheds and outbuildings | Pre-fabricated storage buildings | Vendor delivers and installs |

**Direct-Ship Inventory Procedure:**

1. Customer places order in-store (Pro Desk) or online.
2. ERP creates a purchase order to the vendor with the customer's delivery address.
3. Vendor ships directly; ASN is sent to BRHI ERP.
4. Upon delivery confirmation (vendor POD or customer signature), the ERP records the sale and relieves the PO commitment.
5. Direct-ship items are never received into store on-hand inventory. They are accounted for as non-stock sales.

## 5. Receiving and Putaway

### 5.1 Advance Shipping Notice (ASN) Matching

Every DC-to-store shipment is preceded by an ASN transmitted from the WMS to the store's receiving system at least 2 hours before the scheduled delivery. For DSD vendors, ASNs are required for deliveries exceeding $500 in wholesale value.

**ASN Contents:**

- Shipment ID (BRHI tracking number)
- Purchase order number(s)
- Carrier and PRO number
- Expected delivery date/time
- Line-level detail: SKU, quantity ordered, quantity shipped, unit of measure, lot number (if applicable)
- Pallet/case count and total weight

**Pre-Arrival Preparation:**

1. Receiving associate opens the BRHI Store Companion app and reviews the day's expected deliveries by 6:00 AM.
2. For each shipment, the associate confirms dock door assignment and staging area availability.
3. The associate prints the ASN summary report for use during the receiving process.
4. If an expected shipment has not arrived within 2 hours of its ETA, the receiving associate contacts the DC dispatch desk or carrier directly to obtain an updated ETA.

### 5.2 Three-Way Match (PO, ASN, Physical Receipt)

All DC and DSD receipts require a three-way match between the Purchase Order, the ASN, and the physical goods received.

**Three-Way Match Process:**

| Step | Action | System |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Scan the carrier's PRO barcode or enter the shipment ID | Zebra TC73 / Store Companion app |
| 2 | System retrieves the matching ASN and displays expected SKUs and quantities | ERP |
| 3 | Scan each pallet/case barcode. The system matches each scan against the ASN line items | Zebra TC73 → ERP |
| 4 | For each SKU, the system compares: PO quantity → ASN quantity → Scanned quantity | ERP |
| 5 | If all three quantities match within tolerance, the receipt is auto-confirmed | ERP |
| 6 | If a discrepancy exceeds tolerance, the system flags the line for manual resolution | ERP |

**Tolerance Thresholds:**

| Condition | Tolerance | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Quantity over-shipment | Up to +5% of PO quantity (max +10 units) | Accept; excess units are recorded as overage and added to on-hand |
| Quantity over-shipment | More than +5% or more than +10 units | Reject excess; segregate for return shipment to DC |
| Quantity under-shipment | Up to -5% of PO quantity | Accept short; ASN line marked "short received"; backorder triggered automatically |
| Quantity under-shipment | More than -5% | Escalate to DC operations for investigation; short received with exception code |
| Wrong SKU received | Any | Do not scan into inventory; segregate and log as "mis-ship"; initiate DC claim |
| Damaged goods | Any | Scan into damaged inventory; photograph damage; create damage claim |
| Missing ASN | N/A | Do not unload until DC dispatch confirms shipment details; if confirmed, receive manually with supervisor override |

### 5.3 Damage Documentation

All damaged goods must be documented at the point of receiving:

1. **Photograph** the damage using the Zebra TC73 camera (minimum 3 photos: overall pallet/case, close-up of damage, label showing SKU and lot number).
2. **Scan** the SKU barcode and select the "Damage at Receipt" disposition code.
3. **Classify** the damage type using the standard damage taxonomy:

| Damage Code | Description | Typical Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| D1 – Carrier Damage | Visible crushing, water exposure, puncture during transit | Carrier claim filed; RTV or dispose |
| D2 – Vendor Packaging Failure | Insufficient packaging, missing protective wrap | Vendor claim filed; RTV |
| D3 – Manufacturing Defect | Product defect identified at receipt (e.g., missing parts, cosmetic flaw) | Vendor claim filed; RTV |
| D4 – Temperature/Spoilage | Perishable or temperature-sensitive goods compromised | Dispose per environmental guidelines |
| D5 – Unknown/General | Damage source cannot be determined | Markdown or dispose |

4. **Segregate** damaged goods to the designated damaged goods staging area (see Section 11).
5. The system automatically generates a damage claim and routes it to the DC Claims team for carrier or vendor recovery.

### 5.4 Putaway

**Standard:** All received goods must be put away within 24 hours of receipt. The putaway clock starts when the last pallet/case is scanned off the truck.

**Directed Putaway Process:**

1. Upon completion of receiving, the WMS generates a putaway task list for each SKU received.
2. The putaway task specifies the destination location based on the planogram and current bin availability:

| Location Type | Description | Assignment Priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Bin** | Front-facing shelf location on the sales floor (planogram slot) | 1 (primary) |
| **Shelf** | Secondary display shelf in the department | 2 (overflow) |
| **Overhead** | Steel racking above the sales floor, accessed by order picker or ladder | 3 (bulk backup) |
| **Backroom** | Designated backstock area organized by department | 4 (long-term storage) |

3. The associate scans the SKU and then scans the destination location barcode. The system confirms the match and updates the location-level on-hand.
4. If the directed location is full, the associate selects "Location Full" on the handheld. The system suggests the next available location in priority order.
5. Upon completion, the associate closes the putaway task. The system logs the putaway completion timestamp.

**Putaway Compliance Monitoring:**

- Stores are measured on "Putaway within 24 Hours" compliance daily.
- Target: 95% of received units put away within 24 hours.
- Stores below 90% for three consecutive days are escalated to the District Manager.

### 5.5 Cross-Docking for Promotional Items

Promotional items for major events (Spring Black Friday, Black Friday, Holiday) may be cross-docked at the DC, meaning they bypass standard putaway and are pre-staged for immediate floor placement.

**Cross-Dock Process:**

1. The Merchandising team designates promotional SKUs for cross-dock at least 6 weeks before the event.
2. The DC pre-picks and stages cross-dock items on dedicated pallets labeled with the store's planogram bay number.
3. Upon receipt at the store, cross-dock pallets are delivered directly to the sales floor — no backroom staging.
4. The receiving associate scans the pallet barcode, which automatically updates all SKUs to their planogram floor locations.
5. Cross-dock receipts must be placed on the sales floor within 4 hours of receipt (vs. the standard 24-hour putaway).

## 6. Cycle Counting

### 6.1 Frequency

Cycle counts are the primary method for maintaining perpetual inventory accuracy between annual physical inventories.

| ABC Class | Minimum Count Frequency | Annual Count Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Monthly (every 30 calendar days) | 12× per year |
| **B** | Quarterly (every 90 calendar days) | 4× per year |
| **C** | Semi-annually (every 180 calendar days) | 2× per year |
| **High-shrink SKUs** (regardless of class) | Weekly | 52× per year |
| **Negative on-hand SKUs** | Within 24 hours of detection | As needed |
| **Zero-sales with positive on-hand** | Within 48 hours of detection | As needed |

**High-Shrink Designation:** The Loss Prevention team designates high-shrink SKUs quarterly based on the following criteria:
- SKU shrink exceeds 3% of net sales in the trailing quarter.
- SKU appears on the top 100 shrink list for the store's region.
- SKU is flagged by exception-based reporting as a probable theft target.

### 6.2 Count Methods

| Method | Description | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **Zone-based** | The store is divided into count zones (typically one zone per department bay). All items in a zone are counted in a single session. | Primary method for scheduled A and B item counts |
| **Random sampling** | The system randomly selects a set number of SKUs (minimum 25 per day per store) from the active SKU list for counting. | Daily ongoing accuracy verification; C item counts |
| **Exception-based** | The system flags SKUs with anomalous on-hand conditions for immediate counting. Triggers include: negative on-hand, zero sales with positive on-hand over 14 days, on-hand quantity exceeds max level by >20%. | Triggered automatically by the ERP; highest priority |
| **RFID sweep** | An associate walks the aisle with an RFID wand, which passively reads all RFID-tagged items within range. | RFID-enabled departments; can count 500+ units in under 5 minutes |

### 6.3 Cycle Count Process

**Step-by-Step Procedure:**

**Step 1 — Count Assignment Generation:**

- The ERP automatically generates daily count assignments by 5:00 AM based on the count calendar, ABC classification, and exception triggers.
- The Store Inventory Coordinator (or designated associate) reviews the assignments and assigns them to count associates via the Store Companion app.
- No count associate may count the same zone two consecutive times (rotation policy to ensure independence).

**Step 2 — First Count:**

1. The count associate proceeds to the assigned zone with a Zebra TC73 handheld.
2. The associate selects the count task on the handheld, which displays the list of SKUs and expected locations.
3. For each SKU, the associate physically counts all units in the assigned location — including sales floor bin, overhead, and backroom locations for that SKU.
4. The associate enters the counted quantity on the handheld. The system records the count but does **not** display the expected on-hand to the counter (blind count).
5. For RFID-enabled items, the associate uses the Zebra MC3390xR wand to perform a sweep count. The wand records all RFID tag reads and transmits the count to the ERP.

**Step 3 — Variance Detection:**

- After the first count is submitted, the ERP compares the counted quantity to the system on-hand.
- Variances are flagged if the absolute difference exceeds the following thresholds:

| SKU Cost | Dollar Variance Threshold | Quantity Variance Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| < $5 | $25 | 10 units |
| $5 – $25 | $50 | 5 units |
| $25 – $100 | $75 | 2 units |
| > $100 | $100 | 1 unit |

**Step 4 — Variance Investigation:**

For each flagged variance, the count associate (or a supervisor) must investigate:

1. Check for misplaced inventory — search adjacent bins, overhead, and backroom for units that may belong to the flagged SKU.
2. Review recent transaction history for the SKU — look for unscanned sales, uncompleted transfers, or pending receiving receipts.
3. Check for pending customer holds or layaways that may explain the discrepancy.
4. Document findings in the variance investigation notes on the handheld.

**Step 5 — Recount:**

- If the investigation does not resolve the variance, a second count is performed by a different associate (not the original counter).
- The recount follows the same blind-count procedure.

**Step 6 — Adjustment Approval:**

- If the recount confirms the variance, an inventory adjustment is submitted through the system.
- Adjustments are routed for approval based on the following authority levels:

| Adjustment Dollar Value | Approval Authority |
|---|---|
| Under $100 | Department Supervisor |
| $100 – $1,000 | Assistant Store Manager |
| Over $1,000 | Store Manager |
| Over $5,000 | Store Manager + District Manager (dual approval) |
| Over $10,000 | Store Manager + District Manager + Regional Loss Prevention Director |

- The approver reviews the count results, investigation notes, and recount confirmation before approving or rejecting the adjustment.
- Approved adjustments update the on-hand quantity and create a financial journal entry debiting/crediting the inventory shrink account.
- Rejected adjustments require additional investigation and, if warranted, a third count.

### 6.4 Accuracy Targets

| Metric | Target | Measurement Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| SKU-level accuracy | ≥ 97% | Monthly (rolling 30-day) |
| Dollar-level accuracy | ≥ 99% | Monthly (rolling 30-day) |
| Location-level accuracy | ≥ 95% | Monthly (rolling 30-day) |
| Adjustment approval turnaround | ≤ 24 hours | Per adjustment |

**Accuracy Calculation:**

```
SKU Accuracy = (Total SKUs Counted − SKUs with Variance) / Total SKUs Counted × 100

Dollar Accuracy = 1 − (|Σ(Variance Dollars)| / Σ(System On-Hand Dollars for Counted SKUs)) × 100

Location Accuracy = (Total Locations Counted − Locations with Variance) / Total Locations Counted × 100
```

## 7. Physical Inventory

### 7.1 Annual Wall-to-Wall Count

BRHI conducts a complete, wall-to-wall physical inventory annually at every store location. The count is typically scheduled for the last two weeks of January to align with the fiscal year-end close process.

**Key Dates:**

| Milestone | Target Date |
|---|---|
| Physical inventory scheduling confirmation | November 1 |
| Pre-count preparation begins | January 2 |
| Store count window | January 15 – January 31 |
| Variance analysis and recounts | February 1 – February 7 |
| Financial adjustment booking | February 15 |
| Final report to external auditors | February 28 |

### 7.2 Pre-Count Preparation

**Two weeks before the count:**

- [ ] Clean and organize all sales floor areas — straighten merchandise, face all product forward, remove items from wrong locations.
- [ ] Consolidate backroom inventory — stack neatly, ensure all items are labeled and in designated locations.
- [ ] Process all pending receiving — receive and put away all in-transit shipments.
- [ ] Process all pending transfers — ship out all outbound transfers; receive all inbound transfers.
- [ ] Complete all pending customer returns — scan returns back into inventory or disposition.
- [ ] Process all pending damage and write-off requests — clear the damaged goods area.
- [ ] Verify all location labels are present and readable — replace damaged or missing bin labels.
- [ ] Print count sheets (backup for handheld failure) and zone maps.
- [ ] Test all handheld scanners and ensure batteries are fully charged.
- [ ] Confirm third-party audit firm engagement and schedule their on-site dates.

**Day before the count:**

- [ ] Halt all receiving after the last delivery of the day. No new receipts after the receiving cutoff.
- [ ] Complete the final daily cycle count snapshot — the ERP captures a pre-count on-hand baseline.
- [ ] Freeze inventory transactions in the ERP for the count duration (POS sales continue; all other inventory movements are queued and processed after the count is unfrozen).
- [ ] Brief all count team members on procedures, zone assignments, and the counting protocol.

### 7.3 Count Teams

Each store forms count teams using a two-person model:

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Counter** | Physically counts merchandise by hand. Calls out SKU number (or scans barcode) and quantity to the Recorder. Must count every unit — no estimating. |
| **Recorder** | Enters counts into the handheld scanner (primary) or writes on the count sheet (backup). Reads back the entry for the Counter to verify. |

**Team Composition:**

- Minimum of one team per department zone.
- At least 50% of count teams must include an associate from a different department (cross-department counting) to reduce bias and familiarity errors.
- The Store Inventory Coordinator and Assistant Store Manager(s) serve as roving auditors, performing spot-check recounts on a random sample of at least 10% of zones.

### 7.4 Third-Party Audit

An independent third-party audit firm (currently RGIS or equivalent) is engaged for each store's physical inventory to provide an objective verification layer.

**Third-Party Audit Scope:**

- The audit firm performs a complete recount of a minimum 15% random sample of store zones.
- The firm also recounts 100% of high-value items (SKUs with unit cost > $100) and 100% of the backroom.
- Variance between the store count and the audit count is analyzed:
  - If the audit count matches the store count within tolerance (±1 unit or ±$25 per SKU), the store count is confirmed.
  - If the audit count exceeds tolerance, the entire zone is recounted by the audit firm and the audit count takes precedence.

### 7.5 Variance Analysis

After all counts are completed and the inventory transaction freeze is lifted:

**Materiality Threshold:** A variance is considered material if it exceeds **$500 or 2% of the department's total inventory value, whichever is less**.

**Variance Analysis Process:**

1. The ERP generates a variance report comparing the physical count to the system on-hand for every SKU.
2. The Store Inventory Coordinator reviews the report and categorizes variances:

| Variance Category | Definition | Investigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Unexplained shrink** | Physical count < system on-hand, no known cause | Full root cause investigation required (see Section 10) |
| **Unexplained overage** | Physical count > system on-hand, no known cause | Verify no double-counting; check for mislabeled SKUs |
| **Explained shrink** | Physical count < system on-hand, known cause documented (e.g., theft, damage) | Validate documentation; book adjustment |
| **Explained overage** | Physical count > system on-hand, known cause documented (e.g., unscanned receipt) | Validate documentation; book adjustment |

3. Material variances are investigated by the store management team with support from the District Loss Prevention Manager.
4. All adjustments are booked at the SKU level with a variance category code and investigation notes.

### 7.6 Financial Impact Reporting

The Store Manager must submit a Physical Inventory Summary Report to the District Manager and Regional VP within 5 business days of the count completion. The report includes:

- Total inventory value at system on-hand (pre-count)
- Total inventory value at physical count
- Net shrink dollar amount and percentage of net sales
- Shrink by department and ABC class
- Top 20 shrink SKUs by dollar impact
- Material variance investigation summaries and root causes
- Corrective action plan for any department exceeding the 1.5% shrink target
- Comparison to prior year physical inventory results

## 8. Replenishment

### 8.1 Automated Replenishment

The majority of store inventory is replenished automatically through BRHI's perpetual replenishment system, which operates on a min/max model with safety stock buffers.

**Replenishment Parameters:**

| Parameter | Definition | Set By |
|---|---|---|
| **Min Level (Reorder Point)** | The on-hand quantity at which a new replenishment order is triggered | Merchandise Planning (reviewed quarterly) |
| **Max Level (Order-Up-To Level)** | The on-hand quantity the system aims to reach after a replenishment order is received | Merchandise Planning (reviewed quarterly) |
| **Safety Stock** | Buffer quantity held to protect against demand variability and lead time uncertainty | Calculated formula; overridden only with Merchandise Planning approval |
| **Lead Time** | Number of days between order placement and receipt at the store | DC operations (standard: 2 days for DC-sourced items) |
| **Order Cycle** | How frequently the system evaluates replenishment needs | Daily for A items; bi-weekly for B and C items |

**Reorder Point Calculation:**

```
Reorder Point = (Average Daily Usage × Lead Time in Days) + Safety Stock

Where:
  Average Daily Usage = Trailing 13-week unit sales / 91 days
  Lead Time = DC-to-store transit days + order processing days (standard: 2 days)
  Safety Stock = Z × σ_d × √LT

    Z = Service level factor (1.65 for 95% service level for A items; 1.28 for 90% for B items; 1.04 for 85% for C items)
    σ_d = Standard deviation of daily demand (trailing 13 weeks)
    LT = Lead time in days
```

**Order Quantity Calculation:**

```
Order Quantity = Max Level − Current On-Hand − On-Order Quantity

Where:
  Max Level = Average Daily Usage × (Lead Time + Review Cycle in Days) + Safety Stock
```

### 8.2 Store-Directed Replenishment from DC

DC-to-store replenishment follows a daily delivery schedule:

| Delivery Type | Frequency | Timing | Contents |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard replenishment** | Daily (Monday–Saturday) | Delivery window: 5:00 AM – 10:00 AM | Auto-generated replenishment orders for all departments |
| **Promotional pre-stock** | Per event calendar | 3–5 days before event start | Cross-dock promotional pallets |
| **Seasonal set** | Per seasonal calendar | 2 weeks before season start | Full planogram set for seasonal department |
| **Emergency replenishment** | As needed | Next available delivery slot | Stockout / critical in-stock items |

**Daily Replenishment Process:**

1. **2:00 AM** — ERP evaluates on-hand levels for all store-SKU combinations and generates replenishment orders for items at or below their reorder point.
2. **4:00 AM** — WMS receives the order wave and begins picking at the DC. Pick tasks are prioritized by department and delivery route.
3. **10:00 PM (prior night)** — TMS finalizes the delivery route and dispatches carriers. Carrier loads depart the DC by 1:00 AM.
4. **5:00 AM – 10:00 AM** — Carrier arrives at store. Receiving team unloads and begins the three-way match receiving process (see Section 5.2).
5. **By 2:00 PM** — Putaway of replenishment items onto the sales floor is completed. Fast-moving A items are prioritized for morning putaway.

### 8.3 Direct Store Delivery (DSD)

DSD vendors deliver directly to the store, bypassing the DC. DSD is used for items that are perishable, bulky, locally sourced, or vendor-managed.

**DSD Vendor Check-In:**

1. Vendor arrives at the receiving dock during designated DSD hours (6:00 AM – 12:00 PM, Monday–Friday).
2. Receiving associate verifies vendor identity (BRHI-issued vendor badge) and checks the delivery against the scheduled DSD appointment.
3. For vendors with electronic ASN capability, the associate scans the vendor's DEX invoice barcode to begin the three-way match.
4. For vendors without electronic ASN, the associate manually keys the invoice into the handheld and performs a physical count verification.
5. All DSD receipts are subject to the same tolerance thresholds as DC receipts (see Section 5.2).
6. The associate and the vendor representative both sign the DSD receipt confirmation. A copy is retained by the vendor; the digital copy is stored in the ERP.

**DSD Categories and Schedule:**

| Category | Vendor Type | Delivery Frequency | Replenishment Model |
|---|---|---|---|
| Live nursery goods | Local growers | 2–3× per week (seasonal) | VMI — vendor monitors POS data |
| Propane tank exchange | Blue Rhino / AmeriGas | Weekly | VMI — IoT sensor in cage |
| Seasonal plants (poinsettias, Christmas trees) | Regional farms | Weekly during season | Vendor-directed |
| Snack and beverage (break room / vending) | Premium Refreshments | 2× per week | VMI |
| Lumber (treated, specialty sizes) | Regional lumber mills | As needed | Store-ordered |

### 8.4 Emergency and Expedited Orders

When an item stocks out unexpectedly or a critical customer need arises, the store may request an emergency replenishment.

**Emergency Order Process:**

1. Department Supervisor or Assistant Store Manager identifies the need and opens an Emergency Replenishment Request in the Store Companion app.
2. The request includes: SKU, quantity needed, reason code (stockout, customer special order, damage replacement, promotional over-performance), and required-by date.
3. Requests are routed automatically:

| Request Dollar Value | Approval Required | Fulfillment Target |
|---|---|---|
| Under $500 | Department Supervisor (auto-approved for A items) | Next day DC delivery |
| $500 – $2,500 | Assistant Store Manager | Next day DC delivery |
| Over $2,500 | Store Manager | Next day DC delivery or direct vendor ship |

4. The DC receives the emergency order as a priority pick and includes it in the next available delivery.
5. If the DC cannot fulfill within 24 hours, the system automatically checks alternate DCs within the region and offers an inter-store transfer option.
6. Emergency orders exceeding $5,000 cumulative per week per store are flagged for review by the District Manager.

### 8.5 Inter-Store Transfers

Inter-store transfers move inventory between BRHI locations to balance stock levels, fulfill customer requests, or consolidate overstock.

**Transfer Request Process:**

1. The requesting store (destination) initiates a Transfer Request in the ERP, specifying the SKU, quantity, reason code, and requested delivery date.
2. The system identifies nearby stores (within 50-mile radius) with sufficient on-hand above their safety stock level.
3. The source store (origin) receives the transfer request and the Department Supervisor or Assistant Store Manager approves or denies it within 4 business hours.
4. Upon approval, the source store pulls the merchandise and stages it for pickup.

**Transfer Shipping:**

- Transfers are shipped via BRHI's internal shuttle network (where available) or via common carrier (LTL).
- The source store scans each item into the "Transfer Shipped" status, which reduces their on-hand and creates an in-transit inventory record.
- The TMS generates a tracking number that is shared with both stores.

**Transfer Receiving:**

- The destination store receives the transfer using the standard receiving process (see Section 5.2).
- The receiving associate scans each item, confirming SKU and quantity match the transfer order.
- Discrepancies are documented and reported to the source store for resolution.
- Upon receipt completion, in-transit inventory is relieved and the destination store's on-hand is updated.

**Transfer Policy Limits:**

| Condition | Limit |
|---|---|
| Maximum transfer quantity per SKU per request | 50 units or $2,500 retail value (whichever is less) |
| Transfer frequency | Maximum 10 active transfer requests per store per week |
| Transfer mileage | Maximum 100-mile radius without District Manager approval |
| Transfer age | Transfer must be received within 7 calendar days of shipment or it is escalated |

## 9. Demand Planning and Forecasting

### 9.1 Historical Sales Analysis

Demand planning begins with rigorous analysis of historical sales data. The Merchandise Planning team uses a rolling 52-week sales history as the baseline for all forecasting models.

**Data Inputs:**

- Weekly unit sales by SKU by store
- Weekly revenue by SKU by store
- Average selling price trends
- Promotion event calendar (with pre/post promotional sales periods)
- Weather-normalized demand indices
- Housing starts and building permit data (macroeconomic correlate)
- Competitive store openings/closings within the trade area

**Baseline Forecast Calculation:**

```
Baseline Forecast (units) = Weighted Average of:
  50% — Trailing 13-week unit sales trend (exponential smoothing, α = 0.3)
  30% — Same period prior year unit sales (seasonal base)
  20% — Same period 2 years prior unit sales (long-term trend adjustment)
```

### 9.2 Seasonal Adjustments

Seasonal indices are calculated for each SKU at the category and store level to adjust the baseline forecast for predictable seasonal patterns.

```
Seasonal Index = (Average Weekly Sales for Period t) / (Average Weekly Sales for the Year)

Where Period t is typically a 4-week window (13 periods per year).

Example: If lawnmower units sell an average of 50/week in Period 6 (late April)
and average 12/week for the full year, the Seasonal Index for Period 6 = 50/12 = 4.17
```

**Adjusted Forecast:**

```
Seasonally Adjusted Forecast = Baseline Forecast × Seasonal Index
```

### 9.3 Promotional Lift Modeling

Promotional events generate demand above the seasonal baseline. Lift factors are calculated from historical promotional performance and applied to the forecast.

**Lift Factor Categories:**

| Promotion Type | Historical Lift Range | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Price reduction (10–15% off) | 1.5× – 2.5× normal weekly sales | Weekly circular specials |
| Price reduction (20–30% off) | 2.5× – 4.0× normal weekly sales | Spring Black Friday, Labor Day |
| Buy One Get One (BOGO) | 2.0× – 3.5× normal weekly sales | Seasonal clearance events |
| Bundle deal (e.g., free accessory with tool) | 1.3× – 2.0× normal weekly sales | Power tool promotions |
| Pro Xtra bonus points | 1.1× – 1.5× normal weekly sales | Loyalty-driven events |

**Promotional Forecast:**

```
Promotional Forecast = Seasonally Adjusted Forecast × Lift Factor × Cannibalization Adjustment

Where:
  Cannibalization Adjustment = 1 − (Expected cannibalization rate for substitute items)
  Typical cannibalization rate: 5% – 15% depending on promotion depth
```

### 9.4 Weather Impact Modeling

BRHI uses a weather-adjusted demand model to account for the significant impact of weather on home improvement sales.

**Weather-Sensitive Categories:**

| Category | Weather Driver | Impact Model |
|---|---|---|
| Lumber and building materials | Housing starts, temperature (>50°F) | +15% demand for every 10°F above 50°F in spring |
| Lawn and garden | Rainfall, temperature, frost-free date | Demand spikes 30% in first warm weekend (>65°F) after winter |
| Snow removal / ice melt | Snowfall events, temperature (<32°F) | Demand doubles within 24 hours of first forecasted snowfall >2 inches |
| HVAC / fans / air conditioners | Heat index (>90°F) | Demand increases 40% during heat waves (3+ days above 90°F) |
| Paint | Temperature (55°F–85°F), dry weather | +10% demand during extended dry periods in spring/summer |
| Pipes / plumbing parts | Hard freeze events (<20°F for 48+ hours) | Demand increases 200% for pipe repair materials during freeze events |

**Weather Adjustment Process:**

1. BRHI subscribes to a weather intelligence service (currently Planalytics) that provides 14-day demand forecasts adjusted for expected weather.
2. The demand planning system ingests the weather-adjusted forecast weekly and compares it to the baseline.
3. When the weather-adjusted forecast deviates from the baseline by more than ±10%, the system generates a forecast override for the affected SKUs and stores.
4. The Merchandise Planning team reviews and approves overrides exceeding ±25% before they are applied to replenishment orders.

### 9.5 New Store Planning

New store openings require a unique inventory planning approach since no store-level sales history exists.

**New Store Inventory Planning:**

1. **Store clustering** — The new store is assigned to a cluster of 3–5 comparable existing stores based on trade area demographics, store size, and competitive landscape.
2. **Sales projection** — The demand planning system generates a first-year sales projection by department and SKU using the cluster average, adjusted for:
   - Store square footage (proportional scaling)
   - Trade area population and median household income
   - Distance from nearest BRHI competitor
   - Pro Desk presence (Yes/No — Pro stores carry additional SKUs)
3. **Opening inventory order** — The system generates an opening stock order calculated as:

```
Opening Stock (units) = Projected Weekly Sales × Weeks of Supply Target + Safety Stock

Where:
  Weeks of Supply Target = 6 weeks for A items, 4 weeks for B items, 3 weeks for C items
  Safety Stock = 2 weeks for A items, 1 week for B items, 0 weeks for C items
```

4. **Ramp curve** — New stores are expected to ramp to mature sales levels over 26 weeks:

| Weeks After Opening | Expected Sales vs. Mature Level |
|---|---|
| Week 1 – 4 | 60% – 75% |
| Week 5 – 12 | 75% – 90% |
| Week 13 – 26 | 90% – 100% |
| Week 27+ | 100% (mature) |

Replenishment parameters are adjusted during the ramp period and recalibrated at week 27 based on actual sales data.

### 9.6 Forecast Accuracy Targets

| Level | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| Category level (department) | ≥ 85% | Monthly, measured as 1 − (|Forecast − Actual| / Actual) |
| SKU level (store) | ≥ 75% | Monthly |
| Aggregate store level | ≥ 90% | Monthly |

**Forecast Accuracy Calculation:**

```
Forecast Accuracy = 1 − (|Forecast Units − Actual Units| / Actual Units) × 100

Measured as a weighted average across all SKUs in the category/store.
```

When forecast accuracy falls below target for two consecutive months, the Merchandise Planning team initiates a forecast review, examining data inputs, seasonal indices, and promotional lift assumptions.

## 10. Inventory Accuracy and Integrity

### 10.1 Root Cause Analysis for Shrink

BRHI classifies shrink into four root cause categories. Every material shrink incident (>$500 or exceeding the materiality threshold) requires a root cause analysis documented in the ERP.

| Shrink Category | Typical Causes | Investigation Lead |
|---|---|---|
| **Theft — External** | Shoplifting, organized retail crime (ORC), vendor theft | Loss Prevention Manager |
| **Theft — Internal** | Associate theft, collusion, unauthorized discounts | Loss Prevention Manager + HR |
| **Damage** | In-store damage, customer damage, transit damage not claimed | Department Supervisor |
| **Administrative Error** | Receiving errors, pricing errors, missed scans, incorrect counts | Store Inventory Coordinator |
| **Vendor Fraud** | Short shipments, substituted product, phantom invoicing | DC Claims Team + Loss Prevention |

**Root Cause Investigation Procedure:**

1. Identify the shrink incident (from cycle count, physical inventory, or exception report).
2. Review the transaction history for the affected SKU — look for patterns in the 30 days preceding the incident.
3. Interview associates in the affected department (conducted by Loss Prevention, not store management).
4. Review CCTV footage if the shrink is time-bounded and camera coverage exists.
5. Classify the root cause and document findings in the ERP shrink incident record.
6. Develop and implement corrective actions:

| Root Cause | Corrective Action Examples |
|---|---|
| External theft | Increase LP presence; relocate SKU to higher-visibility area; add security tagging; adjust planogram |
| Internal theft | Progressive discipline per HR policy; increase transaction auditing; implement dual-control procedures |
| Damage | Improve handling training; add protective packaging at receiving; relocate fragile items |
| Administrative error | Retrain associates; implement dual-scan verification; correct system configuration |
| Vendor fraud | Initiate vendor audit; implement mandatory blind receiving for the vendor; escalate to vendor compliance team |

### 10.2 Shrink Target

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| **Total shrink as % of net sales** | < 1.5% | Trailing 12 months |
| **Shrink by department** | < 2.0% per department | Trailing 12 months |
| **Shrink for high-risk categories** | < 2.5% (power tools, copper, electrical) | Trailing 12 months |

**Shrink Calculation:**

```
Shrink Rate = (Book Inventory − Physical Inventory) / Net Sales × 100

Where:
  Book Inventory = System on-hand dollar value at retail
  Physical Inventory = Actual counted dollar value at retail
  Net Sales = Total net sales for the same period
```

### 10.3 Exception-Based Reporting

The ERP automatically generates exception reports daily to identify potential inventory integrity issues. These reports are reviewed by the Store Inventory Coordinator and the Loss Prevention team.

**Key Exception Reports:**

| Report | Trigger | Frequency | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Negative on-hand** | Any SKU with on-hand < 0 | Daily (real-time alert) | Cycle count within 24 hours; investigate transaction history |
| **Zero sales with positive on-hand** | SKU with on-hand > 0 and 0 units sold for 14+ consecutive days | Weekly | Cycle count within 48 hours; evaluate for markdown or RTV |
| **High-velocity shrink** | SKU with shrink exceeding 3% of sales in trailing 30 days | Weekly | Add to high-shrink watch list; increase LP attention |
| **Void / post-void anomaly** | Associate with void rate exceeding 5% of total transactions | Daily | LP investigation for potential sweet-hearting or pass-through fraud |
| **Receiving discrepancy** | Receiving variance exceeding tolerance on 3+ shipments in 7 days | Weekly | DC claims investigation; potential vendor issue |
| **Transfer mismatch** | Inter-store transfer with variance > 2% | Per occurrence | Both stores investigate; physical reconciliation |
| **Price override** | More than 10 price overrides per associate per day | Daily | LP review for unauthorized discounting |
| **Return anomaly** | Returns exceeding 3% of gross sales; high-value returns without receipt | Weekly | LP investigation for return fraud |

### 10.4 On-Hand Accuracy Audit

In addition to cycle counts, each store performs a monthly on-hand accuracy audit to provide a statistically valid measure of inventory accuracy.

**Audit Procedure:**

1. On the first business day of each month, the ERP randomly selects 100 SKUs (stratified: 40 A items, 30 B items, 30 C items) for each store.
2. The Store Inventory Coordinator assigns the count to a trained count associate (not the department's regular associates).
3. The associate performs a blind count of all 100 SKUs within 3 business days.
4. The ERP calculates the store's SKU-level and dollar-level accuracy (see Section 6.4 formulas).
5. Results are reported to the Store Manager, District Manager, and Regional VP.

**Escalation Thresholds:**

| Accuracy Level | Action |
|---|---|
| ≥ 97% SKU accuracy | No action — store is meeting target |
| 95% – 96.9% SKU accuracy | Store Manager develops corrective action plan within 5 business days |
| 93% – 94.9% SKU accuracy | District Manager conducts on-site inventory review within 10 business days |
| < 93% SKU accuracy | Regional LP Director conducts comprehensive audit; store receives intensive counting support |

## 11. Damaged and Defective Inventory

### 11.1 Identification and Segregation

All damaged and defective inventory must be identified, documented, and segregated from saleable inventory immediately upon discovery.

**Designated Damaged Goods Area:**

- Every store maintains a Damaged Goods staging area in the backroom, separate from saleable backstock.
- The area must be clearly marked with signage: "DAMAGED GOODS — DO NOT RESTOCK WITHOUT APPROVAL."
- The area is divided into four zones by disposition:

| Zone | Label | Color Code |
|---|---|---|
| Return to Vendor (RTV) | "RTV — Awaiting Authorization" | Red |
| Markdown | "MARKDOWN — Pending Price Change" | Yellow |
| Donate | "DONATE — Pending Pickup" | Green |
| Dispose | "DISPOSE — Pending Destruction" | Black |

**Identification Process:**

1. Associate discovers damaged or defective merchandise (on the sales floor, during putaway, or after customer return).
2. Associate scans the item barcode with the Zebra TC73 and selects the "Report Damage" function.
3. Associate enters:
   - Damage type code (D1–D5, see Section 5.3)
   - Disposition recommendation (RTV, markdown, donate, dispose)
   - Photographs of the damage
   - Brief description of how the damage occurred (if known)
4. The item is physically moved to the appropriate zone in the Damaged Goods area.
5. The ERP updates the item's status to "Damaged" and removes it from the saleable on-hand count. The item remains in inventory but is flagged as non-saleable until final disposition.

### 11.2 Disposition Options

| Disposition | Criteria | Process Owner | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Return to Vendor (RTV)** | Vendor accepts returns; item has vendor return authorization; recovery value exceeds return shipping cost | Department Supervisor | Vendor authorization within 5 business days; shipment within 10 business days |
| **Markdown** | Item is saleable at a reduced price (cosmetic damage, opened box, missing non-essential parts) | Department Supervisor (markdown up to 50%); Assistant Store Manager (50–75%); Store Manager (>75%) | Price change applied within 24 hours; item moved to clearance area |
| **Donate** | Item is functional but not saleable at any price; qualifies for charitable donation; tax benefit exceeds disposal cost | Store Manager approval | Donation pickup scheduled within 14 business days |
| **Dispose** | Item is non-functional, hazardous, or has no recovery value | Department Supervisor (initiate); Store Manager (approve) | Disposal within 5 business days; hazardous materials require special handling |

### 11.3 Return to Vendor (RTV) Process

**Step-by-Step RTV Procedure:**

1. **Authorization Request** — Department Supervisor submits an RTV request in the ERP, including the damage code, photos, and vendor information.
2. **Vendor Authorization** — The system routes the request to the vendor (or buyer for manual vendor contact). The vendor has 5 business days to respond with:
   - Return Authorization Number (RA#)
   - Return shipping instructions (vendor pickup vs. store ship)
   - Credit amount or replacement confirmation
3. **Credit Verification** — Upon vendor confirmation, the buyer verifies the credit terms match the purchase agreement. Standard credit terms:
   - Full credit for manufacturing defects (D3) and vendor packaging failures (D2)
   - Partial credit (typically 50–80%) for carrier damage (D1), depending on vendor agreement
   - No credit for in-store damage
4. **Shipping** — If the store ships, the associate packs the item, affixes the RA# label, and schedules a carrier pickup via TMS. If the vendor picks up, the item is staged in the RTV zone with the RA# prominently displayed.
5. **Credit Receipt** — The Accounts Payable team verifies the vendor credit against the RA#. If credit is not received within 30 days of shipment, the claim is escalated to the buyer for follow-up.
6. **System Closure** — Upon credit verification, the ERP relieves the damaged inventory record and books the credit to the appropriate recovery account.

### 11.4 Write-Off Authority Levels

Inventory write-offs remove damaged, lost, or unsaleable inventory from the books at cost. Write-offs require approval based on the following authority matrix:

| Write-Off Value (at Cost) | Approval Authority |
|---|---|
| Under $50 | Department Supervisor |
| $50 – $250 | Assistant Store Manager |
| $250 – $1,000 | Store Manager |
| $1,000 – $5,000 | Store Manager + District Manager |
| Over $5,000 | Store Manager + District Manager + Regional VP |

All write-offs are recorded in the ERP with:
- SKU(s) affected
- Quantity and cost value
- Reason code (damage, theft, obsolescence, vendor write-off)
- Disposition method (RTV, donate, dispose, markdown)
- Approver name and date

### 11.5 Environmental Disposal Requirements

Certain product categories contain hazardous materials that require special disposal in compliance with EPA, state, and local regulations.

**Hazardous Material Categories:**

| Category | Examples | Disposal Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Paint and stains | Latex paint, oil-based paint, wood stain | Latex: dried with absorbent, dispose as solid waste. Oil-based: licensed hazardous waste hauler. |
| Solvents and chemicals | Mineral spirits, acetone, driveway sealer | Licensed hazardous waste hauler. Store in sealed original containers in hazmat cabinet. |
| Pesticides and herbicides | Roundup, insect killer, fungicide | Licensed hazardous waste hauler. Never pour down drain. |
| Batteries | Lead-acid (auto), lithium-ion (tool), NiCad | Recycle via approved battery recycler. Do not dispose in regular waste. |
| Fluorescent bulbs | CFLs, fluorescent tubes | Recycle via approved recycler (contains mercury). |
| Pressure-treated lumber | Copper-treated, ACQ-treated lumber | Dispose as construction debris in approved landfill. Do not burn. |
| Propane tanks | 20-lb exchange tanks, 1-lb camping cylinders | Return to vendor. Never dispose in regular waste. |

**Disposal Procedure:**

1. The Store Safety Coordinator (or designated associate) segregates hazardous materials in the designated Hazmat Storage area (ventilated, locked cabinet in the backroom).
2. Materials are logged in the Hazmat Disposal Log with the date, item description, quantity, and condition.
3. The licensed hazardous waste hauler is scheduled for pickup at least monthly (or more frequently if the storage area reaches 75% capacity).
4. Upon pickup, the hauler provides a Hazardous Waste Manifest (EPA Form 8700-22). The Store Manager signs the manifest and retains a copy on file for 3 years.
5. The ERP records the disposal and writes off the inventory at cost.

## 12. Returns and Reverse Logistics

### 12.1 Customer Return Receiving

All customer returns are processed at the Returns Desk (or Pro Desk for commercial accounts) and immediately dispositioned.

**Return Receiving Procedure:**

1. Associate scans the customer's receipt barcode (or looks up the transaction by loyalty card / credit card / date range).
2. Associate scans the returned item(s) and selects the return reason code:

| Reason Code | Description | Default Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| R1 — Changed mind | Customer no longer wants the item; item is in original condition | Return to shelf |
| R2 — Defective | Item does not function as intended | RTV or dispose |
| R3 — Wrong item | Customer purchased the wrong item | Return to shelf |
| R4 — Missing parts | Item is missing components | RTV or markdown |
| R5 — Damaged in use | Item was damaged during customer use (not warrantable) | Dispose or markdown |
| R6 — Warranty return | Item is covered under manufacturer warranty | Process through warranty claim |
| R7 — Online return to store | Item purchased on BRHI.com, returned to store | See Section 12.4 |

3. Associate performs a condition assessment:
   - **A — Like new:** Original packaging intact, all parts present, no signs of use. → Return to shelf.
   - **B — Good:** Packaging may be opened, minor cosmetic wear, all functional parts present. → Markdown (typically 15–25% off) or return to shelf.
   - **C — Fair:** Noticeable wear, missing non-essential parts, but item is functional. → Markdown (typically 40–60% off).
   - **D — Non-functional:** Item does not work or is missing essential components. → RTV, donate, or dispose.

4. The refund is processed through the POS, which updates the ERP inventory. The item's on-hand status changes based on the disposition selected.

### 12.2 Product Disposition

| Disposition | Criteria | Processing |
|---|---|---|
| **Return to shelf** | Condition A; original retail price is still valid | Associate re-labels (if needed) and places item back in its planogram location within 2 hours |
| **Return to shelf (repackaged)** | Condition A/B but packaging is damaged | Associate repackages in a clear "Open Box" poly bag with BRHI Open Box label; placed in the Open Box section of the department |
| **RTV** | Condition D with vendor return authorization available | Follow RTV process (see Section 11.3) |
| **Liquidation** | Condition B/C; store has been unable to sell after 30 days | Consolidate to clearance endcap; if unsold after 60 additional days, transfer to liquidation partner |
| **Disposal** | Condition D; no vendor return available; hazardous material | Follow disposal procedures (see Section 11.5) |

### 12.3 Core and Chargeback Returns

Certain product categories have core charge programs where the customer pays a deposit at purchase and receives a credit upon returning the used item.

**Core Return Categories:**

| Category | Example Items | Core Charge | Process |
|---|---|---|---|
| Automotive batteries | Car and truck batteries | $15 – $25 core charge | Customer returns old battery; associate scans core return; core charge refunded; used battery placed in core return pallet for vendor pickup |
| Power tool batteries | Lithium-ion battery packs | $10 – $30 core charge | Customer returns depleted battery; associate scans; credit issued; battery sent to vendor recycling program |
| Small engine parts | Mower blades, tiller tines | $5 – $15 core charge | Customer returns worn part; associate scans; credit issued; core shipped to vendor for refurbishment |

**Core Return Accounting:**

- Core charges are recorded as a liability at the time of sale (deposit held on customer's account).
- When the core is returned, the liability is relieved and the customer receives the credit.
- Unreturned cores (customer does not return within 90 days) are automatically converted to a sale of the core charge amount.

### 12.4 Online Return to Store Processing

Customers who purchase items on BRHI.com may return them to any BRHI store location.

**Online Return Procedure:**

1. Customer presents the online return QR code (from the BRHI.com return confirmation email) or the packing slip with the order number.
2. The Returns Desk associate scans the QR code or enters the order number in the POS.
3. The system retrieves the order details, including item(s), purchase price, and payment method.
4. The associate verifies the item identity (scan barcode) and performs a condition assessment (A–D).
5. The refund is processed to the original payment method.
6. **Disposition routing depends on the item:**

| Item Type | Disposition |
|---|---|
| Standard item, Condition A | Return to shelf at the store |
| Standard item, Condition B–D | Follow standard disposition (markdown, RTV, dispose) |
| Ship-from-vendor (drop ship) item | Item is logged as "Online Return — Do Not Restock." Item is staged in the backroom for weekly pickup by the returns consolidation carrier, which ships it back to the vendor. |
| Oversize / heavy item | Customer retains the item; store processes the refund as a "Keep It" return. Store's inventory is not affected since the item was never in store stock. |

7. The ERP records the return against the online order and updates store-level on-hand only for items that are returned to shelf.

## 13. Overstock and Clearance Management

### 13.1 Aging Inventory Review

The ERP automatically flags inventory that has not sold within defined aging thresholds. The Store Inventory Coordinator reviews the aging report weekly.

**Aging Tiers:**

| Age Tier | Definition | Required Action |
|---|---|---|
| **90 days** (slow-moving) | SKU with positive on-hand and zero or minimal sales in the trailing 90 days | Department Supervisor reviews for possible markdown, RTV, or inter-store transfer. No mandatory action yet. |
| **180 days** (aged) | SKU with positive on-hand and sales velocity below 50% of plan in the trailing 180 days | Mandatory markdown to 25% off original retail. Department Supervisor must document disposition plan. |
| **270 days** (deep aged) | SKU still on-hand after 180-day markdown | Markdown increased to 50% off original retail. |
| **365 days** (dead stock) | SKU still on-hand after 270-day markdown | Markdown increased to 75% off original retail. If the item does not sell within 30 additional days at 75% off, the item is written off or donated. |

**Aging Report Fields:**

- SKU number and description
- Original retail price
- Current retail price
- Current on-hand quantity
- Days since last sale
- Trailing 90-day sales velocity (units/week)
- Planned sales velocity (units/week)
- Variance from plan (%)
- Recommended action (markdown, RTV, transfer, donate, dispose)

### 13.2 Markdown Cadence

The markdown process follows a structured cadence designed to maximize recovery value while clearing aged inventory.

| Markdown Phase | Discount | Timing | Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | 25% off original retail | When item reaches 180-day aging tier (or seasonal markdown trigger date) | Department Supervisor |
| Phase 2 | 50% off original retail | 30 days after Phase 1, if unsold | Assistant Store Manager |
| Phase 3 | 75% off original retail | 30 days after Phase 2, if unsold | Store Manager |
| Phase 4 | $0.01 (nominal) or write-off | 30 days after Phase 3, if unsold | Store Manager |

**Markdown Price Calculation:**

```
Phase 1 Price = Original Retail × 0.75  (rounded down to nearest $0.01)
Phase 2 Price = Original Retail × 0.50  (rounded down to nearest $0.01)
Phase 3 Price = Original Retail × 0.25  (rounded down to nearest $0.01)
Phase 4 Price = Write-off at cost

Minimum Markdown Price = $0.50 (anything below $0.50 after markdown is written off)
```

**Markdown Labeling:**

- All markdown items must display a BRHI Markdown label (yellow background, black text) showing:
  - Original price (with strikethrough)
  - Markdown price
  - Reason code (age, damage, seasonal, open box)
- Markdown labels are generated by the Zebra TC73 or the price change workstation at the Returns Desk.

### 13.3 Consolidation to Clearance Endcap

Every store maintains dedicated clearance areas for markdown merchandise:

- **Clearance Endcap** — One endcap per department (minimum) designated for markdown items.
- **Clearance Aisle** — In larger stores (>$10M annual sales), a dedicated clearance aisle consolidates all markdown items from all departments.
- **Clearance Pallet** — For oversized markdown items (e.g., display models, furniture) a clearance pallet is staged at the back of the department.

**Consolidation Rules:**

1. All Phase 2 (50% off) and Phase 3 (75% off) markdown items must be moved from their planogram location to the clearance area within 48 hours of the markdown being applied.
2. Phase 1 (25% off) items may remain in their planogram location at the Department Supervisor's discretion. If space is needed, Phase 1 items are consolidated to the clearance area.
3. Clearance items are not replenished. When a clearance item sells out, the slot is filled with the next available markdown item.
4. Clearance areas are refreshed weekly. Items that have been in clearance for more than 30 days without a sale are escalated to the next markdown phase.

### 13.4 Liquidation and Donation Programs

When markdown items reach Phase 4 (write-off), they are disposed of through one of the following programs:

**Liquidation Partners:**

| Partner | Terms | Categories Accepted |
|---|---|---|
| BRHI Liquidation Auctions (B2B online marketplace) | Items listed in bulk lots; sold to highest bidder; BRHI retains 15% of sale price | All categories |
| National Liquidators, Inc. (contracted) | Flat-rate purchase at 5% of original retail; weekly pickup | Non-hazardous hard goods, seasonal |
| Habitat for Humanity ReStore (donation) | Tax-deductible donation at fair market value; BRHI receives tax benefit; items must be functional | Building materials, appliances, fixtures, paint |
| Goodwill Industries (donation) | Tax-deductible donation; mixed loads | General merchandise, tools, hardware |

**Liquidation/Donation Decision Tree:**

```
Is the item functional?
  ├── YES → Is the item a building material, fixture, or appliance?
  │         ├── YES → Donate to Habitat for Humanity ReStore
  │         └── NO  → List on BRHI Liquidation Auctions
  └── NO  → Is the item hazardous?
            ├── YES → Dispose per environmental requirements (Section 11.5)
            └── NO  → Dispose as solid waste OR donate to Goodwill (if cosmetically acceptable)
```

### 13.5 Write-Off Procedures

When inventory is written off (sold at $0 or disposed of), the following financial process is followed:

1. The Store Inventory Coordinator submits a write-off batch in the ERP containing all items being written off.
2. Each item is valued at cost (not retail).
3. The write-off is routed for approval per the authority levels in Section 11.4.
4. Upon approval:
   - The on-hand quantity is reduced to zero.
   - A journal entry is created debiting the Inventory Write-Off expense account and crediting the Inventory asset account.
   - If the item is donated, the fair market value is recorded as a charitable contribution.
5. Monthly write-off totals are reported to the District Manager and included in the store's P&L.

## 14. Vendor-Managed Inventory (VMI)

### 14.1 Eligible Product Categories

VMI is used for product categories where the vendor has superior demand visibility, where perishability requires frequent replenishment, or where local sourcing is necessary.

**Eligibility Criteria:**

- Product has a short shelf life or seasonal perishability (e.g., live plants, propane).
- Product requires specialized handling or delivery equipment that the vendor provides (e.g., propane trucks, refrigerated trucks).
- Vendor has demonstrated the capability to access BRHI POS data and manage replenishment within agreed parameters.
- Product category has stable, predictable demand patterns with low forecast variability.

### 14.2 Vendor Responsibilities

VMI vendors are contractually obligated to:

| Responsibility | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Demand monitoring** | Access BRHI POS data via the Vendor Portal (daily feed); monitor store-level sell-through rates |
| **Replenishment ordering** | Generate and submit replenishment orders via the Vendor Portal; orders must stay within the agreed min/max parameters |
| **Delivery scheduling** | Schedule deliveries within the store's DSD receiving window (6:00 AM – 12:00 PM, Monday–Friday); provide ASN at least 2 hours before delivery |
| **In-stock accountability** | Maintain a minimum 95% in-stock rate for all VMI SKUs, measured weekly |
| **Inventory freshness** | Ensure delivered product meets agreed freshness / shelf-life standards; remove and replace expired or unsaleable items at no cost to BRHI |
| **Merchandising compliance** | Stock shelves per the BRHI planogram; maintain clean, organized displays; apply BRHI-approved pricing labels |
| **Damage and returns** | Accept returns of unsaleable, damaged, or expired items; issue credit within 15 business days |
| **Reporting** | Submit a monthly VMI performance report via the Vendor Portal, including units sold, units delivered, fill rate, and in-stock rate |

### 14.3 Performance Metrics

VMI vendors are evaluated on the following key metrics, reviewed monthly by the Merchandise Planning team:

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| **In-stock rate** | ≥ 95% | Weekly POS scan vs. SKU assortment; any VMI SKU with 0 on-hand for 2+ consecutive days counts as out-of-stock |
| **Fill rate** | ≥ 98% | Units delivered / units ordered per replenishment cycle |
| **Inventory turns** | ≥ 12x annually (for VMI categories) | Trailing 12-month COGS / Average VMI inventory at cost |
| **Days of supply** | ≤ 21 days | Average VMI on-hand at retail / Average daily sales at retail |
| **Damage / spoilage rate** | < 2% of deliveries | Units damaged or expired / Total units delivered |
| **Delivery on-time rate** | ≥ 95% | Deliveries within scheduled window / Total scheduled deliveries |

### 14.4 Audit Procedures

BRHI conducts quarterly audits of all VMI programs to ensure compliance with contractual terms and data accuracy.

**VMI Audit Scope:**

1. **On-hand accuracy** — Cycle count of a random sample of 20% of VMI SKUs. Compare physical count to the vendor's reported on-hand and the ERP on-hand.
2. **Pricing compliance** — Verify shelf labels match the current BRHI retail price. Flag any discrepancies.
3. **Freshness / expiration** — Check all perishable items for compliance with agreed shelf-life standards.
4. **Merchandising standards** — Visual inspection of VMI displays for planogram compliance, cleanliness, and organization.
5. **Invoice reconciliation** — Compare vendor invoices to the ASN and physical receipts for a sample of the last 10 deliveries. Verify quantities, prices, and credit terms.
6. **Performance scorecard** — Review the vendor's trailing 3-month performance against all targets (Section 14.3).

**Audit Outcome:**

| Performance Rating | Action |
|---|---|
| All targets met | Continue VMI; no action |
| 1–2 targets missed | Vendor receives a Performance Improvement Notice; must submit corrective action plan within 10 business days |
| 3+ targets missed | VMI program placed on probation; Merchandise Planning reviews the program for possible termination |
| On-hand accuracy < 90% | Immediate blind receiving requirement imposed; full recount of all VMI SKUs; vendor bears the cost of the audit |

## 16. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The following KPIs are the primary measures of inventory management effectiveness at BRHI. They are tracked at the store, district, region, and company level.

### 16.1 KPI Summary

| KPI | Definition | Target | Calculation | Measurement Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Inventory Turns** | Number of times inventory is sold and replaced in a year | **7.0x** annually | `Annual COGS / Average Inventory at Cost` | Monthly (rolling 12-month); reported quarterly |
| **GMROI** | Gross margin dollars returned per dollar of average inventory investment | **$3.00** | `Gross Margin $ / Average Inventory at Cost $` | Quarterly |
| **In-Stock Rate** | Percentage of active SKUs with on-hand > 0 at the time of measurement | **97%** | `(Active SKUs with OH > 0) / (Total Active SKUs) × 100` | Daily snapshot; reported weekly |
| **Shrink Rate** | Inventory loss as a percentage of net sales | **< 1.5%** | `(Book Inventory − Physical Inventory) at Retail / Net Sales × 100` | Trailing 12-month; updated monthly |
| **Days of Supply** | Average number of days the current inventory will last at the current sales rate | **45 days** | `Average Inventory at Retail / (Annual Net Sales / 365)` | Weekly; reported monthly |
| **Fill Rate** | Percentage of ordered units actually delivered | **98%** | `Units Delivered / Units Ordered × 100` | Weekly |
| **Cycle Count Accuracy (SKU)** | Percentage of counted SKUs matching the system on-hand within tolerance | **≥ 97%** | `(SKUs Counted − SKUs with Variance) / SKUs Counted × 100` | Monthly (rolling 30-day) |
| **Cycle Count Accuracy (Dollar)** | Percentage of counted dollar value matching the system on-hand within tolerance | **≥ 99%** | `1 − (\|Σ Variance Dollars\| / Σ System OH Dollars) × 100` | Monthly (rolling 30-day) |
| **Putaway Compliance** | Percentage of received units put away within 24 hours | **≥ 95%** | `Units Put Away ≤ 24 hrs / Total Units Received × 100` | Daily; reported weekly |
| **Forecast Accuracy (Category)** | Accuracy of demand forecast at the merchandise category level | **≥ 85%** | `1 − (\|Forecast − Actual\| / Actual) × 100` | Monthly |
| **Forecast Accuracy (SKU)** | Accuracy of demand forecast at the individual SKU level | **≥ 75%** | `1 − (\|Forecast − Actual\| / Actual) × 100` | Monthly |

### 16.2 KPI Escalation Matrix

When a KPI falls below target, the following escalation procedures are triggered:

| KPI | Below Target | Watch Level (1st Month) | Warning Level (2nd Month) | Critical Level (3rd Month) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Inventory Turns | < 7.0x | Store Manager reviews slow-moving categories; develops action plan | District Manager conducts on-site review; markdown / clearance accelerated | Regional VP involved; Merchandise Planning reviews assortment; potential SKU rationalization |
| GMROI | < $3.00 | Merchandise Planning reviews margin and turn components; identifies low-performing categories | Action plan required; markdown slow-moving inventory; reduce safety stock | Category review; vendor negotiations; potential category exit for persistent underperformers |
| In-Stock Rate | < 97% | Store Manager investigates OOS root causes; emergency replenishment initiated | District Manager escalates to DC operations; fill rate investigation | Regional VP escalates to VP Supply Chain; systemic root cause analysis |
| Shrink Rate | > 1.5% | Loss Prevention increases presence; high-shrink SKUs identified and protected | District LP Manager conducts full shrink audit; CCTV review | Regional LP Director involved; potential associate investigation; physical security upgrades |
| Fill Rate | < 98% | Store Manager reports to DC operations; investigates specific SKUs | District Manager escalates to DC General Manager; vendor performance review | Regional VP escalates to VP Supply Chain; vendor compliance penalties assessed |
| Cycle Count Accuracy | < 97% SKU / < 99% $ | Additional cycle counts scheduled; retraining of count associates | District Manager on-site review; count process audit; additional count resources deployed | Regional LP audit; store may require full physical inventory recount |

### 16.3 KPI Reporting Dashboard

All KPIs are available in real-time on the BRHI Inventory Dashboard (accessible via the Store Companion app and the corporate BI portal). The dashboard provides:

- **Store-level view** — Current KPI values vs. targets with red/yellow/green indicators.
- **Trend analysis** — 13-week and 52-week trend charts for each KPI.
- **Peer comparison** — Comparison to the store's peer group (similar sales volume and format stores within the region).
- **Drill-down** — Click on any KPI to drill down to the department, category, or SKU level.
- **Alerts** — Push notifications to the Store Manager when any KPI enters the Watch, Warning, or Critical level.
