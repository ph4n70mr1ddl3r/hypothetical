## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the policies, processes, and controls governing all procurement and vendor management activities at BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. (BRHI). It ensures that purchasing decisions are made in the best interest of the company, comply with applicable regulations (including Sarbanes-Oxley Act requirements), and support BRHI's strategic objectives of delivering quality products at competitive prices to our customers.

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to all purchasing activities conducted on behalf of BRHI, from initial vendor identification through final payment. Specifically, it covers:

- Resale merchandise procurement (all categories within BRHI's product assortment)
- Non-merchandise procurement (store supplies, fixtures, equipment, IT, professional services, construction, maintenance, and staffing)
- Vendor selection, onboarding, performance management, and exit
- Contract negotiation and management
- Import and domestic sourcing
- Rebate, allowance, and markdown money programs
- Payment processing and procure-to-pay cycle

### 1.3 Applicability

This SOP applies to all BRHI associates involved in purchasing decisions, including but not limited to:

- Merchandise Buyers and Senior Buyers
- Divisional Merchandise Managers (DMMs)
- Vice Presidents of Merchandising
- Senior Vice President of Merchandising
- Chief Merchandising Officer (CMO)
- Procurement Operations team members
- Vendor Compliance team members
- Store Operations personnel who initiate purchase requisitions
- Finance and Accounts Payable personnel involved in procurement
- Any associate authorized to commit company funds for goods or services

### 1.4 Regulatory Compliance

All procurement activities must comply with:

- Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) Section 302 and 404 internal control requirements
- Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) regarding purchase commitments
- Federal Trade Commission (FTC) regulations on advertising and pricing
- U.S. Customs and Border Protection regulations for imported goods
- Robinson-Patman Act (fair pricing practices)
- Applicable state and local procurement regulations
- BRHI Code of Business Conduct and Ethics

## 2. Procurement Governance Structure

### 2.1 Organizational Hierarchy

BRHI's procurement function operates under a layered governance model designed to balance strategic oversight with operational agility.

#### 2.1.1 Chief Merchandising Officer (CMO)

- **Role**: Provides strategic direction for all merchandising and procurement activities across BRHI.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Set enterprise-wide procurement strategy and annual merchandising objectives
  - Approve purchases exceeding $1,000,000
  - Approve new vendor relationships projected to exceed $2,000,000 in annual spend
  - Chair the Quarterly Merchandise Review with the executive team
  - Approve private label strategy and major category shifts
  - Present procurement performance to the Board of Directors quarterly
  - Authorize entry into or exit from major product categories
- **Reports to**: Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

#### 2.1.2 Senior Vice President (SVP) of Merchandising

- **Role**: Oversees day-to-day merchandising operations across all product divisions.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Approve purchases from $250,001 to $1,000,000
  - Manage the divisional merchandising structure and buyer team
  - Approve annual category business plans
  - Oversee vendor compliance programs
  - Coordinate with Supply Chain on inventory planning and allocation
- **Reports to**: CMO

#### 2.1.3 Vice President (VP) of Merchandising

- **Role**: Manages a portfolio of merchandise divisions and provides category-level strategic leadership.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Approve purchases from $50,001 to $250,000
  - Develop and execute category strategies for assigned divisions
  - Conduct quarterly business reviews with top-tier vendors
  - Approve vendor agreements under $500,000 annually
  - Lead buyer development and training
  - Review and approve assortment plans for assigned categories
- **Reports to**: SVP of Merchandising

#### 2.1.4 Divisional Merchandise Manager (DMM)

- **Role**: Manages a specific merchandise division (e.g., Building Materials, Electrical, Plumbing, Paint, Lawn & Garden, Tools, Hardware).
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Approve purchases from $5,001 to $50,000
  - Develop seasonal merchandise plans and open-to-buy budgets
  - Supervise Merchandise Buyers within the division
  - Review vendor scorecards and initiate corrective actions
  - Coordinate with Marketing on promotional events
  - Approve new item additions within assigned categories
- **Reports to**: VP of Merchandising

#### 2.1.5 Merchandise Buyer / Senior Buyer

- **Role**: Responsible for day-to-day purchasing decisions within assigned product categories.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Approve purchases up to $5,000
  - Select merchandise assortment within approved category plan
  - Negotiate cost, terms, and allowances with vendors
  - Create and manage purchase orders
  - Monitor inventory levels and sell-through rates
  - Analyze market trends and competitive pricing
  - Maintain vendor relationships for assigned categories
- **Reports to**: DMM

#### 2.1.6 Procurement Operations

- **Role**: Manages the transactional and compliance aspects of the procurement process.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Process purchase orders and maintain PO accuracy
  - Perform three-way match (PO, receipt, invoice) for all invoices
  - Manage the procurement system (ERP) master data
  - Monitor and resolve pricing discrepancies and invoice exceptions
  - Ensure SOX compliance controls are followed
  - Generate procurement reports and analytics
  - Manage blanket purchase orders and contract compliance
- **Reports to**: Director of Procurement Operations

#### 2.1.7 Vendor Compliance Team

- **Role**: Ensures vendors adhere to BRHI standards, policies, and performance expectations.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Administer the vendor scorecard program
  - Conduct vendor audits (announced and unannounced)
  - Manage chargebacks for compliance violations
  - Coordinate corrective action plans with underperforming vendors
  - Monitor vendor insurance, certifications, and licensing
  - Manage the vendor exit process
  - Oversee the diversity supplier program tracking
- **Reports to**: VP of Merchandising

### 2.2 Committees

#### 2.2.1 New Vendor Committee

- **Composition**: VP of Merchandising (chair), DMM for the category, Procurement Operations Manager, Vendor Compliance Manager, Finance representative, Legal representative (as needed)
- **Meeting Cadence**: Bi-weekly (every other Tuesday, 10:00 AM)
- **Purpose**: Review and approve new vendor applications, evaluate vendor risk, approve vendor master data setup
- **Quorum**: VP of Merchandising plus at least three additional members
- **Decision Authority**: Approve/reject new vendor applications; approve exceptions to standard vendor terms

#### 2.2.2 Capital Expenditure Committee

- **Composition**: CFO (chair), CMO, VP of Store Operations, VP of IT, Director of Finance
- **Meeting Cadence**: Monthly (first Wednesday, 2:00 PM)
- **Purpose**: Review and approve all capital expenditure requests, including fixtures, equipment, construction, and major IT purchases
- **Authority**: All capital expenditures regardless of dollar amount require Committee approval before a purchase order is issued

#### 2.2.3 Procurement Review Board

- **Composition**: SVP of Merchandising (chair), all VPs of Merchandising, Director of Procurement Operations, Controller
- **Meeting Cadence**: Quarterly
- **Purpose**: Review overall procurement performance, policy compliance, spend analytics, vendor concentration risk, and approve policy changes

## 3. Vendor Selection and Onboarding

### 3.1 Vendor Application Process

All prospective vendors must complete the BRHI Vendor Application Package (Form BRHI-PROC-001), which includes:

1. **Vendor Information Form** — Company legal name, DBA names, tax ID (EIN or SSN), address(es), ownership structure, years in business, annual revenue, number of employees
2. **Product / Service Catalog** — Description of goods or services offered, product specifications, pricing sheets, minimum order quantities
3. **Insurance Verification** — Certificate of insurance evidencing:
   - Commercial General Liability: minimum $2,000,000 per occurrence
   - Product Liability: minimum $2,000,000 per occurrence
   - Automobile Liability: minimum $1,000,000 (if delivery services provided)
   - Workers' Compensation: as required by state law
   - Umbrella / Excess Liability: minimum $5,000,000
   - BRHI must be named as Additional Insured and Certificate Holder
4. **Financial Statements** — Most recent audited financial statements or, for privately held companies, a Dun & Bradstreet business credit report with a minimum score of 60
5. **Reference List** — Minimum three trade references (customers with comparable volume) and one banking reference
6. **Sustainability Questionnaire** (Form BRHI-PROC-002) — Environmental practices, waste management, packaging sustainability, carbon footprint reduction initiatives
7. **Diversity Certification** (if applicable) — Copy of certification from a recognized certification body
8. **W-9 Form** — IRS Form W-9, Request for Taxpayer Identification Number and Certification

### 3.2 Due Diligence Requirements

The Vendor Compliance Team conducts due diligence on every prospective vendor before presentation to the New Vendor Committee. Due diligence includes the following checks:

#### 3.2.1 Financial Stability Assessment

- Obtain and review Dun & Bradstreet report — minimum PAYDEX score of 60
- Verify no outstanding tax liens, judgments, or bankruptcies within the past seven years
- Review audited financial statements for vendors projected to exceed $500,000 in annual spend
- For vendors projected to exceed $2,000,000 in annual spend, require a financial statement review or audit by a CPA firm
- Re-evaluate financial stability annually for all active vendors with annual spend exceeding $500,000

#### 3.2.2 Insurance and Risk

- Verify all required insurance coverage is in force and BRHI is named as Additional Insured
- Confirm insurance expiration dates and establish automatic renewal tracking in the vendor master system
- Flag vendors with lapsed insurance for immediate suspension of new purchase orders
- Assess product liability risk for merchandise categories with higher safety profiles (electrical, power tools, chemicals, ladders)

#### 3.2.3 References

- Contact all three trade references and document responses on Form BRHI-PROC-003 (Reference Check Form)
- Verify product quality claims with reference accounts
- Confirm delivery performance and fill-rate reliability
- Check for any history of product recalls or safety issues

#### 3.2.4 Sustainability Practices

- Evaluate packaging materials — preference for recyclable, minimal, and sustainable packaging
- Assess manufacturing processes for environmental impact
- Review transportation and logistics practices for carbon efficiency
- Score vendor on BRHI Sustainability Scorecard (Form BRHI-PROC-004) — minimum passing score of 60 out of 100
- Vendors scoring below 60 may be approved conditionally with a required improvement plan

#### 3.2.5 Product Quality and Safety

- Require product samples for evaluation by the BRHI Quality Assurance team
- Verify compliance with applicable safety standards:
  - Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) regulations
  - Underwriters Laboratories (UL) listings where applicable
  - ASTM International standards for relevant product categories
  - California Proposition 65 compliance
  - EPA regulations for chemicals, paints, and pesticides
- Confirm the vendor has a product recall procedure in place

### 3.3 Diversity Supplier Program

BRHI is committed to promoting supplier diversity and economic inclusion. The company maintains a target of 25% of total addressable procurement spend with diverse suppliers.

#### 3.3.1 Qualifying Diverse Supplier Categories

BRHI recognizes the following diverse supplier classifications:

| Category | Certifying Body Examples |
|---|---|
| Minority-Owned Business Enterprise (MBE) | NMSDC, local MSDC affiliates |
| Women-Owned Business Enterprise (WBE) | WBENC, NWBOC |
| Veteran-Owned / Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business (VOSB/SDVOSB) | VA CVE, NVBDC |
| Small Business Enterprise (SBE) | SBA, state/local agencies |
| HUBZone Business | SBA HUBZone Program |
| Disability-Owned Business Enterprise (DOBE) | Disability:IN |
| LGBT-Owned Business Enterprise (LGBTBE) | NGLCC |

#### 3.3.2 Program Mechanics

- All RFPs and competitive bids must include at least one diverse supplier candidate where available
- Buyers must document diverse supplier participation (or lack thereof) in the vendor selection worksheet for every new vendor decision
- The Vendor Compliance Team tracks diversity spend monthly and reports to the SVP of Merchandising
- Annual diversity supplier goal: 25% of total addressable spend
- Quarterly diversity supplier networking events are hosted by BRHI to develop the diverse supplier pipeline
- Diverse suppliers that meet qualification standards may receive up to a 3% price preference in competitive bid evaluations (i.e., a diverse supplier bid within 3% of the lowest non-diverse bid will be deemed competitive)

#### 3.3.3 Reporting

- Monthly diversity spend report distributed to CMO, SVP Merchandising, all VPs of Merchandising
- Quarterly diversity supplier dashboard presented to the Procurement Review Board
- Annual Diversity Supplier Report included in BRHI's corporate social responsibility disclosure

All master data changes for active vendors require approval from the Procurement Operations Manager and must be documented in the vendor change log. Changes to banking or payment information require verbal verification with the vendor using a known phone number (not a number provided in the change request) and approval from the Controller.

### 3.6 Vendor Agreement Execution

Every approved vendor must execute the BRHI Standard Vendor Agreement (Form BRHI-PROC-005) before the first purchase order is issued.

#### 3.6.1 Standard Terms and Conditions

The BRHI Standard Vendor Agreement includes the following key provisions:

- **Payment Terms**: Net 60 days from receipt of invoice, unless otherwise negotiated
- **Delivery Terms**: FOB Destination, unless otherwise agreed; vendor bears risk of loss during transit
- **Return-to-Vendor (RTV)**: BRHI may return unsold merchandise for a full credit within the agreed return window (category-specific, minimum 90 days from receipt)
- **Product Warranties**: Vendor warrants all products are merchantable, fit for their intended purpose, and free from defects
- **Indemnification**: Vendor indemnifies BRHI against product liability claims arising from defective products
- **Insurance**: Vendor maintains required insurance coverage for the duration of the relationship
- **Compliance**: Vendor complies with all applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations
- **Confidentiality**: Mutual non-disclosure of proprietary business information
- **Term and Termination**: Initial one-year term, auto-renewing for successive one-year periods; either party may terminate with 90 days written notice
- **Chargebacks**: BRHI may assess chargebacks for non-compliance with vendor guidelines (shipping, labeling, EDI, etc.)
- **Audit Rights**: BRHI may audit vendor records related to the business relationship upon 30 days notice

#### 3.6.2 Legal Review Requirements

- Standard Vendor Agreement (no modifications): DMM may execute; no Legal review required
- Modified Standard Agreement: Legal review required; Legal has 5 business days to review
- Vendor's Own Contract: Legal review required; Legal has 10 business days to review
- Agreements over $500,000 annually: Legal review required regardless of form
- Agreements over $1,000,000 annually: Legal review and CFO approval required
- All vendor agreements are stored in the BRHI Contract Repository (see Section 12)

## 4. Purchasing Process

### 4.1 Purchase Requisition

A Purchase Requisition (PR) is required for all non-merchandise purchases. Merchandise purchases are initiated directly via Purchase Order through the open-to-buy process managed by the Buyer.

#### 4.1.1 When a Purchase Requisition Is Required

- Store supplies, fixtures, and equipment
- IT hardware and software
- Professional services (consulting, legal, marketing agencies)
- Construction and maintenance services
- Temporary staffing
- Any non-resale goods or services
- Any purchase where a vendor relationship does not yet exist

#### 4.1.2 Purchase Requisition Process

1. Requesting associate completes the Purchase Requisition Form (BRHI-PROC-006) in the ERP system
2. Fields required: description of goods/services, estimated cost, suggested vendor (if known), required delivery date, budget account code, business justification
3. Requisition is routed for approval per the authority matrix below
4. Approved requisition is forwarded to Procurement Operations for PO creation
5. Procurement Operations creates the Purchase Order and transmits to the vendor

#### 4.1.3 Purchase Requisition Approval Matrix (Non-Merchandise)

| Dollar Range | Approver |
|---|---|
| $0 - $5,000 | Department Manager |
| $5,001 - $25,000 | Director / Senior Director |
| $25,001 - $100,000 | VP of functional area |
| $100,001 - $500,000 | SVP of functional area |
| $500,001+ | CEO or delegate |

### 4.2 Purchase Order Creation

A Purchase Order (PO) is mandatory for all purchases exceeding $500. No vendor should ship goods or provide services without a valid BRHI PO number.

#### 4.2.1 Required PO Information

Every PO must contain the following:

- Unique PO number (system-generated, sequential)
- Vendor number and name
- Ship-to location (store number, distribution center, or corporate office)
- Ordered items: SKU, description, quantity, unit of measure, unit cost, extended cost
- Requested delivery date
- Shipping method and freight terms
- Payment terms
- Any special instructions or reference numbers
- Approving buyer name and date of approval

#### 4.2.2 Purchase Order Approval Authority Matrix (Merchandise)

All merchandise POs must be approved at the appropriate authority level based on total PO dollar value:

| PO Value Range | Required Approver | Backup Approver |
|---|---|---|
| $0 - $5,000 | Merchandise Buyer | Senior Buyer |
| $5,001 - $50,000 | Divisional Merchandise Manager | VP of Merchandising |
| $50,001 - $250,000 | VP of Merchandising | SVP of Merchandising |
| $250,001 - $1,000,000 | SVP of Merchandising | CMO |
| $1,000,001 and above | CMO | CEO (co-approval required for >$5,000,000) |

**Capital Expenditures**: All capital expenditure POs follow a separate approval chain regardless of dollar amount:

| PO Value Range | Required Approval Chain |
|---|---|
| $0 - $25,000 | Department Manager → VP of functional area → Controller |
| $25,001 - $250,000 | VP of functional area → SVP of functional area → Controller |
| $250,001 - $1,000,000 | Capital Expenditure Committee approval → CFO |
| $1,000,001+ | Capital Expenditure Committee approval → CFO → CEO |

#### 4.2.3 PO Processing Timeframes

| Action | Standard Processing Time | Expedited Processing Time |
|---|---|---|
| PO creation (from approved requisition) | 2 business days | Same business day |
| Approval routing | 2 business days per approver | 4 hours per approver |
| PO transmission to vendor | 1 business day after approval | Same business day |
| Total standard cycle | 5 business days | 2 business days |

### 4.3 Three-Way Match Requirement

All invoices must pass a three-way match before payment is authorized. This is a critical SOX control.

#### 4.3.1 Three-Way Match Components

| Match Element | Source Document | Tolerance |
|---|---|---|
| **Purchase Order** | PO in ERP | Reference |
| **Goods Receipt / Service Confirmation** | Receiving ticket in ERP | Quantity tolerance: ±2% |
| **Vendor Invoice** | Invoice received (EDI, mail, email) | Price tolerance: ±$0.01 per unit |

#### 4.3.2 Match Process

1. Invoice is received by Accounts Payable and entered into the ERP (or received via EDI 810)
2. ERP system automatically matches the invoice to the open PO and goods receipt
3. **Full Match** (within tolerances): Invoice is automatically approved for payment per terms
4. **Partial Match or No Match**: Invoice is flagged as an exception and routed to Procurement Operations for investigation
5. Procurement Operations resolves the discrepancy within 5 business days by:
   - Contacting the vendor for a corrected invoice
   - Requesting a price variance approval (if cost difference)
   - Verifying receiving discrepancies with the warehouse/store
   - Rejecting the invoice if it cannot be reconciled
6. All resolved exceptions are logged with resolution notes

#### 4.3.3 Exception Handling

| Exception Type | Resolution Authority | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Quantity variance (≤2%) | Procurement Operations Analyst | 3 business days |
| Quantity variance (>2% and ≤10%) | Procurement Operations Manager | 5 business days |
| Quantity variance (>10%) | DMM | 5 business days |
| Price variance (≤$100 total) | Buyer | 3 business days |
| Price variance ($101 - $1,000 total) | DMM | 5 business days |
| Price variance ($1,001 - $10,000 total) | VP of Merchandising | 5 business days |
| Price variance (>$10,000 total) | SVP of Merchandising | 5 business days |
| Unauthorized PO (no PO on file) | VP of Merchandising + Controller | 10 business days |

Unauthorized purchases (invoices with no matching PO) require VP-level and Controller approval. A written explanation must accompany the approval and be filed with the invoice. Repeated unauthorized purchases from the same department or associate will be escalated to the SVP and HR.

### 4.4 Blanket Purchase Orders

Blanket Purchase Orders (BPOs) are used for repetitive, low-value purchases from the same vendor over a defined period.

#### 4.4.1 When to Use a BPO

- Repetitive purchases of the same goods or services
- Ordering frequency of at least once per month
- Individual order values typically under $5,000
- Common examples: store cleaning supplies, uniform services, office supplies, routine maintenance

#### 4.4.2 BPO Parameters

- **Duration**: Maximum 12 months, aligned with fiscal year (January 1 – December 31)
- **Total Value**: Maximum $250,000 per BPO
- **Release Mechanism**: Releases against the BPO are initiated by the requesting department via a BPO Release Form (BRHI-PROC-007) in the ERP
- **Tracking**: Procurement Operations monitors cumulative releases against the BPO ceiling monthly
- **Renewal**: BPOs must be re-approved annually following the standard PO approval matrix based on projected annual spend

### 4.5 Contract Purchases

For services and construction projects, a Contract Purchase is used when the scope of work, deliverables, and payment terms are defined in a signed contract rather than a traditional PO.

#### 4.5.1 Applicability

- Construction and renovation projects
- Professional consulting engagements
- IT implementation services
- Maintenance service agreements
- Marketing agency retainers
- Any engagement where deliverables are project-based

#### 4.5.2 Process

1. The requesting department develops a Statement of Work (SOW)
2. The SOW is reviewed by Procurement Operations and Legal (if value exceeds $50,000)
3. A contract is negotiated and executed (see Section 12)
4. A Contract Purchase Order is created in the ERP referencing the contract number
5. Invoices are matched to the contract PO with proof of milestone completion or deliverable acceptance
6. The requesting department's authorized representative must confirm receipt/acceptance of services before payment

## 5. Negotiation and Pricing

### 5.1 Cost Negotiation Guidelines

All merchandise costs must be negotiated using a structured approach:

#### 5.1.1 Pre-Negotiation Preparation

- Conduct a Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) analysis including unit cost, freight, handling, carrying cost, and shrink
- Gather market intelligence through competitive price shopping, trade publications, and market reports
- Review historical cost data and trend analysis for the item or category
- Understand the vendor's cost structure, raw material trends, and competitive pressures
- Identify BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement) — have at least two alternative vendors for critical categories
- Prepare a negotiation plan documenting target cost, walk-away cost, and key terms to negotiate

#### 5.1.2 Negotiation Principles

- Negotiate in good faith; maintain professional relationships
- Seek mutually beneficial outcomes (win-win long-term partnerships)
- Never disclose a vendor's pricing to a competitor
- Document all negotiated terms in writing
- Obtain DMM approval for any cost commitments exceeding the Buyer's authority
- All negotiated costs are confidential and must not be shared outside the Merchandising team

### 5.2 Volume Discount Structures

BRHI leverages its scale to negotiate volume-based pricing tiers. Standard volume discount structures include:

| Discount Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Tiered Pricing | Unit cost decreases as order volume increases | 1-99 units: $10.00; 100-499: $9.50; 500+: $9.00 |
| Annual Volume Rebate | Retroactive discount based on cumulative annual purchases | 2% rebate when annual spend exceeds $500,000 |
| Growth Incentive | Additional discount for year-over-year volume growth | 1% additional rebate for >10% growth vs. prior year |
| Full-Line Discount | Discount for carrying a vendor's complete product line | 3% off-invoice when BRHI carries the vendor's top 50 SKUs |
| Seasonal Pre-Booking | Discount for placing orders before the season | 5% discount for spring seasonal orders placed before November 1 |

### 5.3 Rebate and Allowance Programs

#### 5.3.1 Billback Allowances

- Vendor provides an allowance per unit sold, billed retroactively on a monthly or quarterly basis
- Billback rate is negotiated and documented in the Vendor Agreement
- Buyer tracks eligible units through POS sales data
- Vendor issues a credit memo or ACH payment within 30 days of the billback period
- Common for promotional pricing support and co-op advertising

#### 5.3.2 Off-Invoice Allowances

- Allowance is deducted directly from the invoice at the time of purchase
- Preferred method for BRHI (improves cash flow and reduces reconciliation burden)
- Off-invoice allowances must be clearly documented on the PO and the invoice
- ERP system must be updated to reflect the net cost after off-invoice allowance

#### 5.3.3 Lump-Sum Allowances

- Fixed-dollar allowance paid by the vendor for a defined program or period
- Typical uses: new store opening support, market entry support, fixture funding, remodel allowance
- Must be documented in a written agreement specifying the program terms, payment schedule, and any performance conditions
- Lump-sum allowances are recognized as a reduction of cost of goods sold in the period earned, per GAAP

#### 5.3.4 Rebate Accounting and Controls

- All rebate and allowance agreements must be documented in writing and entered into the ERP rebate module
- The Finance team accrues estimated rebates monthly based on actual purchase volumes
- Rebates receivable are reconciled quarterly and any variance is investigated
- Year-end rebate true-up must be completed within 30 days of fiscal year-end
- SOX Control: Rebate accruals are reviewed by the Controller and subject to external audit testing

### 5.4 Markdown Money Negotiation

Markdown money (vendor-funded markdown allowance) is negotiated to offset the cost of clearing slow-moving or seasonal inventory.

#### 5.4.1 When to Negotiate Markdown Money

- Seasonal merchandise not sold through by the planned clearance date
- Slow-moving items that have been in inventory for more than 120 days
- Vendor-initiated product changes or discontinuations that render existing inventory obsolete
- Competitive price reductions that require BRHI to match market pricing
- New item failures where the item has not met minimum sell-through targets within 90 days of introduction

#### 5.4.2 Markdown Money Process

1. Buyer identifies eligible merchandise and calculates the required markdown to clear
2. Buyer submits a Markdown Money Request (Form BRHI-PROC-008) to the DMM for approval
3. Approved request is communicated to the vendor with supporting sell-through data
4. Vendor agrees to markdown money (negotiation may involve partial funding)
5. Markdown money is recorded as a vendor allowance in the ERP
6. Markdown is executed at the store level per the markdown calendar
7. Markdown money received is reconciled against actual markdowns taken

### 5.5 Return-to-Vendor (RTV) Terms

RTV provisions are negotiated as part of the Vendor Agreement and must include:

| RTV Provision | BRHI Standard |
|---|---|
| Return window | Minimum 90 days from receipt for seasonal; 180 days for basic goods |
| Return reason categories | Unsold, defective, damaged in transit, recall, wrong item shipped |
| Return freight cost | Vendor bears freight for defective/damaged/wrong item; BRHI bears freight for unsold |
| Restocking fee | BRHI does not accept restocking fees |
| Credit timing | Vendor must issue credit within 30 days of receipt of returned goods |
| Return authorization | Buyer must obtain RMA (Return Merchandise Authorization) from vendor before shipping |
| Unsaleable goods | Vendor must accept return or provide destruction authorization with full credit |

### 5.6 Payment Terms

#### 5.6.1 Standard Payment Terms

| Term | Description | Applicability |
|---|---|---|
| **Net 60** | Payment due 60 days from receipt of invoice or goods, whichever is later | Default for all merchandise vendors |
| **2/10 Net 60** | 2% discount if paid within 10 days; otherwise Net 60 | Available when negotiated; requires Finance approval |
| **Net 45** | Payment due 45 days from invoice/goods receipt | Available for strategic vendors or where volume justifies |
| **Net 30** | Payment due 30 days from invoice/goods receipt | Available only with SVP Merchandising approval |
| **Monthly Statement** | Payment on monthly statement, net 60 from statement date | For small vendors with frequent small invoices; min. 10 invoices/month |

#### 5.6.2 Early Payment Discount Program

- Early payment discounts (e.g., 2/10 Net 60) must be evaluated using the Annualized Cost of Foregoing Discount calculation
- Finance must approve all early payment discount agreements
- The ERP system automatically applies early payment discounts when payment is made within the discount window
- Annualized savings from early payment discounts are tracked and reported monthly

### 5.7 Cost Change Process

#### 5.7.1 Vendor-Initiated Cost Increases

- Vendors must provide a minimum of **60 calendar days' written notice** before any cost increase takes effect
- Notice must specify: item(s) affected, current cost, new cost, effective date, and justification for the increase
- The Buyer evaluates the increase against market benchmarks and raw material indices
- The Buyer may: (a) accept the increase, (b) negotiate a lesser increase, (c) reject the increase, (d) identify an alternative vendor, or (e) plan to discontinue the item
- Any accepted increase exceeding 5% on an individual item requires DMM approval
- Any accepted increase exceeding 10% on an individual item or exceeding $50,000 in aggregate annual impact requires VP of Merchandising approval
- Cost increases exceeding 15% on an individual item require SVP Merchandising approval

#### 5.7.2 BRHI-Initiated Cost Decrease Requests

- Buyers may request cost reductions based on: competitive bids from alternative vendors, volume growth, market price decreases, raw material cost decreases
- Cost decrease requests must be supported by documentation (competitive bid sheets, market data, raw material indices)
- The vendor has 30 days to respond to a cost decrease request
- If the vendor declines, the Buyer may pursue alternative sourcing with DMM approval

#### 5.7.3 Cost Change Documentation

All cost changes are documented in the ERP with:

- Old cost and new cost
- Effective date
- Reason code (vendor increase, negotiated decrease, promotional, new item, etc.)
- Approver name and date
- Supporting documentation reference

### 6.6 New Item Introduction Process

#### 6.6.1 Stage Gate Process

```
Stage 1: Idea Generation
  ├── Buyer identifies opportunity via market analysis,
  │   customer feedback, or vendor presentation
  ├── Complete New Item Proposal (Form BRHI-PROC-010)
  └── DMM initial approval to proceed

Stage 2: Evaluation (2-4 weeks)
```

- Competitive landscape
- Space requirement and recommended store tiers
- Marketing and promotional support plan
- Projected first-year sales and margin

### 6.7 Item Discontinuation

#### 6.7.1 Discontinuation Criteria

Items are candidates for discontinuation when they meet one or more of the following:

- Sales velocity below 50% of plan for two consecutive months
- GMROI below category minimum for two consecutive quarters
- Ranked in bottom 10% of category by sales dollars
- Vendor has announced discontinuation of the product
- Product has been superseded by a newer version or replacement
- Quality issues that cannot be resolved with the vendor
- Strategic category exit decision

#### 6.7.2 Discontinuation Process

1. **Identification** (Week 1): Buyer identifies discontinuation candidates using the criteria above and the assortment scoring model
2. **DMM Approval** (Week 2): Buyer presents discontinuation recommendation with supporting data to DMM for approval
3. **90-Day Sell-Through Plan** (Week 3): Buyer develops a 90-day sell-through plan including:
   - Current on-hand inventory by location
   - Projected sell-through at current retail
   - Markdown strategy if sell-through is projected below 80%:
     - Week 1-4: No markdown — sell at current retail
     - Week 5-8: 15% markdown if sell-through below 50%
     - Week 9-12: 30% markdown if sell-through below 70%
     - Week 10: Negotiate RTV or markdown money for remaining inventory
4. **Execution** (Weeks 3-14): Markdowns are executed per the plan; remaining inventory is RTV'd or liquidated
5. **System Cleanup** (Week 14-15): Item is deactivated in the ERP; planogram is updated; shelf标签 ordered for replacement item

## 7. Vendor Performance Management

### 7.1 Vendor Scorecard

Every active vendor is evaluated monthly using the BRHI Vendor Scorecard. The scorecard comprises the following weighted metrics:

| Metric | Weight | Target | Measurement Method | Data Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **On-Time Delivery** | 25% | ≥95% | Percentage of PO lines delivered on or before the requested delivery date (±2 business days) | ERP receiving data |
| **Fill Rate** | 25% | ≥98% | Percentage of ordered units actually shipped (confirmed vs. ordered) | ERP PO vs. receipt |
| **Invoice Accuracy** | 20% | ≥99% | Percentage of invoices matching PO without exception (within tolerances) | AP matching data |
| **Damage Rate** | 10% | <1% | Percentage of received units damaged or unsaleable | Claims data |
| **Cost Competitiveness** | 10% | Competitive | Cost compared to market benchmarks and alternative vendor quotes | Buyer assessment (quarterly) |
| **Vendor Guideline Compliance** | 10% | 100% | Compliance with EDI, labeling, routing, and packaging guidelines | Compliance system | 

#### 7.1.1 Scoring Methodology

Each metric is scored on a 100-point scale:

| Performance vs. Target | Score |
|---|---|
| Meets or exceeds target | 100 |
| Within 2% of target | 90 |
| Within 5% of target | 75 |
| Within 10% of target | 50 |
| More than 10% below target | 25 |
| No data / not applicable | Excluded (weight redistributed) |

The overall Vendor Score is calculated as the weighted average of all applicable metrics.

#### 7.1.2 Scorecard Rating Thresholds

| Overall Score | Rating | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| 90 – 100 | **Preferred Vendor** | Eligible for increased volume, strategic partnership status, co-development opportunities |
| 75 – 89 | **Approved Vendor** | Standard business relationship; eligible for volume growth |
| 60 – 74 | **At Risk** | Corrective action required (see Section 7.3); no new item awards; volume may be reduced |
| Below 60 | **Probation** | Immediate corrective action plan; 90-day improvement window; failure to improve triggers vendor exit process |

### 7.2 Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs)

#### 7.2.1 Top 100 Vendor Program

BRHI conducts Quarterly Business Reviews with its top 100 vendors by annual spend (representing approximately 80% of total procurement spend).

#### 7.2.2 QBR Agenda

5. **Innovation and Collaboration** — Review new product opportunities, sustainability initiatives, and process improvements
6. **Financial Review** — Confirm rebate accruals, outstanding credits, payment terms compliance

#### 7.2.3 QBR Attendees

**BRHI Side**: DMM (lead), Buyer, Vendor Compliance representative, Supply Chain representative (as needed)
**Vendor Side**: National account manager, logistics representative, product representative (as needed)

#### 7.2.4 QBR Follow-Up

- Open action items are reviewed at the next QBR

### 7.3 Corrective Action Process

#### 7.3.1 Trigger Points

A Corrective Action Request (CAR) is issued when:

- Vendor score drops below 75 for two consecutive months
- A single critical failure occurs (e.g., product recall, safety issue, major delivery failure during peak season)
- Vendor fails to resolve more than 5 invoice exceptions in a single month
- Vendor is non-responsive to routine business inquiries for more than 5 business days

#### 7.3.2 Corrective Action Steps

```
Step 1: Issue CAR (Form BRHI-PROC-011)
  ├── Vendor Compliance Manager sends formal CAR to vendor
  ├── CAR includes: specific deficiency, data/evidence, expected
  │   improvement, timeline (typically 30 days)
  └── Copy to DMM and VP of Merchandising
          │
          ▼
Step 2: Vendor Response (within 10 business days)
  ├── Vendor submits a written Corrective Action Plan (CAP)
  ├── CAP must identify root cause(s), corrective steps, responsible
  │   parties, and timeline for improvement
  └── If vendor fails to respond, escalate to Step 4
          │
          ▼
Step 3: Monitoring Period (30-90 days)
  ├── Vendor Compliance monitors performance weekly during CAP period
  ├── Bi-weekly status calls with vendor
  ├── At end of monitoring period, vendor is re-scored
  └── If score returns to ≥75: CAR closed; vendor returns to normal monitoring
          │
          ▼
Step 4: Escalation (if unresolved)
  ├── VP of Merchandising conducts executive-level discussion with vendor
  ├── Options presented: revised CAP with stricter timeline,
  │   volume reduction, or vendor exit
  └── Final decision documented and communicated within 15 business days
```

## 8. Import Procurement

### 8.1 Overview

BRHI sources approximately 30-35% of its merchandise from international suppliers. Import procurement requires additional controls for customs compliance, logistics coordination, and quality assurance.

### 8.2 Sourcing from International Suppliers

#### 8.2.1 Approved Sourcing Countries

BRHI maintains a list of approved sourcing countries based on quality capability, trade relations, logistics reliability, and risk assessment:

| Region | Countries | Primary Product Categories |
|---|---|---|
| Asia-Pacific | China, Vietnam, India, Taiwan, South Korea, Japan | Tools, hardware, electrical, plumbing, décor, small appliances |
| North America | Mexico, Canada | Building materials, paint raw materials, seasonal, hardware |
| Europe | Germany, Italy, United Kingdom | Premium tools, decorative hardware, specialty products |
| South America | Brazil, Colombia | Seasonal, lawn & garden, raw materials |

#### 8.2.2 Country Risk Assessment

- New sourcing countries require a risk assessment by Procurement Operations and Legal, evaluating:
  - Political stability and trade relations with the U.S.
  - Intellectual property protection
  - Labor and environmental standards
  - Logistics infrastructure and transit time
  - Tariff and duty rates
  - Sanctions and trade restrictions (OFAC compliance)
- The risk assessment must be approved by the SVP of Merchandising before any POs are placed

### 8.3 Customs Compliance

#### 8.3.1 HTS Classification

- All imported goods must be classified under the Harmonized Tariff Schedule (HTS) of the United States
- HTS classifications are determined by the BRHI Customs Compliance team (or licensed customs broker) at the item level
- Classifications are reviewed annually and whenever product specifications change
- Erroneous classifications resulting in underpayment of duties are corrected immediately and disclosed to CBP as required

#### 8.3.2 Duty Rates and landed Cost Calculation

- Duty rates are maintained in the ERP for each imported item
- Landed cost includes: product cost + ocean freight + insurance + duty + harbor maintenance fee (HMF) + merchandise processing fee (MPF) + brokerage fees + drayage
- Landed cost is calculated at the item level and used as the true cost of goods for margin analysis and pricing decisions
- Duty rates are updated within 30 days of any tariff change announcement

#### 8.3.3 Country of Origin Marking

- All imported goods must comply with CBP country of origin marking requirements (19 CFR Part 134)
- Marking must be conspicuous, legible, indelible, and in English
- "Made in [Country]" must appear on the product, its container, or both as required
- The Buyer is responsible for ensuring vendor compliance with marking requirements before the first shipment
- Non-compliant shipments are held at the distribution center until corrected; associated costs are charged to the vendor

### 8.4 Foreign Trade Zone (FTZ) Utilization

BRHI utilizes Foreign Trade Zone designations at designated distribution centers to:

- Defer duty payments until goods enter U.S. commerce
- Reduce duty liability on goods re-exported or destroyed in the zone
- Take advantage of inverted tariff benefits (lower duty rate on finished goods vs. components)
- Streamline customs entry processing

The FTZ Administrator (within Supply Chain) manages zone compliance, inventory tracking, and CBP reporting.

### 8.5 Logistics

#### 8.5.1 Import Shipping Methods

| Method | Typical Transit Time | Cost Level | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full Container Load (FCL) — Ocean | 14-35 days (port to port) | Lowest per unit | Standard replenishment, bulk items |
| Less Than Container Load (LCL) — Ocean | 21-45 days (port to port) | Moderate | Smaller quantities, test orders |
| Air Freight | 3-7 days | Highest | Urgent needs, high-value/low-weight, stock-outs |
| Rail (from Mexico/Canada) | 5-14 days | Moderate | USMCA sourcing |
| Expedited Ocean | 10-21 days | Moderate-High | Time-sensitive ocean shipments |

#### 8.5.2 Freight Terms for Imports

- BRHI standard import terms: **FOB Port of Exit** (BRHI controls ocean freight)
- Ocean freight is managed through BRHI's contracted freight forwarders (minimum two forwarders under annual contract)
- Drayage from port to distribution center is arranged by BRHI's transportation team
- Domestic distribution from DC to stores follows the standard BRHI supply chain process

### 8.6 Lead Time Management

| Sourcing Type | Typical Lead Time | Components |
|---|---|---|
| **Import (Asia)** | 8-12 weeks | Production: 4-6 weeks + Ocean transit: 2-4 weeks + Customs clearance: 3-7 days + DC processing: 3-5 days |
| **Import (Mexico/Canada)** | 4-6 weeks | Production: 2-3 weeks + Transit: 1-2 weeks + Customs: 2-3 days + DC processing: 3-5 days |
| **Import (Europe)** | 6-10 weeks | Production: 3-5 weeks + Ocean transit: 2-3 weeks + Customs: 3-7 days + DC processing: 3-5 days |
| **Domestic** | 1-4 weeks | Production/availability: 1-7 days + Ground transit: 3-7 days + DC processing: 1-3 days |

Import buyers must maintain a rolling 16-week forward order book to ensure continuous supply.

### 8.7 Quality Inspection

#### 8.7.1 Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI)

- All import orders exceeding $50,000 require a pre-shipment inspection by a BRHI-approved third-party inspection agency
- Inspection is conducted at the vendor's facility (or port of loading) before shipment
- Inspection covers: product quality, specifications compliance, quantity verification, packaging, labeling, and country of origin marking
- Inspection standard: AQL (Acceptable Quality Level) 2.5 for major defects, 4.0 for minor defects, per ANSI/ASQ Z1.4
- Failed inspections result in: shipment hold, vendor notification, corrective action, and re-inspection before release
- Cost of re-inspection is charged to the vendor

#### 8.7.2 Dock Audit

- A sample of every import shipment (minimum 10% of SKUs per container) is audited at the receiving dock
- Dock audit checks: quantity accuracy, product damage, packaging integrity, labeling compliance, and random quality verification
- Discrepancies are documented and communicated to the vendor within 48 hours
- Consistent dock audit failures (3 or more in a 6-month period) trigger a Corrective Action Request

### 8.8 Compliance with Trade Regulations

#### 8.8.1 Anti-Dumping and Countervailing Duties (AD/CVD)

- BRHI Customs Compliance monitors all AD/CVD orders relevant to BRHI product categories
- Current categories with active AD/CVD orders include: certain steel nails, aluminum extrusions, wooden cabinets and vanities, certain hand tools
- Vendors must disclose if any AD/CVD orders apply to their products at the time of onboarding
- AD/CVD deposits are included in landed cost calculations

#### 8.8.2 Section 301 Tariffs

- BRHI tracks Section 301 tariff actions and maintains updated duty rates for all affected products
- When new Section 301 tariffs are announced, the Import Buyer has 15 business days to assess the impact and present options to the DMM: absorb the cost, negotiate vendor cost sharing, raise retail, or source from an unaffected country
- Section 301 tariff impact exceeding $100,000 annually requires SVP Merchandising approval for the mitigation plan

#### 8.8.3 Forced Labor Compliance

- BRHI prohibits the sourcing of any goods produced with forced labor, in compliance with the Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act (UFLPA) and all applicable laws
- Vendors must certify that their supply chains are free of forced labor as part of the Vendor Agreement
- BRHI reserves the right to audit vendor supply chains for forced labor compliance
- Violations result in immediate vendor suspension and potential termination

Non-merchandise procurement covers all purchases not intended for resale:

### 9.2 Competitive Bidding Requirements

| Estimated Value | Bidding Requirement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| $0 - $5,000 | No competitive bid required | Single source permitted; use existing vendor if available |
| $5,001 - $10,000 | Minimum 2 written quotes | Document quotes on file with the PO |
| $10,001 - $100,000 | Minimum 3 written quotes/bids | Quotes must be documented using Bid Comparison Worksheet (Form BRHI-PROC-012) |
| $100,001 - $500,000 | Formal RFP (Request for Proposal) | Managed by Procurement Operations; evaluation committee of at least 3 members |
| $500,001+ | Formal RFP with executive committee review | Procurement Operations manages RFP; SVP of functional area and CFO review recommendation |

#### 9.2.1 RFP Process

1. **RFP Development** (2-3 weeks): Procurement Operations works with the requesting department to develop the RFP document, including requirements, evaluation criteria, timeline, and terms
2. **Vendor Identification** (1 week): Procurement Operations identifies prospective bidders (minimum 3); at least one diverse supplier must be invited when available
3. **RFP Issuance** (1 day): RFP is distributed to prospective bidders with a minimum 3-week response window for values $100,001-$500,000 and 4-week response window for values above $500,000
4. **Bid Evaluation** (2 weeks): Evaluation committee scores bids against pre-defined weighted criteria
5. **Shortlist and Presentations** (1-2 weeks): Top 2-3 bidders may be invited for presentations or interviews
6. **Selection and Award** (1 week): Committee recommends a vendor; approval obtained per authority matrix
7. **Contract Execution** (1-2 weeks): Legal reviews contract; parties execute
8. **Notification**: All bidders are notified of the outcome within 5 business days of award

- Annual spend reviews are conducted for all non-merchandise vendors exceeding $100,000
- IT vendors must comply with BRHI's Information Security Policy and sign a Data Processing Agreement
- Professional service vendors must sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement before accessing any BRHI confidential information
- Construction vendors must provide proof of applicable state licensing and bonding

## 10. Ethical Procurement

### 10.1 Zero Tolerance for Corruption

BRHI maintains a zero-tolerance policy for kickbacks, bribes, or any form of corrupt practice in procurement.

- **Kickbacks**: No BRHI associate may solicit, accept, or offer any kickback, bribe, or improper payment to or from any vendor, prospective vendor, or third party
- **Bribery**: This includes both monetary and non-monetary inducements, such as excessive gifts, entertainment, or personal favors, intended to influence a procurement decision
- **Facilitation Payments**: Even small "facilitation" or "grease" payments to expedite routine government actions (e.g., customs clearance) are prohibited
- **Reporting**: Any associate who becomes aware of a potential violation must report it immediately through the BRHI Ethics Hotline (1-800-555-0199) or to their supervisor, HR, or Legal

### 10.2 Conflict of Interest Disclosure

- All associates involved in procurement must complete an annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure (Form BRHI-HR-020)
- Conflicts of interest include, but are not limited to:
  - Ownership of a financial interest (greater than 1%) in a vendor or prospective vendor
  - Employment by or consulting for a vendor or prospective vendor
  - A family member (spouse, domestic partner, parent, child, sibling, in-law) who is employed by or has a financial interest in a vendor
  - Personal relationships with vendor representatives that could influence procurement decisions
- Disclosed conflicts are reviewed by the VP of Merchandising and Legal. The associate may be required to recuse themselves from procurement decisions involving the conflicted vendor
- Undisclosed conflicts discovered later are grounds for disciplinary action up to and including termination

### 10.3 Vendor Gifts Policy

The vendor gifts policy aligns with the BRHI Corporate Gifts and Entertainment Policy:

- **$50 Limit**: Associates may accept gifts from vendors with a value of $50 or less per gift, not to exceed $150 in aggregate from any single vendor in a calendar year
- **Permissible Gifts**: Modest meals during business meetings, branded promotional items (pens, notepads), holiday gift baskets of nominal value
- **Prohibited Gifts**: Cash or cash equivalents (gift cards, prepaid cards), tickets to sporting events or concerts (unless attended jointly with the vendor for a legitimate business purpose and approved by a VP), travel or lodging, loans, or any gift that could influence a procurement decision
- **Declining Gifts**: Associates must politely decline gifts that exceed the $50 limit. If the gift cannot be returned (e.g., perishable items), it should be shared with the team or donated
- **Reporting**: Gifts received must be logged in the BRHI Gift Registry within 5 business days of receipt

### 10.4 Fair Dealing with All Vendors

- All vendors are treated fairly, respectfully, and professionally
- Procurement decisions are based on objective criteria: quality, price, service, reliability, and strategic fit
- No vendor is given preferential treatment based on personal relationships
- Vendors are given a reasonable opportunity to compete for BRHI business
- BRHI does not share one vendor's confidential pricing or terms with another vendor
- Vendor complaints about unfair treatment are investigated by the VP of Merchandising and reported to the Procurement Review Board

### 10.5 Supplier Code of Conduct

All BRHI vendors must acknowledge and comply with the BRHI Supplier Code of Conduct (Form BRHI-PROC-009), which requires:

1. **Legal Compliance**: Comply with all applicable laws and regulations in every jurisdiction where the vendor operates
2. **Labor Standards**: No forced, bonded, or child labor; fair wages and working hours; freedom of association; safe working conditions
3. **Environmental Responsibility**: Comply with all environmental laws; minimize waste and emissions; use sustainable materials where feasible
4. **Product Safety**: Ensure all products meet applicable safety standards and are accurately labeled
5. **Ethical Business Practices**: No bribery, corruption, or anti-competitive behavior; accurate books and records
6. **Subcontractor Management**: Ensure subcontractors comply with the same standards
7. **Cooperation with Audits**: Allow BRHI or its agents to audit facilities, records, and practices with reasonable notice

Violations of the Supplier Code of Conduct may result in corrective action requirements or immediate termination of the vendor relationship, depending on severity.

## 11. Procure-to-Pay Process Flow

### 11.1 End-to-End Process

The procure-to-pay (P2P) process encompasses all steps from identifying a need through final payment and reconciliation.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    PROCURE-TO-PAY PROCESS FLOW                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Step 1: IDENTIFY NEED
  ├── Merchandise: Buyer identifies replenishment or new item need
  │   via open-to-buy plan, inventory report, or assortment review
  ├── Non-Merchandise: Department associate identifies requirement
  └── Verify budget availability with Finance
          │
          ▼
Step 2: CREATE REQUISITION / PURCHASE ORDER
  ├── Merchandise: Buyer creates PO directly in ERP (within
  │   open-to-buy budget)
  ├── Non-Merchandise: Requestor creates Purchase Requisition
  │   in ERP (Form BRHI-PROC-006)
  ├── Required fields: vendor, items, quantities, costs,
  │   delivery date, GL account
  └── System validates: vendor is active, buyer has authority,
      GL account is valid
          │
          ▼
Step 3: OBTAIN APPROVAL
  ├── PO routes to approver per authority matrix
  │   (Section 4.2.2 for merchandise, Section 4.1.3 for non-merchandise)
  ├── Approver reviews: cost accuracy, budget availability,
  │   business justification, vendor selection
  ├── Approved → proceeds to Step 4
  ├── Rejected → returned to requestor with explanation
  └── Approval is timestamped and logged in the ERP audit trail
          │
          ▼
Step 4: TRANSMIT PO TO VENDOR
  ├── Preferred methods (in order):
  │   1. EDI 850 (electronic data interchange) — required for top 200 vendors
  │   2. Vendor portal / email
  │   3. Fax (only if EDI and email unavailable)
  ├── Vendor confirms PO within 2 business days
  ├── If no confirmation: Buyer follows up; escalate after 5 business days
  └── PO confirmation is recorded in the ERP
          │
          ▼
Step 5: RECEIVE GOODS
  ├── Goods arrive at Distribution Center or Store
  ├── Receiving associate scans/enters receipt in ERP
  ├── Receiving confirms: quantities, item descriptions, condition
  ├── Short shipments: recorded as partial receipt; Buyer notified
  ├── Damaged goods: recorded as damage claim; freight carrier
  │   and vendor notified within 24 hours
  └── Receipt is now available for three-way matching
          │
          ▼
Step 6: RECEIVE INVOICE
  ├── Preferred method: EDI 810 (electronic invoice)
  ├── Alternative: email to ap@buildright.com or mail to AP department
  ├── AP associate enters invoice into ERP (if not received via EDI)
  ├── Required invoice fields: vendor name, invoice number, invoice date,
  │   PO number, item details, quantities, unit costs, extended totals
  └── Invoice is queued for three-way match
          │
          ▼
Step 7: THREE-WAY MATCH
  ├── ERP system performs automated match: PO ↔ Receipt ↔ Invoice
  ├── Tolerances: quantity ±2%, price ±$0.01 per unit
  ├── MATCH (within tolerance): Auto-approved for payment → Step 8
  ├── NO MATCH: Exception generated → routed for investigation
  │   (see Section 4.3.3 for exception handling)
  └── SOX Control: Three-way match is a key internal control;
      bypass requires VP + Controller approval
          │
          ▼
Step 8: APPROVE PAYMENT
  ├── Matched invoices are scheduled for payment per vendor terms
  ├── Payment batch is generated weekly (every Thursday)
  ├── Early payment discounts are automatically applied if within
  │   discount window
  ├── AP Manager reviews and releases the payment batch
  └── Payments exceeding $500,000 in a single batch require
      Controller approval before release
          │
          ▼
Step 9: ISSUE PAYMENT PER TERMS
  ├── Payment methods:
  │   - ACH (preferred) — 98% of payments
  │   - Wire transfer — for payments over $500,000 or international
  │   - Check — only when ACH is not available
  ├── Payment date is calculated based on vendor terms
  │   (e.g., Net 60 = 60 days from invoice/receipt date)
  ├── Payment remittance is sent to vendor electronically (EDI 820 or email)
  └── Payment is recorded in the general ledger
          │
          ▼
Step 10: RECONCILE STATEMENT
  ├── Monthly vendor statements are reconciled to AP subledger
  ├── AP team performs reconciliation within 15 days of month-end
  ├── Discrepancies investigated and resolved within 10 business days
  ├── Unmatched debits/credits are escalated to the Buyer
  ├── Aging report reviewed weekly; past-due items flagged
  └── Monthly AP aging report submitted to Controller and CFO
```

### 11.2 Process Metrics

| Metric | Target | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| PO cycle time (requisition to vendor transmission) | ≤5 business days | Monthly |
| Three-way match auto-match rate | ≥90% | Monthly |
| Invoice exception resolution time | ≤5 business days | Monthly |
| On-time payment (within terms) | ≥99% | Monthly |
| Early payment discount capture rate | ≥95% of eligible | Monthly |
| Duplicate payment rate | <0.1% | Monthly |

### 12.2 Legal Review Requirements

| Contract Value | Legal Review Required? | Additional Approvals |
|---|---|---|

| $10,000 - $50,000 (standard template, no modifications) | No | Approving manager per authority matrix |

| Any value with non-standard indemnification or IP terms | Yes (10 business days) | VP of functional area + Legal sign-off |

### 12.3 Contract Repository

- All executed contracts are stored in the BRHI Contract Repository (SharePoint-based, access-controlled)
- **Naming Convention**: `[Vendor Name] - [Contract Type] - [Effective Date YYYY-MM-DD] - [Contract ID]`
- **Contract ID**: Assigned sequentially by Procurement Operations (format: CTR-YYYY-####)
- **Access**: Procurement Operations has full access; Legal has full access; Business users have read access to contracts within their category
- Contracts must be uploaded to the repository within 5 business days of execution

### 12.4 Renewal and Expiration Tracking

- Procurement Operations maintains a contract tracking log with the following key dates:
  - Effective date
  - Expiration date
  - Auto-renewal date (if applicable)
  - Cancellation notice deadline (typically 90 days before expiration or auto-renewal)
- **90-Day Alert**: An automated notification is sent to the Buyer, DMM, and Procurement Operations Manager 90 days before contract expiration or auto-renewal
- **60-Day Action**: Buyer must confirm renewal intent, renegotiate terms, or initiate alternative vendor sourcing
- **30-Day Final Alert**: If no action taken, escalated to VP of Merchandising
- **Expired Contracts**: No POs may be issued against an expired contract. An emergency 30-day bridge agreement may be executed with VP approval to prevent supply disruption

### 12.5 Amendment Process

- Contract amendments are required for any change to: scope, term, pricing, payment terms, liability caps, indemnification, or termination provisions
- Amendments must follow the same approval chain as the original contract based on total contract value (original + amendment)
- All amendments are numbered sequentially (Amendment 1, Amendment 2, etc.) and attached to the original contract in the repository
- Informal agreements (emails, verbal commitments) that modify contract terms are not binding — all changes must be documented in a formal amendment

### 13.2 Spend Analysis

Monthly spend analysis is conducted across the following dimensions:

#### 13.2.1 Analysis Dimensions

| Dimension | Metrics | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **By Category** | Total spend, number of vendors, average cost, margin contribution | Identify high-spend categories for strategic sourcing |
| **By Vendor** | Total spend, spend trend (YoY), percent of category spend, payment terms compliance | Monitor vendor concentration and leverage |
| **By Store** | Replenishment spend vs. plan, fill rate, stock-out rate | Identify store-level procurement issues |
| **By Buyer** | Total spend managed, cost savings achieved, vendor scorecard average | Evaluate buyer performance |
| **By Sourcing Type** | Domestic vs. import spend, duty cost, freight cost | Optimize sourcing mix |

#### 13.2.2 Action Thresholds

- Any vendor representing more than 30% of a category's spend is flagged for concentration risk review
- Any category where actual spend exceeds plan by more than 10% triggers a root cause analysis
- Any store with a fill rate below 90% is escalated to Supply Chain and Procurement Operations

### 13.3 Cost Savings Tracking

Cost savings are tracked in the following categories and reported monthly:

| Savings Type | Definition | Validation Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Negotiated Savings** | Reduction in unit cost vs. prior period or initial quote | Documented cost comparison; PO data |
| **Volume Discount** | Savings from volume-based pricing tiers | ERP cost data; rebate accruals |
| **Substitution Savings** | Lower-cost alternative item meeting same specifications | Item-level cost comparison; quality sign-off |
| **Process Savings** | Reduced procurement cycle time, automated matching, reduced exceptions | Process metrics improvement |
| **Specification Savings** | Cost reduction from product specification changes without quality impact | Engineering/quality sign-off; cost comparison |
| **Payment Terms Savings** | Value of early payment discounts captured | AP discount capture report |
| **Total Procurement Savings** | Sum of all savings types | Reconciled by Finance quarterly |

Annual cost savings targets are established by the CMO and CFO at the beginning of each fiscal year. Progress against targets is reported monthly and reviewed by the Procurement Review Board quarterly.

### 13.4 Vendor Concentration Analysis

Quarterly analysis to identify and mitigate vendor concentration risk:

| Risk Level | Criteria | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Low** | No vendor >20% of category spend | Standard monitoring |
| **Medium** | One vendor 20-30% of category spend | Document mitigation plan; identify backup vendor |
| **High** | One vendor >30% of category spend or one vendor >10% of total BRHI spend | Mandatory dual-sourcing plan required; present to Procurement Review Board |
| **Critical** | Single source vendor with no alternative | Risk mitigation plan required; present to CMO; explore development of alternative source |
