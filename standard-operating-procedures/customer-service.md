## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the policies, behavioral standards, and step-by-step procedures that govern every customer interaction at BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. (BRHI). It is designed to:

- Deliver a consistently excellent customer experience across all channels — in-store, phone, email, and live chat.
- Establish measurable service benchmarks rooted in retail customer service best practices, mystery shopper evaluation standards, and hospitality industry benchmarks.
- Equip every associate with the knowledge, authority, and processes to resolve customer needs on the first contact whenever possible.
- Protect BRHI's brand reputation through professional, empathetic, and solution-oriented service.
- Serve as the primary training reference for new associates and the authoritative guide for service-related decisions.

Every associate — regardless of department, tenure, or employment status — represents BRHI to the customer. This SOP applies from the moment a customer enters the parking lot to the moment they leave with a positive impression.

---

## 2. Scope

This document applies to:

- **All store associates** (full-time, part-time, seasonal, and temporary) in every department.
- **Store management** (Department Supervisors, Assistant Store Managers, Store Managers).
- **Pro Desk associates** and Pro relationship managers.
- **Customer Contact Center** associates handling phone, email, and chat interactions.
- **Special Services associates** (Tool Rental, Key Cutting, Load Assistance, etc.).
- **Third-party contractors and demonstrators** operating on BRHI premises.

Exclusions: Corporate-level customer experience strategy, marketing campaign management, and e-commerce platform development are covered under separate SOPs.

## 3. Service Standards

BRHI's service standards are non-negotiable minimum expectations. Every associate must meet or exceed these standards during every shift.

### 3.1 The 10-Foot Rule

**Standard:** Acknowledge every customer who comes within 10 feet with eye contact, a genuine smile, and a verbal greeting.

**Procedure:**

1. As a customer approaches within approximately 10 feet, make natural eye contact.
2. Offer a genuine smile — not a forced or mechanical expression.
3. Deliver a warm verbal greeting such as:
   - "Good morning! Welcome to BuildRight."
   - "Hi there, how are you doing today?"
   - "Welcome in — let me know if I can help you find anything."
4. If actively assisting another customer, make brief eye contact and a acknowledging nod or wave, then say, "I'll be right with you."
5. The 10-Foot Rule applies in all aisles, at the Pro Desk, in the parking lot, and at the returns counter.

**Why it matters:** The first 10 seconds of a customer's in-store experience determine their overall satisfaction more than any other factor. Research shows that acknowledged customers are 70% more likely to make a purchase and 85% more likely to return.

### 3.2 Walk, Don't Point

**Standard:** Never point a customer toward a product. Personally escort them to the exact location.

**Procedure:**

1. When a customer asks where to find something, say: "I'd be happy to take you right to it."
2. Begin walking with the customer immediately. Do not give aisle numbers or vague directions.
3. Walk at the customer's pace — do not rush ahead or lag behind.
4. Along the way, engage the customer: "Is this for a project you're working on?"
5. Upon reaching the product, hand the item to the customer when possible, or place your hand near it on the shelf.
6. Do not leave until you have asked: "Is there anything else you need for this project?" or "Would you like me to explain anything about this product?"
7. If you are unsure of the exact location, walk with the customer and use the BRHI Associate App to locate the item by SKU or description. Do not guess.

**Exceptions:** If you are actively engaged with another customer who requires immediate assistance (e.g., safety situation, emergency), briefly explain: "I'm helping another customer right now — let me find a team member to walk you there." Use the radio or Associate App to dispatch help immediately.

### 3.3 The 3-Minute Checkout Guarantee

**Standard:** No customer should wait more than 3 minutes in a checkout line.

**Procedure:**

1. The Front End Supervisor monitors line lengths continuously using the queue management system (QMS) dashboard.
2. When any register line reaches 3 customers, the Front End Supervisor calls for backup cashiers via overhead radio: "Front end backup, register [X]."
3. Backup cashiers (pre-assigned from the sales floor) must be at their assigned register and processing transactions within 90 seconds of the call.
4. If all available registers are open and the wait still exceeds 3 minutes:
   - The Front End Supervisor opens the Express Lane (10 items or fewer).
   - The Assistant Store Manager on duty is notified immediately.
   - Self-checkout attendants actively redirect customers to self-checkout stations.
5. If wait times exceed 5 minutes, the Store Manager is notified and additional measures are taken, which may include:
   - Opening a temporary register using a mobile point-of-sale (mPOS) device.
   - Pulling trained associates from any non-critical task.
   - Calling in an off-duty associate if the situation is due to a call-out.
6. Wait times are tracked electronically via the POS queue timer and reported weekly in the store's Service Scorecard.

**Holiday and promotional event periods:** During anticipated high-traffic events (Memorial Day, 4th of July, Black Friday, Spring Outdoor Event, etc.), the Store Manager must pre-schedule sufficient cashier coverage and open all available registers 30 minutes before door-opening.

### 3.4 Phone Response Standard

**Standard:** Every incoming customer call must be answered within 3 rings.

**Procedure:**

1. All store phones — main line, department extensions, and Pro Desk — must be answered within 3 rings (approximately 12 seconds).
2. Use the mandatory standard greeting:

   > "Thank you for calling BuildRight [Store #], this is [First Name], how may I help you?"

   Example: "Thank you for calling BuildRight 1427, this is Maria, how may I help you?"

3. If the call must be transferred:
   - Explain to the customer: "I'm going to connect you with [Department/Person] who can help you with that. May I put you on a brief hold?"
   - Wait for the customer's acknowledgment before placing on hold.
   - Never leave a customer on hold for more than 90 seconds without checking back: "I apologize for the wait — I'm still working on getting you to the right person. Would you like to keep holding, or may I take a message?"
   - Provide the caller with the direct extension before transferring whenever possible.
4. If the customer needs to leave a voicemail, return the call within 1 hour during store hours.
5. The after-hours voicemail greeting must include:
   - Store hours
   - Website address for online orders
   - Emergency Pro Desk number
   - Expected callback time

**Call center metrics:**

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Answer within 3 rings | ≥ 95% of calls |
| Average handle time | ≤ 5 minutes |
| First-call resolution | ≥ 85% |
| Abandonment rate | ≤ 5% |
| Transfer rate | ≤ 12% |

### 3.5 Email Response Standard

**Standard:** All customer emails must receive a substantive response within 4 business hours.

**Procedure:**

1. Customer emails are routed through the BRHI Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system to the appropriate store or contact center queue.
2. An acknowledgment auto-reply is sent within 2 minutes confirming receipt and providing a case number.
3. A substantive response — one that addresses the customer's specific concern or question, not merely a template acknowledgment — must be sent within 4 business hours (8:00 AM – 6:00 PM local store time, Monday through Saturday; 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM Sunday).
4. Emails received outside of business hours start the 4-hour clock at 8:00 AM the next business day.
5. If a full resolution requires more than 4 hours, the responding associate must send an interim update: "I'm working on your request regarding [topic]. I expect to have a complete answer for you by [specific time]. Your case number is [number]."
6. All email responses must include:
   - Associate's first name and title
   - Store number (if applicable)
   - Case number
   - Direct phone number for follow-up
7. The CRM automatically flags any email approaching the 4-hour threshold and escalates to the Customer Service Supervisor.

### 3.6 Chat Response Standard

**Standard:** Live chat initiated by a customer must receive an initial response within 30 seconds.

**Procedure:**

1. When a customer initiates a chat session on buildright.com or the BRHI mobile app, the system assigns the session to the next available Contact Center associate.
2. The initial response must be sent within 30 seconds of the customer's first message.
3. Subsequent responses during an active session must be sent within 45 seconds.
4. If the queue exceeds 10 waiting sessions, the system displays a message: "We're experiencing high demand. Expected wait time: [X] minutes. You can also call us at 1-800-BRHI-HELP."
5. Chat sessions must not exceed 15 minutes. If the issue requires extended support, offer to continue via phone or email: "I want to make sure we get this fully resolved for you. Would it be helpful if I called you, or would you prefer I follow up by email?"
6. After the session, the customer receives a brief satisfaction survey (1–5 stars) and a transcript via email.
7. Chat associates may handle a maximum of 2 simultaneous chat sessions.

**Chat metrics:**

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Initial response within 30 seconds | ≥ 95% |
| Average chat duration | ≤ 8 minutes |
| Customer satisfaction (post-chat) | ≥ 4.2 / 5.0 |
| First-contact resolution | ≥ 80% |

### 3.7 Pro Desk Response Standard

**Standard:** Every Pro customer who approaches or calls the Pro Desk must be acknowledged within 30 seconds and served by a dedicated Pro Desk associate within 1 minute.

**Procedure:**

1. The Pro Desk must be staffed with at least one trained Pro Desk associate during all Pro Desk operating hours (see SOP-001, Section 3.1).
2. When a Pro customer approaches the desk, the associate must:
   - Make eye contact and greet by name when possible: "Good morning, Mr. Gonzalez! What can I help you with today?"
   - If assisting another customer, acknowledge within 30 seconds: "I'll be right with you."
3. Pro customers must never be directed to a general checkout line for account inquiries, returns, or order pickups.
4. During peak hours (6:00 AM – 9:00 AM and 4:00 PM – 6:00 PM), a second Pro Desk associate or a trained backup must be available.
5. Pro Desk phone calls follow the same 3-ring standard with a Pro-specific greeting:

   > "Thank you for calling BuildRight [Store #] Pro Desk, this is [Name], how may I help you today?"

## 4. Customer Interaction Protocol

Every customer interaction — whether in-store, on the phone, via email, or through chat — follows a consistent five-step framework.

### 4.1 The Five-Step Interaction Framework

**Step 1: Greeting**

- Make the customer feel welcome within the first 5 seconds of contact.
- In-store: Apply the 10-Foot Rule (see Section 3.1). Smile, eye contact, verbal welcome.
- Phone/chat: Use the standard greeting (see Sections 3.4 and 3.6).
- Use the customer's name when known (e.g., loyalty program lookup, Pro account).

**Step 2: Needs Assessment**

- Ask open-ended questions to understand the full scope of the customer's project or need.
- Examples of effective open-ended questions:
  - "What kind of project are you working on?"
  - "Tell me a little about what you're trying to accomplish."
  - "What does the space look like where this will be installed?"
  - "Have you used this type of product before, or is this your first time?"
- Listen actively. Do not interrupt. Take notes if the request is complex.
- Avoid closed-ended (yes/no) questions at this stage. Instead of "Do you need help?", ask "What can I help you find today?"

**Step 3: Solution**

- Based on the needs assessment, recommend the best product or service for the customer's specific situation.
- Offer options at multiple price points when available: "We have three options that would work — let me show you the differences."
- Explain features and benefits in plain language. Avoid jargon unless speaking with an experienced tradesperson.
- If the exact product is unavailable, proactively offer alternatives and, if needed, check other store locations or buildright.com.
- Walk the customer to the product (see Section 3.2, Walk, Don't Point).

**Step 4: Add-on / Suggestion**

- Before concluding the interaction, suggest related items the customer may need to complete their project.
- Frame suggestions as helpful, not pushy:
  - "Since you're installing a faucet, you'll also need supply lines and Teflon tape — they're right here."
  - "For that deck project, have you picked up deck screws and joist hangers?"
  - "We have a protective finish that works great with that stain — would you like me to show you?"
- Suggest services when relevant:
  - "We offer delivery for large items — would that be helpful?"
  - "Our Tool Rental department has a floor sander if you don't want to buy one."
- Project consultation approach: For customers describing multi-step projects, offer to walk through the full project together and compile a complete material list. This is expected for any project estimated at $500+.

**Step 5: Thank You**

- End every interaction with a sincere thank you.
- In-store: "Thank you for shopping BuildRight! If you need anything else, I'm here until [time] or just ask any associate."
- Phone: "Thank you for calling BuildRight. Have a great day!"
- Chat: "Thank you for chatting with BuildRight today. Is there anything else I can help with before we wrap up?"
- When appropriate, invite the customer to return: "We'd love to see you back when you start the next phase of your project."

### 4.2 Product Knowledge Requirements

**Standard:** Every associate must complete product knowledge training for their assigned department within 30 calendar days of their start date.

**Requirements:**

1. **New Associate Product Knowledge Training (within 30 days):**
   - Complete the BRHI Product Foundations e-learning module for the assigned department (approximately 4–6 hours, completed in segments during the first 30 days).
   - Pass the Department Product Knowledge Assessment with a score of 80% or higher.
   - Shadow an experienced associate in the department for a minimum of 16 hours.
   - Demonstrate competency in the top 50 SKUs for the department (location, features, common uses, pricing tier).
   - Be observed by a Department Supervisor completing at least 3 full customer interactions using the Five-Step Framework.

2. **Ongoing Product Knowledge:**
   - Complete quarterly product knowledge updates (see Section 12, Service Training).
   - Attend vendor-sponsored product training sessions when scheduled.
   - Review the weekly "New & Noteworthy" product bulletin distributed every Monday via the Associate App.

3. **Cross-Department Awareness:**
   - All associates must know the location of every major department and key service areas (Restrooms, Returns, Pro Desk, Tool Rental, Garden Center, Break Room for associates).
   - All associates must be able to explain the store layout to a customer and physically escort them (see Section 3.2).

4. **When You Don't Know the Answer:**
   - Never guess, bluff, or provide inaccurate information.
   - Say: "That's a great question — let me find the exact answer for you."
   - Use the Associate App knowledge base, product specification sheets, or contact the vendor's technical support line.
   - Involve a department specialist, Department Supervisor, or manager when the question exceeds your expertise.
   - Follow up with the customer if the answer requires research: "I want to make sure I give you accurate information. May I have your phone number? I'll call you back within [timeframe]."

### 4.3 Upselling and Cross-Selling Guidelines

Upselling (encouraging a higher-quality or higher-priced item) and cross-selling (suggesting complementary products) are natural extensions of good service when done with the customer's best interest in mind.

**Principles:**

1. **Start from the customer's need.** Only suggest products or upgrades that genuinely benefit the customer's project.
2. **Educate, don't pressure.** Explain the value difference: "This model costs $20 more, but it has a 10-year warranty instead of 5 and a brushless motor that lasts significantly longer."
3. **Use the project consultation approach.** Understanding the full project naturally reveals cross-sell opportunities — not as a sales tactic but as genuine project support.
4. **Offer protection plans and services when relevant:** Extended warranties on power tools over $100, appliance delivery and installation, paint color matching, and project consultation.
5. **Pro customers:** Focus on time savings, volume pricing, and job-site delivery. Pro customers value efficiency above all.

**What NOT to do:**

- Never suggest a product the customer clearly does not need.
- Never use high-pressure tactics, urgency language ("This is the last one!"), or manipulative framing.
- Never criticize a customer's product choice. If they selected an inappropriate product, educate gently: "That's a popular option. Can I ask about your project? I just want to make sure it will perform the way you need it to."

## 5. Returns and Exchanges

BRHI's return policy is designed to be fair, generous, and hassle-free for legitimate customers while protecting the company from fraud and abuse.

### 5.1 General Return Policy

| Category | Return Window | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Standard merchandise (with receipt) | 90 days from purchase | Unused, in original packaging; used items accepted if defective |
| BuildRight branded products | 365 days from purchase | Satisfaction guaranteed; packaging not required |
| Seasonal items | 90 days from purchase or end of season (whichever is longer) | Unused, in sellable condition |
| Plants and live goods | 1 year from purchase (BuildRight Plant Guarantee) | Receipt or plant receipt; replacement or store credit |
| Power tools | 90 days | With all accessories, case, and documentation |
| Major appliances | 48 hours after delivery | Unused, undamaged, in original packaging |
| Paint | 30 days | Custom-tinted paint non-returnable unless defective |
| Gift cards | Non-returnable | No exceptions |
| Hazardous materials | Non-returnable | See Section 5.6 |

**Receipt requirements:**

- **With receipt or receipt lookup:** Full refund to original tender.
- Receipt lookup is available via credit/debit card, phone number, BRHI loyalty account, or transaction number.
- Returns without a receipt follow the no-receipt return procedure (Section 5.2).

### 5.2 No-Receipt Returns

**Policy:** Customers who do not have a receipt and whose transaction cannot be located through receipt lookup may receive store credit at the lowest sale price in the last 90 days.

**Procedure:**

1. Attempt receipt lookup using all available methods: credit/debit card, phone number, loyalty account, transaction number, and date range.
2. If the transaction cannot be located:
   - Request a valid, government-issued photo ID (driver's license, state ID, passport, or military ID).
   - Enter the ID information into the POS system. The system tracks no-receipt returns by ID.
   - The system will display the lowest sale price in the last 90 days for the item(s).
   - Issue a BRHI Store Credit Card in the calculated amount.
3. **Limits:** A maximum of 3 no-receipt returns per customer per rolling 12-month period. The system enforces this automatically.
4. **Exceptions:** If the customer exceeds 3 no-receipt returns but insists the return is legitimate, escalate to a Supervisor. The Supervisor may override the limit with documented justification and Manager notification.
5. **Fraud prevention:** If the POS system flags a no-receipt return as suspicious (e.g., item not in BRHI's inventory system, serial number mismatch, or excessive frequency), the associate must involve the Loss Prevention team before processing.

### 5.3 Special Order Returns

**Policy:** Special order items may be returned subject to a 15% restocking fee.

**Procedure:**

1. Verify the special order in the POS system using the order number or customer account.
2. Inspect the item for damage. Special order items must be in unused, resalable condition.
3. Calculate the restocking fee: 15% of the original purchase price (excluding delivery and installation charges).
4. Inform the customer: "I can process this return. There is a 15% restocking fee of $[amount], which covers the cost of returning the item to our vendor. Your refund will be $[amount]."
5. The restocking fee may be waived at Store Manager discretion if:
   - BRHI made an error in the order (wrong item, wrong specifications).
   - The item arrived damaged due to BRHI or vendor handling.
   - The customer is a Pro Elite tier member and the fee is under $100.

### 5.4 Custom Products

**Policy:** Products that have been customized, personalized, cut to specification, or specially fabricated are non-returnable.

**Non-returnable custom products include:**

- Custom-blended paint colors (unless defective)
- Custom-cut glass, mirrors, or countertops
- Custom-fabricated blinds and window treatments
- Engraved or personalized items
- Custom cabinetry and vanity tops
- Custom-ordered appliances with non-standard finishes or configurations

**Exception:** If the custom product is defective, does not match the customer's order specifications, or was fabricated incorrectly by BRHI or the vendor, it must be re-made or refunded at no charge to the customer.

### 5.5 Appliance Returns

**Policy:** Major appliances may be returned within 48 hours of delivery, provided they are unused and undamaged.

**Appliance return procedure:**

1. The customer must contact the store or call 1-800-BRHI-HELP within 48 hours of the delivery time stamp.
2. The Appliance Specialist or Supervisor verifies the return reason:
   - Cosmetic damage discovered after delivery
   - Wrong model delivered
   - Appliance does not fit the designated space
   - Appliance is defective on first use
3. Schedule a return pickup within 5 business days. There is no pickup fee for qualifying returns.
4. The appliance must be in its original packaging and in the same condition as delivered. If original packaging is unavailable, the customer should use blankets/padding. BRHI delivery team will bring packaging materials if needed.
5. **Restocking fee:** No restocking fee is charged if the return is within 48 hours and the appliance is unused. If the appliance shows signs of use (scratches, dents from installation attempts, water line connected, etc.), a 15% restocking fee applies.
6. **Installation charges** are non-refundable once installation has been completed.
7. **Non-major appliances** (microwaves, range hoods, garbage disposals, etc.) follow the standard 90-day return policy.

### 5.6 Hazardous Materials

**Policy:** Hazardous materials are non-returnable due to safety and regulatory requirements.

**Non-returnable items include:**

- Flammable liquids (gasoline cans with fuel, solvents, adhesives with volatile organic compounds)
- Pesticides and herbicides (once opened)
- Pool chemicals
- Asphalt sealants and roofing compounds (once opened)
- Compressed gas cylinders (once filled)
- Batteries containing lead acid (once activated)
- Epoxy and two-part chemical adhesives (once mixed or opened)

**Exception:** Unopened hazardous materials in their original, sealed, and undamaged packaging may be returned within 30 days with a receipt. The associate must inspect the packaging for leaks, damage, or tampering before accepting the return.

**Safe handling:** Any return of hazardous materials must be processed by a trained associate wearing appropriate PPE (gloves, safety glasses). The item must be placed in the designated hazardous materials return bin in Receiving, not on the sales floor.

### 5.7 Pro Customer Returns

**Policy:** Pro customers have a streamlined return process at the Pro Desk.

**Procedure:**

1. Pro customers may return merchandise at the Pro Desk without going to the general Returns counter.
2. The Pro Desk associate looks up the transaction using the Pro account number — no physical receipt required for Pro account purchases.
3. Standard return windows apply, with the following Pro-specific enhancements:
   - **Pro Xtra VIP and Elite members:** Extended return window of 120 days on standard merchandise (vs. 90 days).
   - **Job-site returns:** If materials from a bulk delivery are unused and in sellable condition, they may be picked up during the next scheduled delivery to the job site, with credit issued to the Pro account.
   - **Pallet returns:** Full, unopened pallets may be returned within 90 days. Broken pallets or partial pallets are subject to a restocking fee.
   - **Invoice credits:** For Pro accounts with Net 30 billing, returns are processed as credits against the outstanding invoice rather than as cash refunds.
4. The Pro Desk associate documents the return in the CRM with the Pro account number, items returned, reason, and credit amount.

### 5.8 Refund Methods

Refunds are issued according to the following priority:

| Original Tender | Refund Method |
|---|---|
| Credit/debit card | Refund to the same card (processed immediately; may take 3–5 business days to appear on customer's statement) |
| Cash | Cash from register; Supervisor override required for amounts over $100 |
| BRHI gift card | Store credit on a new BRHI Store Credit Card |
| BRHI Store Credit Card | Store credit on a new BRHI Store Credit Card |
| Check | Corporate check mailed within 10 business days for amounts over $50; cash for $50 or less with Supervisor approval |
| Mixed tender | Refund to each tender type proportionally, starting with the most recent payment method |
| No receipt | BRHI Store Credit Card at lowest sale price |

**Store Credit Card rules:**

- Store Credit Cards never expire.
- Store Credit Cards may be used at any BRHI location or on buildright.com.
- Store Credit Cards cannot be redeemed for cash.
- Lost Store Credit Cards may be replaced with valid ID at the Customer Service desk.

### 5.9 Return Authorization Levels

| Authorization Level | Associate Role | Return Amount Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Cashier | $0 – $50 |
| Level 2 | Department Supervisor / Front End Supervisor | $50.01 – $200 |
| Level 3 | Assistant Store Manager | $200.01 – $1,000 |
| Level 4 | Store Manager | $1,000.01 and above |

**Authorization procedures:**

1. **Level 1 (Cashier, $0–$50):** Cashier may process any return of $50 or less without supervisory approval, provided the customer has a valid receipt or the transaction is found in receipt lookup.
2. **Level 2 (Supervisor, $50.01–$200):** Cashier requests Supervisor authorization via POS prompt. Supervisor reviews the return, verifies the item and receipt, and enters their credentials to approve.
3. **Level 3 (Assistant Store Manager, $200.01–$1,000):** Supervisor contacts the ASM on duty. The ASM must personally inspect the merchandise, review the receipt and customer ID, and approve the return in the POS system.
4. **Level 4 (Store Manager, $1,000+):** The ASM contacts the Store Manager. The Store Manager reviews all documentation, speaks with the customer, and approves the return. For returns exceeding $5,000, the Store Manager must also notify the District Manager via email within 24 hours.
5. **All returns of $200+** require a government-issued photo ID, which is recorded in the POS system.
6. **All returns of $500+** require a completed Return Authorization Form (available on the Associate App), which includes the customer's name, ID number, items returned, reason, and authorization signature.

## 6. Special Services

BRHI stores offer a range of special services that differentiate us from competitors and drive store traffic.

### 6.1 Tool Rental

**Overview:** BRHI offers a full inventory of professional-grade tools and equipment for rent by the hour, day, week, or month.

**Rental procedure:**

1. Customer presents a valid government-issued photo ID and a credit or debit card. The card is kept on file as a deposit.
2. The Tool Rental associate inspects the tool with the customer before rental, noting its condition on the Rental Agreement.
3. The associate explains safe operation, provides any required PPE (safety glasses, ear protection), and offers the optional Damage Waiver (10% of rental cost, limits customer liability to $100).
4. Rental periods:
   - Minimum: 4 hours
   - Half day: 4 hours
   - Full day: 24 hours
   - Weekly: 7 days
   - Monthly: 28 days
5. Late returns are charged at 1/4 of the daily rate per additional hour, capped at the daily rate.
6. Upon return, the Tool Rental associate inspects the tool for damage. Normal wear is expected. Excessive damage is charged to the card on file based on the repair cost estimate.
7. **Cleaning fee:** A $15 cleaning fee is applied if the tool is returned excessively dirty (concrete on mixers, paint on sprayers, etc.).

### 6.2 Key Cutting

**Service:** Duplication of standard residential and commercial keys, automotive keys (select models), and programmable smart keys.

**Procedure:**

1. Customer brings the original key to the Key Cutting station (located at the front of the Hardware department).
2. Associate selects the correct key blank by matching the original key to the blank chart.
3. The key is cut on the automated key cutting machine.
4. Associate tests the new key in the verification gauge.
5. **If the key does not work:** Recut at no charge. If the second cut also fails, refund the customer and suggest a locksmith.
6. Pricing:

   | Key Type | Price |
   |---|---|
   | Standard house key (Kwikset, Schlage) | $2.49 each |
   | Standard commercial key (Best, Falcon) | $3.99 each |
   | Specialty / high-security key blank | $6.99 – $12.99 |
   | Automotive key (non-transponder) | $5.99 |
   | Automotive key (transponder/chip) | $29.99 – $79.99 |
   | Smart key / key fob programming | $49.99 – $129.99 |

7. **Restricted keys** (marked "Do Not Duplicate") will not be cut unless the customer presents written authorization from the lock manufacturer or property owner.

### 6.3 Screen Repair

**Service:** Repair and replacement of window and door screens.

**Procedure:**

1. Customer brings the damaged screen or frame to the Special Services desk.
2. Associate measures the frame and assesses the damage.
3. Associate and customer select the screen material:

## 7. Order Fulfillment Services

**Standard:** BOPIS orders must be picked and staged for customer pickup within 2 hours of order confirmation during store hours.

**Procedure:**

   - Place items in a BRHI-branded bag or box with the customer's name and order number.

   - Customer presents a photo ID and the order confirmation (printed or on phone).
   - Associate verifies the order number and ID, then hands over the merchandise.
   - If the customer has items to return, process the return at the same time.
5. **Hold period:** BOPIS orders are held for 48 hours. If not picked up within 48 hours, the order is automatically canceled and the customer is refunded. The items are returned to the sales floor.

## 8. Complaint Handling

### 8.1 The LAST Method

Every customer complaint — whether in-person, by phone, email, or chat — is handled using the **LAST** method:

**L — Listen**

- Give the customer your full, undivided attention.
- Do not interrupt. Let them express their frustration completely.
- Maintain open body language (in-person): uncrossed arms, facing the customer, nodding to show understanding.
- On the phone or chat: Use verbal cues — "I understand," "I hear you," "Please go on."
- Take notes on the specific issue, names, dates, order numbers, and any other relevant details.
- If other customers are nearby, move the conversation to a quieter area: "I want to give this my full attention — would you mind stepping over here with me?"

**A — Apologize**

- Apologize sincerely, even if the complaint is not BRHI's fault. The apology is for the customer's negative experience, not necessarily an admission of error.
- Examples:
  - "I'm really sorry you've had this experience. That's not what we want for you."
  - "I apologize for the inconvenience. Let me see what I can do to make this right."
  - "I understand how frustrating that must be. I'm sorry."
- Do NOT say: "That's our policy," "There's nothing I can do," or "You should have..." Blaming the customer, another department, or a vendor in front of the customer is never acceptable.

**S — Solve**

- Take ownership of the problem. Even if it is not your department or your error, you own it until it is resolved.
- Propose a solution. If possible, offer options: "I can [Option A] or [Option B]. Which would you prefer?"
- If you cannot resolve the issue within your authority level, involve a Supervisor or Manager immediately. Do not make the customer repeat their story. Brief the Supervisor/Manager yourself and hand off the customer personally.
- Empowerment levels (see Section 8.2) define what you can offer without additional approval.
- If a resolution requires more than 24 hours, set a specific follow-up time: "I'm going to have our team look into this. I'll personally call you by [specific day and time] with an update."

**T — Thank**

- Thank the customer for bringing the issue to your attention.
- Examples:
  - "Thank you for letting us know about this. We appreciate the opportunity to make it right."
  - "Thank you for your patience while we worked through this."
- Confirm the resolution and ask: "Is there anything else I can do for you today?"
- If appropriate, provide the customer with your name and a direct way to reach you if the issue recurs.

### 8.2 Associate Empowerment Levels

BRHI empowers front-line associates and managers to resolve complaints immediately without bureaucratic delays. The following table defines the maximum value of goods, services, discounts, or courtesies each role may offer without seeking additional approval:

| Role | Empowerment Level | Maximum Value |
|---|---|---|
| Associate | Level 1 | Up to $50 |
| Department Supervisor | Level 2 | Up to $200 |
| Assistant Store Manager | Level 3 | Up to $1,000 |
| Store Manager | Level 4 | Full authority (no cap) |

**Examples of empowered actions:**

- **Associate (up to $50):** Offer a 10% discount on a damaged-item purchase, waive a cutting fee, provide a free small accessory (e.g., screws, tape) to compensate for an inconvenience, offer a $25 BRHI gift card for a significant wait time.
- **Supervisor (up to $200):** Issue a store credit for a defective product beyond the return window, waive a delivery fee, offer a free tool rental to compensate for a project delay, provide a $50–$200 BRHI gift card for a serious service failure.
- **ASM (up to $1,000):** Replace a high-value defective item at no charge, authorize a full project re-do (e.g., replace a miscut countertop), issue a significant store credit for a multi-point service failure.
- **Store Manager (full authority):** Resolve any complaint regardless of value, authorize a complete kitchen or bathroom re-do, issue any store credit or refund, make exceptions to any policy.

**Guidelines for using empowerment:**

1. Always try the simplest, most direct resolution first.
2. Document every empowerment use in the CRM system (see Section 8.4) within 1 hour of the interaction.
3. Empowerment is intended to make things right for the customer, not to cover up recurring problems. If you find yourself using empowerment frequently for the same type of issue, report it to your Supervisor so the root cause can be addressed.
4. Empowerment does not extend to health and safety exceptions, harassment situations, or legal matters. These must always involve the Store Manager and, when appropriate, the BRHI Legal or HR departments.

### 8.3 Escalation Path

When an issue cannot be resolved at the current level, follow this escalation path:

```
Customer Complaint
       │
       ▼
  Associate (Level 1)
       │
       ▼
  Department Supervisor (Level 2)
       │
       ▼
  Assistant Store Manager (Level 3)
       │
       ▼
  Store Manager (Level 4)
       │
       ▼
  District Manager
       │
       ▼
  Regional Vice President
       │
       ▼
  Corporate Customer Relations (1-800-BRHI-HELP)
       │
       ▼
  SVP Store Operations
```

**Escalation rules:**

1. Each level must attempt resolution before passing to the next. "Passing the buck" without attempting a solution is a violation of this SOP.
2. When escalating, the current associate briefs the next level verbally and provides the CRM case number so the receiving person has full context.
3. The customer must never be told "There's nothing more I can do." Instead: "I'd like to involve my [Supervisor/Manager] who has additional options. Let me get them for you right now."
4. **Immediate escalation to the Store Manager is required for:**
   - Threats of legal action
   - Threats of physical harm to associates or property
   - Allegations of discrimination or harassment
   - Media or social media threats
   - Injuries on BRHI premises
   - Any interaction involving law enforcement
5. **District Manager escalation** is required when:
   - A customer requests to speak with someone above the Store Manager.
   - The complaint involves the Store Manager directly.
   - The resolution requires resources beyond the store's control.
6. **Corporate Customer Relations** handles all complaints submitted through buildright.com, social media channels, or the corporate 800 number that cannot be resolved at the store level.

### 8.4 Documentation in CRM

**Standard:** Every customer complaint, compliment, and service recovery action must be documented in the BRHI CRM system.

**Procedure:**

1. The associate who receives the complaint creates a CRM case within 30 minutes of the interaction.
2. Required fields:
   - Customer name and contact information
   - Date, time, and channel (in-store, phone, email, chat)
   - Associate name and ID
   - Detailed description of the complaint
   - Products, orders, or services involved (SKU, order number, receipt number)
   - Resolution provided or status if pending
   - Empowerment level used and value
   - Follow-up actions required
   - Case category (Product Quality, Service Failure, Pricing Error, Delivery Issue, Returns, Other)
3. If the case is resolved during the initial interaction, the associate closes the case with the resolution details.
4. If the case requires follow-up, the associate assigns it to the appropriate owner with a due date.
5. **All open cases must be resolved within 5 business days.** Cases approaching the 5-day mark are automatically escalated to the Store Manager.

### 8.5 Follow-Up

**Standard:** Every customer complaint that is not resolved during the initial interaction must receive a follow-up contact within 24 hours.

**Procedure:**

1. The case owner contacts the customer within 24 hours by the customer's preferred method (phone, email, or text).
2. The follow-up includes:
   - An update on the status of the resolution.
   - A specific timeline for final resolution, if not yet complete.
   - An apology for the ongoing inconvenience.
   - The case owner's direct contact information for future questions.
3. If the issue is fully resolved, confirm with the customer: "I want to make sure everything has been taken care of to your satisfaction. Is there anything else I can help with?"
4. After the customer confirms satisfaction, close the CRM case.
5. If the customer is dissatisfied with the resolution, re-escalate to the next authority level (see Section 8.3).
6. **Post-resolution survey:** 48 hours after case closure, the CRM system sends the customer a brief satisfaction survey (1–5 rating + optional comment). Results are tracked in the store's Service Scorecard.

**Confidentiality:**

### 10.3 Online Review Management

**Standard:** All online reviews (Google, Yelp, Facebook, BBB, and buildright.com) are monitored daily, and responses are posted within 24 hours.

**Procedure:**

1. The BRHI Social Media and Review Management team monitors all review platforms using a centralized review management tool.
2. **Positive reviews (4–5 stars):**
   - Respond within 24 hours with a personalized thank you.
   - Template: "Thank you, [Name]! We're glad to hear about your experience at BuildRight [Store #]. We'll share your kind words with the team. We hope to see you again soon!"
   - Personalize each response — reference specific details from the review.
3. **Neutral reviews (3 stars):**
   - Respond within 24 hours with an acknowledgment and an invitation to improve.
   - Template: "Thank you for your feedback, [Name]. We appreciate your business and take your comments seriously. We'd love the chance to make your next visit a 5-star experience. Please reach out to us at [store email] or [phone number] so we can learn more."
4. **Negative reviews (1–2 stars):**
   - Respond within 4 hours.
   - Acknowledge the issue, apologize, and invite offline resolution.
   - Template: "We're sorry to hear about your experience, [Name]. This is not the standard we hold ourselves to. We'd like to make this right. Please contact our Store Manager, [Name], at [phone/email] at your convenience. Your feedback helps us improve."
   - The Store Manager is notified immediately and is expected to attempt direct contact with the reviewer within 24 hours.
   - Document the review and resolution in the CRM system.
5. **Fake or fraudulent reviews:** Report to the platform for removal. Do not engage publicly. Document in the review management tool.

## 11. Accessibility and Inclusion

### 11.1 ADA Compliance

All BRHI stores must comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and applicable state and local accessibility regulations.

**Physical accessibility requirements:**

1. **Parking:** Accessible parking spaces near the main entrance, clearly marked with signs and painted lines, meeting ADA dimensions (van-accessible spaces included).
2. **Entrances:** Automatic doors at the main entrance and Pro entrance. Doors must open with less than 5 pounds of force if manual.
3. **Aisles:** Minimum 36-inch clear width in all aisles (42 inches preferred). Merchandise displays must not encroach on aisle width.
4. **Checkout:** At least one accessible checkout lane with a maximum counter height of 36 inches and a clear width of 36 inches.
5. **Restrooms:** ADA-compliant restrooms with grab bars, accessible sinks, and minimum turning radius.
6. **Pro Desk:** Accessible counter section at the Pro Desk.
7. **Service animals:** Service animals (as defined by the ADA) are welcome in all areas of the store. Associates may ask: "Is this a service animal?" and "What task is it trained to perform?" but may NOT ask about the customer's disability or require documentation for the animal.
8. **Signage:** ADA-compliant signage with raised characters and Braille at permanent rooms and spaces (restrooms, exits, elevators).

### 11.2 Bilingual Services

**Standard:** BRHI provides Spanish-language support in all stores.

**Requirements:**

1. At least 2 Spanish-speaking associates must be scheduled during every shift at stores where the local demographic data indicates a Hispanic population of 10% or greater.
2. Spanish-language signage is available at key service points: entrances, checkout, returns, Pro Desk, and restrooms.
3. Spanish-language versions of key documents are available: return policy, Special Order Agreement, Tool Rental Agreement, Pro Xtra enrollment form, and delivery terms.
4. Spanish-language phone menu option is available on all store phone systems: "Para español, oprima el dos."
5. Associates who are fluent in Spanish and complete the Bilingual Service Certification receive a $0.50/hour premium.
6. Spanish-language product labels and signage are maintained in the following departments: Paint, Lawn & Garden, Building Materials, and Plumbing.

### 11.3 Assistive Shopping Services

**Standard:** BRHI provides personalized shopping assistance to customers who request it.

**Services:**

1. **Personal shopper:** Any customer may request a personal shopper at the Customer Service desk. A dedicated associate will accompany the customer through the store, help locate and select products, and assist with checkout and loading. No minimum purchase required. No fee.
2. **Large-print and Braille materials:** Store directories, return policies, and key department guides are available in large print (18-point font) at the Customer Service desk. Braille versions are available upon request with 2 business days' notice.
3. **Hearing assistance:** Portable hearing amplification devices are available at the Customer Service desk and Pro Desk. Associates facing hearing-impaired customers should speak clearly, face the customer directly, and be prepared to communicate in writing if needed.
4. **Vision assistance:** Associates offer to read product labels, prices, and instructions to customers with visual impairments.
5. **Cognitive accessibility:** Associates use plain, simple language and step-by-step explanations for customers who may need additional support understanding product information or instructions.

### 11.4 Motorized Carts

**Standard:** Each BRHI store must maintain a minimum of 3 motorized shopping carts for customer use.

**Procedures:**

1. Motorized carts are located just inside the main entrance, near the Customer Service desk.
2. Carts are available on a first-come, first-served basis. No reservation system.
3. **Condition check:** The opening shift associate tests all motorized carts daily before store opening. Carts with dead batteries, damaged steering, or other malfunctions are tagged "Out of Service" and reported to the Facilities team immediately.
4. **Charging:** Carts must be plugged into their charging stations every night. The closing shift associate is responsible for ensuring all carts are charging.
5. **Usage guidelines:**
   - Customers must be 18+ to operate a motorized cart.
   - Maximum weight capacity: 350 pounds.
   - Speed is governed at 2.5 mph (walking speed).
   - Carts are for in-store use only — not in the parking lot or Garden Center outdoor area.
6. **Incident protocol:** If a customer is involved in a motorized cart incident (collision, tip, fall), the Store Manager must be notified immediately, an incident report must be filed, and the customer must be offered first aid. The cart must be inspected before returning to service.

## 12. Service Training

### 12.1 New Hire Orientation — Customer Service Module

**Standard:** All new associates complete an 8-hour Customer Service orientation within their first week of employment.

**Agenda:**

| Hour | Topic |
|---|---|
| Hour 1 | BRHI Culture and Values — "The Customer Is Why We're Here" |
| Hour 2 | The Five-Step Interaction Framework — classroom instruction and role-play |
| Hour 3 | The 10-Foot Rule, Walk Don't Point, and checkout standards |
| Hour 4 | Phone, email, and chat service standards |
| Hour 5 | Returns and exchanges policies — hands-on POS practice |
| Hour 6 | The LAST Method for complaint handling — role-play scenarios |
| Hour 7 | Product knowledge fundamentals, Associate App training, and store navigation |
| Hour 8 | Assessment — written quiz (minimum passing score: 80%) and observed role-play |

**Completion requirements:**

- Attend all 8 hours (no partial credit).
- Pass the written assessment with 80% or higher.
- Successfully complete 3 observed role-play scenarios (greeting, needs assessment, complaint handling).
- Receive a satisfactory rating from the training facilitator.

Associates who do not pass the assessment on the first attempt are given one additional attempt within 48 hours. If the second attempt is unsuccessful, the Store Manager and HR are notified for additional support planning.

### 12.2 Annual Service Training

**Standard:** All associates complete 4 hours of annual customer service refresher training.

**Schedule:** Annual training is delivered in January and February, with all associates completing it by February 28 each year.

**Content (updated annually):**

| Segment | Duration | Topic |
|---|---|---|
| Segment 1 | 60 minutes | Service standards review and policy updates |
| Segment 2 | 60 minutes | Advanced complaint handling — new scenarios and role-play |
| Segment 3 | 60 minutes | Product knowledge update — new products and categories |
| Segment 4 | 60 minutes | Technology training — POS updates, Associate App features, CRM enhancements |

**Delivery format:** Blended learning — 2 hours of e-learning modules (completed on the Associate App or a store kiosk) and 2 hours of instructor-led group session (facilitated by the ASM or a designated Service Champion).

### 12.3 Quarterly Product Knowledge Training

**Standard:** All associates complete department-specific product knowledge training each quarter.

**Schedule:**

| Quarter | Focus Areas |
|---|---|
| Q1 (January–March) | Spring outdoor, lawn & garden, power tools |
| Q2 (April–June) | Building materials, decking, paint, plumbing |
| Q3 (July–September) | HVAC, insulation, storage & organization, back-to-school |
| Q4 (October–December) | Holiday décor, heating, indoor projects, holiday selling |

**Format:**

- 60-minute e-learning module assigned on the 1st of each quarter month.
- 15-question assessment (minimum passing score: 80%).
- 30-minute vendor-led product demonstration (scheduled in-store, optional but encouraged).
- Associates who score below 80% receive additional one-on-one coaching from their Department Supervisor.

### 12.4 Pro Customer Training

**Standard:** Pro Desk associates and designated Pro support associates must complete Pro Customer Training.

**Requirements:**

1. **Pro Certification (required before working the Pro Desk independently):**
   - 4-hour classroom / e-learning module covering:
     - Pro Xtra program tiers, benefits, and enrollment
     - Commercial credit application and account management
     - Pro-specific pricing, bulk orders, and Bid Desk procedures
     - Job-site delivery scheduling and logistics
     - Pro returns and exchanges
     - Understanding contractor terminology and project workflows
   - Pass the Pro Certification Assessment with a score of 85% or higher.
   - Shadow a certified Pro Desk associate for 20 hours.
2. **Pro Advanced Training (annual):**
   - 2-hour refresher covering program updates, new Pro benefits, and advanced scenario role-play.
3. **Pro App Training:**
   - All Pro Desk associates must demonstrate proficiency in the Pro App, including order placement, delivery tracking, account management, and Bid Desk functions.

### 12.5 Conflict Resolution Training

**Standard:** All associates complete annual conflict resolution training.

**Content:**

1. De-escalation techniques:
   - Maintaining calm tone and body language
   - Using the customer's name
   - Validating emotions without agreeing with accusations
   - Offering choices rather than ultimatums
   - Knowing when to step away and involve a manager
2. Recognizing and responding to:
   - Verbally abusive customers
   - Threatening or intimidating behavior
   - Discrimination or harassment from customers toward associates
   - Intoxicated customers
   - Customers experiencing a mental health crisis
3. Associate safety protocols:
   - When to call the Store Manager
   - When to call Loss Prevention
   - When to call 911
   - Safe exit strategies
4. Post-incident support:
   - Filing an incident report
   - Debriefing with a manager
   - Accessing BRHI's Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

**Delivery:** 2-hour instructor-led session (mandatory in-person attendance; virtual attendance permitted only with Store Manager approval). Conducted annually in Q4.

### 12.6 Service Awards

BRHI recognizes outstanding customer service through the following award programs:

**Monthly — Service Star**

- One associate per store per month is recognized as the Service Star.
- Selection criteria: Customer commendations (verbal, email, survey comments), mystery shopper scores, POS transaction ratings, and peer nominations.
- Award: $50 BRHI gift card, "Service Star" pin for name badge, photo on the store's Service Recognition Board, mention in the monthly store newsletter.
- Nominations are reviewed by the Store Manager and ASM team. Winners are announced on the first Monday of the following month.

**Quarterly — District Service Champion**

- One associate per district per quarter is selected from the pool of monthly Service Stars.
- Selection criteria: Consistency of excellent service over the quarter, impact on store NPS and CSAT scores, customer testimonials, and leadership among peers.
- Award: $250 bonus, certificate, recognition at the quarterly district meeting, featured in the BRHI internal newsletter.

**Annual — BRHI Customer Service Excellence Award**

- The highest customer service honor at BRHI.
- One associate per region (approximately 20 winners company-wide) is selected annually.
- Selection criteria: Year-long performance metrics (NPS impact, mystery shopper scores, customer commendations, CRM resolution rates), peer and manager evaluations, demonstration of BRHI values.
- Award: $1,000 bonus, engraved plaque, invitation to the annual BRHI Leadership Summit (all-expenses-paid trip), featured in the BRHI Annual Report and on the company intranet.

**Other recognitions:**

- **Five-Star Feedback:** When a customer names a specific associate in a positive survey response, the associate receives a "Five-Star Feedback" card from the Store Manager and a $5 coffee voucher.
- **Pro Desk MVP:** Monthly recognition for the Pro Desk associate with the highest Pro customer satisfaction scores. Award: $75 BRHI gift card.
- **Team Service Award:** Quarterly recognition for the department with the highest overall CSAT score. Award: Department pizza party and $200 department budget allocation.
