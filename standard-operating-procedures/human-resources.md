## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes uniform policies, processes, and controls governing Human Resources operations at BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. ("BRHI," "the Company"). It ensures consistent, lawful, and equitable treatment of all applicants and team members while protecting the Company from regulatory risk and supporting its strategic workforce objectives.

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to:

- All BRHI locations (retail stores, distribution centers, corporate office, and field operations)
- All workforce categories: full-time, part-time, seasonal, temporary, and contract workers engaged through BRHI payroll
- Independent contractors and staffing-agency workers to the extent specified in individual sections
- All members of management who carry out HR-related functions

### 1.3 Authority and Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Director of Human Resources** | Overall SOP ownership, interpretation, and enforcement |
| **HR Business Partners** | Day-to-day execution, coaching, compliance monitoring |
| **Store Managers / Department Heads** | Frontline workforce management within their units |
| **Legal Counsel** | Review of terminations, accommodations, investigations, and compliance matters |
| **Payroll Manager** | Wage calculations, timekeeping administration, tax compliance |

### 1.4 Related Documents

- BRHI Employee Handbook (current edition)
- BRHI-SOP-001 Store Operations
- BRHI-SOP-005 Loss Prevention
- BRHI Compensation Structure Manual
- BRHI Benefits Summary Plan Descriptions

## 2. Recruitment and Hiring

### 2.1 Workforce Planning

#### 2.1.1 Annual Workforce Plan

The Director of Human Resources shall produce an annual workforce plan no later than November 1 of each year for the following calendar year. The plan must include:

- Headcount by department, location, and employment status (FT/PT/seasonal)
- Projected turnover rates (benchmarked against trailing 12-month actuals)
- Budgeted labor cost per location
- Diversity hiring goals aligned with the Company's EEO-1 reporting categories
- Strategic hiring needs tied to the Company's annual business plan

#### 2.1.2 Seasonal Staffing

BRHI experiences predictable demand increases during spring and summer home-improvement seasons. The following seasonal staffing protocol applies:

| Period | Volume Increase | Action Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| **Spring Surge (March–June)** | +30% above baseline headcount | Seasonal requisitions submitted by January 15; postings active by February 1 |
| **Summer Peak (June–August)** | +15% above baseline headcount | Supplemental requisitions submitted by May 1 |
| **Holiday Season (November–December)** | +10% above baseline headcount | Seasonal requisitions submitted by September 15 |

Seasonal employees hired under these plans shall be classified as temporary with a defined end date not to exceed 180 days unless converted to regular status through the standard hiring process.

### 2.2 Job Requisition Process

#### 2.2.1 Requisition Submission

All open positions require an approved Job Requisition Form (JRF) before any recruitment activity begins.

1. **Hiring manager** completes the JRF in the HRIS, including: position title, grade, FLSA classification, department, location, business justification, desired start date, and approved salary range.
2. **Department Head** reviews and approves the JRF.
3. **HR Business Partner** validates against the annual workforce plan and confirms budget allocation with Finance.
4. **Director of HR** gives final approval for any new positions not included in the annual plan or any positions at Grade 10 or above.
5. Upon approval, the HRBP creates the job posting within 2 business days.

#### 2.2.2 Job Description Requirements

Every requisition must reference a current, reviewed job description that includes:

- Essential functions (with or without reasonable accommodation)
- Physical requirements and working conditions
- Minimum qualifications (education, experience, licensure)
- Preferred qualifications
- FLSA exemption classification and rationale
- Salary grade and range

Job descriptions must be reviewed and updated at least annually or whenever significant duties change.

### 2.3 Job Posting

#### 2.3.1 Internal Posting Period

All open positions shall be posted internally for a minimum of **5 calendar days** before external advertising begins.

- Internal postings are displayed on the BRHI intranet, break-room bulletin boards, and distributed via company email.
- Current employees who have completed at least 6 months of continuous service and are in good standing (no active PIP or performance rating below 2) are eligible to apply.
- Internal applicants must submit a Transfer Request Form through the HRIS within the posting period.

#### 2.3.2 External Advertising

After the internal posting period closes, the position may be advertised externally through:

- BRHI careers website
- Job boards (Indeed, LinkedIn, industry-specific platforms)
- Staffing agencies (for temporary or hard-to-fill positions, with Director of HR approval)
- Community organizations and educational institutions for entry-level roles
- Employee referral program — current employees receive a $500 referral bonus for hires who complete 90 days of employment ($1,000 for Grade 10+ positions)

#### 2.3.3 Diversity and Inclusion

All job postings must include BRHI's Equal Employment Opportunity statement. Recruiting sources must be reviewed quarterly to ensure diverse candidate pipelines. Hiring managers are expected to present diverse interview slates (minimum two candidates from underrepresented groups for positions at Grade 8 and above).

### 2.4 Interview Process

#### 2.4.1 Resume Screening

The HRBP and hiring manager jointly screen applications within 5 business days of the posting close date using the following criteria:

- Meets minimum qualifications: advance to phone screen
- Meets preferred qualifications: priority scheduling
- Does not meet minimum qualifications: disposition with respectful rejection letter within 3 business days

#### 2.4.2 Phone Screen

A 20–30 minute phone screen is conducted by the HRBP for all qualified candidates. The phone screen evaluates:

- Salary expectations relative to the approved range
- Availability and scheduling compatibility
- Basic qualifications verification
- Interest in and understanding of the role
- Authorization to work in the United States

Candidates who pass the phone screen are advanced to the in-person or video interview stage.

#### 2.4.3 Structured Interview — First Round

All interviews must use BRHI's Structured Interview Guide, which includes:

- **Behavioral questions** tied to the position's core competencies (minimum 5 questions)
- **Situational questions** relevant to the role (minimum 2 questions)
- **Technical or skills assessment** where applicable (e.g., for trades positions, a hands-on practical evaluation)
- **Consistent scoring rubric** — each question is rated 1–5 using the following scale:

| Score | Rating | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Unsatisfactory | Response shows no relevant experience or competency |
| 2 | Below Expectations | Response shows limited relevance; significant gaps |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Response adequately demonstrates the competency |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Response demonstrates strong competency with examples |
| 5 | Exceptional | Response demonstrates outstanding mastery with measurable impact |

#### 2.4.4 Panel Interviews — Manager and Above

For all positions at Grade 10 (Manager) and above, a **panel interview** is required:

- Panel must include a minimum of 3 interviewers: the hiring manager, one peer, and one HR representative.
- At least one panelist must be trained in BRHI's Interviewing and Selection Workshop (completed within the past 24 months).
- Each panelist independently completes an Interview Scorecard before any discussion of the candidate.
- Panelists convene to share scores, discuss discrepancies exceeding 2 points on any competency, and reach consensus.
- The consensus score and written justification are documented in the HRIS.

#### 2.4.5 Interview Documentation

All interview notes, scorecards, and evaluations must be uploaded to the candidate's HRIS record within 2 business days of the interview. Notes must focus on job-related observations only. Subjective or discriminatory commentary is prohibited and will be subject to disciplinary action.

### 2.5 Background Checks

#### 2.5.1 Authorization and Timing

Background checks are initiated **only after** a conditional offer of employment has been extended and the candidate has signed the Background Check Authorization and Disclosure Form. Background checks are conducted through BRHI's contracted third-party Consumer Reporting Agency (CRA).

#### 2.5.2 Scope of Background Check

| Check Type | Scope | Disqualifying Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| **Criminal History** | 7-year lookback in all jurisdictions where the candidate has resided | Any felony conviction involving violence, theft, fraud, or sexual offenses. Other offenses evaluated on a case-by-case basis using BRHI's Individualized Assessment Matrix (nature of offense, time elapsed, nature of job). |
| **Employment Verification** | 5-year lookback; previous 3 employers or most recent 5 years, whichever is greater | Falsification of employment history on application or resume; undisclosed termination for cause. |
| **Education Verification** | Highest degree or certification claimed | Falsification of any degree, certification, or credential. |
| **Drug Screen** | 10-panel urine test conducted at a certified laboratory | Any non-negative result. Positive results are reviewed by a Medical Review Officer (MRO). Candidates may request a retest within 72 hours of MRO notification at their own expense. |
| **Motor Vehicle Record** | Required only for positions involving operation of company vehicles | Any DUI/DWI within the past 5 years; 3 or more moving violations within the past 3 years. |

#### 2.5.3 Adverse Action Process

If the background check reveals information that may disqualify the candidate:

1. The HRBP sends a **Pre-Adverse Action Notice** to the candidate via certified mail and email, including a copy of the report and a summary of rights under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA).
2. The candidate has **5 business days** to respond with additional information or dispute the findings.
3. After the response period, if the decision remains to withdraw the offer, the HRBP sends an **Adverse Action Notice** including the CRA's contact information, a statement that the CRA did not make the employment decision, and the candidate's right to obtain a free copy of the report and to dispute its accuracy.

### 2.6 Offer Process

#### 2.6.1 Verbal and Written Offers

1. The hiring manager extends a **verbal contingent offer** to the selected candidate, clearly stating that the offer is contingent upon satisfactory completion of the background check and drug screen.
2. Upon successful completion of all pre-employment checks, the HRBP generates a **written Offer Letter** through the HRIS within 2 business days. The offer letter must include:
   - Position title, department, reporting relationship, and FLSA classification
   - Starting salary or hourly rate and pay frequency (bi-weekly)
   - Scheduled start date and work location
   - Employment-at-will statement
   - Contingencies (if any remaining)
   - Signature lines for both the candidate and the HRBP

#### 2.6.2 E-Verify

BRHI participates in the E-Verify program as required by applicable federal and state law. The HRBP or designated representative must:

- Submit the candidate's Form I-9 information to E-Verify **no later than the third business day** after the start date.
- Inform the new hire of the E-Verify requirement and display the E-Verify Participation Poster and Right to Work Poster in English and Spanish in all locations.
- If E-Verify returns a Tentative Nonconfirmation (TNC), follow the prescribed E-Verify referral process and notify the employee in writing within the required timeframe.

#### 2.6.3 Form I-9 Compliance

- Section 1 of Form I-9 must be completed by the employee **no later than the first day of work**.
- Section 2 must be completed by an authorized BRHI representative **within 3 business days of the employee's start date**.
- Original documents must be examined in person; photocopies are not acceptable for verification.
- Re-verification is required when employment authorization documents expire. The HRBP must track expiration dates and initiate re-verification at least 90 days before expiration.

## 3. Onboarding Process

### 3.1 Pre-Start Checklist

The HRBP must complete the following **Pre-Start Checklist** items before the new hire's first day:

| # | Task | Responsible Party | Deadline |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Offer letter signed and returned | HRBP | 5 business days before start |
| 2 | Background check cleared | HRBP | 3 business days before start |
| 3 | Drug screen passed | HRBP | 3 business days before start |
| 4 | HRIS profile created with all required fields | HRBP | 2 business days before start |
| 5 | Payroll setup completed (direct deposit, W-4, state withholding) | Payroll | 2 business days before start |
| 6 | Benefits enrollment packet prepared | Benefits Coordinator | 2 business days before start |
| 7 | IT accounts provisioned (email, HRIS, timekeeping, systems access) | IT | 1 business day before start |
| 8 | Workspace / locker / uniform assigned | Hiring Manager | 1 business day before start |
| 9 | Name badge and access credentials issued | Security / Facilities | 1 business day before start |
| 10 | Buddy / mentor identified and notified | Hiring Manager | 3 business days before start |
| 11 | First-week schedule confirmed and communicated to new hire | Hiring Manager | 3 business days before start |

### 3.2 Day 1 Orientation (4 Hours)

All new hires must attend Day 1 Orientation, conducted by the HRBP or designated trainer. The session is scheduled from 8:00 AM to 12:00 PM (or 1:00 PM to 5:00 PM for afternoon starts) and covers:

| Time Block | Topic | Materials |
|---|---|---|
| **8:00–8:30** | Welcome and Company Overview | BRHI mission, vision, values, organizational chart, company history |
| **8:30–9:15** | Employment Policies and Handbook | Employee Handbook review and acknowledgment signature; at-will employment; anti-harassment and anti-discrimination policies; code of conduct; social media policy |
| **9:15–9:45** | Benefits Overview | Medical, dental, vision, 401(k), ESPP, EAP, tuition reimbursement — enrollment deadlines and procedures |
| **9:45–10:00** | Break | — |
| **10:00–10:30** | Safety and Compliance | Workplace safety, emergency procedures, OSHA reporting, PPE requirements (role-specific), injury reporting process |
| **10:30–11:00** | Timekeeping and Payroll | Electronic timekeeping system training, schedule access, meal/rest break policy, overtime policy, pay dates and pay stubs |
| **11:00–11:30** | IT Systems and Security | Email, HRIS self-service, timekeeping app, password policy, acceptable use policy, data privacy obligations |
| **11:30–12:00** | Introduction to Department | Hiring manager meets new hire for department tour, introductions, and immediate task assignments |

All orientation materials and signed acknowledgment forms are scanned and uploaded to the employee's HRIS personnel file by end of Day 1.

### 3.3 Week 1 — Department Integration

During the first week, the following activities must be completed:

- **Buddy Assignment**: The designated buddy meets with the new hire at least once daily during Week 1. The buddy is a tenured peer (minimum 1 year at BRHI) in the same or adjacent department who helps the new hire navigate culture, processes, and informal networks. Buddies receive a $50 stipend per new hire they support.
- **Department-Specific Training**: The hiring manager delivers or arranges role-specific training including equipment operation, safety procedures, product knowledge, customer interaction standards, and department workflows.
- **System Access Verification**: The hiring manager confirms the new hire has access to all required systems and tools.
- **Week 1 Check-In**: The hiring manager conducts a 30-minute one-on-one meeting on the last day of Week 1 to answer questions, address early concerns, and confirm training progress.

### 3.4 Day 30, 60, and 90 Check-Ins

| Milestone | Format | Participants | Focus Areas | Documentation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Day 30** | 30-minute one-on-one | New hire + Hiring Manager | Culture fit, training completion, early performance observations, workload comfort, any barriers to success | 30-Day Check-In Form uploaded to HRIS |
| **Day 60** | 45-minute one-on-one | New hire + Hiring Manager + HRBP | Progress against 90-day plan, skills development, goal refinement, feedback from buddy and peers | 60-Day Check-In Form uploaded to HRIS; any concerns escalated to Department Head |
| **Day 90** | Formal review meeting (1 hour) | New hire + Hiring Manager + HRBP | Comprehensive assessment against 90-day performance plan, decision on continued employment, establishment of annual performance goals, benefits enrollment confirmation | 90-Day Review Form uploaded to HRIS; rating of Meets Expectations (3) or above required for continued employment; below 3 triggers a PIP or separation |

Employees who do not achieve a minimum rating of 3 (Meets Expectations) at the 90-day review are subject to a Performance Improvement Plan or termination during the introductory period, at the Company's discretion.

## 4. Time and Attendance

### 4.1 Electronic Timekeeping

All non-exempt employees must record their work time using BRHI's approved electronic timekeeping system. The following rules apply:

- Employees must **clock in** at the start of each shift and **clock out** at the end of each shift.
- Employees must clock in/out for all meal periods.
- Clocking in more than **7 minutes** before or after a scheduled shift requires manager approval.
- **Buddy punching** (clocking in/out on behalf of another employee) is strictly prohibited and constitutes grounds for immediate termination of both parties.
- Timekeeping records are locked at the end of each pay period. Adjustments after locking require HRBP approval.
- Exempt employees are not required to clock in/out but must record leave usage (PTO, sick, holiday) in the system.

### 4.2 Schedule Posting

- Work schedules must be posted **at least 2 weeks (14 calendar days) in advance** on the department schedule board and in the HRIS self-service portal.
- Schedule changes within the 14-day window require the employee's consent. If an employee declines a schedule change within the 14-day window, no adverse action shall be taken.
- Shift swaps between employees must be submitted through the HRIS and approved by the supervisor at least 48 hours before the affected shift.
- Employees are expected to be at their workstations, ready to work, at the start of their scheduled shift.

### 4.3 Meal and Rest Breaks

| Break Type | Eligibility | Duration | Paid / Unpaid | Rules |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Meal Break** | Employees working 6 or more hours in a shift | 30 minutes | Unpaid | Must be taken no later than the end of the 5th consecutive hour of work. Employee must be fully relieved of duties. If duties are required during the meal break, it must be recorded as paid working time. |
| **Rest Break** | Employees working 4 or more hours | 15 minutes | Paid | One 15-minute rest break per 4-hour work period (or major portion thereof). Rest breaks should be taken approximately at the midpoint of each 4-hour period. |

Supervisors are responsible for monitoring break compliance. Missed or shortened meal breaks must be reported to the HRBP within the same pay period. Employees who are not provided required breaks must be compensated for the missed break time at their regular hourly rate.

### 4.4 Overtime

#### 4.4.1 Overtime Definition and Calculation

- **Overtime** is defined as any hours worked in excess of 40 hours in a single workweek.
- The BRHI workweek runs from **Sunday 12:00 AM through Saturday 11:59 PM**.
- Overtime is calculated weekly; there is no daily overtime obligation under current federal law, but BRHI monitors state and local requirements and will comply where daily overtime applies.
- Overtime is compensated at **1.5 times the employee's regular hourly rate**.

#### 4.4.2 Overtime Pre-Approval

All overtime must be pre-approved by the employee's direct supervisor before the overtime is worked. The approval workflow is:

1. Supervisor projects overtime need and communicates to affected employees.
2. Employee acknowledges the overtime assignment in the HRIS.
3. Supervisor approves the overtime entry.
4. If unapproved overtime is worked, it must still be paid, but the employee and supervisor will receive a counseling conversation. Repeated unapproved overtime may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 4.5 Attendance Point System

BRHI utilizes a point-based attendance management system to ensure fairness and consistency. Points are assessed as follows:

| Occurrence | Points | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| **Unexcused absence (full day)** | 1.0 | Failure to report to work without prior approval or acceptable documentation |
| **Unexcused absence (partial day, more than 2 hours)** | 0.5 | Arriving more than 2 hours late or leaving more than 2 hours early without prior approval |
| **Tardy (up to 2 hours)** | 0.5 | Arriving up to 2 hours late without prior approval |
| **Early departure (up to 2 hours)** | 0.5 | Leaving up to 2 hours early without prior approval |
| **No-call / No-show** | 3.0 | Failing to report to work and failing to notify the supervisor within 1 hour of the scheduled start time |
| **Unexcused absence on a scheduled holiday or the day before/after a holiday** | 2.0 | Absent without approved leave on these high-demand days |
| **Failure to call in for 3 consecutive days** | 6.0 | Treated as job abandonment and voluntary resignation |

#### 4.5.1 Point Duration and Reduction

- Points remain active in the employee's attendance record for **12 rolling months** from the date of the occurrence.
- An employee may request **point reduction** (0.5 points) for each rolling 90-day period of perfect attendance following a counseling conversation about attendance.
- An employee who reaches **6 active points** receives a written warning and mandatory counseling session with the HRBP.
- An employee who reaches **8 active points within any 12-month period** is subject to **termination**.

#### 4.5.2 Excused Absences (No Points Assessed)

The following absences are not assessed points when properly documented:

- Pre-approved PTO or sick leave
- FMLA leave (intermittent or continuous)
- Jury duty or subpoenaed court appearance
- Military duty
- Bereavement leave (up to 3 days for immediate family; 1 day for extended family)
- Accommodated absences under ADA
- Workers' compensation leave

## 5. Performance Management

### 5.1 Annual Review Cycle

- The performance review cycle runs on a **calendar year basis (January 1 – December 31)**.
- All performance reviews must be completed and submitted to HR by **February 15** of the following year.
- Merit increases tied to performance ratings take effect on **March 1**.
- New hires who have not completed 90 days by December 31 will receive their first formal review at their 90-day mark; subsequent reviews follow the standard calendar cycle.

### 5.2 Performance Rating Scale

BRHI uses a **1–5 rating scale** for all performance evaluations:

| Rating | Category | Description | Guideline Distribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| **5** | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds all expectations; demonstrates outstanding leadership, innovation, and measurable impact beyond the role. Role model for the organization. | Up to 5% of evaluated employees |
| **4** | Exceeds Expectations | Frequently exceeds expectations in key areas; demonstrates strong initiative, quality, and productivity. Reliable high performer. | 15–25% of evaluated employees |
| **3** | Meets Expectations | Consistently meets all job requirements and expectations; performs competently and reliably; achieves established goals. | 50–65% of evaluated employees |
| **2** | Below Expectations | Partially meets expectations; performance gaps exist in one or more key areas; requires improvement. | 10–15% of evaluated employees |
| **1** | Unsatisfactory | Fails to meet minimum expectations in multiple areas; immediate and significant improvement required to sustain employment. | Up to 5% of evaluated employees |

**Forced Distribution Guidance**: Managers are expected to differentiate performance. The total of ratings 4 and 5 should generally not exceed 30% of the team. The total of ratings 1 and 2 should generally not exceed 20% of the team. Exceptions require written justification and Director of HR approval.

### 5.3 Mid-Year Check-In

A **mid-year performance check-in** is conducted during July for all employees. This is a mandatory, documented conversation between the employee and their manager covering:

- Progress toward annual goals
- Any changes to goals or priorities
- Development needs and training plans
- Identification of performance concerns requiring intervention before year-end

The Mid-Year Check-In Form must be completed and uploaded to the HRIS by **July 31**. No formal rating is assigned at mid-year; however, any employee receiving an informal assessment of "at risk" (equivalent to a projected year-end rating of 1 or 2) must be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (see Section 5.4).

### 5.4 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) Process

#### 5.4.1 PIP Initiation

A PIP may be initiated at any time during the year when an employee's performance falls below expectations. The PIP process is:

1. **Manager documents** specific performance deficiencies with dates, examples, and impact on the business.
2. **HRBP reviews** the documentation, confirms the concerns are performance-based (not discriminatory or retaliatory), and ensures the employee has received prior verbal and/or written feedback.
3. **PIP meeting** is held with the employee, manager, and HRBP to discuss expectations, the improvement plan, timeline, and consequences of not meeting the plan.

#### 5.4.2 PIP Timeline and Structure

The PIP follows a structured 90-day process:

| Phase | Duration | Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1 — Assessment and Action Plan (Days 1–30)** | 30 days | Clear, measurable performance goals established; required training or resources identified; weekly check-in meetings between employee and manager documented. |
| **Phase 2 — Progress Review (Day 30)** | 1 day meeting | Formal 30-day review meeting with employee, manager, and HRBP. If satisfactory progress is demonstrated, PIP continues. If no meaningful improvement, the Company may proceed to termination with HR and Legal approval. |
| **Phase 3 — Continued Improvement (Days 31–60)** | 30 days | Continued monitoring with bi-weekly check-ins. Employee must demonstrate sustained improvement, not just temporary compliance. |
| **Phase 4 — Second Progress Review (Day 60)** | 1 day meeting | Formal 60-day review. If sustained improvement is demonstrated, PIP continues. If improvement has plateaued or regressed, the Company may proceed to termination with HR and Legal approval. |
| **Phase 5 — Sustained Performance (Days 61–90)** | 30 days | Final 30-day period to demonstrate fully sustained performance at the Meets Expectations (3) level. |
| **Phase 6 — Final Determination (Day 90)** | 1 day meeting | Final review. Three possible outcomes: (1) PIP closed successfully — employee returns to normal performance management; (2) PIP extended by up to 30 additional days (one-time only, requires Director of HR approval); (3) Employment terminated. |

#### 5.4.3 PIP Documentation

All PIP documents, meeting notes, check-in records, and outcome determinations are maintained in the employee's personnel file and HRIS. PIPs that result in termination are reviewed by Legal Counsel before action is taken.

### 5.5 360-Degree Feedback (Managers and Above)

All employees at **Grade 10 (Manager) and above** participate in a 360-degree feedback process as part of their annual review:

- **Reviewers**: Each participant selects (with manager approval) a minimum of 8 and a maximum of 12 reviewers: 2–3 direct reports, 2–3 peers, 1–2 cross-functional partners, and 1 external stakeholder (vendor or client) where applicable.
- **Confidentiality**: Responses are aggregated and anonymized. Individual responses are not shared with the participant or their manager. The aggregated report is prepared by HR and shared with the participant and their manager.
- **Content**: Reviewers assess the participant on BRHI's Leadership Competency Model: Strategic Thinking, People Development, Communication, Results Orientation, Collaboration, and Integrity.
- **Timeline**: 360 surveys are distributed by December 1 and must be completed by December 20. Aggregated reports are available by January 10.
- **Integration**: 360 feedback is one input to the annual performance rating but does not replace the manager's assessment.

## 6. Compensation Administration

### 6.1 Pay Grade Structure

BRHI maintains a **15-grade compensation structure**:

| Grade | Typical Roles | FLSA Classification |
|---|---|---|
| Grade 1 | Entry-level associate, warehouse helper | Non-Exempt |
| Grade 2 | Sales associate, stock clerk | Non-Exempt |
| Grade 3 | Senior associate, specialist | Non-Exempt |
| Grade 4 | Lead associate, senior specialist | Non-Exempt |
| Grade 5 | Team lead, shift supervisor | Non-Exempt |
| Grade 6 | Assistant department manager | Non-Exempt |
| Grade 7 | Department manager, senior technician | Non-Exempt |
| Grade 8 | Senior department manager, area supervisor | Non-Exempt |
| Grade 9 | Assistant store manager, technical lead | Non-Exempt / Exempt |
| Grade 10 | Store manager, functional manager | Exempt |
| Grade 11 | Senior manager, district support | Exempt |
| Grade 12 | District manager, director | Exempt |
| Grade 13 | Vice president, senior director | Exempt |
| Grade 14 | Senior vice president | Exempt |
| Grade 15 | C-suite, president | Exempt |

Each grade has a defined salary range with a minimum, midpoint, and maximum. Ranges are reviewed annually against market data (see Section 6.4).

### 6.2 Merit Increases

Merit increases are awarded annually based on the employee's performance rating and their position within the grade range. The following merit increase guidelines apply:

| Performance Rating | If Below Midpoint | If At or Above Midpoint |
|---|---|---|
| **5 — Exceptional** | 6–8% | 4–6% |
| **4 — Exceeds Expectations** | 4–6% | 3–4% |
| **3 — Meets Expectations** | 2–4% | 1–3% |
| **2 — Below Expectations** | 0–2% | 0% |
| **1 — Unsatisfactory** | 0% | 0% |

- Employees on an active PIP are not eligible for merit increases.
- Merit increases cannot push an employee above the maximum of their current grade range.
- All merit recommendations are reviewed by the HRBP and approved by the Department Head and Director of HR before implementation.

### 6.3 Promotion Increases

When an employee is promoted to a higher grade:

- The minimum promotion increase is **8%**, even if the employee's current salary already falls within the new grade range.
- The maximum promotion increase is **15%**.
- The target promotion increase places the employee between the **minimum and the first quartile** of the new grade range.
- Promotions require: updated job description, approved JRF (if a new position), completed Promotion Justification Form signed by the hiring manager, Department Head, and Director of HR.
- Lateral transfers (same grade) do not include a compensation increase unless a documented equity adjustment is warranted.

### 6.4 Annual Market Analysis

The Director of HR, in collaboration with the Compensation Analyst, conducts an annual market compensation analysis:

- **Data sources**: Radford, Mercer, and Bureau of Labor Statistics surveys appropriate to BRHI's industry, geography, and company size.
- **Timing**: Analysis is completed by **October 1** each year.
- **Scope**: All 15 grades are benchmarked. A minimum of 3 survey sources or data points per grade is required.
- **Adjustments**: If the market midpoint for any grade has moved more than 5% from the current midpoint, the Director of HR recommends a grade range adjustment effective March 1 of the following year.
- **Budget**: The Finance department incorporates market adjustment funding into the annual labor budget based on the HR recommendation.

### 6.5 Pay Equity Review

BRHI conducts an annual pay equity review to identify and remediate unexplained pay disparities by gender, race, and ethnicity.

- **Methodology**: Multivariate regression analysis controlling for legitimate factors (grade, tenure, performance rating, location, FLSA status, education, and experience).
- **Timing**: The review is completed by **November 30** each year.
- **Threshold**: Any unexplained disparity exceeding **3%** for any protected group within a grade triggers a remediation review.
- **Remediation**: The HRBP and Compensation Analyst propose individual salary adjustments for affected employees. Adjustments are reviewed by Legal Counsel and approved by the Director of HR and CFO. Remediation payments are effective January 1 of the following year.
- **Documentation**: A summary report (excluding individual identifiers) is presented to the Executive Committee. Individual adjustment details are maintained as Confidential — Legal Privileged in a separate file.

### 7.2 Enrollment Periods

#### 7.2.1 Initial Enrollment

New hires must complete their benefits enrollment within **30 calendar days** of their start date. Coverage elected during initial enrollment is effective retroactive to the employee's start date. If the employee does not enroll within 30 days, they default to:

- Medical: Employee-only coverage under the default plan (high-deductible health plan)
- 401(k): Auto-enrollment at 4% (see Section 7.3)
- All other benefits: Waived until the next Open Enrollment period

#### 7.2.2 Open Enrollment

Annual Open Enrollment is held each year during **November 1–15**, with elected changes effective **January 1** of the following year. During Open Enrollment, employees may:

- Change medical, dental, or vision plan elections
- Add or remove dependents
- Enroll in or change voluntary benefits (life insurance, supplemental disability, etc.)
- Change 401(k) contribution rates (can also be changed anytime during the year)

#### 7.2.3 Qualifying Life Events

Employees may make mid-year changes to benefits elections within **30 calendar days** of a qualifying life event:

- Marriage, divorce, or legal separation
- Birth, adoption, or placement for adoption of a child
- Loss or gain of other coverage (e.g., spouse's employer coverage)
- Death of a spouse or dependent
- Change in employment status affecting eligibility
- Dependent reaching maximum age for coverage

### 7.3 401(k) Retirement Plan

- **Auto-Enrollment**: All eligible employees are automatically enrolled at a **4% pre-tax contribution rate** on their first eligible pay date. Employees may opt out or change their contribution rate at any time through the HRIS or the plan administrator's website.
- **Auto-Escalation**: Each January, the contribution rate for auto-enrolled employees increases by 1% until it reaches 10%, unless the employee elects otherwise.
- **Company Match**: BRHI matches employee contributions as follows:
  - **100% of the first 3%** of eligible compensation contributed
  - **50% of the next 3%** (i.e., contributions between 3% and 6%)
  - Maximum company match: **4.5% of eligible compensation**
- **Vesting Schedule**: Company matching contributions vest according to the following schedule:

  | Years of Service | Vesting Percentage |
  |---|---|
  | Less than 1 year | 0% |
  | 1 year | 25% |
  | 2 years | 50% |
  | 3 years | 75% |
  | 4+ years | 100% |

- **Eligible Compensation**: Base salary or wages up to the IRS annual limit ($345,000 for 2026, adjusted annually).
- **Investment Options**: The plan offers a minimum of 15 investment options including target-date funds, index funds, bond funds, and a self-directed brokerage window.
- **Loans and Hardship Withdrawals**: Available per plan document and IRS regulations.

### 7.4 Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP)

- **Discount**: Eligible employees may purchase BRHI common stock at a **15% discount** from the lower of the fair market value on the first or last day of each offering period.
- **Contribution**: Employees may contribute 1–15% of eligible compensation (maximum $25,000 per calendar year per IRS Section 423 limits).
- **Offering Periods**: Two 6-month offering periods per year — January 1 through June 30 and July 1 through December 31.
- **Enrollment**: Employees must enroll at least 15 days before the start of an offering period. Enrollment carries forward to subsequent offering periods unless the employee changes or cancels the election.

### 7.5 Tuition Reimbursement

- **Eligibility**: Full-time and part-time (20–29 hrs/wk) employees with at least 1 year of continuous service.
- **Annual Maximum**: Up to **$5,000 per calendar year** for undergraduate programs; up to **$5,000 per calendar year** for graduate programs (combined maximum of $5,000/year across both).
- **Eligible Programs**: Degree programs from regionally accredited institutions and approved professional certification programs related to the employee's current role or a reasonable career path at BRHI.
- **Minimum Grade**: Reimbursement requires a grade of **C (2.0)** or better for undergraduate courses, **B (3.0)** or better for graduate courses, or a "Pass" in pass/fail courses.
- **Reimbursement Schedule**:

  | Grade / Result | Reimbursement |
  |---|---|
  | A (or equivalent) | 100% of eligible expenses |
  | B (or equivalent) | 100% of eligible expenses |
  | C (undergraduate only) | 75% of eligible expenses |
  | Pass (pass/fail) | 100% of eligible expenses |

- **Eligible Expenses**: Tuition, required fees, and required textbooks. Not covered: parking, supplies, optional materials, late fees.
- **Application Process**: Employee submits a Tuition Reimbursement Request Form to the HRBP **before** the course begins. Post-course, the employee submits grades and receipts within 60 days. Reimbursement is processed within 2 pay periods.
- **Retention Requirement**: Employees who receive tuition reimbursement must remain employed at BRHI for **12 months** after each reimbursement payment. Voluntary separation within 12 months requires repayment on a prorated basis.

### 7.6 Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

- **Provider**: BRHI contracts with a licensed EAP provider offering confidential counseling, referral, and support services.
- **Sessions**: Employees and their household members are entitled to **8 free counseling sessions per issue per year** at no cost.
- **Covered Issues**: Stress, anxiety, depression, substance abuse, relationship issues, grief, financial concerns, legal consultation (initial 30-minute consultation per issue), eldercare/childcare referrals, and critical incident stress debriefing.
- **Confidentiality**: The EAP provider does not disclose individual usage information to BRHI. BRHI receives only aggregate utilization reports.
- **Access**: Available 24/7 by phone, online chat, and in-person appointments. Contact information is included in new-hire orientation materials, posted in break rooms, and available on the intranet.

## 8. Employee Relations

### 8.1 Complaint Intake

#### 8.1.1 Reporting Channels

Employees may report workplace concerns through any of the following channels:

1. **Direct supervisor or any member of management** (verbal or written)
2. **HRBP** (in person, by phone, or by email)
3. **Director of Human Resources** (in person, by phone, or by email)
4. **Ethics Hotline** — a confidential, third-party-operated hotline available 24/7 by phone and online. Reports may be made anonymously where permitted by law.
5. **Chief Operating Officer** (for complaints involving the Director of HR)

#### 8.1.2 Intake Timeline

- All complaints must be **acknowledged within 24 hours** of receipt by any of the above channels.
- The HRBP (or Director of HR, if the complaint involves the HRBP) must complete a **Complaint Intake Form** and conduct a preliminary assessment within **48 hours** of acknowledgment.
- The preliminary assessment determines: (a) the nature and severity of the complaint, (b) whether interim protective measures are needed (e.g., schedule adjustment, reporting change, administrative leave), and (c) the appropriate investigation track.

### 8.2 Investigation Process

#### 8.2.1 Investigation Timeline

- **Target completion**: 30 calendar days from complaint intake. Extensions may be granted by the Director of HR in complex cases, not to exceed 60 calendar days total.
- If the investigation cannot be completed within 30 days, the complainant and respondent must be notified in writing of the delay and the expected completion date.

#### 8.2.2 Investigation Steps

1. **Assign Investigator**: The HRBP or a designated neutral investigator is assigned. For complaints involving senior leadership (Grade 12+), an external investigator is engaged.
2. **Notify Respondent**: The respondent is notified in writing of the complaint, their right to respond, the confidentiality expectations, and the prohibition against retaliation.
3. **Gather Evidence**: The investigator interviews the complainant, respondent, and relevant witnesses. Documentary evidence (emails, messages, photos, video, records) is collected and preserved.
4. **Interim Measures**: If necessary, interim measures are implemented to protect the complainant and maintain workplace order. These may include schedule adjustments, temporary reassignment, or administrative leave (with pay) for the respondent.
5. **Analysis and Findings**: The investigator evaluates all evidence using a preponderance-of-the-evidence standard and prepares a written Investigation Report including findings of fact, credibility assessments, and a determination of whether the alleged conduct occurred.
6. **Remedial Action**: If the complaint is substantiated, the Director of HR (in consultation with Legal Counsel and the relevant Department Head) determines appropriate remedial action, which may include: verbal counseling, written warning, mandatory training, suspension, demotion, or termination, depending on the severity and frequency of the conduct.
7. **Closure**: The complainant and respondent are notified of the investigation outcome to the extent permitted by privacy law. The complaint file is closed and stored in the confidential Employee Relations file (separate from the personnel file).

#### 8.2.3 Non-Retaliation

BRHI strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who reports a concern in good faith or participates in an investigation. Any act of retaliation must be reported immediately through any of the reporting channels listed in Section 8.1.1 and will be investigated promptly. Substantiated retaliation is grounds for disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 8.3 Mediation

Where appropriate, BRHI offers voluntary mediation as an alternative or supplement to formal investigation:

- Mediation is facilitated by a trained HRBP or an external mediator (for Grade 10+ matters).
- Participation is voluntary for both parties and does not replace the Company's obligation to investigate serious policy violations.
- Mediation discussions are confidential and are not documented in either party's personnel file.
- If mediation results in a resolution, the terms are documented in a Mediation Agreement signed by both parties and the mediator, and maintained in the confidential Employee Relations file.

### 8.4 Workplace Violence Prevention and Assessment

#### 8.4.1 Zero Tolerance

BRHI maintains a zero-tolerance policy for workplace violence, threats, intimidation, and harassment. This includes violence or threats directed at employees, customers, vendors, or visitors, whether committed on Company premises or during Company-related activities.

#### 8.4.2 Threat Assessment Team

BRHI maintains a **Threat Assessment Team (TAT)** consisting of:

- Director of HR (Chair)
- Director of Loss Prevention
- Legal Counsel
- Employee's direct manager
- External threat assessment consultant (engaged as needed)

The TAT convenes within **24 hours** of any reported threat or violent incident to assess the level of risk and determine the appropriate response.

#### 8.4.3 Risk Levels and Response

| Risk Level | Indicators | Response |
|---|---|---|
| **Low** | Vague or indirect threat with no specific target or plan; no history of violence | Documented counseling; referral to EAP; increased monitoring |
| **Medium** | Direct threat to a specific individual; escalating behavior; access to weapons | Temporary removal from workplace (administrative leave); safety plan for affected individuals; external threat assessment consultation |
| **High** | Specific, credible threat with means and intent; history of violence; stalking behavior | Immediate removal from workplace; law enforcement notification; workplace security measures; potential termination |

### 8.5 ADA Accommodation Process

BRHI provides reasonable accommodations to qualified individuals with disabilities in accordance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and applicable state and local laws.

#### 8.5.1 Accommodation Request

- Employees may request accommodations verbally or in writing to their supervisor, HRBP, or the Director of HR.
- Upon receiving a request, the HRBP initiates the **Interactive Process** within **5 business days**.

#### 8.5.2 Interactive Process

1. **Acknowledgment**: HRBP acknowledges the request and provides the employee with the ADA Accommodation Request Form and Information about the process.
2. **Medical Documentation**: If the disability or need for accommodation is not obvious, the HRBP may request medical documentation from the employee's healthcare provider. The request is limited to information necessary to confirm the existence of a disability and the functional limitations requiring accommodation. Medical records are maintained in the employee's **confidential medical file**, separate from the personnel file.
3. **Discussion**: The HRBP, employee, and supervisor engage in a good-faith interactive dialogue to identify effective accommodations. The employee's preference is given primary consideration.
4. **Assessment of Reasonableness**: Each proposed accommodation is evaluated against the following criteria:
   - Does it enable the employee to perform the essential functions of the job?
   - Does it impose an undue hardship on the Company (considering nature, cost, financial resources, and operational impact)?
   - Does it pose a direct threat to the health or safety of the employee or others?
5. **Decision and Implementation**: The HRBP documents the accommodation decision in writing, including the rationale. If the requested accommodation is denied, the written notice must explain the reason and offer alternative accommodations that were considered. Decisions are made within **15 business days** of receiving all necessary information.
6. **Follow-Up**: The HRBP follows up with the employee at **30 days** after implementation and then at **90 days** to assess effectiveness. Adjustments are made as needed.

## 9. Separation Process

### 9.1 Voluntary Resignation

#### 9.1.1 Notice Requirements

| Employee Type | Required Notice Period |
|---|---|
| **Non-Exempt (Grade 1–8)** | 2 weeks (14 calendar days) |
| **Exempt (Grade 9–15)** | 4 weeks (28 calendar days) |

#### 9.1.2 Resignation Process

1. Employee submits a written resignation letter (email acceptable) to their direct supervisor with a copy to the HRBP.
2. Supervisor acknowledges receipt within 1 business day and forwards to HRBP.
3. HRBP initiates the **Offboarding Checklist** in the HRIS within 1 business day.
4. During the notice period, the employee is expected to fulfill normal duties, complete transition tasks, and train successors as directed by the supervisor.
5. The Company reserves the right to accept the resignation effective immediately and pay the employee in lieu of working the full notice period, at the Company's sole discretion.

#### 9.1.3 Final Pay

- **Final paycheck** is issued on the next regular pay date following the separation, or sooner if required by state law.
- Final pay includes all earned wages through the last day worked, accrued unused PTO (up to the carryover limit per the PTO policy), and any applicable overtime.
- Deductions for unreturned Company property (uniforms, equipment, keys, etc.) are made only to the extent permitted by applicable law.

### 9.2 Involuntary Termination

#### 9.2.1 Pre-Termination Requirements

No involuntary termination may be executed without the following completed steps:

1. **Documentation Review**: The HRBP reviews the employee's complete personnel file, including performance reviews, disciplinary records, attendance records, PIP history, and any pending complaints or investigations.
2. **Legal Review**: For all involuntary terminations (except probationary-period releases within 90 days of hire), the HRBP submits a **Termination Review Package** to Legal Counsel, including: reason for termination, supporting documentation, prior corrective actions, applicable policy violations, and any known protected activity (FMLA, workers' comp, EEOC charge, etc.). Legal Counsel must approve the termination in writing before it is executed.
3. **Risk Assessment**: The HRBP and Legal Counsel assess the risk of claims and determine whether a severance offer or enhanced separation package is appropriate.

#### 9.2.2 Termination Meeting

- The termination meeting is conducted **in person** whenever possible. Remote terminations are permitted only when the employee works remotely and no in-person meeting is feasible.
- The meeting is attended by the **HRBP** and the **direct supervisor** (or Department Head for supervisor-level employees).
- The meeting is **brief, professional, and private**, conducted in a confidential location.
- The HRBP communicates: the termination decision (final and not subject to appeal within the Company), the effective date (typically same day), the reason in general terms, and next steps regarding final pay, benefits continuation (COBRA), and return of Company property.
- The employee is given a **Separation Packet** containing: COBRA election notice, unemployment insurance information, final pay details, benefit continuation options, 401(k) distribution options, and EAP contact information.
- The employee's access to Company systems (email, HRIS, building access, VPN) is terminated **simultaneously** with the meeting or within 15 minutes. IT is pre-notified and standing by.

#### 9.2.3 Immediate Termination

The following conduct may result in immediate termination without prior progressive discipline:

- Theft, fraud, or embezzlement
- Violence, threats of violence, or possession of weapons on Company premises
- Use, sale, or possession of illegal drugs or unauthorized alcohol on Company premises
- Falsification of Company records (including timekeeping fraud)
- Insubordination (refusal to perform assigned duties after direct instruction)
- Sexual harassment or severe harassment
- Disclosure of confidential Company information
- Conviction of a felony related to the employee's job duties
- Buddy punching or timekeeping fraud

### 9.3 Reduction in Force (RIF)

#### 9.3.1 WARN Act Compliance

BRHI complies with the Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) Act and any applicable state mini-WARN laws:

- **Plant Closing**: 60 calendar days' advance written notice to affected employees, their union representatives (if applicable), the state dislocated worker unit, and the chief elected official of the local government when a closing will result in 50 or more employee losses at a single site during any 30-day period.
- **Mass Layoff**: 60 calendar days' advance written notice when a layoff results in 500 or more employee losses at a single site, or 50–499 losses if they constitute 33% or more of the active workforce at that site, during any 30-day period.
- Notices are sent by certified mail and email. The notice must include: the expected date of the RIF, whether the action is expected to be permanent or temporary, whether bumping rights exist, and the name and contact information of a Company representative.

#### 9.3.2 RIF Selection Criteria

Affected positions and employees are selected based on objective, documented criteria:

- Business necessity (elimination of function, reduction in volume, restructuring)
- Skills and qualifications retained vs. those eliminated
- Performance history (most recent two annual reviews)
- Disciplinary record
- Seniority (as a tiebreaker when all other factors are substantially equal)

Selection decisions must be reviewed by the HRBP and Legal Counsel to ensure compliance with anti-discrimination laws. A disparate impact analysis by protected class (age, race, gender, disability, etc.) is conducted before finalizing the RIF list.

#### 9.3.3 Severance — RIF

Severance for RIF-impacted employees is calculated as follows:

| Tenure | Severance |
|---|---|
| Less than 1 year | 2 weeks base pay |
| 1–5 years | 2 weeks base pay per full year of service |
| 6–10 years | 3 weeks base pay per full year of service |
| 11+ years | 4 weeks base pay per full year of service |

- **Maximum severance**: 26 weeks of base pay.
- Severance is paid as a lump sum (or in regular pay-period installments, at the Company's discretion) and is contingent upon the employee signing a **Severance Agreement and General Release** approved by Legal Counsel.
- The Severance Agreement includes a 21-day consideration period (45 days if the RIF involves a group program as defined by the Older Workers Benefit Protection Act) and a 7-day revocation period.

#### 9.3.4 Outplacement Services

All RIF-impacted employees receive **90 days of outplacement services** through BRHI's contracted outplacement provider, including:

- One-on-one career coaching
- Resume and cover letter development
- Interview preparation and mock interviews
- Job search strategy and market intelligence
- Access to online job-search tools and networking events
- Emotional support and transition counseling

### 9.4 Rehire Eligibility

| Separation Type | Rehire Eligibility | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Voluntary resignation with proper notice | Eligible | After 90-day waiting period; rehired at same or lower grade; prior tenure recognized for benefits eligibility |
| Voluntary resignation without proper notice | Eligible — conditional | After 12-month waiting period; requires Director of HR approval; rehired at starting pay for the grade |
| Involuntary termination — performance | Not eligible | For a minimum of 24 months; after 24 months, may be considered only with Director of HR and Legal approval |
| Involuntary termination — misconduct | Not eligible | Permanently ineligible for rehire |
| RIF / Layoff | Eligible | Preferred rehire status for 12 months from separation date; rehired at same grade with restoration of prior tenure for benefits and vesting purposes |
| End of seasonal assignment (good standing) | Eligible | No waiting period; may be considered for future seasonal or regular openings |

### 10.4 Data Privacy and Access Controls

#### 10.4.1 Access Principles

Employee records are accessible on a **need-to-know basis** only:

- **Employee self-access**: Employees may view their own personnel file (excluding references, investigation records, and management deliberations) by submitting a written request to the HRBP. Access must be provided within **5 business days**. Employees may request copies of documents in their file at no charge.
- **Manager access**: Managers may view performance-related documents for their direct and indirect reports but may not access medical files, I-9 files, investigation files, or payroll records without HRBP authorization.
- **HR access**: HRBPs and the Director of HR have broad access to support their job functions. Medical files and investigation files are restricted to the HRBP assigned to the case, the Director of HR, and Legal Counsel.
- **Third-party access**: External access (subpoenas, government audits, legal proceedings) is routed exclusively through Legal Counsel.

#### 10.4.2 HRIS Audit Trail

All access to employee records in the HRIS is logged. The audit trail captures:

- User identity
- Date and time of access
- Record accessed (employee name and file type)
- Action taken (view, edit, download, print, delete)

Audit logs are reviewed quarterly by the Director of HR and retained for 3 years. Unauthorized access is investigated and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

#### 10.4.3 Data Security

- HRIS data is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+).
- Multi-factor authentication is required for all HRIS users.
- Employee Social Security numbers are stored in a masked format and are accessible only to Payroll, the Director of HR, and Legal Counsel.
- Annual penetration testing of the HRIS is conducted by a qualified third-party vendor.
- Data breach response procedures follow the BRHI Incident Response Plan, with notification to affected individuals within the timeframes required by applicable state breach notification laws.
