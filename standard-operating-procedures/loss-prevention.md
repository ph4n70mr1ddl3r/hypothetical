## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure is to establish a comprehensive framework for the protection of BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. (BRHI) assets, the reduction of inventory shrinkage, and the assurance of safety for all associates, customers, and visitors across all BRHI retail locations and supporting facilities.

This SOP provides specific, actionable guidance to all Asset Protection personnel and establishes expectations for store-level associates in support of loss prevention objectives. It is designed to:

- Protect company assets — including merchandise, cash, fixtures, equipment, intellectual property, and facilities — from loss due to theft, fraud, error, or damage.
- Reduce inventory shrinkage to a level at or below 1.5% of net sales company-wide.
- Ensure the physical safety and personal well-being of every associate, customer, and vendor on BRHI premises.
- Maintain compliance with all applicable federal, state, and local laws governing retail security, detention, use of force, privacy, and employment.
- Provide a consistent, repeatable methodology for the prevention, detection, investigation, and resolution of loss events across all locations.
- Foster a culture of accountability and awareness in which every associate understands their role in protecting company assets.

## 2. Scope

This SOP applies to all BRHI-operated retail stores, distribution centers, corporate offices, and any other facilities under company control. It applies to all associates, contractors, vendors, and temporary workers while on BRHI property or acting in a capacity on behalf of the company.

## 3. Loss Prevention Organization

### 3.1 Organizational Structure

The Asset Protection department operates as a dedicated function within BRHI, organized in the following hierarchical structure:

```
Vice President of Asset Protection
├── Regional LP Managers (4 regions)
│   ├── District LP Managers (2–4 per region)
│   │   ├── Store LP Managers (1 per store)
│   │   │   └── LP Associates (1–4 per store, based on volume)
```

**Vice President of Asset Protection** — Reports directly to the Chief Operating Officer. Holds ultimate accountability for all loss prevention strategy, policy, budget, and outcomes company-wide. Chairs the quarterly Shrink Steering Committee.

**Regional LP Managers** — Each responsible for one of four geographic regions (Northeast, Southeast, Central, West). Oversee 8–16 District LP Managers. Responsible for regional shrink performance, ORC investigations spanning multiple districts, and coordination with regional law enforcement task forces.

**District LP Managers** — Each responsible for a district of 6–10 stores. Conduct regular store audits, manage complex investigations, support Store LP Managers on high-risk apprehensions, and ensure policy compliance across their district.

**Store LP Managers** — One per store. Responsible for day-to-day loss prevention operations within their assigned store, including associate training, CCTV monitoring, apprehensions, investigations, and shrink reduction initiatives.

**LP Associates** — Staff the LP desk, monitor CCTV and EAS systems, conduct floor surveillance, support apprehensions under the direction of the Store LP Manager, and maintain daily activity logs.

### 3.2 Reporting Structure

All Asset Protection personnel follow a dual-reporting structure:

- **Solid-line report** — All LP personnel report upward through the Asset Protection chain of command (LP Associate → Store LP Manager → District LP Manager → Regional LP Manager → VP of Asset Protection). The Asset Protection chain has primary authority over LP policy, investigations, apprehensions, and personnel decisions including hiring, discipline, and termination within the AP department.
- **Dotted-line report** — The Store LP Manager maintains a collaborative, dotted-line relationship with the Store Manager. This ensures LP operations are integrated into daily store management while preserving the independence of the AP function. The Store Manager does not have authority to direct, modify, or interfere with apprehensions, investigations, or LP policy decisions.

This dual-reporting structure exists specifically to preserve the integrity and independence of loss prevention operations and to prevent conflicts of interest.

### 3.3 Staffing Requirements

| Store Classification | Annual Revenue | Minimum LP Staff |
|---|---|---|
| Tier A (High Volume) | > $40M | 1 Store LP Manager + 3–4 LP Associates |
| Tier B (Medium Volume) | $20M–$40M | 1 Store LP Manager + 2 LP Associates |
| Tier C (Standard Volume) | < $20M | 1 Store LP Manager + 1 LP Associate |

At least one trained and certified LP associate must be on duty during all hours the store is open to the public. Stores that cannot meet this minimum must immediately escalate to the District LP Manager for coverage.

## 4. Shrink Reduction Program

### 4.1 Shrink Categories and Benchmark Targets

BRHI recognizes four primary categories of inventory shrinkage. Historical analysis of company data and industry benchmarks indicate the following proportional breakdown of total shrink:

| Category | Proportion of Total Shrink | Primary Countermeasures |
|---|---|---|
| **External Theft** | 40% | EAS, CCTV, customer service, apprehensions, ORC task forces |
| **Internal Theft** | 35% | POS exception monitoring, background checks, investigations, cash controls |
| **Administrative Error** | 15% | Training, process audits, receiving controls, cycle counts |
| **Vendor Fraud** | 10% | Vendor audits, three-way invoice matching, dummy shipments, escort policy |

### 4.2 Shrink Target

The company-wide shrink target is **less than 1.5% of net sales**, measured on a trailing twelve-month basis. Individual store targets are set annually by the VP of Asset Protection in consultation with the CFO and regional leadership, based on the following factors:

- Historical shrink performance (three-year trend)
- Store location risk profile (crime index, demographic data, competitor proximity)
- Store format and merchandise mix
- Staffing levels and turnover rate

Store-specific shrink targets are communicated in writing to each Store Manager and Store LP Manager no later than January 31 of each fiscal year.

### 4.3 Shrink Allocation

Shrink is allocated and tracked at two levels:

**By Store** — Each store is assigned an annual shrink budget expressed as a percentage of net sales and a dollar amount. Shrink is calculated after each physical inventory and adjusted quarterly based on cycle count results.

**By Department** — Within each store, shrink is further allocated to departments (e.g., Power Tools, Electrical, Plumbing, Lumber, Garden) using a combination of:
- Physical inventory results
- Cycle count data
- EAS alarm data by department
- Point-of-sale exception data mapped to department
- Known theft and damage incidents attributed to department

Department-level allocation enables targeted LP interventions. Department managers are accountable for shrink within their areas and receive monthly shrink reports from the Store LP Manager.

### 4.4 Shrink Bonus Program

BRHI operates a Shrink Bonus Program to incentivize store teams to achieve or outperform shrink targets:

- **Full Bonus** — Awarded when a store achieves shrink at or below its assigned target for the fiscal year. The bonus pool equals 15% of the dollar value of shrink savings below target, distributed among all associates who were employed at the store for at least 6 months during the fiscal year.
- **Partial Bonus** — Awarded when a store achieves shrink between its target and target + 0.25%. The bonus pool equals 7.5% of the dollar value of shrink savings below target.
- **No Bonus** — Stores exceeding their target by more than 0.25% are not eligible for a shrink bonus.

Bonus calculations are performed after the annual physical inventory. Payments are made in the first paycheck of the following fiscal year. The Store Manager and Store LP Manager each receive a separate performance bonus tied to shrink results, calculated as part of their annual incentive compensation.

### 4.5 Shrink Steering Committee

A cross-functional Shrink Steering Committee meets quarterly to review company-wide shrink performance, evaluate the effectiveness of LP programs, approve capital expenditures for security technology, and set strategic priorities. Membership includes:

- VP of Asset Protection (Chair)
- CFO or designee
- VP of Store Operations
- VP of Human Resources
- VP of Supply Chain
- General Counsel or designee
- Regional LP Managers (rotating)

## 5. External Theft Prevention

### 5.1 Customer Service as Primary Deterrent

BRHI's first and most effective line of defense against external theft is proactive, engaged customer service. Research and internal data consistently demonstrate that acknowledged, approached customers are significantly less likely to steal. Every store associate is trained to:

- Greet every customer within 10 seconds of entry into their department or aisle zone.
- Make eye contact and offer assistance.
- Remain in the customer's proximity if the customer exhibits behaviors associated with potential theft (e.g., excessive browsing without purchasing, examining security devices, avoiding associate contact, wearing oversized clothing inappropriate for the season).
- Offer to hold merchandise at the register or service desk for later pickup.
- Provide exceptional service in high-theft departments (Power Tools, Fasteners, Electrical, Plumbing) as a standing priority.

The Store LP Manager shall audit customer service greeting compliance a minimum of twice per week using direct observation and mystery shopper programs. Results are included in the weekly LP metrics report.

### 5.2 Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS)

All BRHI stores operate an Electronic Article Surveillance system (acusto-magnetic or RF, as installed per location) at all customer entry/exit points.

**Source Tagging Program**

BRHI participates in a source tagging program with major vendors. Merchandise in the following categories arrives from the distribution center or vendor with EAS tags already applied:

- Power tools and hand tools (all items over $25)
- Drill bits and accessories
- Copper wire and cable (all packaged lengths)
- Premium paints and stains (containers over 1 gallon)
- Locks and security hardware
- Small electronics (smart home devices, security cameras)
- Premium fasteners (boxed sets over $15)

**In-Store Tagging**

Merchandise not source-tagged but exceeding $25 in retail value must be tagged in-store by receiving associates upon check-in. The Store LP Manager is responsible for maintaining a tagging compliance rate of at least 95%, verified through weekly spot audits of 50 random items across the store.

EAS tags and labels must be deactivated or removed at the point of sale. All POS stations are equipped with EAS deactivators/detachers. Associates must verify deactivation before bagging merchandise.

**EAS Alarm Response**

When an EAS alarm activates at an exit:

1. The nearest associate or LP associate will calmly and politely approach the customer: *"Excuse me, it looks like a security tag may have been missed. May I check your bag and receipt to make sure everything was rung up?"*
2. The associate verifies that all merchandise in the customer's possession has been scanned and paid for by checking against the receipt.
3. If all items are accounted for, the associate apologizes for the inconvenience and thanks the customer.
4. If merchandise has not been paid for, the associate offers to ring it up at the nearest register. If the customer refuses and attempts to leave, the associate does NOT attempt to physically stop the customer. The LP team will evaluate whether apprehension criteria (see Section 6) have been met.
5. All EAS alarm events are logged in the Daily LP Activity Log, noting the time, outcome, and associate involved.

### 5.3 Closed-Circuit Television (CCTV)

Every BRHI store is equipped with a CCTV system meeting the following minimum specifications:

- **Camera count** — Minimum 32 cameras per store (Tier A stores: minimum 48 cameras).
- **Coverage** — 360° coverage of the following areas:
  - All customer entrance and exit points (interior and exterior)
  - Sales floor — full coverage of all departments, with additional cameras in high-theft areas (Power Tools, Electrical, Fasteners, Small Electronics)
  - All POS registers — overhead cameras capturing clear views of the counter surface and customer interaction
  - Customer Service / Returns desk
  - Receiving dock (interior and exterior)
  - Cash office
  - LP office
  - Break room
  - Exterior parking lot — sufficient cameras to capture license plates at entrances/exits and provide general lot coverage
  - Loading and lumber yard areas
- **Recording quality** — Minimum 1080p resolution on all cameras; 4K resolution required on POS cameras and entrance/exit cameras.
- **Retention** — All footage retained for a minimum of 90 days. Footage related to active investigations, incidents, or litigation holds must be preserved indefinitely until released by Legal.
- **Storage** — Network Video Recorder (NVR) with sufficient storage capacity to meet the 90-day retention requirement at full frame rate (minimum 15 fps).
- **Access** — Live and recorded footage is accessible to the Store LP Manager, District LP Manager, and above. LP Associates may view live feeds under the direction of the Store LP Manager. Store Managers may access footage only in the presence of the Store LP Manager or with written approval from the District LP Manager.
- **Monitoring** — During operating hours, at least one LP associate must actively monitor CCTV at all times. After hours, the system records continuously and is monitored remotely by a contracted central station monitoring service that will alert the Store LP Manager and law enforcement upon intrusion alarm activation.
- **Maintenance** — Cameras must be inspected daily for proper positioning, focus, and functionality. Any non-functioning camera must be reported immediately and repaired or replaced within 72 hours. The Store LP Manager documents daily camera checks in the Daily LP Activity Log.

### 5.4 Point-of-Sale Exception Monitoring

BRHI employs a POS exception-based reporting (EBR) system that automatically flags anomalous register transactions for review. The system monitors and reports on the following exception categories:

| Exception Type | Trigger Threshold | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Voids (post-sale) | > 3 per shift or > $100 aggregate | High |
| Voids (pre-sale) | > 10 per shift | Medium |
| No-sales (drawer opens without transaction) | > 3 per shift | High |
| Refunds/Returns | Any refund over $100 or > 3 per shift | High |
| Refunds to credit card not used for original purchase | All | Critical |
| Manager overrides | > 3 per shift | Medium |
| Excessive discounts | > 5 per shift or > $200 aggregate | High |
| Sweethearting indicators (scan avoidance) | Pattern detection | Critical |
| Employee sale to self | All | Critical |
| Refund with no merchandise presented | All | Critical |
| Coupon value exceeds merchandise value | All | High |
| Line-item deletions | > 5 per shift | Medium |

The Store LP Manager must review exception reports daily by 10:00 AM for the prior day's activity. Exceptions rated Critical must be investigated immediately and resolved within 72 hours. High-priority exceptions must be resolved within 5 business days. Medium-priority exceptions are reviewed for patterns weekly.

The District LP Manager reviews exception data weekly across all stores in the district to identify cross-store patterns and coordinated internal theft.

### 5.5 High-Value Merchandise Security

Merchandise with a retail value exceeding $50 and identified as high-theft-risk must be secured using one or more of the following methods:

**Locked Display Cases** — Required for:
- All power tools (cordless drills, circular saws, reciprocating saws, impact drivers, etc.)
- All items with a retail value exceeding $200
- Smart home devices and security cameras
- High-end hand tools (individual items over $50)

Locked cases must be located in areas with direct CCTV coverage. Keys are held by department associates and the LP desk. An associate must accompany the customer to the register with the merchandise; merchandise is never handed directly to the customer to carry unattended.

**Spider Wraps** — Required for:
- Power tool combo kits
- Medium-value boxed items ($50–$200) not in locked cases
- Air compressors and portable generators

Spider wraps are applied at receiving and removed at the POS. Spider wraps that trigger EAS alarms must be investigated per the EAS alarm response procedure in Section 5.2.

**Keeper Cases** — Required for:
- Premium drill bits and driver sets
- Oscillating tool blades
- Small electronics accessories
- Any small, concealable item with a retail value over $25 and identified as high-theft

**Security Tethers (Cable Locks)** — Used for:
- Display models only — live merchandise available for customer inspection while secured to the fixture.

**Inventory Protection Standards by Value Tier**

| Retail Value | Minimum Security Requirement |
|---|---|
| $0–$25 | EAS tag or label (if in high-theft category) |
| $25–$50 | EAS tag or label (mandatory) |
| $50–$200 | Spider wrap or keeper case |
| $200–$500 | Locked display case |
| > $500 | Locked display case + individual item tracking in inventory system |

### 5.6 Receipt Verification at Exits

BRHI employs a voluntary, customer-friendly receipt verification program at store exits during peak hours (weekends, holidays, and promotional events). Receipt verification is conducted as follows:

- A designated associate or LP associate is stationed at the exit.
- The associate asks customers: *"May I see your receipt?"* — this is a request, not a demand.
- If the customer agrees, the associate briefly verifies that the receipt matches the visible merchandise in the customer's cart or bags.
- If the customer declines, the associate says *"Thank you, have a great day!"* and allows the customer to leave without further delay. Under no circumstances is a customer detained or delayed solely for declining a receipt check.
- Receipt verification is not an apprehension tool. It serves as a visible deterrent and an opportunity to identify obvious discrepancies that may warrant further LP observation.

### 5.7 Uniformed LP Presence

At least one LP associate in BRHI-issued uniform (branded polo or jacket with "ASSET PROTECTION" identification) must be present on the sales floor during all operating hours. The uniformed LP associate serves as a visible deterrent and is the primary responder for EAS alarms, customer altercations, and safety incidents.

Uniformed LP associates shall conduct systematic floor patrols, varying their route and timing to avoid predictability. Each patrol should cover all departments within a 30-minute cycle. The patrol schedule is set by the Store LP Manager and documented.

### 5.8 Undercover / Plainclothes LP

During peak hours (defined as 10:00 AM–4:00 PM on weekdays and 9:00 AM–6:00 PM on weekends and holidays), Tier A and Tier B stores shall deploy at least one plainclothes LP associate on the sales floor. Plainclothes LP associates:

- Wear attire consistent with a typical customer to blend into the shopping environment.
- Carry concealed two-way radios for communication with the LP desk and uniformed associates.
- Conduct surveillance of high-theft areas, known problem spots, and individuals identified through behavioral profiling.
- Do NOT identify themselves as LP associates unless initiating an apprehension per Section 6.
- May NOT carry or use any recording device other than the store CCTV system.

Plainclothes associates must be recertified annually per Section 15 training requirements.

## 6. Shoplifter Apprehension Policy

### 6.1 General Principles

BRHI's shoplifter apprehension policy is designed to protect the safety of associates, customers, and suspects; to comply with all applicable laws; and to mitigate the company's legal exposure. The policy is intentionally conservative — BRHI would rather let a thief go than risk injury, liability, or violation of civil rights.

### 6.2 Apprehension Authority

Only associates who meet all of the following criteria are authorized to detain a suspected shoplifter:

1. Currently employed as a member of the Asset Protection department (LP Associate, Store LP Manager, or above).
2. Have completed the full 80-hour New LP Associate Training Program.
3. Hold a current, valid Shoplifter Apprehension Certification (renewed annually).
4. Are on duty at the time of the apprehension.

No store associate, cashier, department lead, or manager outside the AP department is authorized to detain or confront a suspected shoplifter. Non-AP associates who witness theft must immediately notify the LP desk and provide a description but must not attempt to stop, follow, or confront the suspect.

### 6.3 Five-Step Observation Requirement — Mandatory

A detention is authorized **only** when the certified LP associate has personally observed ALL of the following five steps, in order, without interruption:

**Step 1 — Observe Approach to Merchandise**
The LP associate must see the subject enter the area where the merchandise is located. This establishes the subject's presence in a specific department or aisle.

**Step 2 — Observe Selection and/or Concealment of Merchandise**
The LP associate must see the subject physically select the merchandise from the shelf, peg, or display. If concealment occurs (placing merchandise in a pocket, bag, under clothing, etc.), the LP associate must observe the specific act of concealment and note the exact merchandise and its location on the subject's person.

**Step 3 — Maintain Continuous, Uninterrupted Observation**
From the moment of selection or concealment, the LP associate must maintain continuous visual observation of the subject. If the LP associate loses sight of the subject — even briefly — and the subject could have discarded the merchandise during that time, the observation is broken and the apprehension must NOT proceed. The LP associate must be able to testify that the subject had no opportunity to discard the merchandise unseen.

**Step 4 — Observe the Subject Pass All Points of Sale Without Attempting to Pay**
The LP associate must observe the subject walk past all cashier stations, self-checkout areas, and customer service desks without making any attempt to pay for the concealed or unpurchased merchandise. The subject must cross the final point of sale — typically the exit doors or the clearly marked payment zone.

**Step 5 — Identify the Subject and Merchandise Upon Exit**
The LP associate must approach the subject after the subject has exited or is in the process of exiting the store. The LP associate must be able to identify the specific merchandise stolen, describe the subject, and state the approximate retail value.

**Failure to observe any one of these five steps makes an apprehension unauthorized.** If an LP associate has observed four of five steps and loses the suspect, the incident is documented but no detention occurs. There are no exceptions to this rule.

### 6.4 Apprehension Procedure

When all five steps have been completed and the subject has passed the final point of sale:

1. **Approach** — The LP associate approaches the subject in a calm, professional manner, identifying themselves: *"I'm with BuildRight Asset Protection. I'd like to speak with you about the merchandise in your possession. Would you please step back inside the store with me?"*

2. **Escort** — The subject is escorted to the LP office. If the subject refuses or attempts to flee, the LP associate does NOT pursue beyond the immediate exterior of the building (sidewalk/parking lot boundary). The associate returns inside, documents the incident, and provides a description to law enforcement.

3. **Recovery** — In the LP office, the LP associate requests that the subject return all unpaid merchandise. The associate catalogs each item, its retail price, and its condition.

4. **Identification** — The LP associate requests government-issued photo identification from the subject. If the subject refuses to provide identification, the LP associate notes this in the report and provides the subject's physical description to law enforcement.

5. **Law Enforcement Notification** — Law enforcement is contacted for ALL apprehensions, regardless of the dollar value of merchandise recovered. The LP associate calls the local police department (or 911 if the situation warrants) and requests an officer respond to the store. The apprehension does not conclude until a law enforcement officer has arrived, been briefed, and taken custody of or released the subject.

6. **Photograph and Trespass** — With the subject's consent or as permitted by local law, the LP associate photographs the subject for BRHI's internal trespass database. The subject is issued a written Criminal Trespass Warning prohibiting them from entering any BRHI property for a period of 12 months. If the subject returns during the trespass period, law enforcement is called immediately.

7. **Release** — If law enforcement declines to arrest and the subject is an adult, the subject is released after the trespass warning is issued. If the subject is a juvenile, see Section 6.8 for special handling requirements.

### 6.5 Minimum Theft Threshold for Detention

The minimum retail value threshold for initiating a shoplifter detention is **$25**. If the observed theft involves merchandise valued below $25, the LP associate does not detain the subject. Instead, the associate:

- Approaches the subject and requests the merchandise be returned.
- Issues a verbal trespass warning.
- Documents the incident in the Daily LP Activity Log.

The $25 threshold applies to the aggregate retail value of all merchandise taken in a single incident.

### 6.6 Use of Force Policy

BRHI's use of force policy is strictly defensive. The following rules apply to all LP associates during any apprehension:

- **No offensive force** — LP associates may not strike, tackle, choke, or otherwise assault a subject. Force may only be used to defend oneself or another person from an imminent physical threat.
- **Reasonable restraint only** — If a subject becomes physically combative, LP associates may use only the minimum reasonable force necessary to protect themselves and others. This may include defensive blocks, escort holds (arm control), and creating distance. Neck restraints, chokeholds, joint locks applied with excessive force, and any technique that restricts breathing are absolutely prohibited.
- **Disengagement is preferred** — If a subject becomes violent or brandishes a weapon, LP associates must disengage immediately, create distance, and allow the subject to leave. No merchandise is worth a physical confrontation. The safety of associates, customers, and bystanders is the absolute priority.
- **Weapons prohibited** — LP associates may not carry firearms, TASERs, pepper spray, batons, or any other weapon while on duty, regardless of personal carry permits.
- **Pursuit prohibited** — LP associates may NOT pursue a subject who flees beyond the exterior of the store (building perimeter). If the subject enters a vehicle and drives away, the LP associate records the license plate, vehicle description, and direction of travel and provides this to law enforcement. Under no circumstances does an LP associate enter a vehicle or attempt to block a vehicle.
- **Reporting** — Any use of force during an apprehension, regardless of severity, must be documented in the incident report and immediately reported to the District LP Manager. An investigation into the use of force will be conducted within 24 hours.

### 6.7 Incident Documentation Requirements

All shoplifter apprehensions must be fully documented in the BRHI Case Management System within **1 hour** of the conclusion of the incident (defined as the point at which law enforcement has responded and the subject has been either arrested or released). The incident report must include:

- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Name of the LP associate(s) who conducted the apprehension
- Name, date of birth, address, and identification number of the subject
- Detailed narrative of the five-step observation, written in first person
- Itemized list of all recovered merchandise, including SKU, description, retail price, and condition
- Photographs of all recovered merchandise
- Photograph of the subject (if consented or legally permissible)
- Whether law enforcement responded; officer name, badge number, and disposition (arrest, citation, release)
- Copy of the Criminal Trespass Warning issued
- Any statements made by the subject
- Description of any use of force, even if minimal
- Names of any witnesses (associates or customers)
- CCTV footage reference numbers and time stamps for all relevant cameras
- EAS alarm data, if applicable

The Store LP Manager reviews and signs all incident reports before the end of the shift in which the apprehension occurred. The District LP Manager reviews all reports within 48 hours.

### 6.8 Juvenile Handling

Apprehensions involving juveniles (persons under the age of 18) require special handling to protect the safety and rights of the minor and to reduce the company's legal exposure:

1. **Unaccompanied Juveniles** — If the juvenile is unaccompanied by a parent or legal guardian, the LP associate does NOT detain the juvenile in the LP office. The associate recovers the merchandise if possible, documents the incident, and contacts law enforcement and the parent/guardian. The juvenile is kept in the presence of at least one LP associate in a public area of the store (e.g., the customer service area) until the parent/guardian or law enforcement arrives. Under no circumstances is an unaccompanied juvenile placed in a locked room or physically restrained.
2. **Accompanied Juveniles** — If the juvenile is accompanied by a parent or legal guardian, the standard apprehension procedure applies, with the parent/guardian present at all times during the process.
3. **Parent/Guardian Notification** — The parent or legal guardian must be contacted immediately in all juvenile apprehensions. If the parent/guardian cannot be reached, law enforcement assumes custody.
4. **Law Enforcement** — Law enforcement is called for all juvenile apprehensions, regardless of the dollar value of merchandise involved.
5. **Interviewing** — Juveniles are not interrogated. Questions are limited to confirming the juvenile's identity, the parent/guardian's contact information, and whether the juvenile is in possession of any additional unpaid merchandise. No questioning occurs without the parent/guardian or law enforcement present.
6. **Trespass** — Criminal Trespass Warnings for juveniles are issued to the parent/guardian. The warning period is 6 months (rather than 12 months for adults), with the option for the Store LP Manager to extend to 12 months for repeat offenders.
7. **Documentation** — All juvenile apprehension reports are flagged in the Case Management System and are subject to additional review by the District LP Manager and HR within 24 hours.

## 7. Internal Theft Prevention and Investigation

### 7.1 Pre-Employment Screening

BRHI conducts pre-employment screening for all associates to mitigate the risk of internal theft. The level of screening varies by position:

| Screening Component | All Associates | Cash Handling / LP Positions | Management |
|---|---|---|---|
| Criminal background check (7-year, all jurisdictions) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Drug screening (10-panel) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Employment verification (3 prior employers) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Professional reference checks (minimum 3) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Education verification | No | Yes | Yes |
| Credit history check | No | Yes (cash handling only) | Yes |
| SSN trace and address history | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Sex offender registry check | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Motor vehicle record check | No | No | Yes (if driving required) |

All background checks are conducted in compliance with the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) and applicable state and local "ban the box" and fair chance hiring laws. The HR department administers all background checks; results are reviewed by HR and the hiring manager. For LP positions, the District LP Manager reviews background results before an offer is finalized.

Associates who have been convicted of theft, fraud, embezzlement, or any crime of dishonesty within the past 7 years are ineligible for hire. Associates with active warrants or pending charges for similar offenses are also ineligible.

### 7.2 POS Exception Reporting — Daily Review

As described in Section 5.4, the POS exception-based reporting system generates daily alerts. For internal theft purposes, the Store LP Manager conducts a focused daily review of the following:

**Excessive Voids**
- Pattern: Associate repeatedly voids transactions after the customer has paid, pocketing the cash.
- Indicator: Void rate exceeding 3 per shift or a void pattern that spikes during periods of low supervision.
- Response: Compare void transactions to CCTV footage of the register. Interview cash handling procedures with the associate if a pattern emerges.

**Sweethearting (Passing Merchandise Without Scanning)**
- Pattern: Associate scans some items but intentionally passes others through without scanning, typically for friends, family, or accomplices.
- Indicator: EBR system flags transactions with unusually high ratios of items scanned to basket size; items-per-minute rate anomalies; CCTV observation of deliberate scan avoidance.
- Response: Compile transaction-level data and CCTV evidence. Build a case over a minimum of 5 documented incidents before confronting the associate.

**Refund Fraud**
- Pattern: Associate processes refunds to their own credit/debit cards; creates fictitious refunds with no merchandise returned; processes refunds for accomplices using stolen or fabricated receipts.
- Indicator: Refunds processed without a corresponding return in the inventory system; refunds to cards not associated with the original transaction; refund amounts that consistently round to even numbers; refunds processed outside of normal transaction patterns.
- Response: Immediately escalate to District LP Manager. Preserve all transaction records and CCTV footage. Do not alert the associate until the investigation is sufficiently documented.

**Coupon Abuse**
- Pattern: Associate applies excessive or fraudulent coupons to transactions, either for personal purchases or to under-ring transactions for accomplices.
- Indicator: Coupon redemption rates significantly above store average; coupons scanned that do not match the merchandise purchased; photocopies or fraudulent coupons accepted.
- Response: Audit coupon submissions; compare to manufacturer coupon redemption reports.

**Employee Discount Abuse**
- Pattern: Associate extends their employee discount to non-eligible individuals; makes purchases on behalf of friends/family using their discount.
- Indicator: Employee discount transactions for unusually large amounts; purchases inconsistent with the associate's known needs; multiple transactions on the same day.
- Response: Verify discount usage against policy. All employee purchases must be made by the associate, for the associate's personal use. Purchasing for others is grounds for disciplinary action.

**Manipulation of Transactions**
- Pattern: Associate manipulates transaction types — for example, ringing a cash sale as a refund, processing a sale as a tax-exempt transaction when not warranted, or incorrectly applying price overrides.
- Indicator: Exception reports flagging anomalous transaction patterns.
- Response: Transaction-level audit with CCTV correlation.

### 7.3 Cash Handling Controls

**Dual Custody**
- All cash counts, cash pulls, safe drops, and cash office activities must be conducted by two authorized associates simultaneously. Both associates must sign all cash handling documents.
- No individual associate may be alone in the cash office at any time.

**Cash Drawer Limits**
- Each cash drawer starts the shift with a standard till of $200.
- The maximum amount permitted in any cash drawer at any time is $500. When the drawer exceeds $500, a cash pull must be performed immediately by the cashier and a front-end supervisor, with the excess deposited in the time-delay safe.

**Surprise Cash Audits**
- The Store LP Manager conducts unannounced cash audits of at least 2 cash drawers per week and 1 cash office audit per week.
- The District LP Manager conducts at least 1 surprise cash office audit per store per quarter.
- Audit results are documented in the Case Management System. Variances exceeding $5 require immediate investigation.

**Cash Reconciliation**
- Cash drawers are reconciled at the end of every shift by the cashier and a front-end supervisor. Any variance exceeding $5 must be reported to the Store LP Manager immediately and documented.
- The cash office is fully reconciled at the end of each business day. Results are recorded in the daily cash report and reviewed by the Store Manager and Store LP Manager by 10:00 AM the following morning.

### 7.4 Receiving Controls

**Blind Receiving Prohibited**
- Receiving associates may NOT accept a shipment based solely on the vendor's manifest or bill of lading. Every incoming shipment must be physically verified — item by item — against the purchase order.
- The receiving associate must count each carton and each item, note any discrepancies, damaged goods, or shortages on the receiving document, and sign only for what was actually received.

**Verification of Shipments**
- All shipments from the BRHI distribution center and all direct vendor deliveries are subject to receiving verification.
- High-value shipments (total invoice value exceeding $1,000) must be verified by two associates.
- Receiving discrepancies exceeding $50 or 2% of the shipment value (whichever is greater) must be reported to the Store LP Manager and the Supply Chain department on the same day.

**Receiving Area Security**
- The receiving dock must be monitored by CCTV at all times.
- Vendor and delivery personnel must remain in the designated receiving area and must be escorted if they need to transit the store for any reason.
- The receiving dock doors must be closed and locked when not in active use for deliveries.

### 7.5 Investigation Procedures

When internal theft is suspected based on exception reports, tips, audits, or other indicators, the following investigation procedure is followed:

**Phase 1 — Preliminary Inquiry (1–5 business days)**
- The Store LP Manager compiles all available data: POS exception reports, CCTV footage, transaction logs, cash audit results, time and attendance records, and any tips or reports from other associates.
- The District LP Manager is notified that an investigation has been opened.
- The subject associate is NOT informed that an investigation is underway.
- All evidence is preserved in the Case Management System.

**Phase 2 — Evidence Development (5–15 business days)**
- Additional CCTV review is conducted to build a pattern of behavior.
- Transaction-level analysis is performed for the subject associate's entire shift history over the past 90 days.
- The District LP Manager may assign additional resources (regional investigation support, forensic accounting).
- If sufficient evidence is developed to support a reasonable belief that theft has occurred, the investigation proceeds to Phase 3.

**Phase 3 — Interview**
- The subject associate is interviewed using the **Wicklander-Zulawski (WZ) non-confrontational interview method**, which is the standard investigative interview technique for BRHI.
- The interview is conducted by a certified WZ interviewer (Store LP Manager or above who has completed WZ certification).
- The interview is conducted in the LP office with the door closed. Two LP associates must be present.
- The associate is informed that they are not under arrest and are free to leave at any time.
- The interviewer presents the evidence and provides the associate an opportunity to explain or confess.
- Miranda warnings are NOT required (BRHI is a private employer, not a state actor), but associates are reminded that the interview is voluntary and that they may request representation.
- The interview is documented verbatim (written summary) and, where legally permissible, audio-recorded with the associate's knowledge.

**Phase 4 — Resolution**
- If the associate admits to theft or fraud, the admission is documented in writing and signed by the associate.
- If the associate denies the allegations and the evidence is inconclusive, the investigation may continue or be closed based on the District LP Manager's judgment.
- If the evidence is compelling — regardless of whether the associate admits — the following actions are taken:
  1. The associate is suspended pending final adjudication.
  2. HR is notified and participates in the final decision.
  3. Criminal prosecution is considered based on the dollar amount, pattern, and severity. The decision to prosecute is made jointly by the District LP Manager and the General Counsel's office.
  4. The associate's final paycheck is processed per state law requirements.
  5. All company property (keys, badge, uniform, etc.) is recovered before the associate leaves the premises.

**Evidence Preservation**
- All CCTV footage related to an investigation is flagged for indefinite retention until released by Legal.
- All transaction records, exception reports, and documents are exported and saved to the case file in the Case Management System.
- Physical evidence (recovered merchandise, forged documents, etc.) is photographed, cataloged, and stored in a secure evidence locker in the LP office.

### 7.6 Exit Interviews for Separating Associates

All associates who separate from BRHI — whether voluntarily or involuntarily — must complete a separation process that includes:

1. **Company property return** — All keys, access badges, uniforms, tools, electronic devices, and any other company property must be returned and documented on a Separation Checklist.
2. **Final pay** — Final paycheck is processed in accordance with applicable state law. No final paycheck is released until all company property has been accounted for.
3. **LP review** — The Store LP Manager reviews the separating associate's POS exception history for the 90 days preceding separation. If any unresolved exceptions are identified, the separation is flagged for investigation.
4. **Access termination** — All system access (POS login, email, building alarm codes, etc.) is terminated effective the close of business on the associate's last day. IT is notified via the standard separation workflow.
5. **Debriefing** — The separating associate is asked (voluntarily) to participate in a brief exit interview that includes general questions about workplace integrity, awareness of any policy violations, and suggestions for improvement. Information provided is treated as confidential.

## 8. Vendor Fraud Prevention

### 8.1 Vendor Check-In Procedures

All vendors, contractors, and delivery personnel must comply with the following check-in procedures upon arrival at any BRHI store:

1. **Sign-in** — All visitors enter through the receiving area and sign the Visitor Log, providing their name, company, purpose of visit, and time of arrival.
2. **Badge** — The visitor is issued a temporary "VENDOR" badge, which must be worn visibly at all times while on BRHI property. The badge is returned upon departure.
3. **Escort** — Vendors are escorted to and from their work area by a BRHI associate. Unescorted vendors on the sales floor or in restricted areas are a policy violation and must be reported immediately.
4. **Verification of deliveries** — All deliveries are verified per the receiving controls in Section 7.4.

### 8.2 Dummy Merchandise Audits

The Asset Protection department conducts periodic dummy merchandise audits to test the integrity of the receiving process:

- The Supply Chain department, in coordination with the District LP Manager, prepares test shipments containing a known quantity and mix of merchandise, some of which is intentionally different from the manifest (e.g., fewer items, substituted items, damaged items).
- Dummy shipments are sent to randomly selected stores without advance notice to store personnel.
- The receiving associate's accuracy in identifying discrepancies is evaluated.
- Results are documented and shared with the Store Manager and Store LP Manager.
- Dummy audits are conducted at least once per quarter per store.

### 8.3 Invoice Matching — Three-Way Verification

All vendor invoices are subject to three-way verification before payment is authorized:

1. **Purchase Order (PO)** — The original PO is pulled from the system, confirming the items, quantities, and prices ordered.
2. **Receiving Document** — The receiving report is reviewed to confirm that the items and quantities were actually received at the store or distribution center.
3. **Vendor Invoice** — The vendor's invoice is compared against both the PO and the receiving document.

Discrepancies between any two of the three documents must be resolved before payment is made. Common fraud indicators include:

- Invoicing for items not received (phantom billing)
- Overcharging versus the PO price
- Duplicate invoicing
- Invoicing for a higher quantity than received
- Billing for premium merchandise when lower-grade merchandise was delivered

The Accounts Payable department is responsible for three-way matching. Discrepancies exceeding $25 or 2% of the invoice value (whichever is greater) are escalated to the District LP Manager for investigation.

### 8.4 Vendor Audit Program

The Asset Protection department conducts an annual audit program for BRHI's top 50 vendors by purchase volume. The audit program includes:

- On-site visits to vendor facilities (for the top 20 vendors)
- Review of vendor billing accuracy over the trailing 12 months
- Comparison of invoiced prices to contracted prices
- Analysis of shrink data attributable to specific vendors (receiving discrepancies, damage claims)
- Review of vendor compliance with BRHI's source tagging and packaging requirements
- Evaluation of vendor credit and return processing

Audit findings are reported to the VP of Asset Protection, VP of Supply Chain, and the Shrink Steering Committee. Vendors with significant discrepancies are placed on a corrective action plan and are subject to more frequent audits (quarterly). Vendors who fail to correct identified issues may be referred for legal action and/or termination of the vendor relationship.

## 9. Safety and Security Systems

### 9.1 Alarm Systems

Every BRHI store is equipped with three types of alarm systems:

**Intrusion Alarm**
- Monitored 24/7 by a UL-listed central station monitoring service.
- Sensors on all exterior doors, windows, roof hatches, and the receiving dock.
- Motion detectors in the sales floor, cash office, and LP office for after-hours coverage.
- After-hours entry requires a valid alarm code. Invalid codes or failure to disarm within 30 seconds triggers a dispatch.
- Alarm codes are unique to each authorized user and are changed immediately upon any personnel change.
- All alarm activations (true and false) are logged and reviewed weekly by the Store LP Manager.

**Fire Alarm**
- Monitored 24/7 by the central station.
- Smoke detectors, heat detectors, and pull stations throughout the building.
- Automatic notification to the fire department upon activation.
- Monthly fire alarm tests conducted by the Store LP Manager or facilities maintenance associate.
- Annual inspection and certification by a licensed fire alarm contractor.

**Panic Alarm**
- Silent panic buttons installed at:
  - Every POS register (under-counter button)
  - Customer Service desk
  - Cash office
  - LP office
  - Store Manager's office
- Panic alarms transmit a silent signal to the central station, which dispatches law enforcement. The alarm does NOT sound audibly in the store.
- Panic buttons are tested quarterly by the Store LP Manager (with prior notification to the central station to prevent a dispatch).

### 9.2 Key Control

**Key Distribution**
- Master keys (access to all areas of the store including the cash office, LP office, and server room) are limited to the Store Manager, Assistant Store Managers, and Store LP Manager.
- Department keys are issued only to associates who require access for their job duties.
- No associate may possess a key to an area they are not authorized to access.

**Key Log**
- All keys are tracked in a Key Control Log maintained by the Store LP Manager. The log records:
  - Key number and type
  - Associate name and employee ID
  - Date issued
  - Date returned
  - Reason for issuance
- The Key Control Log is audited quarterly by the District LP Manager.

**Re-Keying**
- All exterior door locks and restricted-area locks must be re-keyed:
  - Annually, as part of the fiscal year preparation.
  - Immediately upon the loss or theft of any key.
  - Immediately upon the termination (voluntary or involuntary) of any associate who held a master key.
  - Immediately upon the discovery that an unauthorized person has gained possession of a key.
- The cost of re-keying due to lost keys may be charged to the store's LP budget.

### 9.3 Safe Access

- All BRHI stores are equipped with a time-delay safe for cash storage.
- The safe operates on a time-delay mechanism: once the correct combination is entered, a mandatory waiting period of 5 minutes elapses before the safe can be opened. This delay is a deterrent to robbery.
- Safe combinations are known only to the Store Manager, Assistant Store Managers, and Store LP Manager.
- Safe combinations are changed:
  - Immediately when any authorized person separates from the company.
  - Immediately if there is any reason to believe the combination has been compromised.
  - At least once per year.
- Safe access requires dual custody — two authorized associates must be present whenever the safe is opened.

### 9.4 Access Control — Restricted Areas

The following areas are designated as restricted and are accessible only to authorized personnel:

| Restricted Area | Authorized Personnel |
|---|---|
| Cash office | Store Manager, Assistant Store Managers, Store LP Manager, designated cash office associates |
| LP office | All AP personnel, Store Manager (with LP present or District LP approval) |
| Server room | Store Manager, IT personnel, Store LP Manager |
| Receiving dock (during deliveries) | Receiving associates, LP associates, authorized vendor escorts |
| Chemical storage | Designated department associates, LP associates, Store Manager |

All restricted areas must be locked when not in active use. Unauthorized associates found in restricted areas are subject to disciplinary action.

### 9.5 Fire Suppression Systems

**Sprinkler System**
- All BRHI stores are equipped with automatic fire sprinkler systems designed and maintained in accordance with NFPA 13.
- Sprinkler systems are inspected annually by a licensed fire protection contractor.
- A minimum clearance of 18 inches below sprinkler heads must be maintained in all storage and display areas.

**Fire Extinguishers**
- ABC-rated fire extinguishers are located throughout the store per NFPA 10 requirements, with a maximum travel distance of 75 feet to the nearest extinguisher.
- Extinguishers are inspected monthly by a designated associate (visual inspection) and annually by a licensed contractor (full service inspection).
- All associates receive fire extinguisher training during orientation and annually thereafter (PASS method: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep).

**Kitchen Hood System**
- Stores with employee break rooms equipped with cooking facilities (stoves, deep fryers, griddles) are fitted with automatic kitchen hood fire suppression systems in accordance with NFPA 96.
- Kitchen hood systems are inspected and serviced semi-annually by a licensed contractor.

## 10. Robbery Response

### 10.1 General Principles

A robbery is defined as the taking of property by force or threat of force. BRHI's absolute priority during any robbery is the safety of associates and customers. No amount of cash or merchandise justifies risking a life.

### 10.2 During a Robbery

All associates must follow these procedures during an active robbery:

1. **Cooperate fully.** Comply with the robber's demands. Open the register, safe, or any other storage the robber demands. Do not argue, resist, or attempt to negotiate.
2. **Do NOT resist or pursue.** Do not attempt to disarm, confront, or physically challenge the robber. Do not reach for a weapon. Do not attempt to follow the robber when they leave.
3. **Observe and remember.** Without staring or drawing attention, try to note the following:
   - Physical description: approximate height, weight, age, race/ethnicity, hair color and style, facial hair, distinguishing marks (tattoos, scars, piercings), clothing (type, color, brand logos), shoes.
   - Voice: accent, tone, any distinctive speech patterns.
   - Weapon: type (handgun, rifle, knife, etc.), color, size.
   - Direction of flight: which exit the robber used, whether they entered a vehicle, vehicle description (make, model, color, license plate), direction of travel.
4. **Remain calm.** Speak in a calm, even tone. Avoid sudden movements. Keep your hands visible.

### 10.3 After the Robber Leaves

1. **Activate the panic alarm** — Press the silent panic button immediately after the robber has left the building.
2. **Call 911** — The Store Manager or ranking associate on duty calls 911 immediately. Provide the store address, the fact that a robbery has occurred, a description of the robber, and the direction of flight.
3. **Secure the scene** — Do not touch anything the robber may have touched (counter, register, door handles, etc.). Do not clean up or straighten the area. Preserve the scene for law enforcement evidence collection.
4. **Account for all associates and customers** — Determine if anyone has been injured. If injuries are present, call for medical assistance immediately.
5. **Lock the doors** — Secure the store to prevent the robber from returning and to preserve the scene. Do not allow non-essential persons to enter. Customers and associates who wish to leave may do so, but their names and contact information should be recorded for law enforcement.
6. **Do not discuss the incident** with anyone other than law enforcement, BRHI management, and the Asset Protection department until authorized by the District LP Manager or General Counsel.

### 10.4 Post-Robbery Procedures

- **Incident report** — The Store LP Manager (or ranking LP associate) completes a detailed robbery incident report in the Case Management System within **1 hour** of the incident. The report must include all observations noted in Section 10.2(3), the 911 call time, officer response time, and a full narrative of events.
- **Notification** — The Store Manager immediately notifies the District Manager and the District LP Manager. The District LP Manager notifies the Regional LP Manager and VP of Asset Protection.
- **Critical Incident Stress Debriefing (CISD)** — A professional CISD session is arranged for all associates who were present during the robbery, within **24 hours** of the incident. Participation is voluntary but strongly encouraged. BRHI contracts with a licensed Employee Assistance Program (EAP) provider for this service.
- **Post-incident review** — The District LP Manager conducts a review of the robbery within 48 hours to identify any security gaps, procedural failures, or lessons learned. Findings are documented and shared with the Regional LP Manager.
- **Media** — All media inquiries are referred to the BRHI Communications department. No associate is authorized to speak to the media about a robbery incident.

## 11. Organized Retail Crime (ORC)

### 11.1 Definition

Organized Retail Crime (ORC) refers to the coordinated, large-scale theft of retail merchandise by professional criminal organizations for the purpose of resale. ORC is distinguished from opportunistic shoplifting by its characteristics: pre-planning, use of multiple participants, targeting of specific high-value merchandise, use of tools (booster bags, foil-lined clothing, signal jammers), and fencing operations for resale through online marketplaces, flea markets, or unauthorized retailers.

### 11.2 ORC Task Force Participation

BRHI actively participates in the following task forces and industry organizations to combat ORC:

- **Regional ORC task forces** — BRHI assigns a District LP Manager or Regional LP Manager to each regional ORC task force where BRHI has a significant presence. Task force participation involves sharing intelligence, coordinating multi-store investigations, and working with law enforcement on joint operations.
- **National ORC initiatives** — BRHI is an active member of the Coalition for Law Enforcement and Retail (CLEAR) and participates in FBI-led ORC task forces where available.
- **State retail federations** — BRHI's VP of Asset Protection or designee participates in state-level retail industry associations that advocate for ORC legislation and enforcement.

### 11.3 ORC Investigations

ORC investigations differ from standard shoplifter apprehensions in scope, complexity, and duration. Key characteristics:

- **Case building** — ORC investigations are intelligence-driven. The District LP Manager or Regional LP Manager is the case agent. Investigations typically span weeks to months and involve surveillance, pattern analysis, and coordination with other BRHI stores (to identify multi-store activity) and other retailers (to identify cross-retailer activity).
- **Surveillance** — Extended surveillance operations may be conducted to identify ORC ring members, their methods, vehicles, and fencing locations. Surveillance is conducted by trained LP associates or contracted licensed private investigators, operating within the bounds of applicable law.
- **Law enforcement cooperation** — ORC investigations are conducted in partnership with local, state, or federal law enforcement. BRHI provides evidence, witness testimony, and expert analysis; law enforcement provides investigative authority, warrants, and arrest capability.
- **Prosecution** — ORC cases are pursued for felony prosecution whenever possible. The District LP Manager coordinates with the local prosecutor's office to ensure cases are properly documented and charged.
- **Recovery** — BRHI seeks full restitution for the value of stolen merchandise in all ORC prosecutions. The General Counsel's office assists with civil recovery efforts where appropriate.

### 11.4 ORC Intelligence Sharing

BRHI shares ORC intelligence through the following channels:

- **ORCA (Organized Retail Crime Association)** — BRHI submits incident data, suspect photographs, and modus operandi information to ORCA's national database.
- **RILA (Retail Industry Leaders Association)** — BRHI participates in RILA's Asset Protection Leaders Council and shares de-identified ORC trend data.
- **Regional ORC task forces** — Real-time intelligence on active ORC rings is shared with task force members and law enforcement.
- **Internal intelligence** — The BRHI Case Management System includes an ORC module that links incidents across stores and regions to identify patterns. LP associates are required to flag any apprehension that exhibits ORC characteristics (multiple participants, pre-planning tools, target merchandise matching known ORC patterns).

### 11.5 Stolen Merchandise Marketplace Monitoring

The Asset Protection department conducts ongoing monitoring of online marketplaces where stolen BRHI merchandise may be resold:

- Dedicated monitoring of eBay, Amazon third-party sellers, Facebook Marketplace, OfferUp, Craigslist, and other platforms for listings matching known stolen merchandise (by SKU, product description, and quantity patterns).
- Automated alerts are configured for bulk listings of high-theft BRHI merchandise (power tools, copper wire, smart home devices) from sellers within a 50-mile radius of BRHI stores experiencing high shrink.
- Suspected stolen merchandise listings are documented, and the seller's identity is preserved for potential investigation.
- When a match is identified, the District LP Manager coordinates with law enforcement for a buy operation or further investigation.

### 11.6 Fence Identification and Investigation

BRHI LP personnel investigate known or suspected fencing operations — businesses or individuals who knowingly purchase stolen merchandise for resale:

- Intelligence from apprehensions, online marketplace monitoring, and task force partners is used to identify potential fences.
- Undercover operations targeting fences must be approved by the Regional LP Manager and General Counsel before execution.
- Law enforcement is the lead agency in any fence investigation. BRHI provides support, evidence, and testimony.
- Identified fences are reported to law enforcement for prosecution and to state attorneys general for potential civil action.

## 12. Cash Management Security

### 12.1 Cash Office Procedures

The cash office is the most secure area in the store. The following procedures are mandatory:

- **Dual custody** — The cash office must never be accessed by a single individual. Two authorized associates must be present whenever the cash office is unlocked.
- **Camera coverage** — The cash office is monitored by a minimum of two CCTV cameras providing overlapping, continuous coverage of the safe, the counting table, and the entrance door. Cameras record 24/7.
- **Limited access** — Cash office access is restricted to the Store Manager, Assistant Store Managers, Store LP Manager, and designated cash office associates. A complete list of authorized personnel is maintained by the Store LP Manager and posted inside the cash office.
- **Door security** — The cash office door is solid-core steel with a deadbolt lock. The door remains locked at all times except when authorized personnel are entering or exiting.
- **Counting procedures** — All cash is counted on the counting table in full view of the CCTV cameras. Cash must never be counted off-camera or in a location outside the cash office.

### 12.2 Cash Drawer Limits

| Parameter | Amount |
|---|---|
| Starting till | $200 (standard denomination mix: 1 × $50, 5 × $20, 4 × $10, 8 × $5, 50 × $1, coin roll) |
| Maximum drawer balance | $500 |
| Cash pull threshold | When drawer exceeds $500, excess must be pulled immediately |
| Minimum cash pull | $100 per pull |
| Acceptable daily variance | Less than $5 (over or short) |

Cashiers whose drawers show variances exceeding $5 are subject to coaching for the first occurrence, written counseling for the second occurrence within 90 days, and investigation for the third occurrence within 180 days.

### 12.3 Safe Procedures

- **Time-delay safe** — All BRHI stores use a time-delay safe with a 5-minute delay between code entry and safe opening.
- **Drop procedures** — Cash drops from registers are placed in a deposit envelope, sealed, and dropped into the safe through a one-way deposit slot. The envelope is labeled with the date, time, register number, cashier name, and amount.
- **Dual verification** — Safe contents are verified at the end of each business day by two authorized associates. Both sign the daily safe count sheet.
- **Armored car preparation** — Cash for armored car pickup is prepared in sealed, tamper-evident deposit bags during the previous close. The deposit amount is recorded on the bag and in the daily cash report.

### 12.4 Cash-in-Transit Security

- **Armored car pickup** — Cash is transported from the store by a contracted armored car service. Pickup schedules vary by store volume:
  - Tier A stores: 3 times per week (Monday, Wednesday, Friday)
  - Tier B stores: 2 times per week (Tuesday and Friday)
  - Tier C stores: 1 time per week (Friday)
- **Pickup procedure** — The armored car guard is met at the receiving dock by two authorized BRHI associates. The deposit bag is handed to the guard through the receiving area. The guard signs a receipt, which is retained in the cash office.
- **No manual cash transport** — Under no circumstances do BRHI associates transport cash to or from the bank in personal vehicles. All cash movement between the store and the bank is handled by the armored car service.

### 12.5 Daily Reconciliation

- Cash reconciliation is performed at the end of each business day.
- All register drawers are counted and reconciled by the cashier and a front-end supervisor.
- The cash office is reconciled against the day's deposits, safe drops, and armored car pickups.
- Variances exceeding $5 (over or short) must be reported to the Store LP Manager immediately and documented in the daily cash report.
- The Store LP Manager investigates all cash variances exceeding $5 within 48 hours. Findings are documented in the Case Management System.

## 13. Cyber and Information Security

*Note: This section provides a summary of cyber and information security measures directly relevant to loss prevention. The complete Information Security Policy (BRHI-POL-012) should be referenced for comprehensive requirements.*

### 13.1 POS Security Monitoring

- All POS terminals are monitored by the IT Security team for anomalous activity, including unauthorized access attempts, malware indicators, and network anomalies.
- POS software is updated with security patches within 72 hours of release.
- POS terminals are on a segmented network, isolated from the general corporate and guest Wi-Fi networks.

### 13.2 Skimmer Detection

Payment card skimmers — devices illegally installed on card readers to capture card data — are a significant threat. BRHI employs the following countermeasures:

- **Daily physical inspection** — The first cashier to open each POS terminal each day must perform a visual and tactile inspection of the card reader. The inspection includes:
  - Checking for loose, bulky, or misaligned components on the card reader.
  - Running a finger along the card slot to detect unusual resistance or attachments.
  - Comparing the card reader to a reference photograph (kept at each register) to identify visual discrepancies.
  - Checking the PIN pad for overlay devices.
- The cashier records the daily skimmer inspection on the register opening checklist.
- **Weekly detailed inspection** — The Store LP Manager conducts a detailed physical inspection of all card readers in the store (including self-checkout, customer service, and any standalone payment kiosks) once per week. This includes removing faceplates where accessible and inspecting for internal skimming devices.
- **Employee awareness** — All associates receive skimmer awareness training during orientation and annually thereafter. Associates are trained to recognize signs of tampering and to immediately report any suspicious device to the Store LP Manager.
- **Response** — If a skimmer is discovered:
  1. Do NOT remove the device.
  2. Power down the affected terminal.
  3. Immediately notify the Store LP Manager, who contacts the District LP Manager, IT Security, and law enforcement.
  4. Secure the terminal and preserve the scene for law enforcement evidence collection.
  5. IT Security initiates a review of all POS terminals company-wide.

### 13.3 Social Engineering Awareness

LP associates and all store associates are trained to recognize and respond to social engineering attacks that target store operations:

- **Phone-based pretexting** — Callers posing as corporate IT, vendors, or law enforcement requesting sensitive information (alarm codes, safe combinations, associate information, access credentials). Associates are trained to verify the identity of any caller requesting sensitive information by calling back through the official corporate directory.
- **Email phishing** — Associates are trained not to click links or open attachments in suspicious emails. IT Security conducts periodic phishing simulation exercises.
- **In-person pretexting** — Individuals posing as vendors, service technicians, or inspectors to gain access to restricted areas. Associates are trained to verify credentials and to never allow unescorted access to restricted areas.

- POS exception summary — top 5 associates by exception count, top 5 by exception dollar value
- Cash variance summary
- Shrink indicator trends (cycle count results if conducted during the week)
- Training completed during the week
- Open investigations and status
- Staffing compliance (LP coverage hours vs. required hours)
- Camera/system downtime

### 14.4 Monthly Shrink Trend Analysis

The District LP Manager prepares the Monthly Shrink Trend Analysis for all stores in the district by the 10th of each month. The report includes:

- Month-over-month and year-over-year shrink trend by store and by department
- Cycle count accuracy trends
- External and internal theft recovery trends
- Comparison of actual shrink to target shrink by store
- Identification of stores trending above target and recommended corrective actions
- Vendor discrepancy trends
- LP staffing and training compliance

### 14.5 Quarterly LP Audit Results

The District LP Manager conducts a comprehensive LP audit of each store in the district at least once per quarter. The audit evaluates:

- Physical security systems (CCTV, EAS, alarms, locks, safes) — operational status and compliance
- Cash handling procedures — compliance with dual custody, drawer limits, reconciliation
- Receiving procedures — compliance with blind receiving prohibition, verification of shipments
- POS exception follow-up — all exceptions investigated within required timeframes
- LP staffing — minimum coverage met during all operating hours
- Training records — all LP associates current on certifications; all store associates current on annual LP awareness training
- High-value merchandise protection — compliance with locked case, spider wrap, and keeper case requirements
- Key control — log current, no unauthorized holders, re-keying schedule met
- Daily LP Activity Logs — complete and properly reviewed
- Case management — all cases properly documented and current

Audit results are scored on a 100-point scale. Stores scoring below 80 are placed on a Corrective Action Plan (CAP) with specific remediation requirements and a 30-day follow-up audit.

### 14.6 Annual Shrink Inventory

BRHI conducts a complete physical inventory of every store at least once per fiscal year, using a third-party inventory service. The physical inventory:

- Counts every item in the store — sales floor, backroom, receiving, and any off-site storage.
- Compares physical count to the book inventory (perpetual inventory system) to calculate shrink.
- Allocates shrink by store, department, and merchandise category.
- Results are compiled into the Annual Shrink Inventory Report, which is presented to the Executive Leadership Team and the Board of Directors (summary).

Interim cycle counts are conducted weekly by store associates under the direction of the Store LP Manager, focusing on high-theft and high-value departments. Cycle count results are used to estimate shrink between physical inventories and to trigger targeted LP interventions when counts are significantly below book values.

### 14.7 Case Management System

All LP investigations, apprehensions, and incidents are documented in the BRHI Case Management System, a secure, web-based application accessible only to authorized AP personnel. The system:

- Assigns a unique case number to each investigation and apprehension.
- Tracks case status (open, under investigation, closed, referred for prosecution).
- Stores all evidence: reports, photographs, CCTV footage references, transaction data, interview summaries.
- Provides reporting and analytics capabilities for trend analysis and management reporting.
- Links related cases (e.g., multiple apprehensions of the same individual, multiple incidents involving the same associate, ORC patterns across stores).
- Maintains a permanent record — cases are never deleted, only closed.
- Access is logged and audited quarterly by the District LP Manager.

## 15. Training

### 15.1 New LP Associate Training — 80 Hours

All newly hired LP associates must complete an 80-hour training program before they are authorized to perform any LP function independently. The training program consists of:

| Module | Hours | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Module 1: BRHI Orientation and LP Department Overview | 4 | Company history, values, LP organization, reporting structure, career development |
| Module 2: Legal Authority and Liability | 8 | Shopkeeper's privilege, citizen's arrest statutes by state, use of force law, civil liability, false imprisonment, defamation, Miranda, Fourth Amendment considerations, state-specific detention laws |
| Module 3: Five-Step Observation and Apprehension Procedure | 12 | Detailed instruction and practice scenarios for the five-step observation method; role-play exercises; pass/fail practical examination |
| Module 4: CCTV Operation and Monitoring | 6 | System operation, camera positioning, live monitoring techniques, footage retrieval, evidence preservation |
| Module 5: EAS System Operation | 4 | Tag application, deactivation, system troubleshooting, alarm response |
| Module 6: Report Writing and Documentation | 6 | Incident report writing, case documentation, Daily LP Activity Log, Case Management System training |
| Module 7: Interview and Interrogation (Wicklander-Zulawski) | 12 | WZ non-confrontational method — pre-interview preparation, behavioral analysis, interview structure, obtaining admissions, documentation |
| Module 8: Internal Theft Detection | 8 | POS exception analysis, cash handling procedures, receiving controls, investigation procedures |
| Module 9: Safety and Emergency Response | 6 | Fire safety, first aid/CPR/AED, robbery response, active shooter response (Run-Hide-Fight), severe weather |
| Module 10: Use of Force | 4 | BRHI use of force policy, defensive tactics, de-escalation techniques, reporting requirements |
| Module 11: ORC Awareness | 4 | ORC definition, indicators, intelligence sharing, marketplace monitoring, fence identification |
| Module 12: Field Training (On-the-Job) | 6 | Shadowing a certified LP associate; observe and participate in apprehensions under supervision |

All modules must be completed with a passing score of 80% or higher on written examinations and a "pass" rating on all practical evaluations. Associates who fail any module may retake it once. A second failure results in reassignment or separation.

### 15.2 Annual Recertification — 16 Hours

All LP associates must complete 16 hours of annual recertification training to maintain their active status. Recertification must be completed by December 31 of each calendar year. Associates who do not complete recertification by the deadline are suspended from LP duties until recertification is completed.

Annual recertification includes:

| Topic | Hours |
|---|---|
| Legal update (new statutes, case law, company policy changes) | 2 |
| Apprehension procedure refresher (including practical scenario) | 3 |
| Use of force refresher (including de-escalation practical) | 2 |
| Interview and interrogation refresher (WZ method) | 2 |
| Report writing and documentation refresher | 1 |
| Safety and emergency response refresher | 2 |
| ORC awareness update | 1 |
| Technology update (CCTV, EAS, POS, Case Management System) | 1 |
| Written examination (must score 80% or higher) | 1 |
| Practical evaluation (simulated apprehension — pass/fail) | 1 |

### 15.3 Shoplifter Apprehension Certification

No LP associate may participate in or conduct a shoplifter apprehension until they have:

1. Completed all 80 hours of New LP Associate Training.
2. Passed the Module 3 practical examination (simulated five-step observation and apprehension).
3. Successfully observed and assisted in a minimum of 3 real apprehensions under the direct supervision of a certified LP associate or Store LP Manager.
4. Received written certification from the Store LP Manager and District LP Manager.

Certification is documented in the associate's personnel file and in the Case Management System.

### 15.4 Interview and Interrogation Certification

LP associates who will conduct investigative interviews of associates or subjects must hold a current Wicklander-Zulawski certification, obtained by:

1. Completing the WZ module in the 80-hour training program (or equivalent external WZ seminar).
2. Passing the WZ practical evaluation.
3. Successfully conducting at least 2 interviews under the supervision of a WZ-certified interviewer.
4. Receiving written certification from the District LP Manager.

WZ certification must be refreshed every 2 years through a WZ-approved refresher course.

### 15.5 Safety and Use of Force Training

All LP associates receive:

- **Initial training** — Included in Modules 9 and 10 of the 80-hour program.
- **Annual refresher** — Included in the 16-hour annual recertification.
- **Quarterly safety drill** — The Store LP Manager conducts a quarterly safety drill (alternating between fire evacuation, severe weather shelter, robbery response, and active shooter response). All LP associates on duty participate. Drill results are documented in the Daily LP Activity Log.

### 15.6 All-Associate LP Awareness Training — 4 Hours Annually

Every BRHI associate — regardless of department or position — must complete 4 hours of LP awareness training annually. This training is delivered through a combination of e-learning modules and in-person sessions facilitated by the Store LP Manager. The curriculum includes:

| Topic | Duration | Format |
|---|---|---|
| Customer service as a theft deterrent | 45 minutes | E-learning |
| EAS alarm response procedures | 30 minutes | E-learning |
| High-value merchandise protection procedures | 30 minutes | E-learning |
| Cash handling and refund procedures (for cashiers) | 45 minutes | In-person |
| Recognizing internal theft indicators | 30 minutes | E-learning |
| Safety and emergency response basics | 30 minutes | In-person |
| Skimmer detection and awareness | 15 minutes | E-learning |
| Social engineering awareness | 15 minutes | E-learning |

Completion is tracked in the Learning Management System (LMS). Associates who do not complete the training by December 31 are subject to progressive discipline up to and including suspension.
