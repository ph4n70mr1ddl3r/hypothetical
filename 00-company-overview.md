## 3. Mission Statement

> *To provide the best customer experience by offering quality products, competitive prices, and expert advice to help every customer build, maintain, and improve their homes and businesses.*

Our mission is brought to life every day through the actions of our associates across more than 2,300 stores, our digital platforms, and our supply chain network. We interpret "best customer experience" broadly — it means providing the right product at the right price, in the right place, at the right time, with knowledgeable guidance that empowers our customers to complete their projects with confidence. Whether a first-time DIYer is choosing paint for a child's bedroom or a professional contractor is sourcing materials for a 200-unit residential development, our mission remains the same: deliver expertise, quality, and value in every interaction.

## 5. Core Values

BuildRight's core values define who we are, how we operate, and what we stand for. Every associate — from the stockroom to the boardroom — is expected to embody these values daily. They are not aspirational slogans; they are non-negotiable standards of conduct.

### 5.1 Customer First

**Statement:** We put the customer at the center of every decision.

**Behavioral Expectations:**

- Greet every customer promptly and genuinely within 10 seconds of entering your area.
- Listen actively to understand the customer's project, skill level, and budget before recommending products.
- Walk the customer to the product location rather than pointing or giving verbal directions alone.
- Follow up on special orders, back-ordered items, and installation projects proactively.
- Resolve complaints at the lowest possible level; escalate only when necessary and always communicate next steps clearly.
- Anticipate customer needs by understanding seasonal trends, local projects, and community events.
- Solicit and act on customer feedback through surveys, in-store conversations, and digital channels.
- Treat every interaction — whether in-store, online, or over the phone — as an opportunity to earn a customer for life.

### 5.2 Excellence in Execution

**Statement:** We deliver results with speed and precision.

**Behavioral Expectations:**

- Set clear, measurable goals for yourself and your team, and track progress weekly.
- When you identify a problem, bring at least one proposed solution.
- Meet or exceed established benchmarks for planogram compliance, inventory accuracy, and shrinkage reduction.
- Respond to internal requests (interdepartmental, cross-functional) within one business day.
- Complete tasks correctly the first time; do not cut corners on safety, quality, or process compliance.
- Embrace continuous improvement — if a process is inefficient, raise it through the appropriate channel.
- Prepare thoroughly for meetings, presentations, and project milestones.
- Hold yourself and your teammates accountable to deadlines and quality standards without exception.

### 5.3 Safety Always

**Statement:** No product, sale, or deadline is worth an injury.

**Behavioral Expectations:**

- Stop work immediately if you observe an unsafe condition — for associates, customers, or vendors.
- Report all incidents, near-misses, and hazards through the SAFE (Safety Action Feedback Entry) system within one hour.
- Wear required personal protective equipment (PPE) at all times in designated areas: safety glasses, steel-toed boots, high-visibility vests, hearing protection, and gloves as applicable.
- Participate fully in monthly safety huddles and complete all required safety training modules on schedule.
- Keep aisles, receiving docks, and break rooms clear of obstructions and spill hazards.
- Operate powered equipment (forklifts, order pickers, reach trucks, powered pallet jacks) only if currently certified.
- Never bypass, disable, or modify safety guards, interlocks, or emergency equipment.
- Customer safety is associate responsibility — address unsafe customer behavior (e.g., improper ladder use, children in restricted areas) courteously but firmly.

### 5.4 Respect for All People

**Statement:** We value diversity, equity, and inclusion in every interaction.

**Behavioral Expectations:**

- Treat every associate, customer, vendor, and community member with dignity — regardless of background, identity, appearance, or position.
- Use inclusive language in all communications — written, verbal, and digital.
- Challenge bias, stereotypes, and exclusionary behavior when you encounter it, whether in yourself or others.
- Participate in at least one diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) learning opportunity per year.
- Support BuildRight's Employee Resource Groups (ERGs) through membership, sponsorship, or allyship.
- Recruit, interview, and promote based on merit, potential, and alignment with our values — never based on personal affinity.
- Accommodate differing abilities, religious observances, and family obligations as required by law and as guided by our commitment to flexibility.
- Celebrate the contributions of all team members and create space for diverse perspectives in decision-making.

### 5.5 Integrity in Everything

**Statement:** We do the right thing, even when no one is watching.

**Behavioral Expectations:**

- Follow all applicable laws, regulations, and BuildRight policies without exception.
- Disclose conflicts of interest promptly and recuse yourself from decisions where a conflict exists.
- Protect confidential information — customer data, financial results, trade secrets, and personnel records — with the highest standard of care.
- Never accept gifts, entertainment, or favors from suppliers that could influence — or appear to influence — business decisions.
- Report suspected violations of the Code of Conduct through the Ethics Hotline (1-800-BUILD-ETHICS) or the online portal; all reports are treated confidentially, and retaliation is strictly prohibited.
- Be transparent about mistakes — own them, correct them, and learn from them.
- Ensure accuracy in all representations to customers, including pricing, product specifications, availability, and lead times.
- Use company assets (vehicles, tools, technology, supplies) only for authorized business purposes.

### 5.6 Giving Back

**Statement:** We invest in the communities we serve.

**Behavioral Expectations:**

- Participate in at least one BuildRight-sponsored community service event per year (e.g., Habitat for Humanity builds, park clean-ups, school supply drives).
- Support the BuildRight Foundation through payroll deductions, volunteer hours, or in-kind contributions.
- Identify local organizations — shelters, food banks, vocational schools — that could benefit from BuildRight's products, expertise, or volunteer labor, and connect them with your store manager or district community captain.
- Mentor new associates and share knowledge generously; our greatest community investment is in each other.
- Promote skilled trades education and careers by supporting local apprenticeship programs and career fairs.
- Advocate for environmentally responsible practices in your store and your personal life.
- Represent BuildRight positively in the community through professional, courteous, and civic-minded behavior at all times.

## 6. Business Segments

BuildRight operates through six interconnected business segments, each contributing to our integrated "one BuildRight" strategy.

### 6.1 Retail Stores

- **Store Count:** 2,312 stores across three countries (as of Q4 FY2025).
- **Average Store Size:** Approximately 105,000 square feet of interior selling space with an additional 25,000 square feet of outdoor garden center space.
- **Store Format:** Warehouse-style layout with high ceilings, wide aisles, and racking that extends to the sales floor for maximum product visibility.
- **SKU Breadth:** Approximately 30,000–40,000 SKUs per store, tailored to local market demographics and climate.
- **Store Departments:** Lumber & Building Materials, Plumbing, Electrical, Tools & Hardware, Paint, Flooring, Kitchen & Bath, Lawn & Garden, Appliances, Outdoor Living, Seasonal, and Millwork.
- **Hours of Operation:** Generally 6:00 AM – 9:00 PM Monday through Saturday; 8:00 AM – 8:00 PM Sunday (varies by location and local ordinance).
- **Key Differentiator:** Product knowledge delivered by associates who are empowered to spend as much time as needed with each customer.

### 6.2 BuildRight Pro (Professional / Contractor Business)

- **Overview:** BuildRight Pro serves professional contractors, remodelers, builders, property managers, and tradespeople — customers who represent a disproportionately large share of revenue.
- **Account Program:** Tiered professional account structure (Pro, Pro Plus, Pro Elite) offering volume pricing, dedicated account management, extended credit terms (Net 30/60/90), and job-site delivery.
- **BuildRight Pro Desks:** Dedicated service counters within each store staffed by Pro-focused associates with deep trade knowledge.
- **Will-Call and Delivery:** Same-day and next-day delivery options; will-call order staging for quick pickup.
- **Pro Mobile App:** A purpose-built mobile application enabling real-time inventory checks, order placement, invoice management, and project-based purchasing.
- **Estimated Pro Revenue Share:** Approximately 45% of total company revenue.
- **Pro Xtra Loyalty Program:** Points-based rewards, member-only pricing events, and tool rental discounts for enrolled professionals.

### 6.3 E-Commerce (buildright.com)

- **Platform:** buildright.com and the BuildRight mobile application.
- **Capabilities:** Buy Online, Pick Up In-Store (BOPIS); Buy Online, Deliver from Store (BODFS); Ship-to-Home; and Same-Day Express Delivery in select metropolitan areas.
- **SKU Count Online:** Over 1.2 million SKUs available online, significantly extending the assortment beyond in-store inventory.
- **Traffic:** Approximately 2.5 billion annual site visits (FY2025).
- **Digital Tools:** Project calculators (paint, flooring, lumber, concrete), how-to guides, video tutorials, live chat with product experts, visualizer tools for kitchen/bath design, and augmented reality (AR) product placement.
- **Interconnected Strategy:** Every digital transaction is connected to our store network — online orders are fulfilled from store inventory in over 90% of Ship-to-Home orders, leveraging our stores as forward fulfillment centers.

### 6.4 Installation Services

- **Overview:** BuildRight offers professional installation services for customers who prefer to have projects completed by licensed, insured, and background-checked contractors.
- **Service Categories:** Roofing, flooring, kitchen cabinets and countertops, bathroom remodels, HVAC, water heaters, electrical panel upgrades, fencing, garage doors, windows, doors, and smart home systems.
- **Process:** Customers receive a free in-home consultation, a detailed project estimate, and a single point of contact throughout the project. BuildRight manages the contractor, scheduling, permits (where required), and warranty.
- **Labor Warranty:** One-year labor warranty on all installation services, with manufacturer warranties applying to materials.
- **Revenue Contribution:** Installation services generate incremental product sales and deepen customer loyalty by providing a turnkey solution.

### 6.5 Tool & Equipment Rental

- **Overview:** BuildRight offers rental of professional-grade tools and equipment — from power drills and pressure washers to excavators and boom lifts — providing customers access to equipment they do not need to own.
- **Fleet Size:** Over 150,000 rental units across the store network.
- **Rental Durations:** Four-hour, daily, weekly, and monthly rates available.
- **Delivery & Pickup:** Equipment delivery and pickup available for large items (dumpsters, scaffolding, heavy machinery).
- **Damage Protection Plan:** Optional damage waiver available at time of rental to limit customer liability for accidental damage.
- **Key Benefit:** Converts a capital expenditure barrier into a manageable operating expense for DIYers and small contractors.

### 6.6 Commercial Business

- **Overview:** BuildRight serves institutional and commercial customers including property management companies, hospitality groups, healthcare facilities, educational institutions, and government agencies.
- **National Accounts Program:** Dedicated national account managers provide customized pricing, consolidated billing, and volume commitments for large commercial customers.
- **MRO (Maintenance, Repair, and Operations):** Broad selection of commercial-grade products for facility maintenance and operations.
- **Government Purchasing:** GSA Schedule holder and participant in state and local cooperative purchasing agreements.
- **Bid Desk:** A centralized team that handles large-quote and competitive-bid opportunities on behalf of stores.

## 9. Key Stakeholders

BuildRight is committed to creating value for all of its stakeholders. We recognize that our long-term success depends on balancing the interests of the individuals and organizations that have a stake in our business.

### 9.1 Customers

Customers are the foundation of our business. We serve three primary customer segments:

- **Do-It-Yourself (DIY) Homeowners:** Individuals and families undertaking home improvement, maintenance, and decorating projects on their own. They value product knowledge, inspiration, and approachable service.
- **Do-It-For-Me (DIFM) Homeowners:** Customers who prefer professional installation and project management. They value convenience, trust, and a single point of accountability.
- **Professional Contractors (Pro):** Licensed tradespeople, general contractors, remodelers, builders, property managers, and facility maintenance professionals. They value product availability, speed, volume pricing, and jobsite delivery.

### 9.2 Associates

Our approximately 503,000 associates are our greatest competitive advantage. We invest in their development, safety, and well-being through:

- Competitive hourly wages and annual merit-based increases.
- Comprehensive benefits: medical, dental, vision, life insurance, 401(k) with company match, tuition reimbursement, and associate stock purchase plan.
- Training programs including BuildRight University (in-store and online learning), product knowledge certifications, leadership development tracks, and skilled trades partnerships.
- Promote-from-within culture: over 85% of store managers and district managers began their careers in hourly store positions.

### 9.3 Shareholders

BuildRight is publicly traded on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol BLDG. We are committed to delivering sustainable, long-term shareholder value through:

- Consistent comparable sales growth and disciplined expense management.
- A progressive dividend policy with 15 consecutive years of dividend increases.
- Strategic share repurchase programs executed opportunistically.
- Transparent financial reporting and proactive investor communication.
- Quarterly earnings calls, annual shareholder meetings, and participation in investor conferences.

### 9.4 Suppliers and Vendor Partners

BuildRight partners with over 10,000 suppliers globally. We maintain ethical sourcing standards and seek vendor relationships built on:

- Mutual accountability for product quality, safety, and compliance.
- Competitive pricing and willingness to support promotional and seasonal programs.
- Alignment with our sustainability standards (responsible forestry, conflict mineral avoidance, fair labor practices).
- Supplier diversity commitments — we actively seek certified minority-owned, women-owned, veteran-owned, and small business suppliers.
- Collaborative innovation in product development, packaging, and logistics.

### 9.5 Communities

BuildRight is present in thousands of communities across North America. We invest in those communities through:

- The BuildRight Foundation, which has donated over $500 million since inception to affordable housing, disaster relief, and skilled trades education.
- Team BuildRight volunteer program: associates receive 16 paid volunteer hours per year.
- Partnerships with Habitat for Humanity, KaBOOM!, local food banks, veterans' organizations, and neighborhood associations.
- In-kind product donations for community projects, schools, and public spaces.

### 9.6 Regulatory Bodies

BuildRight maintains constructive relationships with regulatory authorities at the federal, state/provincial, and local levels, including:

- U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC), Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA).

- We are committed to full compliance with all applicable laws and regulations and to engaging constructively with regulators during policy development.

## 10. Company Culture

### The "BuildRight Blue" Apron

The BuildRight Blue apron is the most visible symbol of our culture and values. Worn by every store associate, the blue apron signifies readiness to serve, product expertise, and membership in the BuildRight family. It is not a uniform — it is a badge of honor. Associates earn name badges, skill pins, and service year markers displayed on their aprons, representing their growth and commitment.

### Promote-from-Within Philosophy

BuildRight is fundamentally a promote-from-within organization. We believe that the best leaders understand our business from the ground up.

- **Hourly Associate → Department Supervisor → Assistant Store Manager → Store Manager → District Manager → Regional Director → Divisional Vice President** is the most common career trajectory.
- Over 85% of current store managers were promoted from hourly positions.
- The average store manager tenure with BuildRight exceeds 12 years.
- Leadership development programs (BUILD Academy, LEAD Track, Executive Emerging Leaders) provide structured pathways for high-potential associates at every level.

### Associate Empowerment

Empowerment is not a buzzword at BuildRight — it is an operational principle.

- Associates are empowered to resolve customer issues on the spot, including price adjustments (up to $50 without supervisor approval) and returns (within policy guidelines).
- Store managers have significant local autonomy over merchandising decisions, community involvement, and staffing.
- The "Solve It Forward" program encourages associates to identify and fix problems without waiting for top-down directives.

### Continuous Learning

BuildRight invests heavily in the ongoing development of its associates:

- **BuildRight University:** A comprehensive learning management system offering over 5,000 courses covering product knowledge, customer service, leadership, safety, compliance, and professional development.
- **Product Knowledge Certifications:** Associates can earn certifications in specific departments (e.g., Certified Plumbing Expert, Certified Electrical Advisor), which are recognized with additional compensation and apron pins.
- **Tuition Reimbursement:** Up to $5,250 per year for approved degree programs and professional certifications.
- **Skilled Trades Partnerships:** Collaborations with community colleges and vocational schools to develop the next generation of skilled trades professionals.

### Associate Recognition

BuildRight recognizes outstanding performance through multiple programs:

- **Builder of the Month / Year:** Store-level recognition for associates who exemplify our values and deliver exceptional results.
- **The Calhoun Award:** Named after co-founder Arthur D. Calhoun, this is the highest associate honor, awarded annually to a small group of associates who have made extraordinary contributions to the company.
- **Service Milestone Awards:** Recognized at 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, and 35+ years of continuous service.
