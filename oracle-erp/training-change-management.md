## 2. Purpose and Scope

### 2.1 Purpose

This document establishes the comprehensive training and change management framework for the Oracle Fusion Cloud ERP implementation at BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. It ensures:

- **Successful Adoption**: All users are adequately trained and prepared for the new system
- **Change Readiness**: The organization is prepared for the cultural and process changes
- **Minimized Disruption**: Business operations continue smoothly during and after transition
- **Maximized ROI**: Full utilization of Oracle Fusion Cloud ERP capabilities
- **Sustained Proficiency**: Ongoing learning and development beyond initial training

### 2.2 Scope

This guide covers:

- All Oracle Fusion Cloud ERP modules being implemented
- All user groups across the organization (503,000 associates)
- All phases of the implementation (pre-go-live, go-live, post-go-live)
- Training delivery methods (instructor-led, virtual, eLearning, on-the-job)
- Change management activities (communication, stakeholder engagement, resistance management)

### 2.3 Guiding Principles

1. **People-Centered Approach**: Focus on user experience and adoption, not just system features
2. **Role-Based Training**: Tailored training content based on job roles and responsibilities
3. **Just-in-Time Learning**: Deliver training when users need it, not just before go-live
4. **Continuous Improvement**: Regularly assess and enhance training based on feedback
5. **Change Champion Network**: Leverage internal advocates to drive adoption

### 3.4 Resistance Management

#### 3.4.1 Common Sources of Resistance

| Resistance Type | Root Cause | Mitigation Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Fear of Change** | Uncertainty about new processes | Clear communication, training, support |
| **Loss of Competence** | Feeling less effective in new system | Adequate training, positive reinforcement |
| **Workload Concerns** | Perceived increase in work during transition | Temporary support staff, phased approach |
| **System Doubts** | Skepticism about new technology | Early demonstrations, pilot programs |
| **Peer Influence** | Negative attitudes from colleagues | Change champion network, success stories |

#### 3.4.2 Resistance Management Strategies

1. **Proactive Communication**
   - Regular updates on implementation progress
   - Transparent about challenges and solutions
   - Highlight benefits for individuals and teams

2. **Engagement and Involvement**
   - Involve users in design and testing
   - Create feedback mechanisms
   - Recognize and address concerns promptly

3. **Training and Support**
   - Comprehensive training programs
   - Multiple support channels
   - Extended support during transition

4. **Positive Reinforcement**
   - Recognize early adopters
   - Celebrate successes
   - Share positive feedback and results

### 4.3 Training Delivery Methods

#### 4.3.1 Instructor-Led Training (ILT)

**Advantages:**
- Interactive, allows for questions and discussion
- Hands-on practice with instructor guidance
- Builds relationships and community

**Disadvantages:**
- Scheduling challenges across time zones
- Higher cost per participant
- Requires physical or virtual classroom

**Usage:** Tiers 1-3, complex topics, hands-on practice

#### 4.3.2 Virtual Instructor-Led Training (VILT)

**Advantages:**
- No travel required
- Can include remote participants
- Recordable for later review

**Disadvantages:**
- Technology dependency
- Less personal interaction
- Screen fatigue

**Usage:** Tiers 2-3, regional training, refresher courses

#### 4.3.3 eLearning

**Advantages:**
- Self-paced, on-demand
- Consistent content delivery
- Scalable to large audiences

**Disadvantages:**
- Less interactive
- Requires self-discipline
- May miss nuanced questions

**Usage:** Tiers 4-5, foundational knowledge, refresher training

#### 4.3.4 Microlearning

**Advantages:**
- Short, focused content (5-10 minutes)
- Just-in-time learning
- Mobile-friendly

**Disadvantages:**
- Limited depth
- May not cover complex topics

**Usage:** Task-specific training, performance support, mobile users

#### 4.3.5 On-the-Job Training (OJT)

**Advantages:**
- Real-world context
- Immediate application
- Peer learning

**Disadvantages:**
- Quality varies by trainer
- May interrupt regular work
- Not scalable

**Usage:** Complement to formal training, new hire onboarding
