## 2. Safety Policy Statement

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **All BRHI associates** — full-time, part-time, seasonal, and temporary personnel employed by BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. at any location.
- **All contractors and subcontractors** performing work on BRHI premises or on behalf of BRHI at any location.
- **All vendors and supplier representatives** while on BRHI premises.
- **All customers and visitors** on BRHI premises, to the extent applicable for their safety and protection.

This policy governs operations at:

- All **2,300+ BRHI retail store locations** across the United States, Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Virgin Islands.
- All **90+ BRHI distribution centers (DCs)** and regional fulfillment hubs.
- All **BRHI corporate offices**, including the corporate headquarters, regional offices, and satellite offices.
- All **BRHI-owned or leased vehicles and equipment** operated on or off BRHI premises.
- Any **temporary or pop-up locations** operated by or on behalf of BRHI (e.g., off-site events, community projects, disaster response sites).

Exemptions or modifications to this policy require written approval from the Corporate Safety Director and must be documented with a risk assessment and compensating controls.

## 4. Safety Management System (ISO 45001)

BRHI's Safety Management System (SMS) is aligned with ISO 45001:2018 — Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems. The SMS provides a systematic framework for managing occupational health and safety risks, driving continual improvement, and ensuring compliance with all applicable legal and other requirements.

### 4.1 Leadership and Worker Participation

#### 4.1.1 Executive Safety Committee

The Executive Safety Committee is the senior governance body for all safety and health matters at BRHI.

- **Membership**: COO (Chair), Corporate Safety Director, VP of Store Operations, VP of Distribution, VP of Human Resources, General Counsel, Chief Medical Officer, and two rotating field representatives (one store manager, one DC manager).
- **Meeting Frequency**: Quarterly — first Wednesday of February, May, August, and November.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Review enterprise-wide safety performance data and trend analysis.
  - Approve annual safety goals and strategic initiatives.
  - Allocate safety capital and operating budgets.
  - Review findings from corporate safety audits and significant incident investigations.
  - Champion safety culture initiatives across the organization.
  - Ensure alignment of safety objectives with business strategy.

#### 4.1.2 Safety in Performance Management

- All manager-level and above performance reviews (annual and mid-year) include **safety performance as a weighted component at 10%** of the overall performance rating.
- Safety performance metrics evaluated include: recordable incident rate vs. target, open corrective actions past due, safety audit scores, training completion rates, and near-miss reporting participation.
- Stores and DCs achieving zero recordable incidents for a full fiscal year receive the **BuildRight Zero Award**, which includes a $2,500 team celebration fund and recognition at the annual leadership conference.

#### 4.1.3 Management Safety Walkarounds

- **Store management** (Store Manager, Assistant Managers) conduct a documented safety walkaround **at least once per month** using the BRHI Safety Walk Form (Form BRHI-SAF-010).
- **DC management** (DC Manager, Operations Managers) conduct a documented safety walkaround **at least twice per month** given the higher hazard profile of distribution operations.
- Walkarounds cover all operational areas, include conversations with associates about safety concerns, and result in documented findings with corrective action assignments.
- Walkaround findings are entered into the BRHI Safety Management System (SMS) within 24 hours and tracked to closure.

#### 4.1.4 Worker Participation

- **Store Safety Committees**: Each store and DC maintains a local safety committee comprising the Store Safety Captain, at least one supervisor, and at least three hourly associates. Committees meet monthly and address local hazards, review incidents, and promote safety awareness.
- **Safety Suggestion Program**: Associates may submit safety suggestions via the SMS portal, the BRHI Safety Hotline (1-800-274-4723), or physical suggestion boxes located in every break room. All suggestions receive a response within 14 business days.
- **Near-Miss Reporting**: Associates are actively encouraged to report near-misses through the non-punitive reporting program. The top reporter each quarter at each location receives recognition and a $50 gift card.
- **Stop Work Authority**: Every BRHI associate, contractor, and vendor has the authority and the responsibility to stop work when they observe an imminent hazard or unsafe condition. No disciplinary action will result from the exercise of stop work authority in good faith.

### 4.2 Planning

#### 4.2.1 Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment

BRHI uses a structured **5×5 Risk Matrix** methodology for hazard identification and risk assessment. This methodology is applied during job hazard analyses (JHAs), workplace inspections, incident investigations, and whenever new processes, equipment, or chemicals are introduced.

**Risk Matrix: Likelihood (1–5) × Severity (1–5) = Risk Score (1–25)**

**Likelihood Scale:**

| Rating | Description | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Rare | Could occur only in exceptional circumstances |
| 2 | Unlikely | Could occur at some time but not expected |
| 3 | Possible | Might occur at some time |
| 4 | Likely | Will probably occur in most circumstances |
| 5 | Almost Certain | Expected to occur frequently |

**Severity Scale:**

| Rating | Description | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Negligible | First aid only, no lost time |
| 2 | Minor | Medical treatment, no lost time |
| 3 | Moderate | Lost time injury, reversible health effect |
| 4 | Major | Serious injury, permanent disability possible |
| 5 | Catastrophic | Fatality or permanent disability |

**Risk Score Categories and Required Actions:**

| Risk Score | Category | Color | Required Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 20–25 | Critical | Red | Stop work immediately. Implement immediate controls. Notify Regional Safety Manager and Corporate Safety Director. Risk must be reduced before work resumes. | Immediate action required |
| 12–19 | High | Orange | Implement interim controls within 24 hours. Develop and implement permanent controls. Notify Regional Safety Manager. | Action within 48 hours |
| 6–11 | Medium | Yellow | Develop and implement controls. Include in area inspection program. Notify Store Safety Captain and supervisor. | Action within 30 days |
| 1–5 | Low | Green | Document hazard. Monitor during routine inspections. Include in standard operating procedures. | Monitor and review quarterly |

#### 4.2.2 Legal and Other Requirements

- BRHI maintains a regulatory register identifying all applicable federal (OSHA, EPA, DOT, CPSC), state, and local safety and health regulations.
- The Legal and Compliance team, in coordination with the Corporate Safety Director, reviews the register semi-annually and whenever new regulations are enacted.
- Changes in legal requirements are communicated to affected locations within 30 days and incorporated into policies, procedures, and training as needed.

#### 4.2.3 Safety Objectives and Targets

BRHI establishes annual enterprise-wide safety objectives. The following are the corporate targets for FY 2026:

| Objective | FY 2026 Target |
|---|---|
| Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR) | ≤ 2.0 per 200,000 hours worked |
| Lost-Time Incident Rate (LTIR) | ≤ 0.8 per 200,000 hours worked |
| Severity Rate | ≤ 15.0 per 200,000 hours worked |
| Near-Miss Reports | ≥ 5 reports per 100 associates per quarter |
| Safety Training Completion | 100% within required timeframes |
| Safety Audit Score (Corporate) | ≥ 90% at all locations |
| Open Corrective Actions Past Due | < 5% of total open actions |

Each region, district, and store cascades these targets into location-specific action plans reviewed monthly by the local safety committee and quarterly by regional leadership.

- **External Communication**: BRHI communicates safety information to contractors and vendors through site-specific orientations, contractor safety packets, and the vendor safety portal. Regulatory agencies are contacted as required by law.
- **Multilingual Communication**: All critical safety communications, signs, labels, and training materials are available in English and Spanish. Additional languages are available upon request for locations with significant non-English-speaking populations.

#### 4.3.5 Documented Information

BRHI maintains the following documented information as part of the SMS:

- **Job Hazard Analyses (JHAs)**: A JHA is completed and maintained for every task performed in BRHI operations. JHAs are reviewed annually or whenever a process change occurs. JHAs are stored in the SMS and posted at workstations where applicable.
- **Safety Procedures**: Written procedures exist for all safety programs identified in Section 6 of this policy. Procedures are reviewed annually.
- **Training Records**: All training events, attendees, test scores, and practical evaluation results are documented and retained for the duration of employment plus 5 years.
- **Inspection and Audit Records**: All inspection checklists, audit reports, and corrective action records are retained for a minimum of 5 years.
- **Incident Records**: Incident reports, investigation records, corrective actions, and OSHA logs are retained per OSHA recordkeeping requirements (current year plus 5 years).
- **SMS Document Control**: All safety documents are subject to the BRHI document control process, including version numbering, approval workflows, distribution tracking, and periodic review.

### 4.4 Operation

#### 4.4.1 Operational Controls

Operational controls are established for each department and work area to manage identified risks to acceptable levels. Controls follow the hierarchy of controls:

1. **Elimination** — Remove the hazard entirely.
2. **Substitution** — Replace the hazard with something less hazardous.
3. **Engineering Controls** — Isolate people from the hazard (e.g., machine guards, ventilation, barriers).
4. **Administrative Controls** — Change the way people work (e.g., procedures, training, signage, job rotation).
5. **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)** — Protect workers with PPE (last resort, used in conjunction with higher-order controls).

Department-specific operational controls include:

**Sales Floor**: Housekeeping standards, merchandise stacking limits, wet floor procedures, aisle clearance requirements, customer safety protocols.

**Warehouse / Receiving**: Forklift operational controls, racking inspection and maintenance, dock safety (wheel chocks, dock locks), overhead hazard protection.

**Lumber and Building Materials**: Hard hat zones, cutting station procedures, overhead protection (netting), forklift pedestrian separation.

**Garden Center**: Chemical handling (pesticides, fertilizers), heat stress management, forklift/tractor operations, seasonal staffing safety.

**Paint Department**: Ventilation requirements, mixing procedures, flammable storage, chemical spill response.

**Tool Rental**: Equipment inspection and maintenance, customer orientation on rented equipment, blade/bit inspection.

**Break Rooms and Offices**: Ergonomic workstations, kitchen safety, emergency egress.

#### 4.4.2 Emergency Preparedness

BRHI maintains comprehensive emergency preparedness programs at every location:

- **Emergency Action Plan (EAP)**: Every location has a written EAP tailored to the facility, covering fire, severe weather, medical emergency, chemical spill, active threat, and power outage. See Section 9 for detailed procedures.
- **Emergency Drills**: Drills are conducted quarterly, rotating through the following scenarios over the course of a year:
  - Q1: Fire/evacuation drill
  - Q2: Severe weather/tornado drill
  - Q3: Medical emergency/AED drill
  - Q4: Active threat/lockdown drill (tabletop exercise for management, shelter-in-place drill for associates)
- **Drill Documentation**: All drills are documented with date, time, scenario, participants, observations, response times, and areas for improvement. Drill records are maintained in the SMS and reviewed by the Regional Safety Manager.
- **Emergency Equipment**: All emergency equipment (fire extinguishers, AEDs, first aid kits, spill kits, emergency lighting) is inspected per the schedules defined in this policy and maintained in ready condition.

#### 4.4.3 Management of Change (MOC)

Any change that could affect workplace safety is subject to the Management of Change process before implementation. Changes requiring MOC include:

- New equipment or machinery introduction.
- Modification to existing equipment or processes.
- New chemicals or significant changes in chemical quantities.
- Organizational changes affecting safety responsibilities.
- Facility modifications, construction, or renovation.
- Changes to operating procedures or work practices.

**MOC Process:**

1. **Initiation**: The requesting party completes the MOC Request Form (BRHI-SAF-020) describing the proposed change, rationale, and timeline.
2. **Risk Assessment**: The Store Safety Captain or District Safety Coordinator conducts a risk assessment using the 5×5 risk matrix to identify new hazards and evaluate controls.
3. **Review and Approval**: MOC requests are reviewed and approved as follows:
   - Low/Medium risk changes: Store Manager approval.
   - High risk changes: Regional Safety Manager approval.
   - Critical risk changes: Corporate Safety Director approval.
4. **Communication**: Approved changes are communicated to all affected associates before implementation.
5. **Training**: Any new training requirements identified by the MOC are completed before the change goes live.
6. **Verification**: Within 30 days of implementation, the change is evaluated to confirm that controls are effective and no new hazards have been introduced.

#### 4.5.2 Lagging Indicators

BRHI tracks the following lagging indicators:

| Indicator | Definition | FY 2026 Target |
|---|---|---|

### 4.6 Improvement

#### 4.6.1 Incident Investigation

All incidents — injuries, illnesses, near-misses, property damage, and environmental releases — are investigated using the following framework:

- **Tier 1 — First Aid / Near-Miss**: Investigated by the Department Supervisor using the abbreviated incident form. 5-Why analysis is encouraged but not required. Completed within 48 hours.
- **Tier 2 — Medical Treatment / Restricted Duty**: Investigated by the Store Safety Captain using the full incident investigation form. 5-Why root cause analysis required. Completed within 48 hours.
- **Tier 3 — Lost Time / Hospitalization / Serious Near-Miss**: Investigated by the District Safety Coordinator and Regional Safety Manager. 5-Why root cause analysis plus contributing factor analysis required. Completed within 48 hours. Corporate Safety Director notified.
- **Tier 4 — Fatality / Catastrophic Event**: Investigated by the Corporate Safety Director with support from an external investigation team. Root cause analysis using multiple methodologies (5-Why, Fishbone, Fault Tree) required. COO and CEO notified immediately. Legal counsel engaged.

#### 4.6.2 Corrective and Preventive Actions

- All identified corrective and preventive actions are entered into the SMS Corrective Action Tracking module.
- Each action is assigned a responsible party, a specific due date based on risk priority, and a verification method.
- **Critical risk**: Due within 7 days. **High risk**: Due within 14 days. **Medium risk**: Due within 30 days. **Low risk**: Due within 90 days.
- Overdue actions are escalated automatically: 7 days overdue → District Safety Coordinator; 14 days overdue → Regional Safety Manager; 30 days overdue → Corporate Safety Director.
- Actions are not closed until verified as effective by the responsible safety team member.

#### 4.6.3 Lessons Learned

- All Tier 2 and above incident investigations generate a Lessons Learned Brief (1-page summary) within 14 days of investigation completion.
- Lessons Learned Briefs are distributed to all locations via the SMS, the monthly safety newsletter, and the safety team email distribution list.
- Regional Safety Managers are required to discuss relevant Lessons Learned in monthly regional safety calls.
- Store Safety Captains are required to present relevant Lessons Learned at monthly store safety meetings.

#### 4.6.4 Continuous Improvement Projects

- Each region is required to initiate at least **two continuous improvement projects** per fiscal year targeting specific safety hazards or trends identified through data analysis.
- Projects follow the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycle with documented objectives, implementation steps, measurement criteria, and results.
- Successful projects are evaluated for enterprise-wide implementation and recognized at the annual Safety Awards ceremony.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### 5.1 Corporate Safety Director

- **Position Level**: Vice President, reporting directly to the Chief Operating Officer (COO).
- **Budget Authority**: $5 million annual operating budget for the Safety Department.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Develop and maintain the enterprise-wide safety and health strategy, policies, and programs.
  - Direct the activities of the 6 Regional Safety Managers and 52 District Safety Coordinators.
  - Serve as BRHI's primary liaison with federal and state OSHA, EPA, and other regulatory agencies.
  - Chair the Technical Safety Subcommittee and serve as a standing member of the Executive Safety Committee.
  - Author and submit the annual safety budget, business plan, and capital request.
  - Lead the investigation of all Tier 4 incidents (fatality, catastrophe) and provide oversight for Tier 3 investigations.
  - Commission and oversee the annual corporate safety audit program.
  - Represent BRHI in industry safety organizations and forums.
  - Ensure the SMS maintains alignment with ISO 45001 and all applicable standards.
  - Report safety performance to the Board of Directors on a quarterly basis.
  - Approve all safety policies, critical-risk MOCs, and major corrective action plans.

### 5.2 Regional Safety Manager

- **Span of Control**: 6 Regional Safety Managers, each supporting **20–25 stores and 2–4 distribution centers** within their assigned region.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Execute the BRHI safety strategy within the assigned region.
  - Conduct **monthly safety audits** of assigned locations using the 100-point Regional Audit Checklist. Ensure all locations are audited at least once per quarter.
  - Support incident investigations for Tier 2 and Tier 3 events. Lead Tier 3 investigations within the region.
  - Deliver specialized safety training programs including forklift train-the-trainer, hazcom, confined space, and hot work.
  - Track and report regional safety metrics (leading and lagging indicators) on a monthly basis.
  - Mentor and develop District Safety Coordinators and Store Safety Captains.
  - Coordinate with regional operations leadership on safety concerns, corrective actions, and capital projects.
  - Conduct post-incident follow-up inspections and verify effectiveness of corrective actions.
  - Facilitate regional safety committee meetings quarterly.
  - Review and approve High-risk Management of Change requests within the region.
  - Maintain relationships with local regulatory agencies, fire departments, and emergency services.

### 5.3 District Safety Coordinator

- **Span of Control**: 52 District Safety Coordinators, each supporting **8–12 stores** within their assigned district.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Conduct **bi-weekly store visits** (each assigned store visited at least twice per month) for safety observation, coaching, and support.
  - Track and report district-level safety metrics including TRIR, LTIR, training completion, audit scores, and corrective action status.
  - Follow up on all open corrective actions within the district, ensuring timely closure and verification.
  - Support Store Safety Captains in incident investigation and root cause analysis.
  - Coordinate safety training scheduling and delivery within the district.
  - Conduct or assist with Tier 2 and Tier 3 incident investigations as directed by the Regional Safety Manager.
  - Review and approve store-level emergency action plans and ensure drill compliance.
  - Serve as the primary safety resource for store management teams within the district.
  - Escalate unresolved safety issues to the Regional Safety Manager.
  - Participate in district operations meetings and provide safety performance updates.

### 5.4 Store Safety Captain

- **Position**: Hourly associate (non-exempt) selected by the Store Manager from the store's associate pool. Receives a **$1.50 per hour premium** for the duration of the assignment.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Conduct a **daily safety walkthrough** of the store using the 10-point Daily Safety Checklist before the store opens to the public.
  - Identify and document hazards during walkthroughs; initiate corrective action or escalate as needed.
  - Deliver **new hire safety orientation** for all new associates within the first day of employment (the department-specific portion is delivered in coordination with the department supervisor).
  - Serve as a **first aid responder**; maintain current first aid and CPR/AED certification.
  - Facilitate the **monthly store safety meeting** (minimum 30 minutes, documented attendance).
  - Serve as the store-level point of contact for safety questions, concerns, and suggestions.
  - Maintain the store's safety bulletin board with current postings including: OSHA 300A summary (during posting period), emergency contact information, safety data sheet location notice, safety meeting minutes, and safety recognition.
  - Coordinate quarterly emergency drills with store management.
  - Monitor PPE inventory and coordinate replenishment with department supervisors.
  - Participate in weekly store management meetings to provide safety updates.
  - Investigate Tier 1 and Tier 2 incidents using the 5-Why methodology.

### 5.5 Department Supervisors

- **Responsibilities**:
  - Conduct a **daily pre-shift safety huddle** (3–5 minutes) with all department associates. Cover one safety topic, review any new hazards or procedure changes, and address associate safety concerns.
  - Enforce all Job Hazard Analyses (JHAs) and safety procedures within the department.
  - Serve as the **first responder** to all incidents within the department: secure the scene, provide first aid, notify the Store Safety Captain and store management.
  - Ensure **PPE compliance** within the department: verify associates are wearing required PPE, issue replacement PPE, and address non-compliance.
  - Complete **daily department safety observations** and document findings.
  - Ensure all associates in the department have completed required safety training.
  - Maintain a clean, organized, and hazard-free work area.
  - Complete incident report forms within **4 hours** of any incident.
  - Enforce housekeeping standards including aisle clearance, proper storage, and spill cleanup.
  - Report all safety concerns, hazards, and incidents to the Store Safety Captain and store management promptly.

### 5.6 All Associates

Every BRHI associate has the following safety responsibilities:

- **Comply** with all BRHI safety rules, policies, procedures, and applicable regulations at all times.
- **Report hazards** immediately to a supervisor, the Store Safety Captain, or through any reporting channel.
- **Participate** in all required safety training and demonstrate competency.
- **Use** required personal protective equipment (PPE) properly and report any damaged or ill-fitting PPE for replacement.
- **Report** all injuries, illnesses, and near-misses to a supervisor promptly (injuries within 1 hour; near-misses within 24 hours).
- **Exercise stop work authority** when observing an imminent danger or unsafe condition.
- **Cooperate** with incident investigations and provide honest, complete information.
- **Maintain** a clean and organized work area.
- **Attend** all required safety meetings and drills.
- **Look out** for the safety of fellow associates, contractors, and customers.
- **Suggest** improvements to safety practices through the safety suggestion program.

### 5.7 Contractors

All contractors performing work on BRHI premises are responsible for:

- Completing **site-specific safety orientation** (30 minutes, documented) before beginning any work at any BRHI location.
- **Complying** with all BRHI safety rules, policies, and procedures while on BRHI premises, in addition to their own company's safety program.
- **Reporting** all incidents, injuries, near-misses, and property damage to BRHI store management immediately upon occurrence.
- Providing **proof of insurance** meeting BRHI minimum requirements (see Section 12) before beginning work.
- Providing **proof of training** and certification for all specialized work (e.g., forklift, electrical, hot work, confined space).
- Designating a **competent person** on site at all times during work activities.
- Providing all necessary PPE for their employees at no cost to BRHI.
- Complying with all BRHI Management of Change requirements.
- Cooperating with BRHI safety audits and inspections.

## 6. Specific Safety Programs

### 6.1 Forklift / Powered Industrial Truck (PIT) Safety

#### 6.1.1 Operator Certification

- No associate may operate any powered industrial truck (forklift, reach truck, order picker, pallet jack, turret truck) without a valid BRHI Forklift Operator Certification.
- **Initial Certification**: Requires successful completion of the BRHI Forklift Operator Training Program:
  - **Classroom training** (2 hours): covers forklift types, operating principles, load capacity charts, stability triangle/ trapezoid, hazard recognition, and BRHI-specific rules.
  - **Written examination**: minimum **80% passing score**; associates scoring below 80% must retake the classroom training and examination.
  - **Practical evaluation** (1 hour): supervised operation in the associate's work area, including pre-trip inspection, traveling, maneuvering, load handling, stacking, dock operations, and pedestrian interaction. Evaluated by a BRHI-certified forklift trainer using the Practical Evaluation Score Sheet.
- **Refresher Certification**: Required every **3 years** from the date of initial certification. Also required immediately after any of the following:
  - The operator is involved in an incident or near-miss.
  - The operator is observed operating unsafely.
  - The operator is assigned to a different type of PIT.
  - Conditions in the workplace change in a way that could affect safe operation.
- **Temporary/Seasonal Associates**: Must present a valid forklift certification from a prior employer (verifiable) or complete the full BRHI certification program before operating. No exceptions.

#### 6.1.2 Pre-Trip Inspection

- Every forklift operator must complete a **17-point pre-trip inspection checklist** (Form BRHI-SAF-030) before operating the equipment at the start of each shift.
- **Checklist Items**: (1) Fluid levels (oil, hydraulic, coolant), (2) Fluid leaks, (3) Tire condition and pressure, (4) Fork condition (bends, cracks, wear), (5) Mast and chain condition, (6) Hydraulic hoses and fittings, (7) Seatbelt condition and function, (8) Horn function, (9) Lights (headlights, tail lights, strobe), (10) Backup alarm function, (11) Steering responsiveness, (12) Brake function (service and parking), (13) Lift and tilt function, (14) Load backrest condition, (15) Overhead guard condition, (16) Data plate legible, (17) Overall cleanliness and condition.
- Any defect affecting safe operation (e.g., brake failure, steering malfunction, hydraulic leak, inoperable horn, damaged forks) results in the forklift being **immediately locked out and tagged out** until repaired. The operator notifies the supervisor and completes a maintenance request.
- Non-critical defects (e.g., minor cosmetic damage, non-functional non-essential light) are documented and reported to maintenance for repair but do not necessarily prevent operation at the supervisor's discretion.

#### 6.1.3 Operating Rules

- **Speed Limits**:
  - **5 mph** in warehouse aisles and open floor areas.
  - **3 mph** at intersections, blind corners, and pedestrian crossings.
  - **2 mph** on loading docks and near building entrances/exits.
- Operators must **sound the horn** at every intersection, blind corner, and when approaching pedestrian traffic.
- **Seatbelts** must be worn at all times during operation. The forklift must not be started until the seatbelt is fastened.
- **No passengers** are permitted on any forklift or PIT at any time, including on the forks, the counterweight, or riding on the exterior of the machine.
- Forks must be traveled with the forks **lowered** (approximately 4–6 inches above the floor), tilted slightly back, with the mast tilted back.
- Operators must maintain a **minimum following distance** of three truck lengths from other PITs.
- Loading dock operations: dock plate/bridge must be secured and rated for the load before forklift crossing. Wheel chocks or dock locks must be engaged on the trailer before forklift entry.
- Refueling and recharging: performed only in designated, well-ventilated areas. Engine off and parking brake set for propane tank changes. No smoking or open flames within 25 feet of refueling/recharging areas.

### 6.2 Ladder Safety

#### 6.2.1 Ladder Inspection

- Every ladder must be inspected before each use. The inspection covers:
  - **Rungs**: secure, not bent, broken, or missing; free of oil, grease, or other slip hazards.
  - **Rails**: straight, not bent, cracked, or split; no visible damage or deterioration.
  - **Feet**: present, not worn, properly functioning (non-slip pads intact).
  - **Labels**: present and legible (duty rating, manufacturer instructions, safety warnings).
  - **Spreader bars** (step ladders): fully functional, not bent; locking mechanisms engaged when ladder is opened.
  - **Rope and pulley** (extension ladders): functional, not frayed.
  - **Hardware**: all rivets, bolts, and joints tight and secure.
- Ladders failing inspection must be **immediately removed from service** and tagged "DO NOT USE" with the date and inspector's name. Damaged ladders are destroyed or repaired by a qualified person — no field repairs permitted.

#### 6.2.2 Ladder Use Requirements

- **3-point contact** must be maintained at all times while ascending, descending, or working on a ladder. This means two feet and one hand, or two hands and one foot, must be in contact with the ladder at all times.
- Associates must **never stand on**:
  - The **top cap** of a step ladder.
  - The **top 3 rungs** of an extension ladder or straight ladder.
  - The **paint shelf** or **tool tray** of a step ladder.
- Straight and extension ladders must be set up at the proper angle (base-to-height ratio of **1:4** — for every 4 feet of height, the base is placed 1 foot away from the wall).
- Extension ladders must **extend at least 3 feet above** the upper landing surface to provide a handhold for mounting and dismounting.
- Ladders must be placed on a firm, level, and stable surface. No boxes, blocks, or other unstable objects may be used to level a ladder or gain additional height.
- Only one person on a ladder at a time.
- Face the ladder when ascending or descending.
- Carry tools in a belt or hoist them up with a rope — do not carry tools in hand while climbing.

#### 6.2.3 Fall Protection

- **Fall protection (harness and lanyard)** is required when working at heights **above 4 feet** in any BRHI facility. This includes work from ladders, platforms, racks, and rooftops.
- Fall protection equipment must meet ANSI Z359.1 standards and be inspected before each use.
- Lanyards must be tied off to an anchor point rated for at least **5,000 pounds** and positioned so that a free fall does not exceed **6 feet**.
- Damaged or defective fall protection equipment is immediately removed from service and destroyed.

### 6.3 Electrical Safety

#### 6.3.1 General Requirements

- All electrical work and electrical safety practices at BRHI comply with **NFPA 70E** (Standard for Electrical Safety in the Workplace) and the National Electrical Code (NEC).
- **Arc flash labels** are affixed to all electrical panels, switchboards, motor control centers, and industrial control panels. Labels identify the incident energy level, arc flash boundary, and required PPE for the hazard category.
- Electrical panels and equipment must have a **minimum 36-inch clearance** in front (42 inches for systems above 600 volts) and 30 inches of clear width, maintained at all times. No storage is permitted in electrical rooms.

#### 6.3.2 Lockout / Tagout (LOTO)

- BRHI maintains written energy control (lockout/tagout) procedures for all maintenance and servicing activities involving hazardous energy (electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, thermal, chemical, gravitational).
- **Each authorized worker applies their own individually keyed lock and tag** to each energy isolation device. Multiple workers performing simultaneous servicing each apply their own lock — no exceptions.
- **LOTO Procedure**:
  1. Notify all affected associates of the lockout.
  2. Shut down the equipment using normal stopping procedures.
  3. Isolate all energy sources (disconnect, close valves, block mechanical motion).
  4. Apply personal lock(s) and tag(s) to each energy isolation device.
  5. Dissipate or restrain any stored or residual energy (capacitors, springs, elevated loads, pressurized systems).
  6. Verify isolation by attempting to restart the equipment using normal operating controls. Return controls to off position after verification.
  7. Perform the maintenance or servicing work.
  8. Upon completion, remove tools and materials, replace guards, verify all personnel are clear, remove personal locks and tags, and restore energy.
- **Group LOTO**: When multiple crafts or crews perform work, a **Group Lockout** procedure is used. Each authorized worker applies a personal lock to a group lockout hasp. A designated LOTO coordinator manages the process.
- **Annual LOTO Procedure Audit**: Each written LOTO procedure is audited at least annually by the authorized workers who use it, observed by a supervisor, to verify that the procedure is accurate and followed correctly.

#### 6.3.3 Qualified Persons

- Only **qualified persons** may perform electrical work at BRHI. A qualified person is defined as one who has received documented training in and has demonstrated skills and knowledge in the construction and operation of electric equipment and installations, and the hazards involved.
- **Qualification Requirements**: Completion of BRHI Electrical Safety Training (8 hours initial, 4 hours annual refresher), demonstrated competency on practical evaluation, and documented experience working on or near energized electrical systems.
- **Insulated tools** rated for the voltage being worked on are required for all electrical work. Insulated tools must be inspected before each use and tested per the manufacturer's recommendations.

#### 6.3.4 Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI) Protection

- GFCI protection is required for **all outdoor electrical outlets and circuits**, and for all outlets in **wet or damp locations** (including garden centers, washdown areas, restrooms, and kitchens).
- Portable GFCI devices are available at all locations for use with extension cords and temporary power in areas without permanent GFCI protection.
- GFCI devices are tested monthly using the built-in test button, with results documented on the GFCI test log.

### 6.4 Hazardous Materials / Hazard Communication

#### 6.4.1 Safety Data Sheets (SDS)

- BRHI maintains **GHS-compliant Safety Data Sheets** for all hazardous chemicals present in the workplace.
- SDS are maintained in two locations for each department:
  - A **physical SDS binder** located in each department, clearly marked, and accessible to all associates during all work hours.
  - An **electronic SDS database** accessible via any BRHI workstation or the BRHI Safety mobile app, available in English and Spanish.
- SDS are obtained from chemical manufacturers or distributors upon first shipment and updated whenever a revised SDS is received.
- No chemical may be brought into any BRHI facility without a current SDS on file.

#### 6.4.2 Training

- **Right-to-Know (Hazard Communication) training** is provided to all associates within **30 days of hire** and refreshed annually.
- Training covers: GHS label elements, SDS format and content (16 sections), physical and health hazards of chemicals in the associate's work area, protective measures, and emergency procedures.
- **Department-specific chemical training** is provided by the department supervisor for chemicals unique to the department (e.g., paint department — solvents, thinners, stains; garden center — pesticides, herbicides, fertilizers; lumber — treated wood, adhesives).

#### 6.4.3 Chemical Inventory

- Each location maintains a **chemical inventory list** identifying all hazardous chemicals present, the quantity on hand, the storage location, and the associated SDS reference.
- The inventory is **updated monthly** by the Store Safety Captain in coordination with department supervisors.
- The chemical inventory is available to associates, contractors, emergency responders, and regulatory agencies upon request.

#### 6.4.4 Labeling

- All chemical containers must be labeled. Original manufacturer containers must retain the GHS-compliant label.
- **Secondary containers** (spray bottles, dispensing containers, mixing vessels) must be labeled with the product identifier and the primary hazard warning(s) at a minimum. BRHI provides pre-printed secondary container labels at no cost.
- Unlabeled containers are immediately removed from use and identified or disposed of properly.

#### 6.4.5 Spill Kits

- Spill kits are located at designated locations throughout each BRHI facility based on the types and quantities of chemicals present:
  - **Lumber department**: general-purpose absorbent spill kit (for oils, fuels, solvents).
  - **Paint department**: solvent-specific spill kit (for paints, stains, thinners, solvents).
  - **Garden center**: pesticide/herbicide spill kit (for pesticides, herbicides, fertilizers, pool chemicals).
- Each spill kit is inventoried monthly and restocked after any use.
- Associates in departments with spill kits are trained in spill kit deployment annually.
- Small spills that can be safely cleaned up by trained associates using the spill kit are addressed immediately. Large spills, spills of unknown substances, or spills presenting fire, health, or environmental hazards require evacuation of the area and notification of store management and emergency services (call 911).

### 6.5 Ergonomics

#### 6.5.1 Lifting Limits and Requirements

| Weight Range | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **0–50 lbs** | Solo lift permitted. Use proper lifting technique (bend at knees, keep load close to body, avoid twisting). |
| **50–75 lbs** | **Team lift required** — minimum 2 associates. Both associates must be trained in team lifting procedures. |
| **Above 75 lbs** | **Mechanical aid required** — forklift, pallet jack, hand truck, dolly, cart, hoist, or other mechanical device. No manual lifting above 75 lbs under any circumstances. |

- **Team lift training** is included in the new hire safety orientation (30-minute module) and refreshed annually.
- Proper lifting technique signs are posted in all receiving areas, warehouse areas, and heavy merchandise departments.

#### 6.5.2 Repetitive Motion Assessments

- **Cashiers**: Anti-fatigue mats are provided at all cashier stations. Cash register platforms are adjustable for associate height. Associates are encouraged to alternate between scanning and bagging tasks. Stretch breaks are provided every 2 hours during shifts exceeding 4 hours at the register. Ergonomic assessments of cashier stations are conducted annually by the District Safety Coordinator.
- **Garden Center**: Associates performing repetitive tasks (potting, watering, loading) are rotated between tasks at least every 2 hours to reduce cumulative strain. Ergonomic tools (padded grips, long-handled implements) are provided. Stretching guidance is posted in the garden center break area.
- **Receiving / Warehouse**: Associates performing repetitive lifting are rotated between tasks. Adjustable-height work platforms are provided for palletizing and depalletizing operations.
- Any associate experiencing symptoms of musculoskeletal discomfort (pain, numbness, tingling, swelling) is encouraged to report to their supervisor and the Store Safety Captain for an ergonomic evaluation.

### 6.6 Slips, Trips, and Falls Prevention

#### 6.6.1 Housekeeping Standard

- All aisles, walkways, and work areas must be kept **clear and unobstructed at all times**. This applies to both the sales floor and back-of-house areas.
- Merchandise, product, and materials must be stored in designated areas only — not in aisles, walkways, or emergency egress paths.
- Tools, equipment, and cords must be returned to their designated storage locations when not in active use.
- Cords and hoses crossing walkways must be covered with cord covers or taped down securely.
- Trash, debris, and packing materials must be removed promptly and placed in designated waste containers.

#### 6.6.2 Wet Floor Procedure

- When a wet floor condition is identified (spill, cleaning, weather-related):
  1. **Immediately** place yellow "Wet Floor" warning signs (minimum of 2 signs bracketing the wet area) and a floor mat if available.
  2. **Clean up the liquid within 5 minutes** of identification using appropriate methods (mop, absorbent, wet vacuum).
  3. Maintain signs until the floor is completely dry.
  4. Report the cause of the wet floor condition to the supervisor for corrective action (e.g., repair leaking fixture, redirect downspout).
- Wet floor signs are located at every entrance, in every department, and in all restroom areas.

#### 6.6.3 Safety Footwear

- **ASTM F2413** compliant safety footwear (steel-toe or composite-toe) is required for all associates working on the **sales floor, warehouse, receiving, garden center, lumber department, and any area with forklift traffic**.
- Safety footwear must have impact resistance (I/75), compression resistance (C/75), and slip-resistant soles.
- BRHI provides a safety footwear allowance of **$150 per year** per associate through the BRHI Safety Footwear Program. Associates may purchase approved footwear through the BRHI online portal or at any authorized retailer and submit for reimbursement.
- Associates reporting to work without required safety footwear are reassigned to a non-floor position for the shift (if available) or sent home with pay pending compliance.

#### 6.6.4 Seasonal Weather

- **Snow and Ice Removal Plan**: Each store maintains a snow and ice removal plan specific to its geographic location:
  - Parking lots and walkways are treated and cleared to be **walkable by 30 minutes before store opening**.
  - Ice melt is applied to all entrances, walkways, and loading areas at the start of each shift during freezing conditions.
  - Mops and signs are stationed at every entrance during wet weather.
  - Parking lot inspections are conducted at the start of each shift during winter months.
  - Contractors are pre-arranged for heavy snowfall events exceeding the store's self-clearance capability.

### 6.7 Machine Guarding

- All machines and equipment with moving parts that could cause injury (saws, drills, conveyors, compacters, balers) must have **guards in place during operation**. Guards must prevent contact with moving parts, prevent objects from falling into moving parts, and not create additional hazards.
- **Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)** is required before any maintenance, adjustment, cleaning, or servicing of machinery. Guards may only be removed for these activities after the equipment is locked out and tagged out per Section 6.3.2.
- **Monthly Inspection**: A documented monthly inspection of all machine guards is conducted using the Machine Guard Inspection Checklist (Form BRHI-SAF-035). The inspection verifies guard integrity, secure attachment, proper adjustment, and absence of bypassed guards.
- **Guard Removal**: Guards may only be removed with **supervisor approval** and documented justification. The removal must be authorized via a short-duration work permit that specifies the reason, the compensating controls in place, and the expected duration. Guards must be replaced immediately upon completion of the activity.

### 6.8 Fire Prevention

#### 6.8.1 Fire Extinguishers

- Fire extinguishers are located so that no point in any BRHI facility is more than **75 feet of travel distance** from an extinguisher.
- Extinguishers are selected based on the hazards in the area:
  - **ABC dry chemical**: general areas, sales floor, offices, break rooms.
  - **CO2**: electrical rooms, server rooms.
  - **Class K wet chemical**: kitchen areas with deep fryers or cooking oil.
- **Monthly Visual Inspection**: A designated associate visually inspects every extinguisher monthly and initials the inspection tag. The inspection verifies: extinguisher is in its designated location, is fully charged (gauge in the green zone), has an intact tamper seal, has no visible damage or corrosion, and the label is legible.
- **Annual Professional Inspection and Certification**: A licensed fire protection contractor inspects and certifies all extinguishers annually. This includes a thorough examination, internal inspection (if due per NFPA 10 schedule), weighing, and any necessary maintenance or recharging. A certification tag is affixed upon completion.

#### 6.8.2 Sprinkler Systems

- All BRHI facilities are equipped with automatic fire sprinkler systems designed and maintained in accordance with NFPA 13.
- **Quarterly Flow Test**: Sprinkler systems are flow-tested quarterly by a licensed fire protection contractor to verify proper operation, alarm function, and water supply adequacy. Results are documented and maintained on file.
- **18-Inch Clearance Rule**: A minimum of **18 inches of clear space** must be maintained below all sprinkler heads at all times. No merchandise, signage, decorations, or other items may encroach within this zone. This rule is enforced through daily walkthrough inspections.
- Sprinkler heads are inspected annually for corrosion, damage, paint accumulation, and proper orientation.

#### 6.8.3 Evacuation Plans and Fire Drills

- **Evacuation plans** are posted at all exits, break rooms, and common areas. Plans show primary and secondary evacuation routes, assembly areas, fire extinguisher locations, AED locations, and emergency contact numbers.
- **Fire drills** are conducted **semi-annually** (at least once per 6-month period). Drills are unannounced and timed. Evacuation of the entire facility must be completed within the target time established for the facility (typically 3–5 minutes depending on facility size).
- Drill results are documented and reviewed for improvement opportunities. Issues identified (blocked exits, malfunctioning alarms, confusion about assembly points) are corrected before the next drill.

### 6.9 Hot Work

#### 6.9.1 Permit Requirements

- A **Hot Work Permit** is required for all welding, cutting, brazing, soldering, grinding, and any other activity producing sparks, flames, or heat outside of designated hot work areas. The permit is issued by the **Store Safety Captain** (or designee) before work begins.
- The permit is valid only for the date, time, location, and specific activity specified.

#### 6.9.2 Hot Work Safety Requirements

- A **fire extinguisher** (minimum 2A:10B:C rated) must be positioned within **10 feet** of the hot work operation.
- A **35-foot clearance** of all combustible materials must be maintained around the hot work area. Combustible materials that cannot be removed must be protected with fire-resistant blankets or shields.
- A **fire watch** must be maintained during hot work and for a minimum of **30 minutes after hot work completion**. The fire watch person must have fire extinguisher training and must continuously monitor the work area and all adjacent areas (including above and below if applicable) for signs of fire.
- Hot work is not permitted in areas with active sprinkler system impairments.
- Hot work permits are posted at the work site during the operation and returned to the Store Safety Captain upon completion of the fire watch period.

### 6.10 Confined Space

#### 6.10.1 Identification and Labeling

- All **permit-required confined spaces** at BRHI facilities are identified, evaluated, and labeled with signage reading "DANGER — PERMIT-REQUIRED CONFINED SPACE — DO NOT ENTER" or equivalent wording.
- Typical permit-required confined spaces at BRHI facilities include: **manholes, underground utility vaults, storage tanks, silos, elevator pits, and certain process vessels**.
- A facility-specific confined space inventory is maintained at each location and reviewed annually.

#### 6.10.2 Entry Requirements

- **Entry Permit**: A confined space entry permit must be completed and signed by the **Operations Manager** (or designated entry supervisor) before any entry into a permit-required confined space. The permit identifies the space, the reason for entry, the hazards, the controls, the entrants, the attendant, and the duration.
- **Attendant**: A trained attendant is stationed at the entry point at all times during entry. The attendant maintains a log of entrants, monitors conditions inside and outside the space, maintains communication with entrants, and orders evacuation if conditions change.
- **Rescue Plan**: A rescue plan is established and reviewed with all participants before entry. The plan identifies rescue personnel, equipment, and procedures. Non-entry rescue is preferred; entry rescue is performed only by trained and equipped rescue teams.
- **Atmospheric Testing**: The atmosphere inside the confined space is tested **before entry and continuously during entry** for oxygen content (acceptable range: 19.5%–23.5%), flammable gases and vapors (below 10% of the Lower Explosive Limit), and toxic contaminants (below applicable Permissible Exposure Limits). Testing is performed with a calibrated, direct-reading gas monitor.
- **Ventilation**: Continuous mechanical ventilation is provided during all confined space entries.
- Entry permits are closed and retained upon completion of the entry.

### 6.11 Bloodborne Pathogens

#### 6.11.1 Exposure Control Plan

- BRHI maintains a written **Exposure Control Plan** for bloodborne pathogens, updated **annually** and accessible to all associates.
- The plan identifies job classifications and tasks with potential occupational exposure to blood or other potentially infectious materials (OPIM), including designated first aid responders, Store Safety Captains, and associates in roles where exposure is reasonably anticipated.

#### 6.11.2 Universal Precautions

- **Universal precautions** are observed for all contact with blood and body fluids. All blood and body fluids are treated as if infectious.
- Associates encountering blood or body fluid spills must not attempt cleanup unless they have received bloodborne pathogen training and have appropriate PPE. Trained associates and custodial staff use the biohazard cleanup kit.

#### 6.11.3 Personal Protective Equipment

- The following PPE is included in all first aid kits and biohazard cleanup kits:
  - **Disposable gloves** (nitrile or latex-free).
  - **Eye protection** (safety goggles or face shield).
  - **CPR mask** (one-way valve barrier device).
  - **Disposable gowns** and **aprons** in biohazard kits.
  - **Red biohazard bags** and **sharps containers** for disposal.

#### 6.11.4 Vaccination

- **Hepatitis B vaccination** is offered at no cost to all designated first aid responders and any associate whose job duties involve reasonably anticipated exposure to blood or OPIM.
- Vaccination is offered within 10 days of initial assignment to a covered role.
- Associates may decline vaccination by signing the Hepatitis B Declination Form. Declining associates may request the vaccination at any later date.

#### 6.11.5 Exposure Incident Response

- In the event of an exposure incident (needlestick, splash to mucous membranes, contact with non-intact skin):
  1. Immediately wash the exposed area with soap and water (mucous membranes: flush with water).
  2. Report the exposure to the supervisor immediately.
  3. Seek medical evaluation at the designated occupational health provider within **2 hours** of exposure.
  4. The supervisor completes an Exposure Incident Report.
  5. The source individual's blood is tested for HBV, HCV, and HIV (with consent or as permitted by law).
  6. Follow-up evaluation and treatment are provided per the Exposure Control Plan and the attending physician's recommendations.

### 6.12 Noise and Hearing Conservation

#### 6.12.1 Monitoring

- Noise monitoring (area dosimetry and/or personal dosimetry) is conducted whenever associate noise exposure may exceed **85 dB TWA** (Time-Weighted Average) as an 8-hour action level.
- Monitoring is performed during typical operations by the District Safety Coordinator or a certified industrial hygienist.
- Results are communicated to affected associates within 15 days.

#### 6.12.2 Hearing Conservation Program

- Associates exposed at or above the 85 dB TWA action level are enrolled in the BRHI Hearing Conservation Program, which includes:
  - **Annual audiometric testing**: Baseline audiogram within 6 months of first exposure; annual audiograms thereafter. Testing is performed by a licensed audiometric technician using a calibrated audiometer in a sound-treated booth.
  - **Hearing protection**: A variety of hearing protectors is made available at no cost to enrolled associates. Options include foam earplugs, pre-molded earplugs, and over-the-ear muffs.
  - **Training**: Annual training on the effects of noise on hearing, the purpose and proper use of hearing protectors, and the purpose and procedures of audiometric testing.
  - **Recordkeeping**: Noise exposure records and audiometric testing records are maintained for the duration of the associate's employment.

#### 6.12.3 Posted Areas

- Areas where noise levels are known to regularly exceed 85 dB are posted with signs: **"HEARING PROTECTION REQUIRED — NOISE HAZARD."**
- Typical posted areas at BRHI locations include: **lumber mills, equipment rental areas, compactor/baler rooms, and certain manufacturing or processing areas**.

### 6.13 Temperature Stress

#### 6.13.1 Heat Illness Prevention

The Heat Illness Prevention Program is activated whenever the ambient temperature or heat index reaches **80°F** or above.

- **Water**: Potable drinking water is positioned **within 50 feet** of all associates working in affected areas. A minimum of 1 quart per associate per hour is available.
- **Shade and Rest Breaks**: Scheduled rest breaks in shaded or air-conditioned areas are provided **every 2 hours** during continuous work in hot conditions. Additional breaks are provided as requested.
- **Buddy System**: Associates working in hot conditions use the buddy system — each associate monitors a partner for signs of heat illness.
- **Acclimatization Plan**: New associates and associates returning from extended absence (7+ days) follow a graduated acclimatization schedule over 7 days:
  - Day 1–2: 50% of normal work duration in heat.
  - Day 3–4: 60% of normal work duration.
  - Day 5: 80% of normal work duration.
  - Day 6–7: 100% of normal work duration.
- **Supervisor Training**: All supervisors of associates working in hot conditions are trained to recognize symptoms of heat illness (heat rash, heat cramps, heat exhaustion, heat stroke) and to respond appropriately, including emergency medical response for heat stroke (call 911, cool the worker rapidly).
- **Daily Monitoring**: During heat events, supervisors check in with outdoor associates at the start of each shift and at each break.

#### 6.13.2 Cold Stress Prevention

The Cold Stress Prevention Program is activated whenever the ambient temperature drops below **32°F**.

- **Layered Clothing**: Associates working in cold conditions are required to wear layered, moisture-wicking clothing. BRHI provides guidance on proper layering.
- **Warm-Up Breaks**: Scheduled warm-up breaks in heated areas are provided **every 2 hours** during continuous work in cold conditions. Additional breaks are provided as requested.
- **Frostbite Recognition Training**: Associates and supervisors are trained to recognize the signs and symptoms of frostbite (numbness, white or grayish-yellow skin, firm or waxy feeling skin) and hypothermia (shivering, confusion, drowsiness, slurred speech) and to respond appropriately.
- **Wind Chill Monitoring**: Supervisors monitor wind chill conditions and adjust work schedules and protective measures accordingly. When wind chill reaches extreme levels (-25°F or below), outdoor work is limited to essential operations only with enhanced warm-up protocols.

### 8.1 Reporting Timelines and Process

| Event | Reporter | Timeline | Report To |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Injury** (any work-related injury or illness) | Injured associate (or witness) | **Within 1 hour** of occurrence | Immediate supervisor |
| **Near-Miss** (event that could have resulted in injury or property damage) | Any associate | **Within 24 hours** | Supervisor, Safety Captain, or via anonymous channels |
| **Property Damage** | Discovering associate | **Within 4 hours** | Immediate supervisor |
| **Environmental Release** | Discovering associate | **Immediately** | Supervisor and Store Manager; 911 for large releases |

### 8.2 Near-Miss Reporting

- BRHI maintains a **non-punitive near-miss reporting program**. No associate will be disciplined for reporting a near-miss in good faith, even if the associate's own action contributed to the event.
- **Anonymous reporting** is available via:
  - Physical **safety suggestion boxes** located in every break room.
  - The BRHI Safety Hotline (1-800-274-4723) — callers may choose to remain anonymous.
  - The SMS portal — submissions may be made without logging in.
- Near-miss reports are reviewed by the Store Safety Captain and investigated when patterns are identified. All near-miss reports are entered into the SMS for trending analysis.

### 8.3 Incident Investigation

- **Supervisor completes the Incident Report Form** (Form BRHI-SAF-040) within **4 hours** of notification of any injury, illness, or near-miss.
- **5-Why Root Cause Analysis** is initiated and completed within **48 hours** for all recordable incidents and serious near-misses:
  - The investigation team typically includes the Department Supervisor, Store Safety Captain, and the injured associate (if able).
  - The team asks successive "Why?" questions (minimum 5 levels) to drill past proximate causes to root causes.
  - Root causes are categorized (human factors, equipment, procedure, training, environment, organizational) and addressed with specific corrective actions.
- **Corrective Actions** are assigned with specific responsible parties and due dates in the SMS. Actions are tracked to closure and verified for effectiveness.

### 8.4 OSHA Recordkeeping

- **OSHA 300 Log**: All OSHA-recordable injuries and illnesses are entered on the **OSHA 300 Log** within **7 calendar days** of notification. Recordability is determined per OSHA recordkeeping guidelines (29 CFR 1904).
- **OSHA 300A Summary**: The **OSHA 300A Annual Summary** is posted in a conspicuous location at each BRHI facility from **February 1 through April 30** of each year. The summary is certified by the location manager.
- **Fatality Notification**: BRHI reports any work-related fatality to OSHA by telephone or in person within **8 hours** of the incident. Call the nearest OSHA Area Office or the OSHA toll-free hotline at 1-800-321-OSHA (1-800-321-6742).
- **Hospitalization Notification**: BRHI reports any work-related inpatient hospitalization, amputation, or loss of an eye to OSHA by telephone or in person within **24 hours** of the incident.
- OSHA 300, 300A, and 301 records are retained for the current year plus **5 preceding years** at each location, available for inspection by OSHA compliance officers upon request.

### 8.5 Trend Analysis

- **Quarterly incident trend analysis** is conducted at three levels:
  - **Store level**: Store Safety Captain compiles a quarterly report of all incidents, near-misses, and corrective actions for the store safety committee meeting.
  - **District level**: District Safety Coordinator aggregates and analyzes district-level trends and presents to the District Manager.
  - **Region level**: Regional Safety Manager produces a comprehensive regional trend analysis including comparison to prior quarters, year-over-year trends, and benchmarking against other regions and BLS industry data.
- Trend analysis examines: incident type, body part, cause, department, time of day, day of week, associate tenure, and root cause categories to identify systemic issues and target interventions.

## 9. Emergency Action Plan

### 9.1 Fire Emergency — RACE Protocol

Upon discovering a fire or smoke condition, associates follow the **RACE** protocol:

1. **R — Rescue** anyone in immediate danger, if it can be done safely and without placing the rescuer at risk.
2. **A — Alarm** — Pull the nearest fire alarm pull station to activate the building alarm system and notify all occupants. Call 911 immediately.
3. **C — Contain** — Close all doors and windows as you evacuate to slow the spread of fire and smoke. Do not lock doors.
4. **E — Extinguish** — If the fire is small (confined to its point of origin, no heavy smoke) and you have been trained, attempt to extinguish using the nearest appropriate fire extinguisher. **PASS technique**: Pull the pin, Aim at the base of the fire, Squeeze the handle, Sweep side to side. If the fire is not extinguished within 30 seconds or if smoke becomes thick, **evacuate immediately**.

**Evacuation Procedures**:

- Upon hearing the fire alarm, all associates and customers proceed to the nearest marked exit using designated evacuation routes.
- Associates guide customers toward exits and assist individuals needing help.
- Do not use elevators.
- Department Supervisors conduct a sweep of their departments to ensure all associates and customers have evacuated.
- All persons report to the designated **outdoor assembly area** (identified on the evacuation plan posted at each exit).
- The Store Manager or designee accounts for all associates using the associate roster. Unaccounted associates are reported to emergency responders immediately.
- No one re-enters the building until the fire department declares it safe to do so.

### 9.2 Severe Weather — Tornado

- **Alert Monitoring**: Each store monitors NOAA Weather Radio and local alert systems during severe weather season. Management receives alerts via the BRHI Weather Alert System (text and email).
- **Tornado Watch**: Store management is notified. Outdoor operations (garden center, loading) are prepared for rapid cessation. Associates and customers are informed that shelter may be needed.
- **Tornado Warning** (tornado sighted or indicated by radar):
  1. Store Manager activates the severe weather alarm (PA announcement: "Attention all associates and customers — a tornado warning has been issued. Proceed immediately to the designated shelter area.").
  2. All associates and customers move to **designated interior shelter areas** — interior rooms away from windows, exterior walls, and large open spans (e.g., break rooms, restrooms, interior offices, stock rooms without exterior walls).
  3. Associates in the lumber department and garden center move indoors immediately, abandoning outdoor merchandise and equipment.
  4. Associates and customers assume a protective posture: crouch down, protect head and neck with arms, stay away from windows and exterior doors.
  5. Accounting for all associates occurs in the shelter area.
  6. All clear is announced only after the tornado warning expires or the Store Manager receives confirmation from the BRHI Weather Alert System.

### 9.3 Active Threat — Run-Hide-Fight

BRHI adopts the nationally recognized **Run-Hide-Fight** protocol for active threat situations:

1. **Run** (preferred option):
   - If there is a safe path, evacuate the building immediately.
   - Leave belongings behind.
   - Help others evacuate if possible, but do not let others slow your escape.
   - Call 911 when safe to do so. Provide location, description of the threat, and number of people in the area.
   - Do not pull the fire alarm — this could drive people toward the threat.

2. **Hide** (if you cannot evacuate safely):
   - Find a room that can be locked and barricaded.
   - Lock and barricade the door with heavy furniture.
   - Turn off lights, silence cell phones, and hide behind large objects away from doors and windows.
   - Remain quiet and still.
   - Do not open the door for anyone until law enforcement provides the all-clear.

3. **Fight** (last resort, only if your life is in imminent danger):
   - Attempt to incapacitate the threat.
   - Act with physical aggression — improvise weapons.
   - Commit to your actions.

**Law Enforcement Response**:

- When law enforcement arrives, they will proceed directly to the threat. Associates should:
  - Remain calm and follow instructions.
  - Drop any items in your hands.
  - Raise hands and spread fingers.
  - Keep hands visible at all times.
  - Avoid quick movements toward officers, pointing, or screaming.
  - Proceed in the direction from which officers are entering the area.

**Lockdown Procedures**:

- The Store Manager or highest-ranking associate initiates lockdown by PA announcement: "Attention — lockdown, lockdown, lockdown. Secure all doors and shelter in place immediately."
- Associates lock exterior doors, customer entrance doors, and department access doors where possible.
- Associates and customers shelter in interior, lockable areas.
- No one leaves shelter until law enforcement provides the all-clear.

### 9.4 Medical Emergency

#### 9.4.1 Automated External Defibrillator (AED) Program

- **AED Placement**: AEDs are positioned so that any point in any BRHI facility is within a **3-minute walk** of an AED. Typical placement: front entrance, lumber entrance, garden center, receiving dock, and break room.
- **AED Cabinets** are wall-mounted in alarmed, clearly marked cabinets.
- **Minimum 2 CPR/AED certified associates** are required per shift at every BRHI location. Certification is maintained through the American Heart Association or equivalent ( Heartsaver First Aid CPR AED, valid for 2 years).
- AED pads and batteries are checked monthly for expiration and charge level. Expired pads and low batteries are replaced immediately.

#### 9.4.2 Medical Emergency Response

1. **Call 911** immediately for any life-threatening emergency (chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, loss of consciousness, seizure, severe allergic reaction, stroke symptoms).
2. **Provide first aid** within your training level. Do not attempt procedures you are not trained to perform.
3. **Retrieve the AED** for any suspected cardiac arrest (unresponsive, not breathing normally).
4. **Do not move** an injured person unless they are in immediate danger (fire, structural collapse, active threat).
5. **Designate someone** to meet emergency responders at the entrance and guide them to the patient.
6. **Store Manager or designee** notifies HR and the District Safety Coordinator as soon as the immediate emergency is addressed.
7. **Complete the Incident Report** per Section 8 procedures.

#### 9.4.3 First Aid Kits

- First aid kits are located in **every department** of every BRHI facility, at the customer service desk, and in every company vehicle.
- Kits are stocked per ANSI Z308.1 requirements plus additional items (CPR mask, trauma shears, elastic bandages, cold packs).
- The Store Safety Captain inventories and restocks first aid kits **monthly**. Used items are replaced immediately.

### 9.5 Chemical Spill

1. **Evacuate** the immediate area. Do not attempt to clean up a spill unless you are trained and have appropriate PPE.
2. **Reference the Safety Data Sheet (SDS)** for the spilled chemical to identify hazards and cleanup procedures. SDS binders are in each department; the electronic database is on the BRHI Safety mobile app.
3. **Small spills** (contained, known chemical, no immediate hazard): Trained associates deploy the appropriate spill kit (see Section 6.4.5) wearing required PPE per the SDS.
4. **Large spills** (uncontrolled, unknown chemical, fire/health/environmental hazard):
   - Evacuate the area. Establish a perimeter.
   - **Call 911** and the local hazardous materials response team.
   - Do not attempt cleanup.
   - Notify the Store Manager and Store Safety Captain.
5. All chemical spills are reported as incidents per Section 8.

### 9.6 Power Outage

1. **Emergency generators** activate automatically within 10 seconds of a power loss, providing power to: emergency lighting, fire alarm and suppression systems, point-of-sale systems, and critical refrigeration.
2. Store operations continue under generator power for **up to 2 hours**. Associates assist customers with current transactions and guide them toward checkout.
3. If power is not restored within **2 hours**, the Store Manager initiates the **controlled closure procedure**:
   - Announce closure to customers via PA system.
   - Complete all pending transactions.
   - Escort customers safely from the building.
   - Secure the building (lock entrances, activate security system).
   - Associates are released per the emergency staffing plan (minimum crew remains for security and reopening preparation).
4. During power outage:
   - Associates use flashlights (available at each register and department) — no open flames.
   - Forklift operations are suspended (reduced visibility).
   - Cutting stations are shut down.
   - Associates monitor refrigerated and frozen merchandise for temperature compliance.
5. Store Manager notifies the District Manager and Regional Director of the outage and closure decision.

## 11. Safety Audits

### 11.1 Daily Department Walkthrough

- **Performed by**: Store Safety Captain (or Department Supervisor as backup).
- **Frequency**: Daily, before the store opens to the public.
- **Checklist**: 10-point Daily Safety Checklist (Form BRHI-SAF-050):

| # | Item | Pass Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Aisles clear | All aisles free of obstructions, minimum 36 inches clear width |
| 2 | PPE compliance | All associates in required PPE for their work area |
| 3 | Housekeeping | Work areas clean, organized, free of debris and trip hazards |
| 4 | Emergency exits clear | All exits unobstructed, illuminated exit signs functional |
| 5 | Fire extinguishers visible | Extinguishers in designated locations, visible and accessible |
| 6 | Spills cleaned | No standing water, oil, or chemical spills on any surface |
| 7 | Storage stable | Merchandise stacked safely, no unstable or leaning stacks |
| 8 | Equipment inspected | Forklifts, equipment, and machinery with current pre-trip inspection |
| 9 | SDS accessible | SDS binders in place and accessible in each department |
| 10 | First aid kits stocked | Kits present, sealed, and within expiration dates |

- Results are documented on the checklist form and entered into the SMS. Any "fail" item requires immediate corrective action.

### 11.2 Weekly Store Inspection

- **Performed by**: Assistant Store Manager.
- **Frequency**: Weekly (scheduled day varies each week).
- **Checklist**: 50-point Weekly Safety Inspection Checklist (Form BRHI-SAF-051).
- **Passing Score**: **90%** (minimum 45 of 50 items passing).
- **Corrective Actions**: All deficiencies must be corrected within **7 days**. Critical safety items (blocked exits, inoperable fire safety equipment, imminent hazards) must be corrected immediately.
- Results are reviewed at the weekly store management meeting and entered into the SMS.

### 11.3 Monthly Regional Audit

- **Performed by**: Regional Safety Manager (or District Safety Coordinator as delegate).
- **Frequency**: Monthly — each location is audited at least once per quarter by the Regional Safety Manager personally.
- **Checklist**: 100-point Regional Safety Audit Checklist (Form BRHI-SAF-052).
- **Passing Score**: **85%** (minimum 85 of 100 points).
- **Corrective Actions**: All deficiencies must be corrected within **30 days**. A corrective action plan is submitted to the District Manager within 7 days of the audit.
- The audit report includes a narrative summary, scoring breakdown, photographs of deficiencies, and the corrective action plan.
- Results are entered into the SMS and shared with the store management team and District Manager.

### 11.4 Annual Corporate Audit

- **Performed by**: Corporate Safety team (2–3 auditors, including at least one ISO 45001 Lead Auditor).
- **Frequency**: Annual — every BRHI location receives a corporate audit at least once every 12 months. Locations with prior scores below 85% or significant incidents may be audited more frequently.
- **Checklist**: 200-point Corporate Safety Audit Checklist (Form BRHI-SAF-053), covering: safety management system, training programs, incident records, PPE compliance, emergency preparedness, specific safety programs, housekeeping, and associate interviews.
- **Passing Score**: **90%** (minimum 180 of 200 points).
- **Results Reporting**: An executive summary of all corporate audits is presented to the COO quarterly.
- **Corrective Action Plan**: Required within **14 days** of audit completion. The plan must address all deficiencies with specific actions, responsible parties, and due dates.
- **Re-Audit**: Any location scoring **below 85%** on a corporate audit is subject to a **re-audit within 60 days** of corrective action plan completion to verify sustained compliance.
- Locations scoring below 70% receive an immediate visit from the Corporate Safety Director and an enhanced oversight plan including monthly audits until sustained compliance is demonstrated.

## 12. Contractor Safety

### 12.1 Pre-Qualification

All contractors performing work on BRHI premises must meet the following pre-qualification criteria before being authorized to begin work:

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Experience Modification Rate (EMR)** | < 1.0 for the most recent available year |
| **OSHA 300 Log Data** | Provide injury and illness data for the past 3 years |

| **Reference Checks** | Minimum 3 references from similar projects completed in the past 2 years |
| **Regulatory Compliance History** | No willful or repeat OSHA citations in the past 3 years |

Pre-qualification is managed through the BRHI Contractor Safety Pre-Qualification Portal. Approved contractors are added to the Approved Contractor List and are subject to periodic re-qualification (every 2 years or after any significant incident on BRHI premises).

### 12.2 Insurance Requirements

Contractors must maintain the following minimum insurance coverage for the duration of work on BRHI premises:

| Coverage Type | Minimum Limit |
|---|---|
| Commercial General Liability | $1,000,000 per occurrence |
| Commercial Umbrella / Excess Liability | $5,000,000 |
| Workers' Compensation | $1,000,000 per accident |
| Commercial Auto Liability | $1,000,000 per accident |

BRHI must be named as an additional insured on the general liability and umbrella policies. Certificates of insurance must be provided before work begins and upon each renewal.

### 12.3 On-Site Requirements

- **Site-Specific Safety Orientation**: All contractor personnel must complete a 30-minute BRHI site-specific safety orientation before beginning work. The orientation covers: BRHI safety rules, emergency procedures, reporting requirements, work area hazards, and permit requirements. Orientation is documented and the record is maintained by the Store Safety Captain.
- **Daily Check-In**: Contractor foreman or competent person must check in with BRHI store management at the start of each workday to review planned activities, coordinate with store operations, and address any safety concerns.

- **Stop-Work Authority**: Any BRHI manager, safety team member, or associate has the authority to **stop contractor work** if unsafe conditions or behaviors are observed. Work may not resume until the hazard is corrected and the stop-work is released by the issuing person or the Store Manager.

Ratings are entered into the Contractor Safety Database and factored into future pre-qualification decisions.

## 13. Workers' Compensation

### 13.1 Injury Reporting and Claim Initiation

| Step | Action | Responsibility | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Report injury to immediate supervisor | Injured associate | Immediately (within 1 hour) |
| 2 | Provide first aid and arrange medical care if needed | Supervisor | Immediately |
| 3 | Report injury to Human Resources | Supervisor or injured associate | Within 24 hours |
| 4 | HR submits First Report of Injury to workers' compensation insurance carrier | Human Resources | Within 24 hours of notification |
| 5 | Supervisor completes Incident Report Form (BRHI-SAF-040) | Supervisor | Within 4 hours |
| 6 | Incident investigation initiated | Store Safety Captain | Within 48 hours |

### 13.2 Medical Treatment

- BRHI maintains a **carrier-approved medical provider network** for workers' compensation treatment. A panel of physicians is posted in every break room and available on the BRHI intranet.
- Associates may select a physician from the panel for initial treatment. In emergency situations, associates should go to the nearest emergency room.
- BRHI covers all reasonable and necessary medical treatment related to the work injury per state workers' compensation law.
- Associates should inform the medical provider that the injury is work-related and provide the BRHI workers' compensation claim number (provided by HR upon claim submission).

### 13.3 Transitional Duty (Modified Duty) Program

- BRHI maintains a **transitional duty program** designed to return injured associates to productive work as soon as medically appropriate.
- When the treating physician releases an associate to modified duty (with restrictions), BRHI provides transitional work assignments within the physician's restrictions for up to **90 days**.
- Transitional duty assignments may include: modified tasks within the associate's regular department, temporary assignment to a different department with appropriate duties, or administrative/clerical tasks.
- A **physician's written release** specifying the work restrictions (weight limits, standing/sitting requirements, prohibited activities) is required before the associate begins transitional duty.
- The supervisor, HR, and the associate review the restrictions together and document the transitional duty assignment plan.
- The associate's restrictions are reviewed at each follow-up medical appointment and the transitional duty plan is adjusted accordingly.

### 13.4 Return to Full Duty

- Return to full, unrestricted duty requires a **physician's written clearance** releasing the associate to full duty without restrictions.
- The clearance note is provided to HR and filed in the associate's personnel and workers' compensation files.

### 13.5 Permanent Restrictions — ADA Interactive Process

- If the treating physician determines that the associate will have **permanent work restrictions**, BRHI engages in an **interactive process** under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) to identify potential reasonable accommodations.
- The interactive process includes: review of the restrictions with the associate and HR, assessment of the essential functions of the associate's current position, exploration of potential accommodations (modified equipment, schedule adjustments, reassignment to a vacant position), and documentation of the process.
- Accommodation decisions are made on a case-by-case basis in consultation with the associate, HR, the associate's medical provider, and BRHI's ADA Coordinator.

## 14. Customer Safety

### 14.1 Aisle and Merchandise Standards

- **Aisle Clearance**: All customer aisles must be maintained at a minimum **36 inches of clear width** at all times. This includes aisles between merchandise displays, end caps, and promotional displays.
- **Merchandise Stacking**: Merchandise displayed or stored above floor level must follow these limits:
  - **Maximum 6 feet** in height without restraint (e.g., on standard shelving).
  - **Maximum 8 feet** in height with restraint (secured to the display fixture, racking, or wall) and a visible sign posted: "CAUTION — OVERHEAD MERCHANDISE."
  - Heavy items are displayed at waist level whenever possible; never above shoulder height.
  - Merchandise on upper shelves must not protrude beyond the edge of the shelf.
- **Overhead Protection in Lumber Department**: The lumber department is designated as a **hard hat zone** and is marked with signage at all entrances to the department: "CAUTION — HARD HAT ZONE — OVERHEAD HAZARD." Protective netting is installed above customer walkways to deflect falling items. Associates working in the lumber department wear hard hats per Section 7.

### 14.2 Parking Lot Safety

- **Daily Parking Lot Inspection**: A designated associate inspects the parking lot at the start of each business day using the Daily Parking Lot Inspection Checklist (Form BRHI-SAF-070). The inspection covers:
  - **Potholes and surface damage**: Identified and marked for repair. Potholes presenting a trip hazard are filled or barricaded within 24 hours.
  - **Lighting**: All parking lot lights are functional. Non-functional lights are reported to facilities maintenance for repair within 48 hours. Lighting deficiencies affecting safety are addressed with temporary lighting.
  - **Cart corrals**: Cart corrals are in good repair and positioned in designated locations. Loose carts in the parking lot are collected at least hourly during business hours.
  - **Snow and ice removal**: Per the Snow and Ice Removal Plan (see Section 6.6.4), lots are treated and cleared by 30 minutes before store opening.
  - **Signage and markings**: Parking space lines, crosswalks, fire lanes, and ADA-accessible spaces are clearly marked and visible. Faded markings are reported for re-striping.
  - **Trip hazards**: Sidewalks, curbs, and walkways are inspected for heaving, cracking, or other trip hazards. Identified hazards are marked and repaired.

### 14.3 Customer Incident Reporting

- Any associate who witnesses or is made aware of a customer injury, illness, or incident on BRHI premises must:
  1. **Offer first aid** immediately (within the associate's training level).
  2. **Notify the Store Manager or manager on duty** immediately.
  3. **Call 911** for any medical emergency.
  4. Complete the **Customer Incident Report Form** (Form BRHI-SAF-080) within **24 hours** of the incident. The form includes:
     - Date, time, and location of the incident.
     - Description of what happened.
     - Injuries or symptoms observed or reported.
     - **Witness statements** (names, contact information, and written accounts from all witnesses).
     - **Photographs** of the scene, hazard, and conditions at the time of the incident (taken by the associate or manager).
     - First aid or medical treatment provided.
     - Corrective actions taken (e.g., spill cleaned, hazard removed, signage posted).
  5. The Store Manager reviews and signs the form.
  6. The form is forwarded to **BRHI Risk Management / Insurance** within **48 hours** of the incident for claims processing and liability assessment.
- All customer incidents are entered into the SMS for trending analysis. Locations with recurring customer incident patterns (e.g., same location, same cause) receive targeted corrective action and additional audit focus.
