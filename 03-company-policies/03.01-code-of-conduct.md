## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Code of Business Conduct and Ethics ("Code") establishes the ethical and legal standards of conduct expected of every individual associated with BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. and its wholly owned subsidiaries (collectively, "BRHI," "the Company," "BuildRight," "our," or "we"). It is designed to:

- Promote honest, ethical, and lawful conduct throughout the organization.
- Provide guidance to assist associates in identifying and resolving ethical dilemmas.
- Establish mechanisms for reporting suspected violations of law or Company policy.
- Protect the Company's reputation, assets, and interests.
- Ensure compliance with applicable legal and regulatory requirements in every jurisdiction where we operate.
- Foster a culture of transparency, accountability, and mutual respect.

### 1.2 Scope

This Code applies to:

- **All members of the Board of Directors** ("Directors").
- **All corporate officers** of the Company and its subsidiaries.
- **All employees** of the Company and its subsidiaries, whether full-time, part-time, temporary, or seasonal (collectively referred to as "Associates").
- **All independent contractors, consultants, and temporary workers** engaged by the Company.
- **All vendors, suppliers, and third-party service providers** to the extent specified in their agreements with the Company or as referenced in Section 20 of this Code.

Where the term "Associate" is used throughout this Code, it refers to all of the above categories unless otherwise specified.

### 1.3 Supplementary Policies

This Code is the cornerstone of the Company's ethics and compliance program but does not stand alone. It is supplemented by detailed corporate policies, standard operating procedures, and guidelines issued by the Company, including but not limited to:

- BRHI-POL-002: Anti-Corruption and Bribery Policy
- BRHI-POL-003: Conflicts of Interest Policy
- BRHI-POL-004: Insider Trading Policy
- BRHI-POL-005: Information Security and Data Privacy Policy
- BRHI-POL-006: Records Retention and Management Policy
- BRHI-POL-007: Anti-Discrimination and Harassment Policy
- BRHI-POL-008: Health, Safety, and Environment Policy
- BRHI-POL-009: Vendor and Supplier Code of Conduct
- BRHI-POL-010: Social Media and Communications Policy
- BRHI-POL-011: Gifts and Entertainment Policy
- BRHI-POL-012: Whistleblower Protection and Reporting Policy

In the event of a conflict between this Code and a supplementary policy, the more restrictive standard shall apply unless otherwise directed by the Ethics and Compliance Office.

### 1.4 No At-Will Employment Limitation

Nothing in this Code constitutes a contract of employment or a guarantee of continued employment. In the United States, employment with BRHI remains at-will unless otherwise specified in a written employment agreement signed by an authorized officer of the Company. This Code does not modify the at-will nature of employment in any jurisdiction where such status applies.

## 2. Core Ethical Principles

The following principles form the ethical foundation upon which all Company activities, decisions, and relationships are built. Every Associate is expected to embody these principles in the performance of their duties.

### 2.1 Honesty

We are truthful in all our interactions — with customers, suppliers, fellow Associates, investors, regulators, and the communities we serve. We do not deceive, mislead, or make false statements. We represent our products, services, and capabilities accurately. We communicate openly and ensure that our disclosures, whether verbal or written, are complete and truthful.

### 2.2 Fairness

We treat every person and entity with equity and impartiality. We do not take unfair advantage of anyone through manipulation, concealment, abuse of privileged information, misrepresentation of material facts, or any other unfair dealing practice. We apply our policies consistently and without favoritism. We negotiate in good faith and honor our commitments.

### 2.3 Respect

We respect the dignity, rights, and contributions of every individual. We value diversity of thought, background, and experience. We create an inclusive environment where all Associates feel welcomed, valued, and empowered. We listen to differing viewpoints and engage in constructive dialogue. We do not tolerate disrespectful, demeaning, or abusive behavior of any kind.

### 2.4 Responsibility

We take ownership of our actions and their consequences. We hold ourselves accountable to the highest standards of professional conduct. We fulfill our obligations to the Company, to each other, and to the stakeholders who depend on us. We exercise prudent stewardship of the Company's resources and the environment. We act in a manner that protects and enhances the Company's long-term interests.

### 2.5 Transparency

We conduct our business in a manner that can withstand public scrutiny. We make disclosures that are clear, timely, and accurate. We do not hide information that should be shared with those who have a legitimate right to know. We cooperate openly and honestly with regulators, auditors, and investigators. We maintain accurate books and records that fairly reflect the Company's transactions and financial condition.

## 3. Compliance with Laws

### 3.1 General Commitment

BRHI is committed to full compliance with all applicable federal, state, provincial, local, and international laws, rules, and regulations in every jurisdiction where the Company conducts business. Every Associate is responsible for understanding and complying with the laws that apply to their role and activities.

Ignorance of the law is not a defense. If an Associate is unsure whether a particular course of action complies with applicable law, they must seek guidance from the Legal Department before proceeding.

### 3.2 Areas of Legal Compliance

While it is not possible to list every law that may be relevant to the Company's operations, Associates should be particularly attentive to compliance in the following areas:

- **Securities laws**, including insider trading prohibitions and public disclosure requirements.
- **Anti-corruption and anti-bribery laws**, including the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), the UK Bribery Act, and the OECD Anti-Bribery Convention.
- **Antitrust and competition laws**, including laws prohibiting price-fixing, bid-rigging, market allocation, and other anti-competitive practices.
- **Employment and labor laws**, including wage and hour laws, anti-discrimination statutes, occupational safety and health regulations, and labor relations laws.
- **Environmental laws**, including regulations governing air and water quality, waste management, hazardous materials, and product safety.
- **Tax laws**, including accurate and timely filing of all required tax returns and payments.
- **Data privacy and information security laws**, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), and other applicable privacy frameworks.
- **Consumer protection laws**, including advertising standards, product labeling requirements, and warranty obligations.
- **Import and export control laws**, including customs regulations, sanctions, and embargo requirements.
- **Intellectual property laws**, including trademark, copyright, patent, and trade secret protections.

### 3.3 International Operations

Associates working in or interacting with jurisdictions outside the United States must comply with both U.S. laws applicable to international operations and the laws of the local jurisdiction. Where U.S. law and local law conflict, Associates must consult the Legal Department to determine the appropriate course of action. Under no circumstances may an Associate violate U.S. law on the basis that local customs or practices permit the conduct.

### 3.4 Cooperation with Government Investigations

The Company will cooperate fully with all lawful government investigations, inquiries, and audits. No Associate may obstruct, impede, or interfere with any government investigation or proceeding. Associates who receive a subpoena, civil investigative demand, or other legal process related to Company business must immediately forward it to the Legal Department. No Associate may destroy, alter, or conceal any document or record in anticipation of or in response to a government investigation without authorization from the Legal Department.

## 4. Anti-Corruption and Bribery

### 4.1 Zero Tolerance Policy

BRHI maintains an absolute zero tolerance policy toward corruption and bribery in all its forms. No Associate, contractor, or third party acting on behalf of the Company may offer, promise, give, authorize, solicit, or accept any bribe, kickback, improper inducement, or anything of value to obtain or retain business, influence any act or decision, or secure any improper advantage.

### 4.2 Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) Compliance

The Company is subject to the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act, which prohibits the payment or offer of anything of value to a "foreign official" (as defined under the FCPA) for the purpose of obtaining or retaining business or securing any improper advantage. The FCPA also requires the Company to maintain accurate books and records and to implement a system of internal accounting controls.

For purposes of this policy:

- **"Foreign official"** includes any officer or employee of a foreign government, any department, agency, or instrumentality of a foreign government, any public international organization, and any person acting in an official capacity for or on behalf of any such entity. This definition is broad and includes employees of state-owned enterprises, political party officials, and candidates for political office.
- **"Anything of value"** is interpreted broadly and includes cash, gifts, meals, entertainment, travel, lodging, services, favors, employment offers, internships, scholarships, charitable contributions, and any other benefit or advantage.

### 4.3 Domestic Anti-Bribery

Associates are also prohibited from offering, giving, or promising anything of value to any domestic government official, elected representative, or political candidate to influence official acts or decisions. The Company complies with all applicable domestic anti-bribery statutes, including 18 U.S.C. § 201 (Bribery of Public Officials and Witnesses) and applicable state and local anti-bribery laws.

### 4.4 Facilitation Payments

Facilitation payments (also known as "grease payments") — small payments made to expedite or secure the performance of a routine governmental action to which the Company is legally entitled — are strictly prohibited, regardless of the amount, local custom, or perceived necessity. Associates who are asked or pressured to make facilitation payments must immediately report the request to the Ethics and Compliance Office.

### 4.5 Third-Party Intermediaries

The Company may be held liable for corrupt payments made by third parties acting on its behalf, including agents, consultants, joint venture partners, distributors, and subcontractors. Accordingly:

- All third-party intermediaries engaged to interact with government officials on behalf of the Company must undergo due diligence screening before engagement and on an ongoing basis.
- Agreements with third-party intermediaries must include anti-corruption representations, warranties, and covenants consistent with this Code and BRHI-POL-002.
- Payments to third-party intermediaries must be reasonable in amount, commensurate with the services provided, and properly documented.
- Associates must not use third-party intermediaries to accomplish indirectly what they are prohibited from doing directly under this Code.

### 4.6 Political Contributions

Corporate funds or resources may not be used to make political contributions to any candidate for public office, political party, political action committee, or ballot initiative without the prior written approval of the General Counsel. This prohibition applies to all jurisdictions, including those where such contributions may be lawful.

Associates may make personal political contributions on their own time and with their own funds. However, no Associate may use Company resources, facilities, or time for political activity, and no Associate may pressure or coerce another Associate to make a political contribution or support a particular candidate or cause.

The Company may, with appropriate approvals, engage in lawful lobbying activities through registered lobbyists in compliance with all applicable lobbying disclosure laws.

### 4.7 Charitable Contributions and Sponsorships

Charitable contributions and community sponsorships made by or on behalf of the Company must be made for legitimate philanthropic purposes and must not be used as a mechanism to improperly influence government officials or secure business advantages. All corporate charitable contributions must be approved in accordance with the Company's charitable giving guidelines and properly recorded in the Company's books and records.

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

### 5.1 Definition

A conflict of interest arises when an Associate's personal interests, relationships, or outside activities interfere — or could reasonably appear to interfere — with their ability to act in the best interests of the Company. A conflict of interest exists when an Associate, or a member of their family or household:

- Has a financial or other interest in a transaction involving the Company.
- Holds a position (employment, directorship, advisory role, or significant ownership) with a competitor, supplier, customer, or other third party that does business with or is seeking to do business with the Company.
- Receives improper personal benefits as a result of their position with the Company.
- Is in a position to influence the Company's dealings in a manner that could lead to personal gain or the gain of a family member or associate.

For purposes of this section, "family member" includes a spouse, domestic partner, parent, parent-in-law, child, sibling, grandparent, grandchild, step-relative, or any person sharing the Associate's household.

### 5.2 Examples of Conflicts of Interest

The following are examples of situations that may constitute or give the appearance of a conflict of interest. This list is illustrative, not exhaustive:

1. **Financial interest in a vendor or supplier.** An Associate or their family member holds a significant ownership interest (5% or more) in or receives compensation from a company that does business with or is seeking to do business with BRHI.

2. **Outside employment.** An Associate accepts employment, consulting work, or a board position with a competitor, supplier, customer, or any entity that does business with or is seeking to do business with BRHI, without prior written approval.

3. **Supervising family members.** An Associate is in a direct reporting relationship with a family member or is in a position to influence hiring, compensation, promotion, or performance evaluations of a family member.

4. **Misuse of Company information.** An Associate uses confidential Company information for personal benefit or the benefit of a family member or outside entity.

5. **Business opportunities.** An Associate diverts or takes for themselves a business opportunity that the Company is pursuing or could reasonably be expected to pursue.

6. **Personal loans.** An Associate receives a personal loan or guarantee of an obligation from the Company or from a vendor, supplier, or customer as a result of their position with the Company, except through generally available programs.

7. **Investments in competitors.** An Associate holds a material financial interest in a direct competitor of BRHI (other than through ownership of less than 1% of the outstanding shares of a publicly traded company).

### 5.3 Disclosure Requirements

All Associates have an affirmative obligation to promptly disclose any actual, potential, or perceived conflict of interest to their supervisor and the Ethics and Compliance Office. Disclosure is required:

- At the time of hire, through the conflicts of interest questionnaire included in the onboarding process.
- Annually, through the Annual Compliance Certification process.
- Promptly upon the emergence of any new or changed conflict of interest.
- Upon the request of the Ethics and Compliance Office or the Legal Department at any time.

Directors and executive officers are subject to additional disclosure requirements under applicable securities laws and stock exchange rules, including the obligation to disclose related-party transactions.

### 5.4 Approval Process

Upon disclosure, the Ethics and Compliance Office, in consultation with the Legal Department and the Associate's supervisor, will evaluate the disclosed interest or relationship and determine whether a conflict exists. If a conflict is identified, the Company will take one or more of the following actions:

- Require the Associate to divest the conflicting interest.
- Reassign the Associate's duties to eliminate the conflict.
- Impose monitoring and oversight mechanisms.
- Prohibit the Associate from participating in the relevant transaction or decision.
- Waive the conflict with appropriate safeguards (waivers for Directors and executive officers require Board approval as described in Section 23).

The Associate must fully cooperate with any inquiry regarding the potential conflict and must comply with all remedial measures imposed.

### 5.5 Outside Employment and Activities

Associates may engage in outside employment, volunteer activities, and community service, provided that:

- The outside activity does not interfere with the Associate's job performance or duties.
- The outside activity does not create an actual or potential conflict of interest.
- The outside activity does not involve the use of Company time, resources, equipment, or confidential information.
- The Associate does not represent themselves as acting on behalf of the Company while engaged in outside activities.
- The outside activity does not violate any applicable non-competition, non-solicitation, or confidentiality agreement between the Associate and the Company.

Associates in management or leadership positions must obtain prior written approval from their supervisor and Human Resources before accepting any outside employment, board position, or advisory role.

## 6. Protection and Proper Use of Company Assets

### 6.1 Duty to Protect Company Assets

Every Associate has a duty to protect the Company's assets from loss, theft, damage, misuse, and waste. Company assets include both tangible and intangible property and are to be used solely for legitimate business purposes unless otherwise authorized by Company policy.

### 6.2 Physical Assets

Physical assets of the Company include, but are not limited to:

- Real property, buildings, warehouses, and distribution centers.
- Retail store facilities, fixtures, and equipment.
- Inventory and merchandise.
- Vehicles, including delivery trucks and fleet vehicles.
- Tools, machinery, and maintenance equipment.
- Computers, laptops, tablets, mobile devices, and peripherals.
- Office equipment, furniture, and supplies.
- Cash, checks, and financial instruments.
- Security systems and access credentials (badges, key cards, alarm codes).

Associates must:

- Use physical assets responsibly and only for authorized business purposes.
- Report any loss, theft, damage, or suspected misuse of Company assets immediately to their supervisor and the Loss Prevention Department.
- Return all Company property upon termination of employment or upon request.
- Not remove Company property from Company premises without proper authorization.

Incidental personal use of certain Company assets (such as a Company-issued mobile phone for personal calls during non-working hours) may be permitted in accordance with applicable Company policies, but such use must not be excessive, inappropriate, or interfere with business operations.

### 6.3 Intellectual Property

The Company's intellectual property is among its most valuable assets. Intellectual property includes:

- **Trademarks and service marks**, including the BuildRight name, logo, taglines, and trade dress.
- **Patents**, inventions, and proprietary processes.
- **Copyrights** in software, publications, training materials, marketing content, and other creative works.
- **Trade secrets** and confidential business information (see Section 8 for further detail).
- **Domain names** and social media handles owned by the Company.
- **Proprietary databases**, including customer databases, supplier databases, and pricing databases.

Associates must:

- Protect the Company's intellectual property from unauthorized use, disclosure, or infringement.
- Not use the Company's intellectual property for personal benefit without prior written authorization.
- Not copy, reproduce, distribute, or transmit Company-owned intellectual property outside the scope of their authorized duties.
- Report any suspected infringement of the Company's intellectual property rights to the Legal Department.
- Comply with all applicable copyright, trademark, and patent laws when using third-party intellectual property.

Work product created by Associates in the course of their employment is the exclusive property of the Company.

### 6.4 Proprietary Information

Proprietary information includes all non-public information about the Company's business, operations, strategies, and plans that gives the Company a competitive advantage. Associates must safeguard proprietary information with the same degree of care they would use to protect their own most valuable personal information.

### 6.5 Company Time

Associates are expected to devote their working hours to their assigned job responsibilities and duties. Company time is a valuable resource, and Associates must not misuse it. Using Company time for personal business, outside employment, or activities unrelated to the Associate's job duties — other than de minimis personal activities during authorized breaks — is a misuse of Company assets and a violation of this Code.

### 6.6 Information Technology Systems

The Company's information technology systems — including email, internet access, intranet, software applications, and network infrastructure — are Company assets provided for legitimate business use. The Company reserves the right to monitor, access, and review all communications and data transmitted, received, or stored on Company systems, to the extent permitted by applicable law. Associates should have no expectation of personal privacy when using Company systems.

Associates must not:

- Use Company IT systems to access, create, store, or distribute content that is illegal, offensive, discriminatory, harassing, or otherwise inappropriate.
- Install unauthorized software or hardware on Company systems.
- Attempt to circumvent or disable security controls, firewalls, or monitoring systems.
- Share their login credentials or allow unauthorized persons to access Company systems.
- Use Company IT systems for any purpose that violates this Code or Company policy.

## 7. Confidentiality

### 7.1 Obligation of Confidentiality

All Associates have a duty to maintain the confidentiality of non-public information about the Company, its customers, suppliers, Associates, and business partners that they obtain in the course of their employment or engagement with the Company. This obligation is fundamental to the trust placed in each Associate and to the Company's competitive position.

### 7.2 Types of Confidential Information

Confidential information includes, but is not limited to:

**Business and Strategic Information:**
- Business plans, strategies, and forecasts.
- Mergers, acquisitions, divestitures, and joint venture negotiations.
- Marketing and advertising strategies, campaigns, and budgets.
- New store locations, expansion plans, and market entry strategies.
- Pricing strategies, cost structures, and profit margins.
- Supplier negotiations, contract terms, and procurement strategies.
- Financial results, projections, and budgets before public disclosure.
- Internal reports, analyses, and management deliberations.

**Trade Secrets:**
- Proprietary processes, formulas, and methods.
- Software source code, algorithms, and technical architecture.
- Product development plans and research and development data.
- Customer lists, supplier lists, and vendor information.
- Logistics and distribution optimization models.
- Merchandising strategies and planogram designs.

**Customer Information:**
- Customer names, contact information, and purchase histories.
- Customer account data, payment information, and credit data.
- Customer preferences, project details, and service records.
- Personally identifiable information (PII) and personal data of customers, as further governed by BRHI-POL-005 (Information Security and Data Privacy Policy).

**Associate Information:**
- Personnel records, compensation data, and benefits information.
- Performance evaluations, disciplinary records, and medical information.
- Social Security numbers, tax identification numbers, and banking information.

**Third-Party Confidential Information:**
- Information received from vendors, suppliers, customers, and business partners under non-disclosure agreements or in circumstances where confidentiality is expected.

### 7.3 Handling Confidential Information

Associates must:

- Access confidential information only as needed to perform their job duties (need-to-know basis).
- Store confidential information securely, whether in physical or electronic form, using appropriate access controls and encryption.
- Not discuss confidential information in public places, including restaurants, airports, elevators, social gatherings, or on social media.
- Not share confidential information with unauthorized Associates, family members, friends, or any third party.
- Clearly label or identify confidential information when sharing it internally with authorized recipients.
- Use secure communication channels when transmitting confidential information.

### 7.4 Non-Disclosure Obligations During Employment

During the term of their employment or engagement, Associates must not disclose, publish, or otherwise disseminate confidential information to any third party without prior written authorization from the Legal Department, except as required by their job duties or as required by law.

### 7.5 Non-Disclosure Obligations After Employment

The obligation to maintain the confidentiality of the Company's confidential information survives the termination of employment or engagement, regardless of the reason for termination. Former Associates must not use, disclose, or benefit from any confidential information obtained during their association with the Company. Upon termination, Associates must return all Company property, including all documents and electronic media containing confidential information, and must not retain copies.

### 7.6 Legally Required Disclosures

If an Associate is required by law, regulation, or legal process to disclose any confidential information, they must provide prompt written notice to the Legal Department before making the disclosure, so that the Company may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. If a protective order or other remedy is not obtained, the Associate may disclose only that portion of the confidential information that is legally required to be disclosed.

### 7.7 Non-Disclosure Agreements

Associates who have access to confidential information may be required to execute a non-disclosure agreement (NDA) as a condition of employment or engagement. These agreements are enforceable and will be enforced. Associates must also ensure that appropriate NDAs are in place before sharing Company confidential information with any third party.

## 8. Fair Dealing

### 8.1 General Principle

BRHI is committed to dealing fairly with its customers, suppliers, competitors, and Associates. Each Associate should endeavor to respect the rights of all stakeholders and to deal with them in good faith. The Company's reputation for integrity and fair dealing is a critical competitive advantage and must be preserved.

### 8.2 Fair Dealing with Customers

Associates must:

- Represent the Company's products and services accurately and honestly, including pricing, availability, features, performance characteristics, and warranty coverage.
- Not engage in deceptive, misleading, or fraudulent sales practices, including bait-and-switch tactics, false advertising, or misrepresentation of product specifications.
- Honor the Company's price-matching, return, and refund policies as written.
- Provide customers with complete and truthful information to enable them to make informed purchasing decisions.
- Respect customer privacy and handle customer data in accordance with applicable privacy laws and Company policy.
- Not take unfair advantage of customers through manipulation, concealment, abuse of confidential information, or misrepresentation of material facts.
- Deliver on the Company's commitments for installation services, special orders, and project consultations in a timely and professional manner.

### 8.3 Fair Dealing with Suppliers

Associates must:

- Treat suppliers with professionalism, courtesy, and respect.
- Evaluate suppliers based on legitimate business criteria, including quality, price, delivery, service, and reliability.
- Not accept kickbacks, bribes, or other improper inducements from suppliers in exchange for favorable treatment.
- Not coerce, threaten, or intimidate suppliers into providing favorable terms.
- Honor contractual commitments and pay suppliers in accordance with agreed-upon terms.
- Protect confidential information shared by suppliers in the course of the business relationship.
- Avoid creating dependencies that unfairly restrict a supplier's ability to do business with others, unless agreed to as part of a legitimate exclusive arrangement.

### 8.4 Fair Dealing with Competitors

Associates must:

- Compete vigorously but lawfully and ethically.
- Not make false, misleading, or disparaging statements about competitors or their products or services.
- Not engage in industrial espionage, unauthorized surveillance, or improper acquisition of competitor information.
- Not hire a competitor's Associates for the purpose of obtaining confidential information.
- Not attempt to induce a competitor's customers to breach their contracts.
- Obtain competitive intelligence only through lawful and ethical means, such as reviewing public filings, attending trade shows, reading published reports, and engaging in legitimate market research.

### 8.5 Anti-Competitive Behavior Prohibition

Associates are strictly prohibited from engaging in any conduct that may violate antitrust or competition laws, including but not limited to:

- **Price-fixing:** Agreements with competitors to set prices, discounts, profit margins, or credit terms.
- **Bid-rigging:** Agreements with competitors to predetermine the outcome of competitive bidding processes.
- **Market allocation:** Agreements with competitors to divide markets, territories, or customers.
- **Group boycotts:** Agreements with competitors to refuse to deal with a particular supplier, customer, or competitor.
- **Tying arrangements:** Requiring customers to purchase one product or service as a condition of purchasing another, where such arrangements are unlawful.
- **Predatory pricing:** Pricing products below cost with the intent to eliminate competition.
- **Resale price maintenance:** Dictating the prices at which resellers may sell the Company's products, where prohibited by law.

Even informal discussions with competitors about prices, costs, marketing strategies, customer allocations, or other competitively sensitive topics can create the appearance of improper collusion and must be avoided. If a competitor initiates such a discussion, the Associate must immediately and clearly state that they cannot participate in such a conversation, disengage, and report the incident to the Legal Department.

## 9. Insider Trading

### 9.1 Prohibition on Trading on Material Non-Public Information

No Associate, Director, officer, contractor, or any family member or designee of any such person may purchase, sell, or otherwise trade in the securities of BRHI or any other company while in possession of material non-public information about that company. It is also prohibited to communicate ("tip") material non-public information to any other person who might trade on the basis of that information.

This prohibition applies to all types of securities, including common stock, preferred stock, bonds, debentures, options, warrants, and derivative instruments.

### 9.2 Definition of Material Information

Information is considered "material" if there is a reasonable likelihood that a reasonable investor would consider it important in deciding whether to buy, hold, or sell a security, or if the information would reasonably be expected to significantly affect the market price of the security. Examples of material information include, but are not limited to:

- Financial results or projections that have not been publicly released.
- Pending or proposed mergers, acquisitions, divestitures, or joint ventures.
- Significant new store openings or closures.
- Major supply chain disruptions or product recalls.
- Significant litigation or government investigations.
- Changes in executive leadership or Board composition.
- Major contract awards or losses.
- Stock splits, dividend changes, or share repurchase programs.
- Cybersecurity incidents or data breaches.

### 9.3 Definition of Non-Public Information

Information is "non-public" if it has not been disseminated in a manner making it available to investors generally (e.g., through a press release, SEC filing, or widely attended public conference call) and sufficient time has not passed for the market to absorb the information. As a general guideline, Associates should consider information to remain non-public until at least two full business days after the Company has publicly released the information.

### 9.4 Blackout Periods

The Company imposes mandatory blackout periods during which certain Associates are prohibited from trading in BRHI securities. The following blackout periods apply:

- **Quarterly blackout period:** Begins on the last business day of each fiscal quarter and ends at the opening of business on the second business day after the Company's quarterly earnings release.
- **Event-specific blackout periods:** May be imposed at any time by the General Counsel when the Company is in possession of material non-public information. Associates subject to an event-specific blackout period will be notified by the Legal Department.

The blackout period policy applies to all Directors, executive officers, and Associates designated by the General Counsel as having regular access to material non-public information. A current list of designated insiders is maintained by the Legal Department.

### 9.5 Pre-Clearance Requirements

All Directors, executive officers, and designated insiders must obtain pre-clearance from the General Counsel (or designee) before engaging in any transaction involving BRHI securities, including purchases, sales, gifts, and exercises of stock options, even outside of blackout periods. Pre-clearance requests must be submitted at least two business days before the proposed transaction.

### 9.6 Additional Prohibitions

- **Short-selling:** No Associate may sell BRHI securities short or sell securities against the box.
- **Trading in derivatives:** No Associate may purchase or sell put options, call options, or other derivative instruments based on BRHI securities.
- **Hedging transactions:** No Director or executive officer may engage in hedging or monetization transactions (such as prepaid variable forward contracts, equity swaps, collars, or exchange funds) with respect to BRHI securities.
- **Pledging:** Directors and executive officers are discouraged from pledging BRHI securities as collateral for loans and must obtain pre-clearance before doing so.

### 9.7 Penalties

Violations of insider trading laws carry severe penalties, including:

- Civil fines of up to three times the profit gained or loss avoided.
- Criminal fines of up to $5,000,000 for individuals and $25,000,000 for entities.
- Imprisonment of up to 20 years.
- Disgorgement of all profits gained or losses avoided.
- Injunctive relief and bars from serving as an officer or director of a public company.

In addition, violations of this policy will result in disciplinary action by the Company, up to and including immediate termination of employment for cause.

- Segregation of duties.
- Authorization and approval requirements.
- Reconciliation procedures.
- Documentation and record-keeping requirements.

- Immediately cease all destruction or disposal of documents and records identified in the hold notice.
- Preserve all relevant documents and records, including electronic communications and files, in their current form and location.
- Not alter, modify, delete, or overwrite any relevant electronic data.
- Comply with all instructions provided in the litigation hold notice.
- Notify the Legal Department if they are aware of relevant documents or records that may be subject to the hold but are not specifically identified in the notice.

Failure to comply with a litigation hold is a serious violation of Company policy and may constitute obstruction of justice, spoliation of evidence, or other violations of law.

## 12. Anti-Discrimination and Harassment

### 12.1 Zero Tolerance Policy

BRHI is unwaveringly committed to providing a workplace free from discrimination, harassment, and retaliation. The Company maintains a zero tolerance policy toward any form of discrimination or harassment based on a protected characteristic.

### 12.2 Equal Employment Opportunity

The Company provides equal employment opportunities to all Associates and applicants for employment without regard to:

- Race, color, or ethnicity.
- National origin or ancestry.
- Sex (including pregnancy, childbirth, and related medical conditions).
- Gender identity or gender expression.
- Sexual orientation.
- Age (40 years of age and older).
- Religion or creed.
- Disability (physical or mental).
- Genetic information.
- Military or veteran status.
- Marital status.
- Family responsibilities.
- Political affiliation.
- Any other characteristic protected by applicable federal, state, provincial, or local law.

This policy applies to all aspects of employment, including recruitment, hiring, placement, promotion, demotion, transfer, layoff, recall, termination, compensation, benefits, training, and all other terms and conditions of employment.

### 12.3 Prohibition of Harassment

The Company prohibits all forms of harassment, including:

**Sexual Harassment:** Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal, visual, or physical conduct of a sexual nature when:
- Submission to such conduct is made either explicitly or implicitly a term or condition of an individual's employment.
- Submission to or rejection of such conduct is used as the basis for employment decisions affecting the individual.
- Such conduct has the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with an individual's work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive working environment.

**Other Harassment:** Verbal, visual, or physical conduct that denigrates, threatens, or shows hostility toward an individual based on any protected characteristic when such conduct:
- Has the purpose or effect of creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive working environment.
- Has the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with an individual's work performance.
- Otherwise adversely affects an individual's employment opportunities.

Examples of prohibited harassment include, but are not limited to:

- Offensive jokes, slurs, epithets, or name-calling.
- Physical assaults or threats of assault.
- Intimidation, ridicule, or mockery.
- Insults or put-downs based on a protected characteristic.
- Offensive objects, pictures, posters, or cartoons.
- Interference with an individual's work or movement.
- Persistent unwelcome comments about an individual's appearance, body, or personal life.
- Unwelcome physical contact, including touching, hugging, or massaging.
- Sexual gestures, leering, or staring.
- Sending or displaying offensive electronic communications, including emails, text messages, or social media posts.

### 12.4 Reporting Procedures

The Company provides multiple channels for reporting discrimination or harassment. Associates are encouraged to report concerns through any of the following channels:

1. **Direct Supervisor.** Associates may report concerns to their direct supervisor. If the concern involves the direct supervisor, the Associate should report to the next level of management.
2. **Human Resources Department.** Associates may report concerns to any Human Resources representative or to the Vice President of Human Resources.
3. **Ethics and Compliance Office.** Associates may report concerns to the Chief Ethics and Compliance Officer or any member of the Ethics and Compliance team.
4. **Ethics Hotline.** Associates may call the confidential Ethics Hotline at **1-800-BUILD-RIGHT** (1-800-284-5374), available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year. Reports may be made anonymously where permitted by applicable law.
5. **Web Reporting.** Associates may submit a report through the Company's secure online ethics reporting portal at **ethics.buildright.com**.
6. **Legal Department.** Associates may report concerns directly to the General Counsel.

The Company will accept and investigate all reports of discrimination or harassment, regardless of the channel through which they are received.

### 12.5 Investigation Process

All reports of discrimination or harassment will be taken seriously and investigated promptly, thoroughly, and impartially. The investigation process includes the following steps:

1. **Acknowledgment.** The Company will acknowledge receipt of the report within two business days.
2. **Assessment.** The Ethics and Compliance Office, in consultation with the Legal Department and Human Resources, will assess the report to determine the appropriate scope and method of investigation.
3. **Investigation.** A qualified investigator will be assigned to conduct the investigation. The investigator will interview the reporting party, the subject of the complaint, and any relevant witnesses, and will review any relevant documents or evidence.
4. **Confidentiality.** The investigation will be conducted with the maximum degree of confidentiality possible. Information will be shared only on a need-to-know basis. However, complete confidentiality cannot be guaranteed, as it may be necessary to disclose certain information to conduct a thorough investigation or to take remedial action.
5. **Findings and Action.** Upon completion of the investigation, the investigator will prepare a written report summarizing the findings. If the investigation substantiates the complaint, the Company will take appropriate corrective action, which may include disciplinary action against the offender (see Section 22).
6. **Communication.** The reporting party and the subject of the complaint will be informed of the outcome of the investigation to the extent permitted by applicable law and Company policy.
7. **Documentation.** All investigation reports and supporting documentation will be maintained in a confidential file in the Ethics and Compliance Office.

### 12.6 Non-Retaliation

The Company strictly prohibits retaliation against any Associate who, in good faith, reports discrimination, harassment, or any other violation of this Code or Company policy, or who participates in an investigation of such a report. Retaliation includes any adverse employment action taken against an Associate because they made a report or participated in an investigation, including but not limited to termination, demotion, transfer, reduction in hours, unfavorable work assignments, harassment, or ostracism.

Any Associate who engages in retaliation will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Associates who believe they have been subjected to retaliation should immediately report the retaliation through any of the reporting channels listed in Section 12.4.

BRHI recognizes that social media and digital communications are integral to modern business and personal life. Only authorized Associates may speak on behalf of the Company or represent the Company on social media platforms. The following rules apply to official Company communications:

## 16. Gifts and Entertainment

### 16.1 General Policy

The exchange of business courtesies, including gifts and entertainment, can build goodwill and strengthen business relationships. However, gifts and entertainment must never inappropriately influence — or appear to inappropriately influence — business decisions. This section establishes specific rules and thresholds governing the giving and receiving of gifts and entertainment in connection with Company business.

### 16.2 Receiving Gifts and Entertainment

Associates may accept gifts and entertainment from suppliers, vendors, customers, and other third parties only in compliance with the following rules:

#### 16.2.1 Permissible Gifts

The following types of gifts are permissible, subject to the thresholds and conditions described below:

- **Nominal gifts** with an estimated value of **$50 or less** per gift, and **$150 or less** in aggregate from the same source in any calendar year. Examples include branded promotional items (pens, mugs, notebooks), modest food items (fruit basket, cookies), and greeting cards.
- **Business meals** with an estimated value of **$100 or less** per person per occasion, provided the meal is attended by both the donor and the Associate (or a Company representative) and involves a bona fide business discussion.
- **Entertainment** with an estimated value of **$100 or less** per person per occasion, provided it is attended by both the donor and the Associate (or a Company representative) and is incidental to a bona fide business purpose. Examples include tickets to sporting events, theater performances, or concerts.

#### 16.2.2 Gifts Requiring Approval

The following gifts and entertainment require prior written approval from the Associate's supervisor and the Ethics and Compliance Office:

- Any gift with an estimated value exceeding **$50** but not exceeding **$250**.
- Any entertainment with an estimated value exceeding **$100** but not exceeding **$250** per person.
- Travel or lodging provided by a third party, regardless of value.
- Invitations to events, conferences, or functions where the primary purpose is entertainment rather than education or business development.

#### 16.2.3 Prohibited Gifts

The following gifts are strictly prohibited, regardless of value:

- Cash or cash equivalents (gift cards, prepaid debit cards, checks, money orders, stock certificates, or similar instruments). **Exception:** Gift cards with a value of $25 or less from a vendor as a general promotional item during the holiday season are permissible but must be reported to the Ethics and Compliance Office.
- Gifts that are solicited by the Associate.
- Gifts that are conditioned on or appear to be conditioned on any action or decision by the Associate.
- Gifts that could create a sense of obligation or could compromise (or appear to compromise) the Associate's objectivity.
- Any gift that would violate applicable law, including gifts to or from government officials (see Section 4).
- Gifts of alcohol or tobacco products.
- Gifts that are lavish, extravagant, or inappropriate in nature.
- Any gift from a supplier or vendor during an active competitive bidding or procurement process in which the supplier or vendor is a participant.

#### 16.2.4 Returning Gifts

If an Associate receives a gift that exceeds the permissible thresholds or is otherwise prohibited, the Associate must:

1. Promptly (within five business days) notify the Ethics and Compliance Office.
2. Return the gift to the donor with a courteous explanation of the Company's gift policy.
3. If returning the gift is impractical (e.g., perishable items), the Associate may donate the gift to a charitable organization approved by the Company, share it among the team, or, with Ethics and Compliance Office approval, retain it and report its value as taxable income in accordance with Company policy.

### 16.3 Giving Gifts and Entertainment

Associates may provide gifts and entertainment to customers, suppliers, and other third parties only in compliance with the following rules:

- All gifts and entertainment must have a legitimate business purpose.
- The value of any individual gift must not exceed **$50**, and the aggregate value of gifts to the same recipient must not exceed **$150** in any calendar year, unless approved in advance by the Ethics and Compliance Office.
- The cost of any business meal or entertainment event provided to a third party must not exceed **$100 per person** per occasion without prior approval from the Ethics and Compliance Office.
- No gift or entertainment may be provided to any government official or government employee without prior approval from the Legal Department (see Section 4).
- No cash or cash equivalents may be given as gifts.
- All gifts and entertainment expenses must be accurately recorded in the Company's books and records with sufficient detail to identify the recipient, business purpose, and amount.
- Associates must comply with the gift and entertainment policies of the recipient's organization. If the recipient's policies are more restrictive than this policy, the recipient's policies shall prevail.

### 16.4 Government Officials

Special rules apply to gifts and entertainment provided to government officials. As described in Section 4, the Company is subject to anti-corruption laws that strictly regulate the provision of anything of value to government officials. No gift, entertainment, meal, travel, or other benefit may be provided to any government official without prior written approval from the Legal Department, regardless of the amount or perceived insignificance.

### 20.1 Duty to Report

Every Associate has a responsibility to report suspected violations of this Code, Company policy, or applicable law. Reporting concerns is not optional — it is an obligation of employment and a cornerstone of the Company's ethics and compliance program. Associates who are aware of or suspect a violation and fail to report it may be subject to disciplinary action.

### 20.2 Reporting Channels

The Company provides multiple channels for reporting concerns, ensuring that every Associate has access to a method with which they are comfortable. Reports may be made through any of the following channels:

1. **Direct Supervisor.** Associates may report concerns to their direct supervisor. Supervisors who receive reports are required to escalate them to the Ethics and Compliance Office within one business day.

2. **Human Resources Department.** Associates may report concerns to any Human Resources representative at any level of the organization. The Vice President of Human Resources is also available to receive reports.

3. **Ethics and Compliance Office.** Associates may contact the Ethics and Compliance Office directly:
   - **Email:** ethics@buildright.com
   - **Phone:** (770) 555-0100
   - **In person:** Suite 400, Corporate Headquarters

4. **Ethics Hotline.** The Company maintains a confidential, toll-free Ethics Hotline operated by an independent third-party provider:
   - **Phone:** **1-800-BUILD-RIGHT** (1-800-284-5374)
   - **Availability:** 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year
   - **Languages:** Reports may be made in over 200 languages
   - **Anonymity:** Reports may be made anonymously where permitted by applicable law. The hotline operator will not attempt to identify the caller.

5. **Web Reporting.** Associates may submit a report through the Company's secure online ethics reporting portal:
   - **URL:** **ethics.buildright.com**
   - **Features:** Secure, encrypted, and accessible from any device; allows anonymous reporting and two-way anonymous communication with the Ethics and Compliance Office

6. **Legal Department.** Associates may report concerns directly to the General Counsel or any member of the Legal Department.

7. **Board Audit Committee.** Associates may report concerns directly to the Chair of the Audit Committee of the Board of Directors by writing to:
   - Chair, Audit Committee
   - BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc.
   - P.O. Box 100000
   - Atlanta, GA 30301

### 20.3 Types of Reports

Associates should report any conduct that they believe in good faith may violate this Code, Company policy, or applicable law, including but not limited to:

- Accounting or financial irregularities.
- Fraud, theft, or embezzlement.
- Bribery, corruption, or kickbacks.
- Discrimination, harassment, or retaliation.
- Safety hazards or environmental violations.
- Conflicts of interest.
- Insider trading.
- Violations of customer privacy or data security.
- Anti-competitive behavior.
- Violations of human rights or labor standards.
- Retaliation against anyone who has made a good faith report.
- Any other unethical, illegal, or improper conduct.

### 20.4 Non-Retaliation Policy

The Company strictly prohibits retaliation against any person who, in good faith:

- Reports a suspected violation of this Code, Company policy, or applicable law.
- Participates in or cooperates with an investigation of a reported concern.
- Files a complaint with a government agency or regulatory body.
- Testifies in a legal proceeding related to a compliance matter.

Retaliation is a serious violation of this Code and will result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. The Company takes non-retaliation seriously and will actively monitor for signs of retaliation following any report or investigation.

If an Associate believes they have been subjected to retaliation, they should immediately report the retaliation through any of the reporting channels described above.

### 20.5 Investigation Process

All reports of suspected violations will be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and impartially. The investigation process is as follows:

1. **Intake.** The report will be received and logged by the Ethics and Compliance Office. The reporting party will receive an acknowledgment within two business days where contact information has been provided.

2. **Triage.** The Ethics and Compliance Office, in consultation with the Legal Department, will assess the report to determine the nature and severity of the alleged conduct, the appropriate investigative approach, and the need for any immediate remedial action.

3. **Assignment.** A qualified investigator will be assigned to the case. Depending on the nature of the allegations, the investigation may be conducted by the Ethics and Compliance Office, the Internal Audit Department, the Legal Department, Human Resources, or an external investigator.

4. **Investigation.** The investigator will gather evidence, interview relevant witnesses, and review relevant documents. All Associates are required to cooperate fully with investigations and to provide truthful and complete information.

5. **Report.** The investigator will prepare a written report summarizing the findings, conclusions, and recommended actions.

6. **Action.** The Ethics and Compliance Office, in consultation with the Legal Department, Human Resources, and management as appropriate, will determine and implement appropriate corrective and remedial actions. Corrective actions may include disciplinary action, process improvements, training, policy changes, or other measures.

7. **Follow-up.** The Ethics and Compliance Office will follow up to ensure that corrective actions have been implemented and that the underlying issue has been resolved. Where contact information has been provided, the reporting party will be informed of the outcome to the extent permitted by applicable law and Company policy.

8. **Documentation.** All investigation records will be maintained in a confidential file in the Ethics and Compliance Office in accordance with the Company's document retention policy.

### 20.6 False Reports

The Company encourages good faith reporting of all suspected violations. However, deliberately making false or malicious reports is a violation of this Code and may result in disciplinary action. This provision is not intended to discourage reports made in good faith, even if the investigation does not substantiate the report. The Company will not take action against any Associate who makes a report in good faith, regardless of the outcome of the investigation.

## 21. Disciplinary Actions

### 21.1 General Principle

Violations of this Code, Company policies, or applicable laws may result in disciplinary action. The Company applies disciplinary measures consistently and fairly, taking into account the nature, severity, and circumstances of the violation, the Associate's role and level of responsibility, the Associate's prior disciplinary history, and the need to deter future violations.

### 21.2 Progressive Discipline

Where appropriate, the Company may follow a progressive discipline approach for non-egregious violations, which may include the following steps (not necessarily in this order, and the Company may skip steps or impose any level of discipline warranted by the circumstances):

1. **Verbal counseling.** A documented conversation between the Associate and their supervisor or Human Resources identifying the violation, explaining the expected standard of conduct, and outlining the consequences of further violations.

2. **Written warning.** A formal written notice documenting the violation and warning that further violations may result in more severe disciplinary action.

3. **Performance improvement plan.** A formal plan outlining specific expectations, milestones, and timelines for the Associate to correct their behavior or performance.

4. **Suspension.** A temporary removal from duties, with or without pay, pending further investigation or as a disciplinary measure.

5. **Demotion or reassignment.** Transfer to a position of lesser responsibility or a different role within the Company.

6. **Termination of employment.** Discharge from employment for cause.

7. **Legal action.** Referral to law enforcement, regulatory authorities, or initiation of civil legal proceedings.

### 21.3 Egregious Violations

Certain violations are considered so serious that they may result in immediate termination of employment without progressive discipline, including but not limited to:

- Engaging in fraud, theft, embezzlement, or other dishonest conduct.
- Engaging in bribery, corruption, or kickbacks.
- Engaging in insider trading.
- Engaging in discrimination, harassment, or retaliation.
- Engaging in workplace violence or making threats of violence.
- Possessing weapons on Company premises.
- Using, possessing, or distributing illegal drugs on Company premises.
- Willfully violating safety rules resulting in serious injury or death.
- Falsifying Company records or financial statements.
- Obstructing or interfering with an investigation.
- Retaliating against a whistleblower.
- Engaging in conduct that constitutes a criminal offense.
- Gross insubordination or refusal to comply with lawful directives.

### 21.4 Accountability for Managers and Supervisors

Managers and supervisors are held to a higher standard of accountability. In addition to being subject to disciplinary action for their own violations, managers and supervisors may be disciplined for:

- Failing to monitor and enforce compliance within their areas of responsibility.
- Failing to report known or suspected violations.
- Creating or contributing to an environment that encourages or tolerates non-compliance.
- Retaliating against or failing to protect Associates who report concerns in good faith.

## 22. Waivers

### 22.1 Waiver Process

Waivers of any provision of this Code may be granted only in exceptional circumstances and only through the process described below. The fact that a waiver was granted in one instance does not constitute a precedent or create a right to a waiver in any other instance.

### 22.2 Waivers for Associates

Waivers of this Code for Associates (other than Directors and executive officers) may be granted only by the Chief Ethics and Compliance Officer, with the concurrence of the General Counsel. A written request for a waiver must be submitted to the Ethics and Compliance Office, describing:

- The specific provision of the Code for which a waiver is requested.
- The reason the waiver is necessary.
- The duration of the requested waiver.
- The potential impact of the waiver on the Company and the Associate.
- Any mitigating measures that will be implemented to address the potential impact.

The Ethics and Compliance Office will evaluate the request and communicate its decision in writing. If a waiver is granted, it will be documented, including the specific terms and conditions, the duration, and any mitigating measures.

### 22.3 Waivers for Directors and Executive Officers

Waivers of this Code for Directors and executive officers may be granted only by the Board of Directors (or a designated Board committee, such as the Nominating and Corporate Governance Committee), acting in its discretion. Any such waiver will be disclosed promptly to the Company's shareholders as required by applicable securities laws and stock exchange listing requirements.

### 22.4 Implied Waivers

The Company's failure to enforce a provision of this Code in a particular instance does not constitute a waiver of that provision for any other instance or for any other Associate. The Company reserves the right to enforce all provisions of this Code at all times.

## 23. Annual Certification

### 23.1 Certification Requirement

All Directors, officers, and Associates are required to certify annually that they have:

1. Read and understood this Code of Business Conduct and Ethics.
2. Complied with all provisions of this Code during the certification period.
3. Reported any known or suspected violations of this Code of which they are aware.
4. Disclosed any actual, potential, or perceived conflicts of interest.
5. Completed all required compliance training programs.

### 23.2 Certification Process

The annual certification will be administered through the Company's learning management system and will be available beginning on the first business day of each calendar year. All Associates must complete their certification within 30 days of the start of the certification period, or within 30 days of their date of hire, whichever is later.

New Associates must complete their initial certification as part of the onboarding process, within 14 calendar days of their start date.

### 23.3 Non-Compliance with Certification

Associates who fail to complete their annual certification within the required timeframe will be subject to escalation, which may include:

- Written reminders from the Ethics and Compliance Office.
- Notification to the Associate's supervisor.
- Suspension of certain system access or privileges.
- Further disciplinary action in accordance with Section 22.

### 23.4 Special Certifications

In addition to the annual certification, the Company may require specific groups of Associates to complete supplemental certifications related to their particular roles or responsibilities. For example:

- Associates with access to material non-public information may be required to certify their compliance with the insider trading policy on a quarterly basis.
- Associates in procurement or vendor management roles may be required to certify their compliance with the anti-corruption policy annually.
- Associates in finance and accounting roles may be required to certify their compliance with the financial integrity and records management policies.

## Appendix B: Decision-Making Guide

When facing an ethical dilemma, Associates should ask themselves the following questions:

1. **Is it legal?** If the answer is no, do not do it. If unsure, consult the Legal Department.

2. **Is it consistent with this Code and Company policies?** If the answer is no, do not do it.

3. **Is it consistent with the Company's Core Ethical Principles?** Would my action reflect honesty, fairness, respect, responsibility, and transparency?

4. **Would I be comfortable if my actions were reported on the front page of a major newspaper?** If the answer is no, reconsider your course of action.

5. **Would I be comfortable if my family, friends, or colleagues knew about my actions?** If the answer is no, reconsider your course of action.

6. **Is there a right thing to do and a wrong thing to do, or is the situation ambiguous?** If ambiguous, seek guidance from your supervisor, the Ethics and Compliance Office, or the Legal Department before acting.

7. **Am I being pressured by anyone to act in a way that feels wrong?** If so, report the pressure to the Ethics and Compliance Office or the Legal Department.

**When in doubt, do not act. Seek guidance first.**
