## 1. Employment Policies

### 1.1 Equal Employment Opportunity

BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. is an equal opportunity employer and is committed to providing an inclusive, non-discriminatory work environment. The Company does not discriminate against any employee or applicant for employment on the basis of race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity), national origin, age (40 and over), disability, genetic information (including family medical history), marital status, veteran status, uniformed service member status, citizenship status, or any other classification protected by applicable federal, state, or local law.

This policy applies to all terms and conditions of employment, including but not limited to hiring, placement, promotion, termination, layoff, recall, transfer, leaves of absence, compensation, benefits, and training. Any employee who believes they have been subjected to discrimination in violation of this policy should immediately report the matter through the procedures described in Section 7 of this document.

Retaliation against any individual who, in good faith, reports discrimination or participates in an investigation is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

The Vice President of Human Resources serves as the Company's EEO Coordinator and is responsible for overseeing compliance with this policy.

### 1.2 At-Will Employment

Employment at BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. is at-will. Nothing in this policy document, any Company communication, or any practice creates an express or implied contract, guarantee of employment for any specific duration, or promise of continued employment. Either the employee or the Company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without advance notice.

This at-will employment relationship cannot be modified by any oral or implied agreement. Only the Chief Executive Officer of BRHI, in a written document signed by both the CEO and the employee, may alter the at-will status of any employee. The existence of this policies document does not in any way alter the at-will nature of employment.

### 1.3 Hiring and Recruitment

#### 1.3.1 Job Postings

All open positions will be posted on the BRHI internal career portal and external job boards for a minimum of five (5) business days before an offer is extended. Postings will include the job title, department, location, employment category, a summary of essential duties, minimum qualifications, and the pay range for the position. Where legally required, postings will also include salary transparency information in accordance with applicable state and local pay transparency laws.

#### 1.3.2 Internal Transfers

Current employees who have completed at least twelve (12) months in their current role and who are in good standing (no active corrective action on file) are eligible to apply for posted positions. Employees interested in a transfer must submit a completed Internal Transfer Request Form through the BRHI career portal and must notify their current supervisor of their application no later than the application deadline. Internal candidates will be given consideration alongside external applicants and will be interviewed if they meet the minimum qualifications for the position.

#### 1.3.3 Background Checks

All offers of employment are contingent upon the successful completion of a background check. BRHI conducts criminal background checks covering the seven (7) years preceding the date of the background check in all jurisdictions where the applicant has resided, worked, or attended school. Background checks may also include verification of education credentials, employment history, and professional licenses or certifications as relevant to the position.

Background checks are conducted by a third-party consumer reporting agency in compliance with the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) and all applicable state and local "ban the box" and fair chance hiring laws. Adverse action procedures, including pre-adverse action notice and the applicant's right to dispute inaccurate information, will be followed when a background check may result in withdrawal of an offer.

#### 1.3.4 Drug Testing

All offers of employment are contingent upon the successful completion of a pre-employment drug screening. Testing is conducted by a certified laboratory and screens for the following substances: amphetamines, cannabinoids (THC), cocaine metabolites, opiates, phencyclidine (PCP), barbiturates, benzodiazepines, methadone, methaqualone, and propoxyphene. Testing may also include alcohol screening where permitted by applicable law.

A confirmed positive result, refusal to submit to testing, or adulteration of a specimen will result in withdrawal of the employment offer. Applicants who are using legally prescribed medications must disclose them at the time of testing to the medical review officer. Results are confidential and shared only with authorized HR personnel.

#### 1.3.5 Employment Eligibility Verification (Form I-9)

In compliance with the Immigration Reform and Control Act (IRCA), all newly hired employees must complete a Form I-9 no later than their first day of work for pay. Employees must present original, unexpired documents that establish both identity and employment authorization within three (3) business days of their start date. BRHI participates in E-Verify where required by law. Re-verification is conducted as required for employees with temporary work authorization. Employees who cannot provide valid documentation within the required timeframe will not be permitted to continue working.

### 1.4 Employment Categories

#### 1.4.1 Full-Time Employees

Full-time employees are those who are regularly scheduled to work thirty (30) or more hours per week. Full-time employees are eligible for all Company benefits described in this document, subject to the applicable waiting periods and eligibility requirements.

#### 1.4.2 Part-Time Employees

Part-time employees are those who are regularly scheduled to work fewer than thirty (30) hours per week. Part-time employees are eligible for certain benefits as required by law (including earned sick leave where mandated) and may be eligible for limited voluntary benefits at their own cost. Part-time employees who average thirty (30) or more hours per week over a measurement period may become eligible for medical benefits in accordance with the Affordable Care Act (ACA) measurement and stability period rules.

#### 1.4.3 Seasonal Employees

Seasonal employees are hired for a defined seasonal period not to exceed six (6) months in duration. Seasonal employees are not eligible for Company benefits unless required by law. Seasonal employees who are retained beyond the defined seasonal period will be reclassified to the appropriate employment category and will become eligible for benefits as of the reclassification date, subject to applicable waiting periods.

#### 1.4.4 Temporary Employees

Temporary employees are engaged for a specific project or limited duration not to exceed twelve (12) months. Temporary employees may be hired directly or through a staffing agency. Directly hired temporary employees are eligible for benefits as required by law. Agency temporary employees are employees of the staffing agency and are not eligible for BRHI benefits.

#### 1.4.5 Exempt and Non-Exempt Classification

All employees are classified as either exempt or non-exempt in accordance with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and applicable state law. Exempt employees are generally salaried and are not entitled to overtime pay. Non-exempt employees are entitled to overtime pay at one and one-half (1.5) times their regular rate of pay for all hours worked in excess of forty (40) hours in a workweek. Job classification and FLSA status are communicated to each employee at the time of hire and are documented in the employee's offer letter. The Company periodically audits classifications to ensure ongoing compliance.

### 1.5 Probationary Period

All newly hired employees serve a ninety (90) calendar day probationary period beginning on their start date. During the probationary period, employees receive orientation and onboarding training, are evaluated for job suitability, and must demonstrate satisfactory performance and conduct. The probationary period may be extended by up to an additional thirty (30) calendar days at the discretion of the department manager in consultation with Human Resources.

During the probationary period, either the Company or the employee may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and without the progressive disciplinary procedures described in Section 6. Probationary employees are not eligible for paid time off (except as required by law), are not eligible for the annual bonus program, and may be subject to additional attendance and performance standards. Upon successful completion of the probationary period, employees will receive written confirmation of their regular employment status.

## 2. Compensation and Benefits

### 2.1 Compensation Philosophy

BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. is committed to providing market-competitive compensation that attracts, retains, and motivates a talented workforce. The Company conducts a comprehensive market compensation survey at least annually, benchmarking positions against comparable roles in the retail home improvement industry and the geographic labor markets in which BRHI operates. Compensation decisions are based on a combination of external market data, internal equity considerations, individual performance, and organizational budget constraints.

### 2.2 Pay Structure

BRHI maintains a fifteen (15) grade pay structure. Each grade has an established pay range with a minimum, midpoint, and maximum. The pay structure is reviewed annually and adjusted as necessary to remain competitive with the external labor market. Placement within a pay range is determined by factors including relevant experience, qualifications, performance, and internal equity relative to other employees in the same grade.

The fifteen grades are organized as follows:

| Grade Range | Typical Positions |
|---|---|
| Grades 1–3 | Entry-level associates, cashiers, lot attendants, stock associates |
| Grades 4–6 | Experienced associates, department specialists, customer service representatives |
| Grades 7–9 | Department supervisors, team leads, skilled specialists |
| Grades 10–12 | Assistant store managers, district specialists, corporate coordinators |
| Grades 13–15 | Store managers, district managers, directors, vice presidents, executive leadership |

### 2.3 Overtime

Non-exempt employees are compensated at one and one-half (1.5) times their regular hourly rate for all hours worked in excess of forty (40) hours in a defined workweek (Sunday through Saturday). All overtime must be pre-approved in writing by the employee's direct supervisor or department manager before the overtime hours are worked. Working unauthorized overtime is a violation of Company policy and may result in disciplinary action; however, the Company will compensate the employee for all hours actually worked regardless of authorization.

Supervisors are responsible for monitoring work schedules and managing overtime to control labor costs while ensuring adequate staffing. Overtime records must accurately reflect all hours worked and are subject to audit.

### 2.4 Payroll

Employees are paid on a bi-weekly basis, with paychecks issued every other Friday. If a scheduled payday falls on a Company-observed holiday, paychecks will be issued on the preceding business day. Direct deposit is available and encouraged for all employees. Paper checks are available for employees who do not enroll in direct deposit.

Pay stubs are available electronically through the BRHI employee self-service portal. Each pay stub includes a detailed breakdown of gross earnings, tax withholdings, benefit deductions, and net pay. Employees are responsible for reviewing their pay stubs for accuracy and reporting any discrepancies to their supervisor or Human Resources within thirty (30) calendar days of the pay date.

### 2.5 Performance Reviews

Annual performance reviews are conducted for all employees who have completed their probationary period. The performance review cycle runs from January 1 through December 31 of each calendar year. Completed reviews must be submitted to Human Resources no later than February 15 of the following year.

Performance is evaluated using a five-point rating scale:

| Rating | Description | Merit Increase Range |
|---|---|---|
| 5 — Exceptional | Consistently exceeds all expectations; demonstrates outstanding contribution | 6%–8% |
| 4 — Exceeds Expectations | Frequently exceeds expectations; demonstrates above-average contribution | 4%–5% |
| 3 — Meets Expectations | Consistently meets expectations; demonstrates solid, reliable contribution | 3% |
| 2 — Needs Improvement | Occasionally falls short of expectations; improvement required | 1%–2% |
| 1 — Unsatisfactory | Consistently fails to meet expectations; significant improvement required | 0% |

Merit increases, when awarded, are effective as of the first pay period in March following the review cycle. Employees on active corrective action at the time merit increases are processed are not eligible for a merit increase. Employees who receive a rating of 1 (Unsatisfactory) or 2 (Needs Improvement) will be placed on a performance improvement plan with defined goals and a timeline of sixty (60) to ninety (90) calendar days.

### 2.6 Bonus Programs

#### 2.6.1 Annual Incentive Bonus

Eligible employees in grades 7 and above may participate in the Annual Incentive Bonus Program. The bonus target is expressed as a percentage of the employee's annual base salary and varies by grade level. Bonus payouts are based on a combination of Company financial performance, business unit results, and individual performance. The annual incentive bonus is paid no later than March 15 following the performance year. Employees must be actively employed on the bonus payment date to receive the award. Probationary employees and employees on active corrective action are not eligible.

#### 2.6.2 Store Bonus Program

Store-level employees (grades 1–9) are eligible to participate in the Store Bonus Program. Each store location has monthly and quarterly sales and operational targets. When a store achieves or exceeds its targets, eligible employees receive a bonus payment. Store bonus amounts are calculated based on the employee's average weekly hours during the measurement period and the degree to which the store exceeded its targets. Store bonus payments are made within fifteen (15) business days following the end of the measurement period.

### 2.7 Benefits Overview

BRHI offers a comprehensive benefits package to eligible full-time employees. Benefits become effective on the first day of the month following sixty (60) calendar days of continuous full-time employment, unless otherwise specified. Part-time, seasonal, and temporary employees may be eligible for certain benefits as required by applicable law.

#### 2.7.1 Medical Insurance

BRHI offers two medical plan options through a Preferred Provider Organization (PPO):

- **PPO Plan:** Features a broad provider network, copays for office visits, and traditional deductible and coinsurance structure. Employees may seek care from any licensed provider; however, out-of-pocket costs are lower when using in-network providers.
- **High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP):** Features a lower premium contribution and a higher annual deductible. The HDHP is paired with a Health Savings Account (HSA) to which the Company contributes seed funding. Eligible employees may also make pre-tax contributions to their HSA up to the annual IRS limit.

Both plans include coverage for preventive care, hospitalization, prescription drugs, mental health and substance abuse treatment, and maternity care. Summary Plan Descriptions (SPDs) are available on the BRHI benefits portal.

#### 2.7.2 Dental Insurance

BRHI offers a comprehensive dental plan covering preventive services (cleanings, exams, X-rays) at 100%, basic services (fillings, extractions) at 80%, and major services (crowns, bridges, root canals) at 50% of reasonable and customary charges, subject to an annual maximum benefit per covered individual. Orthodontic coverage is available for dependents under age 19 at 50% of reasonable and customary charges, subject to a lifetime maximum.

#### 2.7.3 Vision Insurance

BRHI offers a vision plan that covers annual comprehensive eye examinations, eyeglass frames and lenses or contact lenses, and discounts on corrective laser eye surgery. The plan utilizes a provider network, and benefits are paid at higher levels for in-network providers.

#### 2.7.4 Life Insurance

BRHI provides company-paid basic life insurance and accidental death and dismemberment (AD&D) coverage equal to one (1) times the employee's annual base salary, rounded to the nearest thousand dollars, subject to a minimum benefit of $25,000 and a maximum benefit of $500,000. Employees may purchase supplemental life insurance for themselves in increments of $10,000 up to a maximum of five (5) times their annual base salary, not to exceed $500,000, subject to evidence of insurability for amounts exceeding $100,000. Dependent life insurance is also available for spouses and eligible children.

#### 2.7.5 Short-Term Disability (STD)

BRHI provides company-paid short-term disability coverage for eligible full-time employees. The STD plan provides a benefit of sixty percent (60%) of the employee's base weekly earnings, up to a maximum weekly benefit of $1,500. Benefits begin on the fifteenth (15th) consecutive calendar day of disability and may continue for up to twenty-six (26) weeks. The elimination period may be waived if the employee is hospitalized.

#### 2.7.6 Long-Term Disability (LTD)

BRHI provides company-paid long-term disability coverage for eligible full-time employees. The LTD plan provides a benefit of sixty percent (60%) of the employee's base monthly earnings, up to a maximum monthly benefit of $7,500. Benefits begin after the employee has been continuously disabled for twenty-six (26) weeks (the elimination period) and may continue until the employee reaches age 65 or is no longer disabled, whichever occurs first.

#### 2.7.7 401(k) Retirement Plan

BRHI offers a 401(k) retirement savings plan administered by a qualified plan administrator. Eligible employees may contribute up to the annual IRS elective deferral limit through pre-tax (traditional) and/or Roth (after-tax) contributions. BRHI matches employee contributions dollar-for-dollar up to six percent (6%) of the employee's eligible compensation. Company matching contributions vest at a rate of twenty percent (20%) per year of continuous service, achieving one hundred percent (100%) vesting after five (5) years. Employees become eligible to participate in the 401(k) plan on the first day of the month following sixty (60) calendar days of employment.

#### 2.7.8 Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP)

Eligible employees may participate in the BRHI Employee Stock Purchase Plan. Participants may elect to contribute up to ten percent (10%) of their after-tax compensation toward the purchase of BRHI common stock at a discount of fifteen percent (15%) off the fair market value on the purchase date. Purchase periods run semi-annually, with purchase dates occurring on June 30 and December 31 of each year. Employees must be actively employed on the purchase date to receive the stock.

#### 2.7.9 Tuition Reimbursement

BRHI provides tuition reimbursement of up to $5,000 per calendar year for eligible full-time employees who have completed at least one (1) year of continuous service. Reimbursable programs include undergraduate and graduate degree programs at accredited institutions, as well as professional certification programs that are directly related to the employee's current role or a reasonable career path within the Company. Courses must be approved by the employee's supervisor and Human Resources prior to enrollment. Reimbursement is provided at one hundred percent (100%) for a grade of "B" or better, seventy-five percent (75%) for a grade of "C," and zero percent for grades below "C" or for courses that are dropped, withdrawn, or incomplete.

#### 2.7.10 Employee Discount

All regular full-time and part-time employees receive a ten percent (10%) discount on most merchandise purchased at any BRHI store location or through the BRHI e-commerce website. The discount is applied to the regular retail price and may not be combined with certain other promotional offers. The employee discount is for personal use only and may not be used for resale. Certain exclusions apply, including but not limited to gift cards, services, and select vendor-restricted products. Employees must present a valid BRHI employee badge at the time of purchase. The discount becomes available on the employee's first day of active employment.

#### 2.7.11 Vacation Accrual

Paid vacation time is accrued based on the employee's length of continuous service with the Company. Vacation accrual rates are as follows:

| Tenure | Annual Accrual | Accrual per Bi-Weekly Pay Period |
|---|---|---|
| 0 – 5 years | 80 hours | 3.08 hours |
| 5 – 10 years | 120 hours | 4.62 hours |
| 10+ years | 160 hours | 6.15 hours |

Vacation accrual begins on the first day of the pay period following successful completion of the ninety (90) day probationary period. Vacation time accrues each pay period and is available for use as it accrues, subject to supervisory approval. Employees may carry over a maximum of forty (40) hours of unused vacation time from one calendar year to the next. Any unused vacation time in excess of forty (40) hours at the end of the calendar year (December 31) is forfeited and is not compensated.

Upon separation of employment, employees with at least ninety (90) days of service will be paid for accrued and unused vacation time at their current hourly rate or salary equivalent. Vacation time is not earned during unpaid leaves of absence exceeding fourteen (14) consecutive calendar days.

#### 2.7.12 Sick Time

Eligible employees accrue paid sick time at a rate of forty-eight (48) hours per calendar year (1.85 hours per bi-weekly pay period). Sick time accrual begins on the employee's first day of employment. Sick time may be used for the employee's own illness, injury, or medical appointment, or for the care of a family member as defined by applicable law. Sick time may be used in increments of no less than one (1) hour.

Employees may carry over a maximum of forty-eight (48) hours of unused sick time from one calendar year to the next, not to exceed a total bank of ninety-six (96) hours at any time. Sick time is not compensated upon separation of employment.

#### 2.7.13 Paid Holidays

BRHI observes the following eight (8) paid holidays per calendar year:

| Holiday | Date |
|---|---|
| New Year's Day | January 1 |
| Memorial Day | Last Monday in May |
| Independence Day | July 4 |
| Labor Day | First Monday in September |
| Thanksgiving Day | Fourth Thursday in November |
| Christmas Eve | December 24 |
| Christmas Day | December 25 |
| New Year's Eve | December 31 |

To be eligible for holiday pay, an employee must have completed their probationary period, must have worked their last scheduled shift before and their first scheduled shift after the holiday, and must not be on an unpaid leave of absence on the holiday. Holiday pay is calculated at the employee's regular base rate of pay for the hours they would have been scheduled to work on that day.

Non-exempt employees who are required to work on a Company-observed holiday will receive their regular hourly rate for all hours worked, plus holiday pay at their regular hourly rate for the hours they would have been scheduled. Exempt employees who are required to work on a holiday will receive their regular salary plus a floating holiday day to be used within ninety (90) calendar days.

When a holiday falls on a Saturday, it will generally be observed on the preceding Friday. When a holiday falls on a Sunday, it will generally be observed on the following Monday.

## 3. Workplace Conduct

### 3.1 Attendance and Punctuality

Regular attendance and punctuality are essential to the success of the Company and the individual employee. Employees are expected to report to work for all scheduled shifts on time and to remain at work for the entire shift unless an early departure has been authorized by a supervisor.

#### 3.1.1 Call-Out Procedure

Employees who are unable to report to work as scheduled must notify their direct supervisor or the designated store manager on duty no later than sixty (60) minutes before the start of their scheduled shift. The notification must include the reason for the absence and the expected date of return. Leaving a voicemail, sending a text message, or notifying a coworker does not satisfy this requirement unless the employee's supervisor has specifically authorized these methods in advance.

#### 3.1.2 No-Call / No-Show

An employee who fails to report to work for three (3) consecutive scheduled shifts without notifying their supervisor in accordance with the call-out procedure will be considered to have voluntarily resigned their employment as of the beginning of the third missed shift. The voluntary resignation will be recorded in the employee's personnel file. The Company will make a reasonable attempt to contact the employee before processing the voluntary resignation.

#### 3.1.3 Attendance Tracking

All non-exempt employees must accurately record their work time using the Company's designated timekeeping system. Clocking in or out for another employee, requesting that another employee clock in or out for you, or falsifying time records in any manner is strictly prohibited and constitutes grounds for immediate termination. Exempt employees are not required to clock in and out but must record absences and approved time off through the Company's leave management system.

#### 3.1.4 Tardiness

Tardiness is defined as reporting to work later than the scheduled start time. Employees who arrive more than seven (7) minutes after their scheduled start time will be considered tardy. A pattern of habitual tardiness, defined as three (3) or more unexcused tardy arrivals within a rolling thirty (30) calendar day period, may result in corrective action up to and including termination.

### 3.2 Dress Code — BuildRight Blue Apron

All store-level employees are required to wear the official BuildRight Blue apron while on the sales floor and during any customer-facing interaction. The BuildRight Blue apron is provided by the Company at no cost to the employee. Replacement aprons are available through the store manager.

The following dress code standards apply to all employees:

- **Shirts:** Solid-color polo shirts or BRHI-branded shirts in approved colors (navy blue, gray, or white). Shirts must be clean, free of offensive language or imagery, and in good repair. Tank tops, crop tops, and sheer or mesh fabrics are not permitted.
- **Pants:** Clean khaki or navy blue pants, shorts (mid-thigh or longer), or skirts. Jeans, sweatpants, athletic shorts, ripped or torn clothing, and excessively baggy clothing are not permitted.
- **Footwear:** Closed-toe and closed-heel shoes with non-slip soles are required at all times in store and warehouse environments. Sandals, flip-flops, open-toed shoes, and high heels exceeding two inches are not permitted in store or warehouse areas.
- **Headwear:** BRHI-branded hats or beanies are permitted. Non-BRHI headwear is not permitted on the sales floor. Headwear worn for sincerely held religious or medical reasons is fully accommodated.
- **Name Badges:** All employees must wear their BRHI-issued name badge visibly on their apron or shirt at all times while on Company premises.
- **Personal Hygiene and Grooming:** Employees are expected to maintain a clean, neat, and professional appearance consistent with a retail home improvement environment.
- **Jewelry and Accessories:** Jewelry and accessories that pose a safety hazard in a retail or warehouse environment are not permitted. Facial piercings must be secured or removed during work hours if they present a safety risk.

Employees who report to work in attire that does not comply with this dress code may be asked to change before beginning their shift. Repeated dress code violations may result in corrective action.

Corporate office employees are expected to maintain business casual attire. The BuildRight Blue apron is not required for corporate office employees unless they are working in a store location.

### 3.3 Workplace Violence Prevention

BRHI maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward workplace violence, threats of violence, and intimidation. Workplace violence includes, but is not limited to, any act or threat of physical aggression, harm, or intimidation that occurs on Company premises or during any Company-related activity, regardless of the relationship between the parties involved.

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:

- Physical assaults or attempted assaults against any person on Company premises or during Company business
- Verbal or written threats of harm, intimidation, or coercion directed at employees, customers, vendors, or visitors
- Brandishing, possessing, or using any weapon, firearm, or dangerous instrument on Company property or in Company vehicles, including in personal vehicles parked on Company property, except as permitted by applicable law
- Stalking, harassment, or bullying behavior that causes a reasonable person to fear for their safety or the safety of others
- Intentional destruction or sabotage of Company property or another person's property

Any employee who witnesses, experiences, or has knowledge of workplace violence or a threat of violence must immediately report the incident to their supervisor, Human Resources, or the BRHI confidential safety and security hotline. Reports may also be made anonymously through the hotline. All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially to the extent possible. Employees who engage in workplace violence or who make credible threats of violence are subject to immediate termination, removal from Company premises, and potential criminal prosecution.

BRHI will obtain and enforce restraining or protective orders as necessary and will cooperate with law enforcement authorities in the investigation and prosecution of any workplace violence incident. The Company reserves the right to inspect personal property, including vehicles, bags, and lockers, on Company premises when there is reasonable suspicion of a policy violation.

### 3.4 Drug-Free Workplace

BRHI is committed to maintaining a safe, healthy, and productive work environment free from the effects of drugs and alcohol. The manufacture, distribution, dispensation, possession, sale, purchase, or use of illegal drugs, controlled substances (without a valid prescription), or alcohol on Company premises, in Company vehicles, or while conducting Company business off-site is strictly prohibited.

Employees are prohibited from reporting to work or performing work while under the influence of alcohol, illegal drugs, or any substance that impairs their ability to perform their job duties safely and effectively. The lawful use of prescribed or over-the-counter medications is permitted; however, employees must consult with their healthcare provider regarding potential side effects that may impair job performance and must inform their supervisor or Human Resources if such medications may affect their ability to perform their duties safely.

Violations of this policy, including a confirmed positive drug or alcohol test, are subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination. The Company reserves the right to conduct drug and alcohol testing in the following circumstances: pre-employment, reasonable suspicion, post-accident, return-to-duty, and follow-up testing as part of a substance abuse rehabilitation program.

#### 3.4.1 Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

BRHI provides a confidential Employee Assistance Program (EAP) at no cost to all employees and their eligible household members. The EAP offers short-term counseling (up to six (6) sessions per issue per year), crisis intervention, and referral services for a wide range of personal and work-related concerns, including substance abuse, mental health, relationship issues, financial difficulties, legal matters, and stress management. EAP services are available twenty-four (24) hours a day, seven (7) days a week via telephone and online. All EAP communications and records are strictly confidential and are not shared with BRHI without the employee's written consent, except as required by law or when there is an imminent risk of harm.

Employees who voluntarily seek assistance through the EAP for substance abuse issues before being subject to a drug or alcohol test or disciplinary action will not have their employment terminated solely for seeking assistance. However, voluntary self-referral does not shield an employee from disciplinary action for policy violations that have already occurred or for performance issues.

### 3.5 Anti-Harassment Policy

BRHI is committed to maintaining a workplace free from harassment, including sexual harassment, based on any protected characteristic. This policy applies to all employees, supervisors, managers, executives, contractors, vendors, and customers, and covers conduct that occurs in the workplace, at Company-sponsored events, during business travel, and through electronic communications including email, text messaging, and social media.

#### 3.5.1 Definition of Harassment

Harassment is unwelcome conduct based on a person's race, color, religion, sex (including sexual orientation and gender identity), national origin, age, disability, genetic information, marital status, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic that: (a) is made a condition of employment; (b) is used as the basis for employment decisions; or (c) is sufficiently severe or pervasive to create an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal, visual, or physical conduct of a sexual nature. This encompasses both quid pro quo harassment (where submission to or rejection of sexual conduct is used as a basis for employment decisions) and hostile work environment harassment (where the conduct unreasonably interferes with an individual's work performance or creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment).

Examples of prohibited conduct include, but are not limited to, offensive jokes, slurs, epithets, name-calling, physical assaults or threats, intimidation, ridicule, insults, offensive objects or pictures, and interference with work performance based on a protected characteristic.

#### 3.5.2 Reporting and Investigation

Any employee who believes they have experienced or witnessed harassment should promptly report the conduct through any of the following channels:

1. Their direct supervisor or any member of management
2. The Human Resources Department
3. The BRHI confidential ethics and compliance hotline

BRHI is committed to responding to all reports of harassment within five (5) business days of receipt. The response will include acknowledgment of the complaint, an initial assessment of the allegations, and the initiation of an investigation where warranted. All investigations will be completed within thirty (30) calendar days of the initial report, unless circumstances require additional time, in which case the reporting party will be notified of the extended timeline.

Investigations will be conducted by trained Human Resources personnel or, in appropriate circumstances, by an independent third-party investigator. All parties involved will be treated with dignity and respect. The confidentiality of the complaint, the identity of the complainant, and the investigation will be maintained to the greatest extent possible, consistent with the need to conduct a thorough investigation and comply with legal obligations.

Upon completion of the investigation, the Company will take prompt and appropriate corrective action, up to and including termination, to remedy any substantiated harassment and to prevent its recurrence. The complainant and the respondent will both be notified of the outcome of the investigation to the extent permitted by law and policy.

Retaliation against any individual who, in good faith, reports harassment, participates in an investigation, or opposes practices they reasonably believe to be discriminatory or harassing is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 3.6 Bullying Prevention

BRHI is committed to providing a professional, respectful, and collegial work environment. Workplace bullying is defined as repeated, unreasonable actions directed at an employee (or a group of employees) that are intended to intimidate, degrade, humiliate, or undermine the employee's performance, or that a reasonable person would find intimidating, degrading, humiliating, or undermining.

Prohibited bullying behaviors include, but are not limited to:

- Repeated verbal abuse, including shouting, swearing, name-calling, or insulting language
- Deliberate exclusion, isolation, or marginalization of an employee from work activities
- Unjustified removal of responsibilities or assignment of menial tasks designed to humiliate
- Persistent, unwarranted criticism that goes beyond constructive feedback
- Spreading malicious rumors, gossip, or innuendo about an employee
- Deliberately sabotaging an employee's work or preventing them from performing their duties
- Threatening, intimidating, or coercing an employee

Legitimate, constructive, and respectful feedback, counseling, or disciplinary action taken by a supervisor or manager in the course of their management responsibilities does not constitute bullying. Disagreements, differences of opinion, and interpersonal conflicts that are resolved respectfully are a normal part of the workplace.

Employees who believe they have been subjected to workplace bullying should report the behavior using the same reporting channels identified in Section 3.5.2. Reports of bullying will be investigated using the same procedures and timelines described for harassment complaints. Substantiated bullying will result in corrective action appropriate to the severity of the conduct, up to and including termination.

## 4. Leave Policies

### 4.1 Vacation Leave

Paid vacation time is accrued in accordance with the schedule described in Section 2.7.11. Vacation requests must be submitted through the Company's leave management system at least fourteen (14) calendar days in advance for planned absences of one (1) to five (5) business days, and at least thirty (30) calendar days in advance for planned absences exceeding five (5) business days. Vacation requests are subject to supervisory approval based on operational needs and staffing requirements. The Company will make every reasonable effort to accommodate vacation requests, but reserves the right to deny or reschedule vacation time based on business necessity.

No more than two (2) consecutive weeks of vacation may be taken without the written approval of the next-level manager above the employee's direct supervisor. Vacation time must be used in increments of no less than four (4) hours for non-exempt employees. Exempt employees must take vacation in full-day increments to maintain FLSA compliance.

### 4.2 Sick Leave

Paid sick time is accrued in accordance with the schedule described in Section 2.7.12. Sick time may be used for the employee's own illness, injury, or preventive or diagnostic medical care, or for the care of a family member (spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, parent-in-law, grandparent, grandchild, sibling, or any individual whose close association with the employee is the equivalent of a family relationship) who is ill, injured, or in need of medical care.

Employees are not required to provide medical documentation for absences of three (3) consecutive scheduled work days or fewer. For absences exceeding three (3) consecutive scheduled work days, the employee must provide a note from a licensed healthcare provider confirming the need for the absence and the expected date of return. The Company reserves the right to require medical documentation for shorter absences where there is a pattern of abuse or suspected misuse of sick time.

### 4.3 Paid Holidays

BRHI observes the eight (8) paid holidays listed in Section 2.7.13. Holiday policies, including eligibility requirements and premium pay provisions, are detailed in that section.

### 4.4 Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)

Eligible employees are entitled to up to twelve (12) workweeks of unpaid, job-protected leave during a rolling twelve (12) month period under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA). Eligible employees may also take up to twenty-six (26) workweeks of leave during a single twelve (12) month period to care for a covered servicemember with a serious injury or illness.

#### 4.4.1 Eligibility

To be eligible for FMLA leave, an employee must: (a) have been employed by BRHI for at least twelve (12) months (which need not be consecutive); (b) have worked at least 1,250 hours during the twelve (12) month period immediately preceding the commencement of leave; and (c) work at a BRHI location where fifty (50) or more employees are employed within a seventy-five (75) mile radius.

#### 4.4.2 Qualifying Reasons

FMLA leave may be taken for the following reasons:

- The birth of a child and to bond with the newborn child within one (1) year of birth
- The placement of a child with the employee for adoption or foster care and to bond with the newly placed child within one (1) year of placement
- A serious health condition that makes the employee unable to perform the essential functions of their job
- To care for the employee's spouse, son, daughter, or parent who has a serious health condition
- A qualifying exigency arising out of the fact that the employee's spouse, son, daughter, or parent is a covered military member on covered active duty or call to covered active duty status
- To care for a covered servicemember with a serious injury or illness incurred in the line of duty

#### 4.4.3 FMLA Procedures

Employees must provide thirty (30) days advance notice when the need for FMLA leave is foreseeable (such as for the birth or placement of a child or planned medical treatment). When the need for leave is unforeseeable, the employee must provide notice as soon as practicable, generally within one (1) to two (2) business days of learning of the need for leave.

BRHI will respond to an FMLA leave request within five (5) business days of receiving sufficient information to determine eligibility. Employees must provide medical certification supporting the need for leave within fifteen (15) calendar days of the request. Failure to provide required certification may result in denial of the leave.

During FMLA leave, the Company will maintain the employee's group health insurance coverage under the same terms and conditions as if the employee were actively working. Employees are required to continue paying their portion of the premium during the leave. Upon return from FMLA leave, employees are generally entitled to be restored to the same position or an equivalent position with equivalent pay, benefits, and other terms and conditions of employment.

FMLA leave runs concurrently with any applicable paid leave (sick time, vacation, or short-term disability) to the extent permitted by law. Employees should contact Human Resources to initiate the FMLA application process.

### 4.5 Bereavement Leave

BRHI provides paid bereavement leave to eligible full-time employees who have completed their probationary period. Bereavement leave is granted as follows:

- **Immediate Family Member (3 days):** Up to three (3) scheduled work days of paid bereavement leave upon the death of an immediate family member. Immediate family members include spouse, domestic partner, child (including stepchild and foster child), parent (including stepparent and parent-in-law), sibling (including stepsibling), grandparent, and grandchild.
- **Extended Family Member (1 day):** Up to one (1) scheduled work day of paid bereavement leave upon the death of an extended family member. Extended family members include aunt, uncle, niece, nephew, cousin, and any individual whose close association with the employee is the equivalent of a family relationship.

Bereavement leave must be taken within fourteen (14) calendar days of the date of death. Employees must notify their supervisor as soon as reasonably practicable and may be asked to provide documentation (such as an obituary notice or death certificate) upon return to work. Employees who require additional time beyond the paid bereavement leave may use available vacation or sick time, or may request an unpaid leave of absence.

### 4.6 Jury Duty

BRHI provides paid jury duty leave to eligible employees who are summoned to serve on a jury. During jury service, the Company will pay the difference between the employee's regular base pay and the compensation received from the court (excluding mileage and meal allowances) for a period of up to ten (10) consecutive work days per calendar year. Employees must provide a copy of the jury summons to their supervisor and Human Resources as soon as it is received. Upon completion of jury service, the employee must provide proof of service and the amount of compensation received from the court.

Employees are expected to report to work on days when they are excused from jury service early enough to complete a meaningful portion of their work shift. Jury duty leave does not count against the employee's vacation or sick time accruals.

### 4.7 Military Leave — USERRA

BRHI supports the military service obligations of its employees and complies fully with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA) and all applicable state laws. Employees who are members of the uniformed services (including the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, Coast Guard, Army National Guard, Air National Guard, and Public Health Service commissioned corps) are entitled to take unpaid leave for military training, active duty, or other qualifying service.

Employees must provide advance verbal or written notice of their military service obligation to their supervisor or Human Resources, unless military necessity makes notice impossible or unreasonable. The cumulative length of military leave generally may not exceed five (5) years, subject to USERRA exceptions.

During military leave, employees may continue their group health insurance coverage for up to twenty-four (24) months at their own expense, or they may elect to discontinue coverage and reinstate it upon return without any waiting period or pre-existing condition exclusion. Upon timely return from military service, eligible employees are entitled to reemployment in the position they would have attained had they not left for military service, or in a position of comparable seniority, status, and pay, as provided by USERRA.

### 4.8 Parental Leave

In addition to FMLA leave, BRHI provides the following paid parental leave benefits to eligible full-time employees who have completed at least twelve (12) months of continuous service and who have worked at least 1,250 hours in the preceding twelve (12) month period:

- **Birth Parents (6 weeks):** Up to six (6) weeks of paid parental leave at one hundred percent (100%) of the employee's base salary for the birth parent following the birth of a child. This leave must be taken within sixteen (16) weeks of the date of birth and may run concurrently with any applicable short-term disability benefits and FMLA leave.
- **Non-Birth Parents (2 weeks):** Up to two (2) weeks of paid parental leave at one hundred percent (100%) of the employee's base salary for non-birth parents (including spouses, domestic partners, adoptive parents, and intended parents in a surrogacy arrangement) following the birth or placement of a child. This leave must be taken within sixteen (16) weeks of the date of birth or placement and may run concurrently with FMLA leave.

Paid parental leave is available for each qualifying birth or placement event. Employees must provide at least thirty (30) days advance notice of the anticipated start date of parental leave when the need is foreseeable. During paid parental leave, the employee's benefits coverage continues under the same terms and conditions as active employment.

## 6. Separation Policies

### 6.1 Voluntary Separation (Resignation)

Employees who choose to resign from BRHI are requested to provide at least two (2) weeks (fourteen (14) calendar days) advance written notice to their direct supervisor and Human Resources. The notice should include the intended last day of work and a brief reason for resignation. Providing the requested notice allows the Company to plan for a smooth transition and ensures the employee's final paycheck and benefits are processed in a timely manner.

Employees who provide the requested notice period and who are in good standing may be eligible for rehire after a six (6) month waiting period, subject to the rehire provisions in Section 6.5. Employees who resign without providing the requested notice period or who resign while on active corrective action may be classified as not eligible for rehire at the Company's discretion.

During the notice period, employees are expected to continue performing their job duties at a satisfactory level, to cooperate in the transition of their responsibilities, and to return all Company property on or before their last day of work.

### 6.2 Involuntary Separation (Termination)

#### 6.2.1 Progressive Disciplinary Process

BRHI utilizes a progressive disciplinary process to address performance issues and policy violations that do not warrant immediate termination. The progressive disciplinary process consists of the following steps:

1. **Verbal Warning (Documented):** The supervisor discusses the performance issue or policy violation with the employee in a private meeting, clearly communicates the expected standard of conduct or performance, and documents the conversation in a written summary that is placed in the employee's personnel file. The employee is given a specific timeframe (typically thirty (30) calendar days) to demonstrate improvement.
2. **Written Warning:** If the issue persists or recurs, the supervisor issues a formal written warning that outlines the specific nature of the deficiency, references any prior verbal warnings, restates the expected standard, and warns that further violations may result in more severe disciplinary action, up to and including termination. The written warning must be reviewed and approved by Human Resources before delivery. The employee is given a specific timeframe (typically sixty (60) calendar days) to demonstrate sustained improvement.
3. **Final Written Warning / Suspension:** If the issue continues, the employee receives a final written warning and may be placed on an unpaid suspension of one (1) to five (5) business days, at the discretion of the department manager in consultation with Human Resources. The final written warning explicitly states that any further violation of the same or similar nature will result in termination of employment.
4. **Termination:** If the employee fails to meet the expectations outlined in the final written warning, or commits a new violation of equal or greater severity during the warning period, the employee's employment will be terminated.

The Company reserves the right to bypass any or all steps of the progressive disciplinary process and proceed directly to any level of discipline, including immediate termination, when the circumstances warrant such action. The progressive disciplinary process does not alter the at-will nature of employment.

#### 6.2.2 Immediate Termination Offenses

The following behaviors or actions constitute grounds for immediate termination of employment, without prior progressive discipline:

- Theft, misappropriation, or unauthorized removal of Company property, merchandise, funds, or the property of another employee, customer, or vendor
- Fraud, embezzlement, falsification of Company records, including but not limited to time records, expense reports, financial records, or employment applications
- Workplace violence, threats of violence, or intimidation as described in Section 3.3
- Possession, use, sale, or distribution of illegal drugs, controlled substances, or alcohol on Company premises or while on Company business
- Reporting to work under the influence of drugs or alcohol that impairs the employee's ability to perform their job duties safely
- Insubordination, including refusal to perform a lawful and reasonable direct order from a supervisor or manager
- Gross negligence or willful misconduct that results in or could result in serious harm to persons or property
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential, proprietary, or trade secret information
- Sexual harassment or other unlawful harassment that is severe in nature
- Discrimination in violation of Company policy or applicable law
- Conviction of a felony or any crime involving dishonesty, moral turpitude, or violence that is directly related to the employee's job duties or that materially affects the Company's legitimate business interests
- Falsification of drug or alcohol test results or refusal to submit to a required drug or alcohol test
- Unauthorized possession of a weapon or firearm on Company premises
- Engaging in conduct that creates a hostile work environment for other employees, customers, or vendors
- Abandonment of employment (three consecutive no-call/no-show days as described in Section 3.1.2)
- Sleeping on duty during scheduled work hours
- Engaging in a competing business or activity that constitutes a conflict of interest, in violation of Company policy
- Willful violation of safety rules that endangers the employee or others

### 6.3 Reduction in Force (RIF)

In the event that business conditions require a reduction in workforce, BRHI will manage the reduction in a manner that is fair, transparent, and consistent with applicable legal requirements. Selection of positions for elimination will be based on legitimate business considerations, including but not limited to organizational structure, operational needs, skill requirements, performance history, and seniority.

#### 6.3.1 Severance

Employees whose positions are eliminated through a reduction in force and who sign a general release of claims in a form acceptable to the Company will be eligible for severance pay calculated as follows:

- Two (2) weeks of base pay for each full year of continuous service with BRHI
- Maximum severance benefit of twenty-six (26) weeks of base pay
- Severance is paid in regular payroll installments over the severance period

Severance payments are subject to applicable tax withholdings and are contingent upon the employee's execution and non-revocation of the release agreement within the timeframes specified therein. Employees who decline to sign the release are not eligible for severance pay.

#### 6.3.2 WARN Act Compliance

For reductions in force that meet the thresholds of the Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN) Act (generally, a plant closing or mass layoff affecting fifty (50) or more employees at a single site of employment), BRHI will provide affected employees with at least sixty (60) calendar days advance written notice, or pay in lieu of notice, as required by law. State-specific mini-WARN laws will be followed where they provide greater protections.

#### 6.3.3 Outplacement Assistance

BRHI will provide outplacement assistance to employees affected by a reduction in force, including resume writing support, interview preparation, job search resources, and access to career counseling services for a period of ninety (90) days following the separation date.

### 6.4 Exit Process

Upon separation of employment for any reason, the employee must complete the following:

- Return all Company property, including but not limited to keys, access badges, uniforms, aprons, electronic devices, tools, Company credit cards, documents, and any other Company-owned materials, on or before the last day of employment
- Ensure all outstanding expense reports are submitted
- Participate in an exit interview, if requested by Human Resources
- Provide a forwarding address for the delivery of final pay and tax documents

The employee's final paycheck will be processed and delivered in accordance with applicable state law. Accrued and unused vacation time will be paid out in accordance with Section 2.7.11. Benefits continuation rights under the Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act (COBRA) will be communicated in a separate notice within the timeframes required by law.

### 6.5 Rehire

Former employees who separated from BRHI in good standing (voluntary resignation with proper notice, or layoff through a reduction in force) are eligible for rehire after a minimum waiting period of six (6) months from their separation date. Rehired employees will be treated as new hires for purposes of the probationary period, benefits eligibility, and vacation accrual tenure credit, except as follows:

- Employees rehired within twelve (12) months of separation will receive credit for their prior tenure for purposes of the vacation accrual schedule only.
- Employees rehired within twelve (12) months of separation who were vested in the 401(k) plan will retain their vested balance. The vesting schedule for future employer contributions will resume based on the employee's total continuous service, including the prior period of employment.

Former employees who were terminated for cause, who resigned without proper notice, or who were classified as not eligible for rehire at the time of separation are not eligible for rehire. All rehire decisions are subject to the Company's hiring criteria, background check, and drug screening requirements.

## 7. Grievance and Dispute Resolution

### 7.1 Open Door Policy

BRHI maintains an Open Door Policy that encourages open, direct, and honest communication at all levels of the organization. Employees are encouraged to discuss any work-related questions, concerns, suggestions, or complaints with their direct supervisor, any member of management, or Human Resources at any time, without fear of retaliation. No employee will be penalized for raising a concern in good faith.

### 7.2 Four-Step Grievance Procedure

When an employee has a grievance that cannot be resolved informally through the Open Door Policy, the following formal four-step grievance procedure is available. A grievance is defined as a formal complaint regarding the interpretation, application, or alleged violation of a Company policy, practice, or procedure that directly and adversely affects the employee.

#### Step 1: Direct Supervisor

The employee must submit a written grievance to their direct supervisor within fifteen (15) business days of the event giving rise to the grievance or the date the employee became aware of the event. The supervisor will meet with the employee within five (5) business days of receipt and will provide a written response within ten (10) business days of the meeting.

#### Step 2: Human Resources

If the employee is not satisfied with the supervisor's response, the employee may submit a written appeal to the Human Resources Department within ten (10) business days of receiving the supervisor's response. A Human Resources representative will investigate the grievance, which may include interviews with relevant parties and a review of relevant documents, and will provide a written response within fifteen (15) business days of receipt of the appeal.

#### Step 3: Vice President / Senior Leadership

If the grievance remains unresolved, the employee may submit a written appeal to the Vice President of their department (or the next senior-most leader outside the employee's direct chain of command) within ten (10) business days of receiving the Human Resources response. The Vice President or designated senior leader will review the grievance, the prior responses, and any additional information and will issue a final written Company decision within fifteen (15) business days of receipt of the appeal.

#### Step 4: Binding Arbitration

If the employee is not satisfied with the Company's final decision, the employee may request binding arbitration as described in Section 7.3. The request for arbitration must be submitted in writing to Human Resources within twenty (20) business days of the Company's final decision under Step 3.

### 7.3 Arbitration Agreement

As a condition of employment, all employees agree to resolve any dispute, claim, or controversy arising out of or relating to the employment relationship or the termination of that relationship through binding arbitration, rather than through litigation in a court of law. This arbitration agreement covers all claims, including but not limited to claims for wrongful termination, discrimination, harassment, retaliation, wage and hour violations, breach of contract, and any other statutory or common law claims related to employment.

Arbitration will be administered by the American Arbitration Association (AAA) under its National Rules for the Resolution of Employment Disputes then in effect. The arbitration will be conducted before a single arbitrator selected in accordance with the AAA rules. The arbitration hearing will take place in the city nearest to the employee's primary work location. The Company will bear the costs of the arbitrator's fees and any administrative fees charged by AAA that exceed the amount of fees the employee would have incurred to file a similar claim in a court of competent jurisdiction. Each party will bear its own attorneys' fees and costs, unless the arbitrator awards fees and costs as permitted by applicable law.

The arbitrator will have the authority to award any remedy or relief that a court of competent jurisdiction could order, including injunctive relief and damages. The arbitrator's decision will be final and binding on both parties and may be entered as a judgment in any court of competent jurisdiction. This arbitration agreement does not apply to claims for workers' compensation benefits, unemployment insurance benefits, or claims that are expressly excluded from arbitration by law.

Nothing in this arbitration agreement prevents an employee from filing an administrative charge with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC), the National Labor Relations Board (NLRB), or any other federal, state, or local government agency. However, the employee agrees that any lawsuit filed in court by or against the employee will be subject to a motion to compel arbitration.

### 7.4 Confidentiality of Grievance Proceedings

All grievance and dispute resolution proceedings are confidential. Employees, supervisors, managers, and Human Resources personnel involved in a grievance proceeding are expected to maintain the confidentiality of all communications, documents, and outcomes to the greatest extent possible, consistent with the need to conduct a fair and thorough process. Violation of this confidentiality expectation may result in disciplinary action.

## 8. Workplace Safety

### 8.1 Commitment to Safety

BRHI is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment for all employees, customers, vendors, and visitors. Every employee shares in the responsibility for maintaining a safe workplace by following safety rules, using personal protective equipment (PPE) when required, reporting hazards, and participating in safety training programs.

### 8.2 Right to Refuse Unsafe Work

No employee will be required or expected to perform any task or work in any condition that the employee reasonably believes presents an imminent danger of death or serious physical harm. An employee who reasonably believes that a workplace condition or assigned task presents such a danger must immediately notify their supervisor and remove themselves from the hazardous situation. The employee will not be subject to retaliation, disciplinary action, or loss of pay for good faith refusal to perform work under genuinely unsafe conditions.

Upon notification, the supervisor will assess the situation, consult with the store safety coordinator or the Corporate Safety Department as necessary, and take appropriate corrective action before requiring the employee to return to the task. If the supervisor and employee disagree about whether a condition is unsafe, the matter will be escalated to the Corporate Safety Department for resolution before work resumes.

### 8.3 Incident Reporting

All workplace incidents, injuries, illnesses, near-misses, and property damage events must be reported immediately to the employee's supervisor and documented on the Company's Incident Report Form. For injuries and illnesses, the report must be submitted within twenty-four (24) hours of the incident, regardless of severity. Supervisors must forward all incident reports to the store safety coordinator and Human Resources within twenty-four (24) hours of receipt.

Employees who sustain a work-related injury or illness that requires medical treatment beyond basic first aid must be evaluated by a Company-approved occupational health provider, unless the nature of the injury requires emergency medical treatment. The Company will provide transportation to the medical provider when necessary. Employees must obtain a return-to-work clearance from the treating physician before resuming their regular duties.

### 8.4 Return-to-Work Program

BRHI maintains a return-to-work program designed to facilitate the safe and timely return of employees who have been absent due to a work-related injury, illness, or other medical condition. When medically appropriate, the Company will offer transitional duty or modified work assignments that accommodate the employee's medical restrictions while allowing the employee to remain productive and engaged.

The return-to-work process includes the following steps:

1. The employee must provide a release to return to work from their treating physician, specifying any restrictions or limitations.
2. Human Resources and the employee's supervisor will engage in an interactive discussion with the employee to identify available modified duty options that are consistent with the medical restrictions.
3. A written return-to-work plan will be developed that outlines the modified duties, the expected duration of the transitional assignment (not to exceed ninety (90) calendar days without re-evaluation), and the criteria for full-duty return.
4. The employee's progress will be monitored, and the plan will be adjusted as the employee's medical condition improves.
5. Upon full medical clearance, the employee will be returned to their regular position or an equivalent position.

### 8.5 Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Accommodation

BRHI is committed to complying with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and all applicable state and local disability accommodation laws. The Company will provide reasonable accommodations to qualified individuals with disabilities to enable them to perform the essential functions of their job, unless doing so would impose an undue hardship on the Company's operations.

#### 8.5.1 Interactive Process

When an employee requests an accommodation, or when the Company becomes aware that an employee may require an accommodation, BRHI will engage in a timely, good faith interactive process with the employee to identify effective and reasonable accommodations. The interactive process includes the following steps:

1. **Request:** The employee submits a written or verbal request for accommodation to their supervisor or Human Resources. The request should describe the nature of the limitation and the type of accommodation being sought.
2. **Information Gathering:** Human Resources will request relevant medical documentation from the employee's healthcare provider, limited to information necessary to understand the functional limitations and to identify appropriate accommodations. Medical information will be maintained in a confidential medical file separate from the employee's personnel file.
3. **Interactive Discussion:** Human Resources, the employee's supervisor, and the employee will meet to discuss the request, review the medical information, explore potential accommodations, and evaluate the effectiveness of each option. The employee's input and preference will be given primary consideration, but the Company retains the right to select the accommodation that is most effective and least disruptive to operations.
4. **Decision:** Human Resources will communicate the accommodation decision to the employee in writing within ten (10) business days of receiving all necessary information. If an accommodation cannot be provided, the written response will explain the reason.
5. **Implementation and Monitoring:** Once an accommodation is approved, it will be implemented promptly. The accommodation will be monitored and reassessed periodically to ensure its continued effectiveness.

Examples of reasonable accommodations may include, but are not limited to, modified work schedules, ergonomic equipment, job restructuring, reassignment to a vacant position, modified break schedules, assistive technology, and temporary modification of job duties.

The following standards govern the acceptable use of Company technology:

- Employees must not use Company technology to access, create, store, transmit, or distribute material that is illegal, offensive, discriminatory, harassing, sexually explicit, threatening, or otherwise inappropriate in the workplace.
- Employees must not install, download, or execute unauthorized software, applications, browser extensions, or executable files on Company systems without prior approval from the IT Department.
- Employees must not attempt to bypass, circumvent, or disable any Company security controls, including firewalls, antivirus software, access controls, or data loss prevention measures.
- Employees must not share their login credentials, passwords, or access tokens with any other person, including other employees, supervisors, or IT personnel. Employees are responsible for all activity that occurs under their credentials.
- Employees must not use Company technology to operate a personal business, engage in outside employment activities, or pursue personal commercial ventures during work hours.
- The Company reserves the right to monitor, access, review, and disclose all communications, files, and data transmitted, received, or stored on Company technology systems, without prior notice to the employee. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using Company technology resources.
- Employees who use personal devices for work purposes (including personal smartphones, tablets, and laptops) must comply with the Company's Bring Your Own Device (BYOD) policy, which requires the installation of Company-approved mobile device management (MDM) software and adherence to security standards.

### 9.2 Social Media Policy

BRHI recognizes that employees may participate in social media and online communities in their personal capacity. The following guidelines apply to employees' use of social media:

- Employees must not post confidential, proprietary, or trade secret information about BRHI, its customers, vendors, or fellow employees on any social media platform. This includes financial data, strategic plans, customer lists, pricing information, and internal communications.
- Employees must not post content that falsely represents themselves as speaking on behalf of BRHI or that could reasonably be interpreted as an official Company communication, unless authorized by the Marketing or Communications Department. When discussing Company-related topics, employees should clearly state that their views are their own and do not represent the views of BRHI.
- Employees must not post content that is discriminatory, harassing, threatening, defamatory, or that otherwise violates the Company's anti-harassment, anti-bullying, or workplace conduct policies, even if posted outside of work hours and from personal devices.
- Employees must not use BRHI's trademarks, logos, trade dress, copyrighted material, or other intellectual property on personal social media accounts without prior written approval from the Marketing Department.
- Employees are encouraged to report any social media activity by other individuals that falsely claims to represent BRHI or that may harm the Company's reputation to the Marketing or Communications Department.
- Nothing in this policy is intended to restrict employees from exercising their rights under Section 7 of the National Labor Relations Act (NLRA) to discuss wages, hours, working conditions, or other terms and conditions of employment, or to engage in other protected concerted activity.

### 9.3 Data Privacy

BRHI is committed to protecting the personal information of its employees, customers, and business partners. The Company collects, uses, stores, and discloses personal information only for legitimate business purposes and in compliance with applicable federal, state, and local data privacy and data breach notification laws.

- Employee personal information (including but not limited to Social Security numbers, financial information, health records, and performance records) is collected and maintained by Human Resources in secure systems. Access to employee personal information is restricted to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis.
- Customer personal information (including payment card data, contact information, and purchase history) is collected and maintained in accordance with the Company's data privacy notice and applicable laws. Employees who have access to customer data must handle it in accordance with the Company's data handling and privacy policies.
- Employees who have access to personal information must not access, view, copy, share, or disclose such information except as authorized by their job duties and applicable law. Unauthorized access to or disclosure of personal information is a serious policy violation that may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and may also result in civil or criminal liability.
- In the event of a data breach involving personal information, the Company will follow its Data Breach Response Plan, which includes containment and investigation, notification to affected individuals within the timeframes required by applicable law, and remediation measures to prevent future breaches.
- Employees must report any suspected or actual data breach, unauthorized access, or loss of personal information to the IT Security team and their supervisor immediately upon discovery.

## 10. Policy Enforcement and Acknowledgment

### 10.1 Annual Policy Acknowledgment

All employees are required to review and acknowledge receipt of this document on an annual basis. The annual acknowledgment process occurs during the first quarter of each calendar year (January 1 through March 31). During the acknowledgment process, employees will be provided with the current version of this document, will have the opportunity to ask questions, and will sign an acknowledgment form confirming that they have received, read, and understood the policies contained herein.

New employees will complete their initial acknowledgment as part of the onboarding process on their first day of employment. The acknowledgment will be documented and retained in the employee's personnel file.

### 10.2 Policy Violations

Violations of the policies contained in this document may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment. The severity of the disciplinary action will depend on the nature and seriousness of the violation, the employee's prior disciplinary history, and any mitigating or aggravating circumstances. The Company retains full discretion in determining the appropriate disciplinary response for any given situation.

As stated in Section 1.2, nothing in this document creates a contractual obligation to follow any particular disciplinary procedure, and the Company reserves the right to take whatever action it deems appropriate, including immediate termination, regardless of whether progressive discipline has been followed.

### 10.3 Policy Modifications

BRHI reserves the right to modify, amend, suspend, supplement, or discontinue any policy contained in this document at any time, with or without advance notice, at the sole discretion of the Company. When material changes are made, the Company will make reasonable efforts to communicate the changes to employees in a timely manner. The most current version of this document will be available on the BRHI employee self-service portal. In the event of a conflict between this document and any applicable federal, state, or local law, the applicable law will control.

### 10.4 Questions and Clarifications

Employees who have questions about any policy contained in this document, or who need clarification regarding the application of a policy to a specific situation, should contact their direct supervisor, their local Human Resources representative, or the Corporate Human Resources Department.
