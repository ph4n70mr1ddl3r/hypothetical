## 1. Document Control Overview

### 1.1 Purpose

This policy establishes the standards, processes, and responsibilities governing the creation, review, approval, publication, distribution, maintenance, and retirement of all controlled documents within BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. (BRHI). It ensures that associates have access to current, accurate, and authorized documentation necessary to perform their roles effectively and in compliance with regulatory requirements.

### 1.2 Scope

This policy applies to all controlled documents produced, maintained, or distributed by BRHI, including but not limited to policies, standard operating procedures, business processes, governance documents, organizational documents, and reference materials. It applies to all BRHI locations, departments, and associates regardless of employment status.

### 1.3 Authority

The Corporate Compliance department is the designated owner of this policy and the BRHI document control program. The Director of Corporate Compliance has final authority on all matters related to document control standards, exception requests, and policy interpretation.

## 3. Document Lifecycle

### 3.1 Creation

#### 3.1.1 Initiation

A new controlled document may be initiated by any BRHI associate with the sponsorship of a department director or above. The sponsor is responsible for ensuring the document addresses a legitimate business need and does not duplicate existing documentation.

#### 3.1.2 Template Usage

All new controlled documents must be created using the official BRHI Document Template, available in the Document Management System. The template includes:

- Document header with Document ID, version, effective date, classification, and owner
- Table of contents for documents exceeding five pages
- Body sections appropriate to the document type
- Document revision history table
- Footer with confidentiality notice and page numbering

#### 3.1.3 Drafting Guidelines

All documents shall adhere to the following drafting standards:

1. **Clarity** — Use clear, concise, and unambiguous language. Avoid jargon unless defined in the BRHI Glossary (BRHI-REF-001).
2. **Active Voice** — Prefer active voice over passive voice. Use imperative mood for procedural steps (e.g., "Scan the item," not "The item should be scanned").
3. **Structure** — Organize content using numbered headings and subheadings. Use tables for structured data. Use bullet lists for non-sequential items.
4. **Accuracy** — All data, statistics, and references must be verified and current as of the drafting date.
5. **Completeness** — Each document must be self-contained or must explicitly reference other controlled documents for required context.
6. **Formatting** — Use consistent font (Calibri 11pt for body text), heading styles (Heading 1 through Heading 4), and margins (1 inch on all sides) as specified in the BRHI Style Guide.
7. **Terminology** — Use terms as defined in BRHI-REF-001. Where a term is used that is not in the glossary, the document must define it on first use.

#### 3.1.4 Draft Status

Upon creation, the document is assigned a status of "Draft" in the Document Management System. Draft documents are not enforceable and shall not be distributed as official BRHI documents.

### 3.2 Review

#### 3.2.1 Subject Matter Expert Review

Every controlled document must undergo review by at least one subject matter expert (SME) with demonstrable knowledge of the document's content area. The SME review evaluates:

- Technical accuracy and completeness of content
- Applicability and feasibility of requirements
- Consistency with operational realities
- Alignment with other relevant BRHI documents

The SME review must be documented, with the reviewer's name, title, date of review, and any comments or recommended changes recorded in the Document Management System.

#### 3.2.2 Compliance Review

All policies (POL), governance documents (GOV), and standard operating procedures (SOP) must undergo compliance review by the Corporate Compliance department. The compliance review evaluates:

- Adherence to applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations
- Consistency with BRHI's Code of Business Conduct and Ethics
- Proper classification and handling of sensitive information
- Appropriate inclusion of regulatory references

#### 3.2.3 Legal Review

Legal review by the BRHI Legal Department is required for:

- All policies (POL) before initial publication
- All governance documents (GOV) before initial publication
- Any document that addresses regulatory compliance, contractual obligations, or liability
- Any document involving associate rights, disciplinary procedures, or termination processes
- Any document that references or modifies terms of associate benefits or compensation

Legal review is optional but recommended for SOPs, BPs, and REF documents at the discretion of the document owner.

#### 3.2.4 Review Timeline

All reviews shall be completed within the following timeframes:

| Review Type | Target Duration | Maximum Duration |
|-------------|----------------|------------------|
| SME Review | 5 business days | 10 business days |
| Compliance Review | 5 business days | 10 business days |
| Legal Review | 10 business days | 20 business days |

If a reviewer does not respond within the maximum duration, the document owner may escalate to the reviewer's direct supervisor for resolution.

### 3.3 Approval

#### 3.3.1 Approval Matrix

Document approval authority is determined by document type and classification level:

| Document Type | Public / Internal | Confidential | Restricted |
|---------------|-------------------|--------------|------------|
| POL (Policy) | Chief Administrative Officer | Chief Administrative Officer + General Counsel | CEO + General Counsel |
| SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) | VP of applicable functional area | SVP of applicable functional area | CEO |
| BP (Business Process) | Director of applicable department | VP of applicable functional area | SVP of applicable functional area |
| GOV (Governance) | Board of Directors or designated Committee | Board of Directors or designated Committee | Board of Directors |
| ORG (Organizational) | Chief Administrative Officer | Chief Administrative Officer | CEO |
| REF (Reference) | Director of Corporate Compliance | VP of Corporate Compliance | Chief Administrative Officer |

#### 3.3.2 Approval Process

Approvals shall be executed electronically through the Document Management System's workflow engine. Each approver receives an automated notification and can approve, reject, or request changes. An approval record including the approver's name, title, date, and decision is automatically captured.

#### 3.3.3 Approval Requirements

- All required approvals must be obtained before a document can advance to publication.
- No single individual may both draft and approve the same document.
- If an approver rejects a document, it returns to the draft status with documented reasons, and the revision cycle restarts from Section 3.2.

### 3.4 Publication

#### 3.4.1 Document Management System

All controlled documents shall be published exclusively through the BRHI Document Management System (DMS). The DMS serves as the single source of truth for all controlled documentation. Copies stored on local drives, shared folders, email, or other unauthorized locations are considered uncontrolled and may not be relied upon.

#### 3.4.2 Version Control at Publication

Upon publication, the DMS automatically:

1. Assigns the approved version number to the document
2. Sets the effective date (defaulting to the publication date unless otherwise specified)
3. Archives the previous version (if applicable) with a superseded status
4. Updates the document index and search index
5. Triggers distribution notifications per Section 3.5

#### 3.4.3 Access Control at Publication

Access to the published document is configured based on the document's classification level as defined in Section 5. The document owner specifies the access level at the time of submission for approval, and the DMS enforces the specified access restrictions.

### 3.5 Distribution

#### 3.5.1 Distribution Methods

| Audience | Method | Frequency |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| All Associates | DMS notification + email digest | Upon publication or revision |
| Department / Function | DMS notification + email | Upon publication or revision |
| Named Individuals | DMS notification + secure email | Upon publication or revision |
| Board of Directors | Secure board portal | Upon publication or revision |
| External Parties (if applicable) | Secure file share with audit trail | As authorized by document owner |

#### 3.5.2 Distribution Responsibilities

The document owner is responsible for verifying that distribution has occurred correctly. The DMS provides distribution confirmation reports that the document owner must review within five business days of publication.

### 3.6 Training

#### 3.6.1 Acknowledgment Requirements

All associates are required to acknowledge receipt and understanding of newly published or revised policies (POL) and governance documents (GOV) within the following timeframes:

| Document Classification | Acknowledgment Deadline |
|------------------------|------------------------|
| Public | 30 calendar days from publication |
| Internal | 30 calendar days from publication |
| Confidential | 15 calendar days from publication |
| Restricted | 10 calendar days from publication |

Acknowledgment is captured electronically through the DMS and includes the associate's name, employee ID, date, and document version acknowledged.

#### 3.6.2 Training Requirements

When a new or revised document introduces significant procedural changes, the document owner shall coordinate with the Training and Development department to deliver supplementary training. Training may include:

- Instructor-led classroom sessions
- Virtual live sessions
- Self-paced e-learning modules
- Job aids and quick reference guides

#### 3.6.3 Non-Compliance with Acknowledgment

Associates who fail to acknowledge a required document by the deadline receive an automated reminder. If acknowledgment is not received within seven calendar days after the reminder, the associate's direct supervisor is notified. Continued non-compliance is escalated through the management chain and may be addressed through the BRHI progressive disciplinary process.

### 3.7 Periodic Review

#### 3.7.1 Review Schedule

All controlled documents must undergo periodic review according to the following schedule:

| Document Type | Review Frequency | Responsible Party |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| POL (Policy) | Annually (12 months from effective date) | Document owner + Corporate Compliance |
| SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) | Semi-annually (6 months from effective date) | Document owner + applicable department leadership |
| BP (Business Process) | As needed, but no less than every 18 months | Document owner + process stakeholders |
| GOV (Governance) | Annually (12 months from effective date) | Corporate Secretary + Corporate Compliance |
| ORG (Organizational) | As needed, but no less than every 18 months | Human Resources + Corporate Compliance |
| REF (Reference) | Annually (12 months from effective date) | Document owner |

#### 3.7.2 Review Process

The periodic review evaluates whether the document:

1. Remains accurate and reflects current business practices
2. Complies with all applicable laws and regulations
3. Aligns with BRHI's strategic direction and organizational structure
4. Contains no contradictions with other controlled documents
5. Is properly classified and accessible to the intended audience

#### 3.7.3 Review Outcomes

At the conclusion of a periodic review, the reviewer records one of the following outcomes:

- **No Change Required** — The document remains current and accurate. The effective date and version are unchanged; the next review date is extended.
- **Minor Revision Required** — The document requires non-substantive changes (formatting, clarifications, corrections of minor errors). The document proceeds through the revision process per Section 3.8 with a minor version increment.
- **Major Revision Required** — The document requires substantive changes reflecting new regulations, processes, or organizational structures. The document proceeds through the full revision process per Section 3.8 with a major version increment.
- **Retirement Required** — The document is no longer needed and should be retired per Section 3.9.

#### 3.7.4 Overdue Reviews

The DMS generates automated reminders to the document owner 30 days and 7 days before the review due date. If a review becomes overdue by more than 15 calendar days, the document owner's direct supervisor and Corporate Compliance are notified. Documents with overdue reviews exceeding 60 calendar days are flagged in the DMS with a warning banner indicating the document may not reflect current practices.

### 3.8 Revision

#### 3.8.1 Change Request Process

Any BRHI associate may request a change to a controlled document by submitting a Document Change Request (DCR) through the DMS. The DCR includes:

- Document ID and title
- Requestor's name, title, and department
- Description of the requested change
- Business justification for the change
- Urgency level (Critical, High, Medium, Low)
- Supporting materials or references

#### 3.8.2 Change Request Evaluation

The document owner evaluates each DCR within five business days of submission. The evaluation determines whether the request:

- Is valid and justified
- Requires a minor or major revision
- Impacts other controlled documents
- Requires compliance or legal review

The document owner responds to the requestor with the decision (Approved, Approved with Modifications, Deferred, or Rejected) and the rationale.

#### 3.8.3 Urgency-Based Timelines

| Urgency Level | Description | Target Completion |
|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Critical | Regulatory mandate, safety risk, or legal obligation | 5 business days |
| High | Significant operational impact or compliance gap | 15 business days |
| Medium | Operational improvement or process clarification | 30 business days |
| Low | Minor correction, formatting, or non-urgent enhancement | 60 business days |

#### 3.8.4 Revision Workflow

Upon approval of a DCR, the document enters the revision workflow:

1. The current published version is locked in the DMS and remains the active version.
2. A working copy is created in "In Revision" status.
3. The document owner (or designee) incorporates the approved changes.
4. The revised document proceeds through the review and approval process (Sections 3.2 and 3.3).
5. Upon approval, the revised version is published per Section 3.4.

#### 3.8.5 Emergency Revisions

In cases where an immediate change is required due to a regulatory mandate, safety emergency, or legal directive, the following expedited process applies:

1. The document owner or a member of the Executive Leadership Team may initiate an Emergency Change Request.
2. Verbal or email approval from the appropriate approver (per Section 3.3.1) is acceptable, provided it is documented in the DMS within 24 hours.
3. The revised document is published immediately.
4. The standard review and approval process must be completed retroactively within 15 business days of the emergency publication.
5. If the retroactive review identifies issues, a follow-up revision is initiated under the standard process.

### 3.9 Retirement

#### 3.9.1 Retirement Criteria

A controlled document may be retired when:

- The document's content is no longer applicable due to organizational, regulatory, or operational changes
- The document's content has been fully incorporated into another controlled document
- The document is superseded by a new document with a different ID
- The document was created for a time-limited purpose that has been fulfilled

#### 3.9.2 Retirement Process

1. The document owner submits a Document Retirement Request through the DMS.
2. The request is reviewed and approved per the approval matrix in Section 3.3.1.
3. Upon approval, the document status is changed to "Retired" in the DMS.
4. The document is removed from active indexes and search results but retained in the archive.
5. All associates who previously acknowledged the document are notified of its retirement.
6. If the retirement affects operational procedures, the document owner ensures that replacement documentation or interim guidance is communicated.

#### 3.9.3 Archival Requirements

Retired documents are retained in the DMS archive with the following attributes preserved:

- Complete version history
- All review and approval records
- Acknowledgment records
- Change request history
- Retirement date and reason
- Approver of retirement

Retired documents remain accessible to Corporate Compliance, Legal, and Internal Audit for reference purposes.

## 4. Version Control Standards

### 4.1 Version Number Format

All BRHI controlled documents use a major.minor version numbering format:

```
v[major].[minor]
```

Examples: v1.0, v1.1, v1.2, v2.0, v2.1

### 4.2 Version Increment Rules

| Change Type | Version Increment | Examples |
|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| Initial publication | v1.0 | First release of a new document |
| Minor revision | Increment minor number | v1.0 → v1.1 (formatting, clarification, minor corrections) |
| Major revision | Increment major number, reset minor to 0 | v1.2 → v2.0 (substantive changes, new sections, regulatory updates) |

### 4.3 Change Log Requirements

Every version of every controlled document must include a completed Change Log table in the following format:

| Version | Date | Author | Change Description |
|---------|------|--------|--------------------|

The Change Description must be sufficiently detailed to allow a reader to understand the nature and scope of changes without comparing the full document text. At minimum, it must include:

- Sections affected
- Nature of the change (added, modified, removed)
- Brief summary of the change content
- Reference to the DCR number (if applicable)

### 4.4 Superseded Version Handling

When a new version is published, the previous version is automatically assigned "Superseded" status in the DMS. Superseded versions are retained indefinitely and are accessible to authorized users for audit and reference purposes. Only the current version is displayed in standard search results and indexes.

## 5. Document Classification

### 5.1 Classification Levels

| Level | Description | Marking | Examples |
|-------|-------------|---------|----------|
| Public | No restriction on access or distribution. May be shared with external parties without authorization. | **Classification: Public** | Press releases, product descriptions, publicly posted policies |
| Internal | Available to all BRHI associates. Not for external distribution. | **Classification: Internal** | Standard operating procedures, organizational charts, training materials |
| Confidential | Available only to associates with a legitimate business need to know. Distribution on a need-to-know basis as determined by the document owner. | **Classification: Confidential** | Financial data, strategic plans, vendor contracts, associate performance data |
| Restricted | Available only to specifically named individuals. The most sensitive classification. | **Classification: Restricted** | Executive compensation, M&A documents, legal proceedings, security protocols |

### 5.2 Classification Determination

The document owner is responsible for assigning the appropriate classification at the time of document creation. If uncertain, the document owner should consult with Corporate Compliance. Classification must be reviewed at each periodic review and whenever circumstances change.

### 5.3 Classification Marking

All controlled documents must display their classification level:

- **Header** — The classification level appears in the document header on every page.
- **Footer** — A confidentiality statement corresponding to the classification level appears in the footer on every page.

Classification markings by level:

| Level | Footer Statement |
|-------|-----------------|
| Public | "This document is approved for public distribution." |
| Internal | "This document is the property of BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. and is intended for internal use only." |
| Confidential | "This document is the property of BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. and contains confidential information. Distribution is limited to authorized associates with a legitimate business need. Unauthorized distribution is prohibited." |
| Restricted | "RESTRICTED — This document is the property of BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. and contains highly sensitive information. Access is limited to specifically named individuals. Unauthorized access, copying, or distribution is strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action and legal proceedings." |

### 5.4 Reclassification

A document's classification may be changed through the standard revision process. Downgrading classification (e.g., from Confidential to Internal) requires approval by the document owner and Corporate Compliance. Upgrading classification (e.g., from Internal to Confidential) requires approval by the document owner and the next-level management approver.

| Reference Documents | Duration of currency + 3 years after supersession | DMS Archive | Secure digital deletion |
| Document Change Requests | 7 years from resolution date | DMS Archive | Secure digital deletion |
| Review and Approval Records | 7 years from document retirement | DMS Archive | Secure digital deletion |
| Acknowledgment Records | 7 years from document supersession or associate separation | DMS Archive | Secure digital deletion |
| Training Records | 7 years from completion date | DMS Archive + HR System | Secure digital deletion |
| Audit Trail Data | 10 years from date of entry | DMS Archive | Secure digital deletion |

### 6.2 Legal Holds

When litigation is reasonably anticipated or pending, the Legal Department may issue a Litigation Hold Notice that suspends the destruction of specified records regardless of the retention schedule. All associates must comply immediately with Litigation Hold Notices. Destruction of records subject to a litigation hold may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, as well as potential legal liability.

## 7. Access Control

### 7.1 Access Matrix

| Role | Public | Internal | Confidential | Restricted |
|------|--------|----------|--------------|------------|
| All Associates | Read | Read | No Access | No Access |
| Department Members | Read | Read | Read (own department only) | No Access |
| Document Owner | Read/Write | Read/Write | Read/Write | Read/Write |
| Department Director | Read | Read | Read (own department) | Read (if named) |
| VP / SVP | Read | Read | Read (own functional area) | Read (if named) |
| Corporate Compliance | Read | Read | Read | Read |
| Legal Department | Read | Read | Read | Read |
| Internal Audit | Read | Read | Read | Read |
| Executive Leadership | Read | Read | Read | Read |
| Named Individuals (Restricted) | Read | Read | Read | Read |
| External Parties | Read (if published externally) | No Access | No Access | No Access |

### 7.2 Access Provisioning

Access to controlled documents is provisioned as follows:

- **Public documents** — Available to all authenticated DMS users automatically.
- **Internal documents** — Available to all authenticated DMS users automatically.
- **Confidential documents** — Access is granted by the document owner on a need-to-know basis. Access requests are submitted through the DMS and documented with a business justification.
- **Restricted documents** — Access is granted exclusively to named individuals specified by the document owner and approved by the appropriate authority per Section 3.3.1.

### 7.3 Access Reviews

Access control lists for all Confidential and Restricted documents are reviewed by the document owner:

- At each periodic review (per Section 3.7)
- Upon the departure or role change of any named individual
- Upon request by Corporate Compliance or Internal Audit

Stale or unnecessary access is revoked promptly upon review.

- **Cross-reference navigation** — Hyperlinked references between related documents enabling seamless navigation.
- **Saved searches** — Users can save frequently used search queries for quick access.
- **Recent and popular** — The DMS displays recently accessed and most-viewed documents on the user's dashboard.

## 10. Audit Trail

### 10.1 Change History

The DMS automatically captures a complete change history for every controlled document, including:

- All versions created, with timestamps and authors
- Content changes at the section level (sections added, modified, or removed)
- Metadata changes (classification, owner, title, description)
- Status transitions (Draft, In Review, Pending Approval, Published, Superseded, Retired)
- Change request references

### 10.2 Review History

The DMS maintains a record of all review activities, including:

- Reviewer name, title, and department
- Date review was initiated and completed
- Review type (SME, Compliance, Legal, Periodic)
- Review outcome and comments
- Conditions or recommendations provided

### 10.3 Approval History

The DMS records all approval activities, including:

- Approver name, title, and department
- Date approval was requested and completed
- Decision (Approved, Rejected, Changes Requested)
- Comments and conditions
- Delegated approvals (if applicable, with delegation authorization)

### 10.4 Audit Trail Access

Audit trail data is accessible to:

- **Corporate Compliance** — Full access to all audit trail data.
- **Internal Audit** — Full access to all audit trail data during active audits.
- **Legal Department** — Full access to all audit trail data for legal and regulatory purposes.
- **Document Owner** — Access to audit trail data for documents they own.
- **Executive Leadership** — Access upon request, coordinated through Corporate Compliance.

### 10.5 Audit Trail Integrity

Audit trail records are immutable and cannot be modified or deleted by any user, including system administrators. Audit trail data is backed up daily and stored in a separate, secured repository to ensure integrity and availability for regulatory and legal inquiries.
