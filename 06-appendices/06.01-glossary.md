# BRHI Glossary of Terms

**Document ID:** BRHI-REF-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Classification:** Internal
**Owner:** Corporate Communications

---

## Purpose

This glossary provides clear, unambiguous definitions for terms and abbreviations used across all BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. (BRHI) documentation, policies, standard operating procedures, business processes, and communications. All associates should reference this document when encountering unfamiliar terminology.

---

## General Corporate Terms

**3PL (Third-Party Logistics)**
An external provider that manages outsourced logistics operations including warehousing, transportation, and distribution on behalf of BRHI.

**Associate**
Any individual employed by or contracted to work for BRHI, including full-time, part-time, seasonal, and temporary workers.

**BOPIS (Buy Online, Pick Up In Store)**
A fulfillment model allowing customers to place orders through the BRHI website or mobile application and collect merchandise at a designated in-store pickup area.

**B2B (Business to Business)**
Commercial transactions between BRHI and other businesses, including contractor sales, institutional accounts, and wholesale relationships.

**B2C (Business to Consumer)**
Direct retail transactions between BRHI and individual end-use customers.

**CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)**
The mean annual growth rate of a metric over a specified period longer than one year, used in BRHI strategic planning.

**COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)**
The direct costs attributable to the production or procurement of goods sold by BRHI during a reporting period.

**DC (Distribution Center)**
A BRHI-operated or third-party facility used for receiving, storing, and redistributing merchandise to retail locations or directly to customers.

**DTC (Direct to Consumer)**
A sales channel where BRHI ships merchandise directly to the customer's address, bypassing the physical store.

**EIN (Employer Identification Number)**
A unique tax identification number assigned to BRHI by the Internal Revenue Service for business tax reporting.

**FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)**
A compiled list of common inquiries and their answers, maintained for both internal use and external customer communication.

**FY (Fiscal Year)**
BRHI's twelve-month accounting period running from February 1 through January 31 of the following calendar year.

**GM (General Manager)**
The senior associate responsible for overall operations and performance of a single BRHI retail location.

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**
A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively BRHI achieves key business objectives at the corporate, regional, district, or store level.

**MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)**
The smallest quantity of a product that a vendor is willing to sell or that BRHI will purchase in a single order to maintain cost efficiency.

**MVP (Minimum Viable Product)**
The most pared-down version of a new product, service, or initiative that can still be deployed to gather validated learning, as used in BRHI pilot programs.

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)**
A customer loyalty metric calculated by subtracting the percentage of detractors from the percentage of promoters in BRHI customer satisfaction surveys.

**OKR (Objectives and Key Results)**
A goal-setting framework used by BRHI leadership to define and track measurable outcomes aligned with strategic priorities.

**ROI (Return on Investment)**
A performance measure evaluating the efficiency or profitability of a BRHI investment, calculated as net benefit divided by cost of the investment.

**SBU (Strategic Business Unit)**
A discrete operational division within BRHI, such as Pro Services, E-Commerce, or Retail Operations, that operates with its own strategic direction.

**SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)**
A unique alphanumeric identifier assigned to each distinct product and its variants for inventory tracking, pricing, and sales analysis purposes.

**SLA (Service Level Agreement)**
A formal commitment between BRHI and a service provider (or between internal BRHI departments) defining expected performance standards and remedies for non-compliance.

**SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)**
A documented set of step-by-step instructions compiled to help BRHI associates carry out routine operations consistently and in compliance with company standards.

**SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)**
A strategic planning framework used by BRHI to evaluate internal capabilities and external market conditions.

**TBD (To Be Determined)**
Indicates that a decision, value, or assignment has not yet been finalized in BRHI planning documents.

**UPC (Universal Product Code)**
A standardized barcode symbology printed on retail merchandise packaging, scanned at the point of sale for identification and pricing.

**YTD (Year to Date)**
A financial and operational measurement covering the period from the start of the current fiscal year to the present date.

## Financial Terms

**AP (Accounts Payable)**
The aggregate amount of money BRHI owes to its vendors and suppliers for goods and services received but not yet paid.

**AR (Accounts Receivable)**
The aggregate amount of money owed to BRHI by customers, contractors, and business accounts for goods or services delivered but not yet paid.

**BCR (Benefit Cost Ratio)**
A ratio used in capital expenditure analysis at BRHI, comparing the total expected benefits of a project to its total expected costs.

**CapEx (Capital Expenditure)**
Funds used by BRHI to acquire, upgrade, or maintain physical assets such as stores, distribution centers, equipment, and technology infrastructure.

**CFFO (Cash Flow from Operations)**
The net cash generated by BRHI's core business activities during a reporting period, excluding investing and financing activities.

**COGM (Cost of Goods Manufactured)**
Where applicable to BRHI private label products, the total cost incurred to produce finished goods ready for sale.

**DPO (Days Payable Outstanding)**
The average number of days BRHI takes to pay its trade payables, calculated as accounts payable divided by cost of goods sold multiplied by the number of days in the period.

**DSO (Days Sales Outstanding)**
The average number of days it takes BRHI to collect payment after a sale is made, calculated as accounts receivable divided by total credit sales multiplied by the number of days in the period.

**EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization)**
A measure of BRHI's overall financial performance used as an alternative to net income for evaluating operational profitability.

**FIFO (First In, First Out)**
An inventory valuation method in which goods produced or acquired first are assumed to be sold first; also a physical rotation methodology used in BRHI warehouses and stores.

**GMROI (Gross Margin Return on Investment)**
A metric evaluating inventory profitability, calculated by dividing gross margin by average inventory cost, used by BRHI merchandising to assess product performance.

**Gross Margin**
The difference between net sales revenue and cost of goods sold, expressed as a percentage of net sales revenue.

**IRR (Internal Rate of Return)**
The discount rate at which the net present value of a BRHI capital project equals zero, used to evaluate investment attractiveness.

**LTL (Less Than Truckload)**
A freight transportation mode used by BRHI for shipments that do not require a full trailer, consolidating multiple smaller shipments for cost efficiency.

**Markdown**
A reduction in the original selling price of merchandise, applied to clear slow-moving, discontinued, or excess inventory.

**NPV (Net Present Value)**
The difference between the present value of cash inflows and outflows over a period of time, used in BRHI capital budgeting decisions.

**OpEx (Operating Expenditure)**
Ongoing costs for running BRHI's day-to-day operations, including rent, utilities, salaries, and supplies, as distinct from capital expenditures.

**Open-to-Buy (OTB)**
A budgeting and planning tool that calculates the amount of merchandise available for purchase during a given period based on planned sales, markdowns, and desired ending inventory.

**PIP (Period Inventory Planning)**
BRHI's structured approach to planning inventory purchases by period, aligning stock levels with forecasted demand.

**Shrink (Shrinkage)**
The loss of inventory attributable to theft (internal or external), administrative errors, vendor fraud, or damage, measured as a percentage of total inventory or sales.

**Same-Store Sales (Comparable Store Sales)**
A financial metric comparing the revenue of BRHI stores that have been open for at least twelve months across equivalent reporting periods, excluding the impact of new store openings or closures.

**WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital)**
The rate that BRHI is expected to pay on average to all its security holders to finance its assets, used as a hurdle rate for investment decisions.

## Supply Chain Terms

**ASN (Advanced Shipping Notice)**
An electronic document sent by a vendor to BRHI prior to delivery, detailing the contents, quantities, and expected arrival time of a shipment.

**BOL (Bill of Lading)**
A legal transport document issued by a carrier to BRHI (or its vendor) that details the type, quantity, and destination of goods being carried.

**Cross-dock**
A logistics practice in which incoming merchandise is unloaded from an inbound transport and directly loaded onto outbound transport with minimal or no storage time in between, used at BRHI distribution centers for high-velocity items.

**Cycle Count**
An inventory auditing procedure in which a subset of BRHI inventory is counted on a rotating schedule rather than performing a full physical inventory, ensuring continuous accuracy.

**Drop Ship**
A fulfillment method in which BRHI sells merchandise to a customer but the vendor ships the product directly to the customer without the goods passing through a BRHI facility.

**EDI (Electronic Data Interchange)**
The electronic exchange of standardized business documents (purchase orders, invoices, advance shipping notices) between BRHI and its trading partners.

**FIFO (First In, First Out)**
A warehouse rotation methodology ensuring that the oldest inventory is shipped or sold first, reducing the risk of obsolescence and spoilage.

**FOB (Free on Board)**
A shipping term designating the point at which ownership and responsibility for goods transfer from the vendor to BRHI (e.g., FOB Origin, FOB Destination).

**FTL (Full Truckload)**
A freight transportation mode in which a shipment occupies an entire trailer, typically used by BRHI for large-volume replenishment orders.

**Inventory Turns (Inventory Turnover)**
A ratio showing how many times BRHI's inventory is sold and replaced over a given period, calculated as cost of goods sold divided by average inventory.

**JIT (Just In Time)**
An inventory management strategy that aligns raw material orders and finished goods deliveries with production or sales schedules, minimizing holding costs.

**Lead Time**
The total elapsed time from placing a purchase order with a vendor to receiving the goods at a BRHI location, including vendor processing, production, and transit time.

**Lot Number (Batch Number)**
A unique identifier assigned to a specific quantity of merchandise produced or received together, enabling traceability for quality control and recall purposes.

**PO (Purchase Order)**
A formal commercial document issued by BRHI to a vendor authorizing the purchase of specific goods or services at agreed-upon prices, quantities, and delivery terms.

**RMA (Return Merchandise Authorization)**
A numbered authorization issued by BRHI (or by a vendor to BRHI) permitting the return of merchandise for credit, replacement, or repair.

**Safety Stock**
A calculated quantity of extra inventory maintained at BRHI locations as a buffer against demand variability and supply chain disruptions.

**SCM (Supply Chain Management)**
The end-to-end coordination of materials, information, and finances as they move from vendor to BRHI to the end customer.

**SKU Rationalization**
The process of evaluating and optimizing the assortment of SKUs carried by BRHI to maximize profitability and minimize redundancy.

**VMI (Vendor Managed Inventory)**
An arrangement in which the vendor monitors BRHI's inventory levels and is responsible for replenishing stock according to agreed-upon parameters.

**WMS (Warehouse Management System)**
Software used to control and manage BRHI warehouse and distribution center operations, including receiving, putaway, picking, packing, and shipping.

## Technology Terms

**API (Application Programming Interface)**
A set of protocols and tools that allows different software applications used by BRHI to communicate and share data with each other.

**BI (Business Intelligence)**
Technologies, applications, and practices used by BRHI to collect, integrate, analyze, and present business data to support better decision-making.

**CRM (Customer Relationship Management)**
A technology platform used by BRHI to manage interactions with current and prospective customers, track sales leads, and support loyalty programs.

**DMS (Document Management System)**
Software used by BRHI to store, manage, track, and distribute electronic documents and records throughout their lifecycle.

**ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)**
BRHI's integrated software platform that manages core business processes including finance, supply chain, human resources, and operations in a unified system.

**MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)**
A security mechanism requiring BRHI associates to provide two or more forms of verification (e.g., password and a one-time code) before accessing company systems.

**MPOS (Mobile Point of Sale)**
A wireless, portable device used by BRHI associates on the sales floor to process customer transactions, check inventory, and look up product information.

**POS (Point of Sale)**
The hardware and software system used at BRHI checkout locations to process sales transactions, accept payments, and capture customer data.

**RPA (Robotic Process Automation)**
Technology used to automate repetitive, rule-based tasks within BRHI's back-office operations, freeing associates for higher-value activities.

**SaaS (Software as a Service)**
A software licensing and delivery model in which applications are hosted in the cloud and accessed by BRHI associates via the internet on a subscription basis.

**SSO (Single Sign-On)**
An authentication method that allows BRHI associates to access multiple applications and systems with a single set of login credentials.

**UAT (User Acceptance Testing)**
The final phase of BRHI software testing in which end users verify that a system or application meets business requirements before deployment.

**VPN (Virtual Private Network)**
A secure, encrypted connection used by BRHI associates to access the company network remotely, protecting data in transit.

## Safety Terms

**ANSI (American National Standards Institute)**
A private nonprofit organization that oversees the development of voluntary consensus standards for products, services, processes, and systems used in BRHI operations.

**GHS (Globally Harmonized System)**
An international framework for standardizing the classification and labeling of chemicals, adopted by BRHI to ensure consistent hazard communication.

**HAZCOM (Hazard Communication)**
A regulatory program requiring BRHI to inform and train associates about the chemical hazards present in the workplace through labeling, safety data sheets, and training.

**Incident Rate (Recordable Incident Rate)**
A measure of the number of work-related injuries and illnesses per 100 full-time associates during a one-year period, tracked and reported by BRHI Safety.

**LOTO (Lockout/Tagout)**
A safety procedure used in BRHI maintenance and repair operations to ensure that dangerous equipment is properly shut off and cannot be restarted before maintenance is complete.

**Near Miss**
An unplanned event that did not result in injury, illness, or damage but had the potential to do so; reported and tracked by BRHI to prevent future incidents.

**OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Administration)**
The federal agency responsible for enforcing workplace safety and health regulations applicable to BRHI operations.

**PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)**
Equipment worn by BRHI associates to minimize exposure to hazards that can cause serious workplace injuries and illnesses, including hard hats, safety glasses, gloves, and steel-toed boots.

**RIA (Root Cause Analysis)**
A systematic process used by BRHI Safety to identify the fundamental cause of an incident or near miss so that corrective actions can be implemented to prevent recurrence.

**SDS (Safety Data Sheet)**
A standardized document providing information on the properties, hazards, handling, storage, and emergency measures for a chemical product, maintained for all hazardous substances in BRHI locations.

## Human Resources Terms

**ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)**
Federal law requiring BRHI to provide reasonable accommodations to qualified associates and applicants with disabilities and to ensure non-discriminatory practices.

**AT-WILL Employment**
An employment doctrine under which either BRHI or the associate may terminate the employment relationship at any time, for any lawful reason, with or without notice.

**COBRA (Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act)**
Federal law providing BRHI associates and their dependents the right to continue group health insurance coverage for a limited period after a qualifying event such as job loss or reduced hours.

**EEO (Equal Employment Opportunity)**
The principle that all BRHI employment decisions are made without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic.

**ESPP (Employee Stock Purchase Plan)**
A program allowing eligible BRHI associates to purchase company stock at a discounted price through after-tax payroll deductions.

**FLSA (Fair Labor Standards Act)**
Federal law establishing minimum wage, overtime pay eligibility, recordkeeping, and child labor standards affecting BRHI associates.

**FMLA (Family and Medical Leave Act)**
Federal law entitling eligible BRHI associates to unpaid, job-protected leave for specified family and medical reasons with continuation of group health insurance coverage.

**FT / PT (Full-Time / Part-Time)**
Employment status classifications at BRHI: full-time associates regularly work 30 or more hours per week; part-time associates regularly work fewer than 30 hours per week.

**I-9 (Employment Eligibility Verification)**
A federal form that must be completed by both BRHI and each newly hired associate to verify the associate's identity and authorization to work in the United States.

**Open Enrollment**
An annual period during which BRHI associates may enroll in, change, or cancel their benefits elections for the upcoming plan year.

**PIP (Performance Improvement Plan)**
A formal document used by BRHI management to identify specific performance deficiencies and outline measurable goals and timelines for improvement.

**PTO (Paid Time Off)**
BRHI's consolidated leave policy providing associates with a bank of paid hours to use for vacation, personal time, or illness.

**W-2 (Wage and Tax Statement)**
A federal tax form issued by BRHI to each associate annually, reporting wages earned and taxes withheld during the calendar year.

**W-4 (Employee's Withholding Certificate)**
A federal tax form completed by each BRHI associate to indicate tax withholding preferences to the company's payroll department.

**Workers' Compensation**
A state-mandated insurance program providing benefits to BRHI associates who suffer work-related injuries or illnesses, including medical expenses and lost wages.

## Loss Prevention Terms

**BOB (Bottom of Basket)**
A loss prevention check performed at checkout to ensure items placed in the lower rack of a shopping cart are scanned and paid for before the customer exits.

**CCTV (Closed-Circuit Television)**
A video surveillance system deployed across BRHI locations to monitor premises, deter theft, and support investigations.

**EAS (Electronic Article Surveillance)**
An anti-theft technology using tags or labels attached to merchandise that trigger an alarm if not deactivated or removed at the point of sale.

**Exception-Based Reporting (EBR)**
An analytical approach used by BRHI Loss Prevention that identifies anomalous transaction patterns (e.g., excessive voids, refunds, or discounts) for further investigation.

**Internal Theft**
Theft or fraud committed by BRHI associates, including theft of merchandise, cash, or company property, as well as fraudulent transactions.

**LPO (Loss Prevention Officer)**
A trained BRHI associate responsible for preventing theft, conducting investigations, and ensuring compliance with loss prevention policies and procedures.

**ORC (Organized Retail Crime)**
Coordinated theft of BRHI merchandise by professional crime rings for the purpose of resale, as distinct from opportunistic shoplifting.

**OS&D (Over, Short, and Damaged)**
A freight claim classification used when received shipments do not match the expected quantities or condition, indicating items are missing, extra, or damaged.

**Shrinkage Allowance**
A negotiated percentage or fixed amount agreed upon with a vendor that accounts for anticipated loss of merchandise during transit or handling.

**Terroristic Threat**
Any communicated threat to commit violence at a BRHI location, requiring immediate response per company protocols and coordination with law enforcement.

## Merchandising Terms

**Assortment Plan**
A detailed merchandise plan that specifies which SKUs will be carried in a particular BRHI department or category, including depth and breadth of selection.

**BOGO (Buy One, Get One)**
A promotional pricing structure offering a customer a free or discounted item with the purchase of a qualifying item.

**Comps (Comparable Store Sales)**
See Same-Store Sales. The year-over-year performance comparison of BRHI stores open for twelve months or more.

**Dead Stock**
Inventory that has not sold within a defined period and has no foreseeable demand, requiring markdown or liquidation action.

**Endcap**
A promotional merchandise display positioned at the end of a store aisle, designated for high-visibility or seasonal items to drive impulse purchases.

**Facing (Fronting)**
The practice of pulling merchandise forward to the front edge of the shelf to create a full, neat, and shoppable presentation for BRHI customers.

**Planogram**
A detailed visual diagram or model indicating the placement of retail products on shelves and displays, designed to maximize sales and optimize space utilization in BRHI stores.

**POP (Point of Purchase)**
In-store marketing materials and displays placed near the checkout area or product displays to encourage additional purchases.

**Private Label**
Products manufactured for and branded exclusively under a BRHI-owned brand name, typically offering higher margins than national brand equivalents.

**Product Life Cycle**
The progression of a product through introduction, growth, maturity, and decline stages, informing BRHI merchandising and markdown strategies.

**Retail Price**
The price at which BRHI sells merchandise to the end consumer, as distinct from wholesale cost or manufacturer's suggested retail price (MSRP).

**SKU Rationalization**
The systematic process of evaluating each SKU in BRHI's assortment to determine whether it should be retained, replaced, or discontinued based on sales performance, margin contribution, and strategic fit.

## Regulatory and Compliance Terms

**CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)**
State privacy law granting California residents certain rights regarding their personal information collected by BRHI, including the right to access, delete, and opt out of data sales.

**CFPB (Consumer Financial Protection Bureau)**
Federal agency that regulates consumer financial products and services, relevant to BRHI's consumer credit offerings and payment processing.

**CPRA (California Privacy Rights Act)**
An amendment to the CCPA expanding consumer privacy rights and imposing additional obligations on BRHI regarding the handling of personal data.

**DOT (Department of Transportation)**
Federal agency regulating transportation of hazardous materials, relevant to BRHI's handling and shipping of regulated products.

**EPA (Environmental Protection Agency)**
Federal agency responsible for environmental regulations applicable to BRHI operations, including waste disposal, chemical handling, and emissions.

**FTC (Federal Trade Commission)**
Federal agency enforcing consumer protection and antitrust laws applicable to BRHI's advertising, marketing, and competitive practices.

**PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)**
A set of security standards for organizations that handle credit card data, with which BRHI must comply to protect cardholder information.

**SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley Act)**
Federal law establishing requirements for financial reporting and internal controls, applicable to BRHI's financial processes and auditing procedures.

## Quality and Process Terms

**Benchmarking**
The practice of comparing BRHI's processes, performance metrics, and practices against industry best practices or leading competitors to identify improvement opportunities.

**CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action)**
A systematic approach used by BRHI to investigate the root cause of nonconformities or incidents, implement corrective measures, and prevent recurrence.

**Kaizen**
A philosophy of continuous improvement involving all BRHI associates, focusing on incremental changes to processes that enhance quality, efficiency, and safety.

**Muda (Waste)**
Activities that consume resources without creating value, identified and eliminated by BRHI in its process improvement initiatives, including overproduction, waiting, transportation, overprocessing, inventory, motion, and defects.

**PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act)**
A four-step management method used by BRHI for the continuous improvement of processes and products.

**QA (Quality Assurance)**
A proactive process used by BRHI to ensure products and services meet established quality standards before reaching the customer.

**QC (Quality Control)**
A reactive process used by BRHI to identify defects in products or services through inspection, testing, and review after production.

**Six Sigma**
A data-driven methodology used by BRHI to reduce process variation and defects, targeting a defect rate of no more than 3.4 defects per million opportunities.
