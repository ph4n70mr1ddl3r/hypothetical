## 1. Document Control for This File

### 1.1 Purpose

This document serves as the master revision history for all controlled documents within the BuildRight Home Improvement, Inc. (BRHI) documentation suite. It provides a centralized record of all document versions, review dates, change summaries, and approval authorities, enabling associates and auditors to trace the evolution of any BRHI controlled document.

### 1.2 Maintenance Responsibility

This document is maintained by the Corporate Compliance department. Updates are made whenever:

- A new controlled document is published for the first time
- An existing controlled document undergoes a version change (minor or major)
- A controlled document is retired
- A periodic review is completed (regardless of whether changes result)
- Approval authorities are modified

### 1.3 Update Frequency

This document is updated within five business days of any event described in Section 1.2.

### 1.4 Cross-References

Each entry in this master revision history corresponds to the individual Change Log table maintained within the respective controlled document. In the event of a discrepancy between this master record and an individual document's Change Log, the individual document's Change Log takes precedence, and this master record shall be corrected accordingly.

## 4. Approval Authority Matrix

| REF (Reference) | Designated REF Owner | Subject Matter Experts | Not required | Not required | Director of Corporate Compliance (+ VP for Confidential; CAO for Restricted) | VP of Corporate Compliance |

### 4.2 Approval Authority by Classification

| Classification | Standard Approver | Elevated Approver | Maximum Approver |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Public | Per document type matrix above | Not applicable | Not applicable |
| Internal | Per document type matrix above | Not applicable | Not applicable |
| Confidential | One level above standard approver for the document type | SVP or General Counsel | CEO |
| Restricted | Two levels above standard approver for the document type | CEO + General Counsel | Board of Directors |

### 4.3 Delegation of Approval Authority

Approval authority may be delegated in writing (email acceptable) under the following conditions:

1. The delegate must hold a position at or above the organizational level of the original approver.
2. Delegation must be specific to a document or a defined set of documents and must include a time-bound validity period not exceeding 90 days.
3. All delegations must be documented in the DMS audit trail.
4. The original approver remains accountable for the quality and appropriateness of the approval decision.
5. Delegation of Restricted document approval authority requires advance written consent from the CEO.

### 4.4 Emergency Approval Authority

In the event that the designated approver is unavailable and time-sensitive publication is required (e.g., regulatory mandate, safety directive), the following emergency approval chain applies:

1. The designated approver's direct supervisor may provide emergency approval.
2. If the direct supervisor is also unavailable, the next-level supervisor may provide emergency approval.
3. Emergency approvals must be ratified by the designated approver within 10 business days.
4. All emergency approvals are flagged in the DMS audit trail and included in the monthly compliance report.
