## 3. Oracle AI Agent Architecture

### 3.1 What Are AI Agents?

Oracle AI agents are autonomous software entities that:
- **Perceive**: Understand context from data, documents, and user interactions
- **Reason**: Apply business rules, machine learning models, and generative AI
- **Act**: Execute tasks, make recommendations, or trigger workflows
- **Learn**: Improve performance based on outcomes and feedback

Unlike traditional automation, AI agents can:
- Handle unstructured data (emails, documents, images)
- Make complex decisions with multiple variables
- Adapt to changing conditions in real-time
- Provide explainable reasoning for their actions

## 5. AI Governance Framework

### 5.1 Responsible AI Principles

#### 5.1.1 Transparency
- **Explainability**: All AI decisions must be explainable to business users and auditors
- **Documentation**: AI models, training data, and decision logic documented
- **Visibility**: Clear indication when users are interacting with AI vs. humans

#### 5.1.2 Fairness
- **Bias Testing**: Regular testing for algorithmic bias across demographics
- **Inclusive Design**: AI systems designed to serve diverse associate and customer populations
- **Monitoring**: Continuous monitoring of AI outcomes for disparate impact

#### 5.1.3 Privacy
- **Data Minimization**: AI uses only necessary data for intended purposes
- **Consent Management**: Clear consent for AI processing of personal data
- **Anonymization**: Personal data anonymized where possible for AI training

#### 5.1.4 Human Oversight
- **Human-in-the-Loop**: Critical decisions maintain human review and approval
- **Escalation Paths**: Clear processes for escalating AI decisions to humans
- **Override Capability**: Users can override AI recommendations when appropriate

#### 5.1.5 Accountability
- **Clear Ownership**: Defined ownership for AI outcomes and decisions
- **Audit Trails**: Complete audit trails for all AI decisions
- **Continuous Improvement**: Regular review and improvement of AI systems

### 5.2 AI Risk Management

#### 5.2.1 Risk Categories

| Risk Category | Description | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Model Risk** | AI models produce inaccurate or biased results | Model validation, performance monitoring, bias testing |
| **Data Risk** | AI uses poor quality or inappropriate data | Data governance, quality checks, privacy controls |
| **Operational Risk** | AI systems fail or behave unexpectedly | Redundancy, monitoring, human oversight |
| **Compliance Risk** | AI decisions violate regulations or policies | Compliance testing, audit trails, control frameworks |
| **Reputational Risk** | AI decisions damage brand or customer trust | Ethical guidelines, transparency, customer communication |

#### 5.2.2 Risk Controls

1. **Model Validation**: All AI models validated before production deployment
2. **Performance Monitoring**: Continuous monitoring of AI accuracy and performance
3. **Bias Testing**: Regular testing for algorithmic bias
4. **Explainability Requirements**: AI decisions must be explainable to auditors
5. **Human Review**: Critical decisions require human review and approval

### Appendix B: AI Prompt Engineering Guide

#### Effective AI Prompts for BuildRight:

1. **Be Specific**: "Show me DeWalt drill inventory in Southeast stores" vs. "Show inventory"
2. **Provide Context**: "For summer season, what lawn products should we stock?" vs. "What to stock?"
3. **Ask for Reasoning**: "Why do you recommend this supplier?" vs. "Recommend supplier"
4. **Request Alternatives**: "What are other options?" after AI recommendation
5. **Give Feedback**: "This recommendation is too conservative" to improve future responses
